Topic: representably order-reflecting maps

{% raw %}

Jonas Frey (Dec 27 2020 at 20:41):

A morphism f:A→Bf:A\to Bf:A→B in a category CCC is called monomorphism/monic, if all induced postcomposition maps C(X,A)→C(X,B)C(X,A)\to C(X,B)C(X,A)→C(X,B) are injective.

Is there an established term for a map f:A→Bf:A\to Bf:A→B in a locally ordered category CCC such that all induced postcomposition maps C(X,A)→C(X,B)C(X,A)\to C(X,B)C(X,A)→C(X,B) are order-reflecting?.

I know that such maps can be called "full", but that isn't the kind of analogy I'm looking for since "order-reflecting" as well as "full" are terms in concrete "enriching" categories, and I'm looking for a term for the corresponding "representable" notion in enriched categories.
