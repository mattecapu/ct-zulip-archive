Topic: Categorical understanding of subdivision

Brendan Murphy (Mar 27 2024 at 18:28):

Let Poly\mathsf{Poly}Poly denote the category of polytopes (defined as the convex hulls of finite collections of points) embedded in countable dimensional Euclidean space R∞=colimnRn\R^{\infty} = \mathrm{colim}_n \R^nR∞=colimn​Rn with affine maps between them. Recall that a polytopal complex is a finite collection of polytopes closed under taking faces and where the pairwise intersection of any two polytopes in the collection is a face of each. I believe we can understand these as certain objects in Psh⁡(Poly)\operatorname{Psh}(\mathsf{Poly})Psh(Poly). Any polytope has an underlying subset of R∞\R^{\infty}R∞ (by definition) and any polytopal complex does as well, just by taking the union of the underlying sets of each polytope in it. If PPP is a polytope then a subdivision of PPP is a polytopal complex KKK such that ∣K∣=∣P∣|K| = |P|∣K∣=∣P∣. Is there a more categorical definition of this property in terms of Poly\mathsf{Poly}Poly and its presheaf category? The awkward thing is that there's no obvious map from KKK to PPP or vice-versa which exhibits KKK as a subdivision of PPP, or at least I don't think so.
