John Baez (Jul 02 2025 at 20:59):

Jack Jia said:

We can regard the quaternions H\mathbb{H}H as a C\mathbb{C}C-vector space, and I believe this will make H\mathbb{H}H a monoid;

There's no complex algebra (= monoid object in the monoidal category of complex vector spaces) whose underlying real algebra is H\mathbb{H}H.

There are different ways to prove this. The quickest way is to notice that such a complex algebra would need to be 2-dimensional (i.e. have an 2-dimensional underlying complex vector space), and every 2-dimensional algebra over a field is commutative.

To see the latter fact, notice that every 2-dimensional algebra AAA over a field kkk has a basis 1,x1, x1,x for some x∈Ax \in Ax∈A. Thus element in such an algebra is of the form a1+bxa1 + bxa1+bx for a,b∈ka,b \in ka,b∈k, and all such elements commute.

(But perhaps you're not seeing why all such elements commute?)

John Baez (Jul 03 2025 at 03:35):

Jack Jia said:

For example, if I change the monoidal category to AlgR\mathsf{Alg}_RAlgR​, then a monoid will need to be an RRR-bimodule, is this correct?

This is a somewhat strange question since you're not saying what RRR is, and you're acting like we know what AlgR\mathsf{Alg}_RAlgR​ is. If RRR is a noncommutative ring, people don't talk about AlgR\mathsf{Alg}_RAlgR​, at least not very often.

If RRR is a noncommutative ring the category of left RRR-modules is not monoidal in a natural way, nor is the category of right RRR-modules. But the category of R,RR, RR,R-bimodules is monoidal. A monoid in here is not usually called an RRR-algebra, because when RRR is commutative it's not the same as the usual concept of RRR-algebra for commutative RRR. I've seen it called an RRR-ring or an RRR-bimodule algebra, and I find the latter term to be clearer.

(Note: I said if RRR is a noncommutative ring the category of left RRR-modules is not monoidal in a natural way. But of course we can sometimes choose a monoidal structure on this category! For example there's a standard way to do this if RRR is a bialgebra. I don't want to get into this too much, but a good example arises when RRR is a group algebra k[G]k[G]k[G] for some field kkk and some group GGG. Then the category of left RRR-modules gets a monoidal structure because it's equivalent to the category of representations of the group GGG on vector spaces over kkk, and this category has a well-known monoidal structure. A monoid in the category of left RRR-modules with this monoidal structure is the same as a kkk-algebra with an action of GGG as automorphisms.)
