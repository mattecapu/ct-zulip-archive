Topic: relating left and right actions

Matteo Capucci (he/him) (Nov 28 2023 at 10:51):

Sometimes an object XXX is acted upon by another object MMM both on the left and the right, but instead of forming a bimodule structure (meaning (mx)n=m(xn)(mx)n = m(xn)(mx)n=m(xn)), these two actions are such that mx=xmmx = xmmx=xm. This is clearly a trivial concept in sets, since this latter equation implies XXX is in fact receiving a left action of MMM (albeit factoring through M/[M,M]M/[M,M]M/[M,M].

In higher settings, however, this isn't necessarily the case.
The first place I encountered this is [[actegories]], in which one might have non-invertible maps mx→xmmx \to xmmx→xm (or invertible but non-trivial ones, chiefly in the case of actions of braided monoidal categories, which can be 'mirrored'). The second place is in _The formal theory of monads II_, where Lack and Street characterize the Kleisli cocompletion of a 2-category as the 2-category with object monads and morphisms 'intertwined modules', i.e. a 1-cell (a,t)→(b,s)(a,t) \to (b,s)(a,t)→(b,s) is an object xxx with a left ttt-action and a right sss-action and a map λ:xt→sx\lambda : xt \to sxλ:xt→sx satisfying some laws.

So, I'm curious if people have seen such structures around, or just have something interesting to say about these.
