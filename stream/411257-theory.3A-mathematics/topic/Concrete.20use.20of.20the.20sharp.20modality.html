Adrian Clough (May 18 2026 at 18:35):

Conceptually, the sharp modality tells you that your topos (let's call it E\mathcal{E}E) has a final object when viewed as on object in the 2-category of toposes, i.e., the unique geometric morphism E→S\mathcal{E} \to \mathcal{S}E→S to the topos of sets / anima admits a right adjoint. (Note that in the 2-category of categories, a category CCC has a final object iff the unique morphism C→1C \to 1C→1 has a right adjoint). This essentially tells you that the final object (in usual sense) in E\mathcal{E}E geometrically looks like a point.

Adrian Clough (May 18 2026 at 18:37):

Practically, the sharp modality allows you to define concrete objects as objects XXX in E\mathcal{E}E for which the canonical morphism X→♯XX \to \sharp XX→♯X is a monomorphism. This makes it fairly easy for example to prove that that the category of concrete objects in E\mathcal{E}E is presentable.
