Topic: Submonoids of free commutative monoids

John Baez (Apr 14 2025 at 22:35):

Every subgroup of a free abelian group is free - apparently this was proved by Dedekind! Not every submonoid of a free commutative monoid is free - many submonoids of the free monoid on one generator are not free. But I'm wondering:

Question. Suppose FFF is a finitely generated free commutative monoid, N\mathbb{N}N is the free commutative monoid on one generator, and s,t:F→Ns, t: F \to \mathbb{N}s,t:F→N are monoid homomorphisms. Is the equalizer of sss and ttt a free commutative monoid?

Since every finitely generated free commutative monoid is isomorphic to Nk\mathbb{N}^kNk with its usual additionfor some kkk, we can restate the question more concretely as follows:

Here's another cool fact I'd like to deploy somehow:

Say you have a finitely generated free commutative monoid MMM equipped with the partial order where x≤yx \le yx≤y iff y=x+zy = x + zy=x+z for some zzz. Then Dickson's Lemma says every subset of MMM has a finite set of minimal elements!

We can say this in a less fancy way: say we have M=NkM = \mathbb{N}^kM=Nk equipped with the partial order where x≤yx \le yx≤y iff xi≤yix_i \le y_ixi​≤yi​ for all i=1,…,ki = 1, \dots , ki=1,…,k. Then every subset of MMM has a finite set of minimal elements.
