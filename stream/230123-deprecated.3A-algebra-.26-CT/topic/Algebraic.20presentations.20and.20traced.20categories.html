Tobias Fritz (May 10 2023 at 09:48):

That's an interesting question! Here's a partial answer on when the Kleisli cat SetT\mathsf{Set}_TSetT​ of a monad TTT on Set\mathsf{Set}Set is compact closed. First of all, in order for SetT\mathsf{Set}_TSetT​ to be symmetric monoidal, TTT needs to be a commutative monad. This at least makes T1T1T1 into a commutative monoid (which in the semiring case is the underlying multiplicative monoid; if TTT is in addition additive, then T1T1T1 is even a semiring). Assuming compact closure, we then get natural isomorphisms:

TX≅SetT(1,X)≅SetT(X∗,1)≅(T1)X∗.TX \cong \mathsf{Set}_T(1, X) \cong \mathsf{Set}_T(X^*, 1) \cong (T1)^{X^*}.TX≅SetT​(1,X)≅SetT​(X∗,1)≅(T1)X∗.

If one could now argue that XXX must be canonically self-dual, then this turns into your TX=SXTX = S^XTX=SX with S=T1S = T1S=T1. But I'm not sure how to complete the argument.

Nathanael Arkor (May 10 2023 at 10:05):

As an aside, a 2-dimensional version of this argument implies that the bicategory of distributors/profunctors is compact closed, since the bicategory of distributors is the Kleisli bicategory for the presheaf construction, which is pseudo-commutative, and PC≃(P1)C∘≃SetC∘\mathcal P \mathbf C \simeq (\mathcal P 1)^{\mathbf C^\circ} \simeq \mathrm{Set}^{\mathbf C^\circ}PC≃(P1)C∘≃SetC∘ (cf. Remark 5.9 of Di Liberti–Loregian's On the unicity of formal category theories).

Tobias Fritz (May 10 2023 at 10:38):

Conjecture: A Kleisli category SetT\mathsf{Set}_TSetT​ for a commutative monad TTT on Set\mathsf{Set}Set is compact closed if and only if TTT is isomorphic to a monad of the form X↦QXX \mapsto Q^XX↦QX, where QQQ is a commutative quantale.

Here, the functoriality of such a monad on a function f:X→Yf : X \to Yf:X→Y is the map QX→QYQ^X \to Q^YQX→QY given by q↦(y↦⋁x∈f−1(y)q(x))q \mapsto \left(y \mapsto \bigvee_{x \in f^{-1}(y)} q(x) \right)q↦(y↦⋁x∈f−1(y)​q(x)), and the monad multiplication QQX→QXQ^{Q^X} \to Q^XQQX→QX looks like α↦(x↦⋁q∈QXq(x)∗α(q))\alpha \mapsto \left(x \mapsto \bigvee_{q \in Q^X} q(x) \ast \alpha(q) \right) α↦(x↦⋁q∈QX​q(x)∗α(q)). This recovers the usual powerset monad for Q={0,1}Q = \{0,1\}Q={0,1}.

Note also that the above conjecture really is an equivalence of properties rather than structures, since a monad on Set\mathsf{Set}Set is commutative in at most one way, and being compact closed is also a mere property of a symmetric mnoidal category.

Tom Hirschowitz (May 10 2023 at 14:56):

For traced structure wrt coproduct on the Kleisli category, there is a whole bunch of work by @Sergey Goncharov, Stefan Milius, and others on all sorts of iterative monads, see for example Complete Elgot monads and coalgebraic resumptions.

Sergey Goncharov (May 10 2023 at 15:07):

Thanks, @Tom Hirschowitz for popularizing our work! We investigated in depth the case of coCartesian trace on the Kleisli category of a monad. According to Hyland and Hasegawa, such coCartesian trace is the same as a Conway fixpoint operator, i.e. for a monad TTT, it has the profile f:X→T(Y+X)↦(f†:X→TY)f: X\to T(Y+X)\mapsto (f^\dagger: X\to TY)f:X→T(Y+X)↦(f†:X→TY), plus the laws of Conway iteration. These are generally known to be too weak, so a standard remedy is to add the so called uniformilty principle w.r.t. pure morphisms. We call the resulting monads Elgot monads. Regarding Cartesian tensor, let alone proper tensor, we did not analyse them in the context of monads.

Sergey Goncharov (May 10 2023 at 21:48):

Well one difficulty with providing a traced Cartesian structure on a Kleisli category is that unlike coproducts which come for free on a Kleisli category, products do not come for free. So there's even more extra structure needed here.

Yes, exactly. From the semantics perspective, Cartesian trace is recursion (f ⁣:Γ×X→X)↦(f† ⁣:Γ→X)(f\colon \Gamma\times X\to X)\mapsto (f_\dagger\colon\Gamma\to X)(f:Γ×X→X)↦(f†​:Γ→X), and technical aspects aside, Kleisli category is not the best place to interpret it, except for some corner cases, like the lifting monad on dcpo, for which Kleisli and EM coincide. Recursion is dual to iteration. But if we want to dualise iteration on monads, we also should dualize monads and get recursion on co-monads. At least, this is what semantic considerations suggest.
