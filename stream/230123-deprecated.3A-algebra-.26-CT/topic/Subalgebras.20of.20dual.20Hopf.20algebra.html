John Baez (Aug 27 2023 at 21:59):

Alonso Perez-Lona said:

What is the relation between the Hopf subalgebras of a finite-dimensional Hopf algebra H, and the Hopf subalgebras of the dual Hopf algebra H^*? Is it as straightforward as "A is a Hopf subalgebra of H iff A^* is a Hopf subalgebra of H^*" ?

Subalgebras of a finite-dimensional Hopf algebra correspond to quotient algebras of its dual.

Jean-Baptiste Vienney (Aug 27 2023 at 22:01):

How do you know this?

John Baez (Aug 27 2023 at 23:13):

For starters, taking duals is a contravariant functor sending inclusions i:V↪Wi: V \hookrightarrow Wi:V↪W to quotient maps i∗:W∗→V∗i^\ast: W^\ast \to V^\asti∗:W∗→V∗, algebras to coalgebras and coalgebras to algebras.

Jean-Baptiste Vienney (Aug 27 2023 at 23:14):

What are you quotienting by in your quotient map?

John Baez (Aug 27 2023 at 23:27):

The subspace of W∗W^\astW∗ consisting of functionals that vanish on all of im(V)⊆Wim(V) \subseteq Wim(V)⊆W.

David Egolf (Aug 28 2023 at 15:53):

John Baez said:

Assuming i:V→Wi: V \to Wi:V→W is an injective map between finite-dimensional vector spaces, then we get i∗ ⁣:W∗→V∗i^\ast \colon W^\ast \to V^\asti∗:W∗→V∗ in the usual way:

i∗(f)(v)=f(i(v)) i^\ast(f) (v) = f(i(v)) i∗(f)(v)=f(i(v))

for all f∈W∗f \in W^\astf∈W∗. My claim (a well-known fact) is that i∗i^\asti∗ is surjective...

Intuitively, this is interesting - it's like the ways in which VVV relates to the underlying field are all "contained inside" of the ways in which the larger vector space WWW relates to the underlying field. In symbols, I think the surjectivity of i∗i^\asti∗ means that for any g:V→Kg: V \to Kg:V→K, where KKK is the field our vector spaces are defined over (and viewed as a 1D vector space), then we can find a f:W→Kf: W \to Kf:W→K so that f∘i=gf \circ i = gf∘i=g.

I wonder if a related more general statement is true (subject to some conditions to be determined): Let i:V→Wi: V \to Wi:V→W be a representative of a subobject in some category, where we view subobjects as an isomorphism class of monomorphisms. So, i:V→Wi: V \to Wi:V→W is a monomorphism. Then, let g:V→Kg: V \to Kg:V→K be some morphism from VVV to another object KKK. I wonder if we can always find a f:W→Kf: W \to Kf:W→K so that ggg factors through WWW using fff. That is, so that g=f∘ig = f \circ ig=f∘i.

I don't know what the minimum requirements are for this to be true. But let us consider the case where W=V∐V′W = V \coprod V'W=V∐V′ for some object V′V'V′. In this case, let i:V→V∐V′i: V \to V \coprod V'i:V→V∐V′ and i′:V′→V∐V′i': V' \to V \coprod V'i′:V′→V∐V′ be the two coprojection morphisms associated with V∐V′V \coprod V'V∐V′. Then let g:V→Kg: V \to Kg:V→K be the morphism we wish to factor through WWW. Let g′:V′→Kg': V' \to Kg′:V′→K be any morphism from V′V'V′ to KKK. Then, by the universal property of coproducts, there is a unique f:V∐V′→Kf: V \coprod V' \to Kf:V∐V′→K so that f∘i=gf \circ i = gf∘i=g and f∘i′=g′f \circ i' = g'f∘i′=g′.

So, in this case, there is always a way to factor f:V→Kf: V \to Kf:V→K through a g:W→Kg: W \to Kg:W→K. Here are the two assumptions we made to achieve this, in addition to assuming that i:V→Wi: V \to Wi:V→W is a monomorphism:

  • WWW is a coproduct of VVV and some object V′V'V′, and i:V→Wi: V \to Wi:V→W is one of the coprojections
  • there is at least one morphism from V′V'V′ to KKK

John Baez (Aug 30 2023 at 22:46):

I don't know a simple relation between Rep(H) and Rep(H*). Maybe there is one, but this is all I know:

If we start with, not just the category Rep(H) but also the forgetful functor

F: Rep(H) →\to→ Vect

we can recover H itself as the Hopf algebra of natural transformations from F to itself. This is called Tannaka duality. We can then form H* and then form Rep(H*) and its forgetful functor

G: Rep(H*) →\to→ Vect

But it would be nice to go from F: Rep(H) →\to→ Vect to G: Rep(H*) →\to→ Vect more directly!
