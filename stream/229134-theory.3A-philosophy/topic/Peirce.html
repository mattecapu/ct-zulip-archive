Jon Awbrey (Jun 25 2021 at 15:30):

Very briefly, Spencer Brown's Revival — sounds like a great name for a band — augments Peirce's α-level logical graphs in two main ways:

  • Clarifies the relation between primary arithmetic and primary algebra.  I usually say this is implicit in Peirce's use of logical graphs but it's fair to say he never quite broke through to making it explicit.  Understanding this relationship is extremely important — it clears up worries about the “ontological status of variables” (a phrase I got from Quine, I think) and dispenses with the mechanics of substitution as a fundamental notion, replacing it with principles of syntactic analogy.
  • Facilitates the use of equational inference rules (1) (2).  This is a major advance in so far as equational reasoning pervades mathematics but gets short shrifft in traditional approaches to logic.
