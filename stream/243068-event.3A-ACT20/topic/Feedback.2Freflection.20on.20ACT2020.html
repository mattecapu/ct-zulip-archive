Topic: Feedback/reflection on ACT2020

Brendan Fong (Jul 14 2020 at 23:35):

I thought I'd create a thread for anyone that wants to share their thoughts on ACT2020.

It's the first time we've run a fully virtual conference, and it'd be great to hear some thoughts on what worked and what didn't. For me, the talks were great, but the more informal parts of the conference lacked the richness of being in person together. Coordinating across timezones was a bit difficult too. But I think overall the upsides in terms of environmental and financial cost, time, and inclusivity for online conferences are huge, and I hope we'll be able to improve over time, and hold online events regularly.

James Fairbanks (Jul 14 2020 at 23:45):

I think that that the biggest thing missing was the opportunity to meet strangers in the hallway. I made a lot of friends at ACT2019 and didn't make as many new friends this year. The 2d map app where you could hear people virtually standing next to you was a cool idea and I would appreciate having spent more time in there with the critical mass of participants also hanging out in there.

If we have ACT2021 in person, it would be cool to experiment with recording and watching the talks ahead of time and then using more of the time for meetings. Maybe the conference could be shorter and more like a workshop where the goal is interaction and collaboration rather than information broadcasting. I would rather spend 3 days collaborating than 5 days attending talks with a little collaboration in between.

Morgan Rogers (he/him) (Jul 15 2020 at 17:12):

Personally I found the online conference format hard to engage with. The talks were generally of a high quality (at least those that I got around to watching) but I couldn't get into a mindset where it was gratifying to watch them in real time because I'm in exactly the same place physically as I would be when working, and so I inevitably turned back to my work after a very short time. The side-effect was that I also didn't engage socially, mostly due to the same restlessness that put me off the talks.

Morgan Rogers (he/him) (Jul 15 2020 at 17:15):

Having talks which are recorded and available online I'm a big fan of (being able to watch things at 1.5x speed makes me feel like I'm making very efficient use of my time somehow), but I desperately miss the in-person experience of conferences. To be fair, I'm not sure there's a great deal that could be done on the part of the organisers to remedy my problems, given the variety of formats for socialising etc that were made available :shrug: It was a great conference under the circumstances!
