Paolo Perrone (Jun 16 2020 at 14:34):

The boring part

  • Technology: Most of the events will take place on Zoom, YouTube, here on Zulip, or on Jitsi. For troubleshooting or support, see the tech support thread.
  • Privacy: If you take part in the Zoom call, your profile, your picture, and your voice may appear in a video recording. If you don't want this, please connect via the YouTube live stream instead - you can do that in full anonymity.
  • Rules: We will monitor all the channels as best as we can. Please excuse us if you see anything inappropriate, and please report it so that we can deal with it as fast as we can. Also please, don't be that person yourself!
  • Disclaimer: While we do our best to monitor the channels for blatantly inappropriate behavior, we can't and don't want to control or censor what the speakers, or the audience, is saying or asking. The views of the committee, and of each individual member of the committee, do not necessarily reflect the views of the speakers or of the audience.

Paolo Perrone (Jul 06 2020 at 10:28):

Here are Jitsi rooms for social and less-social purposes:

- Coffee – casual chatting
- Pub – casual chatting
- Dynamical – things occurring in time, Petri nets, robotics, etc.
- Quantum – all things quantum
- Linguistics – language, NLP
- Types – type theory, programming language theory
- Probability - random topics are welcome and encouraged
- Philosophy – what is behind it all?
- Tools – what software do we need? how do we design it?
- PureCT – pure category theory discussions
- AppliedApplied – like actually applied
- Flourish – strategies for the flourishing of ACT
- Ethics – what makes a good world and how do we fit into that?
