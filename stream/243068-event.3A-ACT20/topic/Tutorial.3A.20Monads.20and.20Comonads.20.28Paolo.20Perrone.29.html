Christian Williams (Jul 06 2020 at 02:13):

An algebra of TTT, a set AAA equipped with a function α:T(A)→A\alpha:T(A)\to Aα:T(A)→A, is a set "equipped with TTT-structure": the set T(A)T(A)T(A) consists of "formal expressions" with variables in AAA, and the map α\alphaα evaluates these expressions to values. For example if TTT is the "free monoid" monad, an algebra maps [a1,a2,…,an]↦a1a2…an[a_1,a_2,\dots,a_n]\mapsto a_1a_2\dots a_n[a1​,a2​,…,an​]↦a1​a2​…an​, and the algebra laws amount to saying that this operation is associative and unital, i.e. a monoid.

Christian Williams (Jul 06 2020 at 02:14):

A Lawvere theory LTL_TLT​ is the "category of operations" of a monad TTT; there is an equivalence between finitary monads on Set\mathrm{Set}Set and Lawvere theories (see Sec. 111 of https://arxiv.org/pdf/1112.3076.pdf). A model of LTL_TLT​ in Set\mathrm{Set}Set is a functor M:LT→SetM:L_T\to \mathrm{Set}M:LT​→Set which preserves finite products. This maps each "abstract operation" f:n→1f:n\to 1f:n→1 in LTL_TLT​ to a "concrete operation" M(f):An→AM(f):A^n\to AM(f):An→A.

Christian Williams (Jul 06 2020 at 02:14):

Models of LTL_TLT​ are equivalent to algebras of TTT, and there are advantages to each perspective. Theories are nice because they are categories, where you can draw out each operation and specify equations as commutative diagrams, which many may find to be more explicit and intuitive than an endofunctor on Set\mathrm{Set}Set which somehow encapsulates all operations at once. On the other hand, monads are much more general -- as we mentioned briefly at the end, monads exist in any 2-category, and rather than only being maps which add structure to objects, they can themselves be structures of interest.
