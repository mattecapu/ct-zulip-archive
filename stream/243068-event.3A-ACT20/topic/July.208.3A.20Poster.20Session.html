Aleksandar Makelov (Jul 04 2020 at 23:45):

Hi everyone, I'm a newcomer to the applied category theory community! :) I'm
coming from a pure math / theoretical computer science / machine learning
background, and I will be presenting a paper titled "Towards an algebraic model
of data science workflows" as part of the poster session.

The paper's focus is not on proving new theorems (as much fun as it is), but
rather on an examination of a variety of technologies enabling data science
applications, and the design objectives and constraints behind them. As someone
practicing ML engineering, I believe that the friction between these
technologies is a major source of programmer inefficiency. To this end, the
paper proposes ways in which this friction can be reduced by a unifying model
quite close in spirit to the functorial model of data and ontology logs. I am
currently planning an open-source implementation of such a model.

Unfortunately, the version of the paper submitted originally to ACT2020 was...
less than understandable :) and some key definitions were misinterpreted in the
review process. However, the new version has many more clarifications, examples,
and figures (it also ended up being substantially longer). If any of this sounds
interesting, feel free to get in touch with me here or at amakelov@mit.edu. And
finally, here is the paper: https://amakelov.github.io/data/act2020/paper.pdf.
