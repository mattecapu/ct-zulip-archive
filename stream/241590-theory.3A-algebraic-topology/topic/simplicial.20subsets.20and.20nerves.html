Stream: theory: algebraic topology

Topic: simplicial subsets and nerves

Naso (Nov 09 2021 at 14:17):

Is it right that a subobject of the nerve of a category is not necessarily a nerve? or even a quasicategory?

Reid Barton (Nov 09 2021 at 14:41):

Sure, the nerve of a category is determined by its 2-skeleton, but will typically have nondegenerate simplices in all dimensions. If we remove all nondegenerate simplices starting in some dimension n>2n > 2n>2, then it won't be a nerve any more (since we didn't change the 2-skeleton) or even a quasicategory (because we removed an nnn-simplex that was the unique solution to a horn-filling problem whose input involved only simplices below dimension nnn).
