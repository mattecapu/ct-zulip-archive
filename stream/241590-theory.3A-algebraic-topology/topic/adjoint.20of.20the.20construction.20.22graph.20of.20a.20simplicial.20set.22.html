Reid Barton (Oct 19 2021 at 12:17):

Whether a forgetful functor is faithful depends on what sort of stuff you forget. In this case, we normally think of a simplicial set XXX as a collection of sets X0X_0X0​, X1X_1X1​, X2X_2X2​, ... with a whole bunch of maps between them (satisfying some relations). The way we obtain the 1-skeleton is to "remember" X0X_0X0​ and X1X_1X1​ and the maps between them, and "forget" everything else. The fact that we're forgetting entire sets X2X_2X2​, X3X_3X3​, ... is what makes the forgetful functor turn out to not be faithful, but it's still forgetful in the ordinary informal sense.

John Baez (Oct 26 2021 at 21:33):

The two adjoints here give the "skeleton" (left adjoint) and "coskeleton" (right adjoint) of a truncated simplicial set:

The nnn-skeleton produces a simplicial set that is freely filled with degenerate simplices above degree nnn. Conversely, the nnn-coskeleton produces a simplicial set having a simplex of degree m>nm \gt nm>n whenever there is a compatible family of mmm-faces.

In simple terms, the skeleton leaves holes, while the coskeleton fills them in.

Leopold Schlicht (Jan 12 2022 at 18:04):

John Baez said:

Yes, it looks like their nasty definition of "morphism between graphs" is the price they pay for not working with reflexive graphs.

Yes, I agree that this definition is pretty nasty. I found a more conceptual way of explaining the equivalence in Kerodon.001N: observe that the usual "category of directed graphs" (the category of presheaves on ∙⇉∙\bullet\rightrightarrows\bullet∙⇉∙) can be considered as a coreflective subcategory of the category of semisimplicial sets. This adjunction between directed graphs and semisimplicial sets can be composed with the Teixeira–Barton adjunction between semisimplicial sets and simplicial sets. One gets an adjunction F⊣UF\dashv UF⊣U between directed graphs and simplicial sets, which can be restricted to an adjunction F′⊣U′F'\dashv U'F′⊣U′ between directed graphs and simplicial sets of dimension ≤1\leq 1≤1, since all simplicial sets in the image of FFF have dimension ≤1\leq 1≤1. This adjunction F′⊣U′F'\dashv U'F′⊣U′ induces a monad U′F′U'F'U′F′, whose Kleisli category is the category of directed graphs and "nasty" graph morphisms. Since F′F'F′ is essentially surjective, this category is equivalent to the category of simplicial sets of dimension ≤1\leq 1≤1.
