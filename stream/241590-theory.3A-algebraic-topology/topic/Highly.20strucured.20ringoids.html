Mike Shulman (Mar 10 2024 at 01:47):

I can't say for sure about spectra, but in general I think it is very rare that for a monoidal model category VVV the ordinary tensor product of VVV-categories makes VCatV\mathrm{Cat}VCat also a monoidal model category. E.g. it already fails for V=CatV=\mathrm{Cat}V=Cat or even V=GpdV=\mathrm{Gpd}V=Gpd. In fact offhand I can't think of any nontrivial VVV for which I know it is true.

Mike Shulman (Mar 10 2024 at 01:50):

Roughly speaking, the issue is that in the tensor product of VVV-categories you have a strict interchange law (f⊗1)∘(1⊗g)=(1⊗g)∘(f⊗1)(f\otimes 1)\circ (1\otimes g) = (1\otimes g) \circ (f\otimes 1)(f⊗1)∘(1⊗g)=(1⊗g)∘(f⊗1), but to be homotopically well-behaved this should only be an isomorphism. In particular, the tensor product of cofibrant VVV-categories tends to no longer be cofibrant, because this is a "nontrivial equation" that always holds in it. In the case of Cat there is a modified tensor product called the [[Gray tensor product]] for which VCat=2CatV\mathrm{Cat} = 2\mathrm{Cat}VCat=2Cat is a monoidal model category, and there are some generalizations of that to other cases but none quite as well-behaved.
