Topic: fully faithful Exit path functor

Lukas Heidemann (Jul 29 2022 at 15:52):

Suppose that XXX and YYY are piecewise linear conically stratified spaces with path connected strata. Since XXX and YYY are conical the simplicial sets Exit(X)\textup{Exit}(X)Exit(X) and Exit(Y)\textup{Exit}(Y)Exit(Y) (consisting of stratified maps from the stratified standard simplices) are quasicategories and a stratified map f:X→Yf : X \to Yf:X→Y then induces a functor Exit(f):Exit(X)→Exit(Y)\textup{Exit}(f) : \textup{Exit}(X) \to \textup{Exit}(Y)Exit(f):Exit(X)→Exit(Y). Does the assignment f↦Exit(f)f \mapsto \textup{Exit}(f)f↦Exit(f) induce an equivalence between the space of stratified maps X→YX \to YX→Y and the space of functors Exit(X)→Exit(Y)\textup{Exit}(X) \to \textup{Exit}(Y)Exit(X)→Exit(Y)?

This might be provable using Lemma 5.3. in From Homotopy Links to Stratified Homotopy Theories by expressing the stratified spaces as the realisation of the poset of simplices for some triangulation. But perhaps there is a more direct proof?
