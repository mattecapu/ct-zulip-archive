John Baez (Jul 26 2022 at 18:29):

Reading Wikipedia it looks like they really wanted the finite intersections of open sets to have "contractible connected components" in their "relaxed" definition of good cover. Unfortunately, they don't say how this works. I don't think it will be a big problem except for computing HnH^nHn for low nnn, like n=0n = 0n=0 and n=1n = 1n=1, which are probably the cases @naso was looking at.

Their original "non-relaxed" definition was the usual thing: the finite intersections of open sets are "contractible (and therefore connected)".

Peter Arndt (Jul 27 2022 at 07:00):

For a constant sheaf with value Z\mathbb{Z}Z I do actually get the right H1H^1H1 and H0H^0H0. The complex is 0→Z×Z→Z×Z→00 \to \mathbb{Z} \times \mathbb{Z} \to \mathbb{Z} \times \mathbb{Z} \to 00→Z×Z→Z×Z→0 where the middle map is (a,b)↦(a−b,b−a)(a,b) \mapsto (a-b,b-a)(a,b)↦(a−b,b−a). The kernel of this map is the diagonal, hence ≅Z\cong \mathbb{Z}≅Z and the image is the subgroup generated by (1,−1)(1,-1)(1,−1). Therefore the quotient is H1≅ZH^1\cong \mathbb{Z}H1≅Z.
