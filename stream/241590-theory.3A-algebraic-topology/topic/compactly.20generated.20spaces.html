Topic: compactly generated spaces

Patrick Nicodemus (Aug 23 2021 at 00:09):

Question:
Can the category of compactly generated spaces be characterized in any nice categorical way as a cocompletion of the category of compact hausdorff spaces? https://ncatlab.org/nlab/show/free+cocompletion#free_cocompletion_of_large_categories

David Michael Roberts (Aug 23 2021 at 02:15):

Not an answer, but one could perhaps see it as the concrete (?small) sheaves on CompHaus, taking a suitable Grothendieck (pre)topology on CompHaus. I wouldn't call it the free cocompletion, since finite coproducts of compact Hausdorff spaces are still compact Hausdorff. So perhaps the coherent topology is needed, generated by the (finitely) extensive topology and suitable quotient maps of Hausdorff spaces.

Roman Kniazev (Aug 23 2021 at 08:09):

It seems that this article of Escardó, Lawson and Simpson might be useful. In my understanding, in this context, it is somewhat important to distinguish between compactly generated Hausdorff spaces and compactly generated spaces without separation axiom. Jens Hemelaer (Aug 24 2021 at 11:08):

There is an inclusion of T1T_1T1​-topological spaces into condensed sets for which all maps from points are quasicompact, and this inclusion functor has a left adjoint. The left adjoint lands into the compactly generated T1T_1T1​ topological spaces (this is in "Lectures on Condensed Mathematics", Proposition 2.15 and below). So I think that the compactly generated Hausdorff spaces are precisely the condensed sets such that the unit of this adjunction is an isomorphism?

Weakly Hausdorff spaces are not necessarily T1T_1T1​, so then the usual way of associating a condensed set to it fails.

Jens Hemelaer (Aug 25 2021 at 08:59):

In this book by Warner it is stated, on page 21 of the pdf, that a Hausdorff compactly generated space is the filtered colimit of its compact Hausdorff subspaces.

In the category of condensed sets every object is a (small) colimit of compact Hausdorff spaces, so by going through condensed sets you can show that every T1T_1T1​ compactly generated space is a (small) colimit of compact Hausdorff spaces. But probably there is a more direct way to show this as well.
