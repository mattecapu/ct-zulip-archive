Topic: proper dimension of a simplex

Leopold Schlicht (Oct 18 2021 at 17:23):

Intuitively, degenerate nnn-simplices are nnn-simplices that live in the "wrong" dimension. Is there a standard name for the "proper dimension" of an nnn-simplex? By the "proper dimension" of σ∈Xn\sigma\in X_nσ∈Xn​ I mean the smallest m≤nm\leq nm≤n such that σ\sigmaσ, considered as a map Δn→X∙\Delta^n\to X_\bulletΔn→X∙​ (using Yoneda), factors as Δn→Δm→X∙\Delta^n\to \Delta^m\to X_\bulletΔn→Δm→X∙​ (it follows that Δm→X∙\Delta^m\to X_\bulletΔm→X∙​ is nondegenerate).

Is this equivalent to the smallest mmm for which there exist degeneracy maps d1,…,dkd^1, \dots, d^kd1,…,dk and an mmm-simplex τ\tauτ such that σ=(dk∘⋯∘d1)(τ)\sigma = (d^k\circ\dots \circ d^1)(\tau)σ=(dk∘⋯∘d1)(τ)? (It follows that τ\tauτ is nondegenerate, because otherwise mmm is not minimal.) Here, d1d^1d1 should carry mmm-simplices to m+1m+1m+1-simplices, and so on.

Reid Barton (Oct 18 2021 at 18:01):

Here's an even better statement which I think answers your questions: For any simplex σ:Δn→X\sigma : \Delta^n \to Xσ:Δn→X there is a unique factorization of σ\sigmaσ as a degeneracy map Δn→Δm\Delta^n \to \Delta^mΔn→Δm followed by a nondegenerate simplex τ:Δm→X\tau : \Delta^m \to Xτ:Δm→X.

Reid Barton (Oct 18 2021 at 18:01):

Except for the question about the name of course--I don't think I have a name for this "proper dimension". I'd probably call it the dimension of the nondegenerate simplex through which σ\sigmaσ factors.

Reid Barton (Oct 18 2021 at 18:03):

A degeneracy map Δn→Δm\Delta^n \to \Delta^mΔn→Δm is what corresponds to a map [n]→[m][n] \to [m][n]→[m] of the simplex category which is surjective as a map of ordered sets--or if you like, a map Δn→Δm\Delta^n \to \Delta^mΔn→Δm which is surjective on vertices.

Reid Barton (Oct 18 2021 at 18:38):

or in other words a composition of the generating degeneracies, which are usually denoted by the letter sss--I think you used ddd for degeneracy, but unfortunately that letter is traditionally used for the face maps

Ulrik Buchholtz (Oct 18 2021 at 19:10):

Re names: the factorization of a simplex as degeneracy map followed by a nondegenerate simplex is usually called the Eilenberg–Zilber decomposition, and this leads to the notion of an Eilenberg–Zilber category as one where all elements of a presheaf have such a decomposition. I haven't seen a name for the degree of the nondegenerate element in the decomposition, though.
