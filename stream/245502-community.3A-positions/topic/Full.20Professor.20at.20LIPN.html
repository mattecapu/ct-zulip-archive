LIPN (the CS lab at Université Sorbonne Paris Nord) is opening a professeur position (equivalent to Full Professor). The position is open to the every topic within the scope of the lab but priority is given to applications with a strong background in one of the following areas:

  • logic and computer science (which includes category theory);
  • machine learning;
  • natural language processing.

Teaching is at the CS Department. Fluency in French is expected.

Deadline for application is March 29th.

Morgan Rogers (he/him) (Jan 30 2024 at 16:18):

For international applicants, are there any specific prerequisites to be able to apply for a professorship position?

Damiano Mazza (Jan 30 2024 at 19:07):

Excellent question, I forgot to mention this. In theory people applying from outside of the French system (that is, who are not maître de conférence already) need to first obtain something called qualification. The deadline for that is long gone but there's a workaround: applicants can ask a "waiver" to the University, which is granted almost automatically unless the applicant is too young, doesn't have enough experience, etc.

(The difference between qualification and this "waiver" is that, once you get it, the qualification lasts four years and is valid in all French universities, whereas the waiver is granted by a single university for a single application each time).
