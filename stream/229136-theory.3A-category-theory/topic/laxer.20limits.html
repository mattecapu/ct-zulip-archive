Topic: laxer limits

Matteo Capucci (he/him) (Mar 24 2022 at 11:19):

Fix a bicategory X\mathbb XX. The lax limit of a diagram D:I→XD: \mathbb I \to \mathbb XD:I→X in X\mathbb XX is defined to be the pseudorepresenting objects of the functor

L(X)=[I,Cat]l(1,X(X,D−))\mathcal L(X) = [I, Cat]_l(1, \mathbb X(X, D-))L(X)=[I,Cat]l​(1,X(X,D−))

meaning that

X(lim⁡laxD,X)≃L(X).\mathbb X({\lim}_{lax} D, X) \simeq \mathcal L(X).X(limlax​D,X)≃L(X).

We could weaken this notion even more by replacing the latter equivalence with an adjunction. Is this a known definition?
In concrete terms, this would mean to weaken the universal property of lim⁡laxD\lim_{lax} Dlimlax​D as to hold 'up to 2-cells'. For instance, a laxer product of two objects XXX and YYY would look like a normal product, except that given f:Z→Xf:Z \to Xf:Z→X and g:Y→Zg:Y \to Zg:Y→Z, the universal morphism ⟨f,g⟩lax\langle f,g \rangle_{lax}⟨f,g⟩lax​ does not factor fff and ggg through the projections strictly, but only up to a 2-cell:
