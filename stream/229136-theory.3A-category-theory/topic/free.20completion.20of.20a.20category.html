Topic: free completion of a category

Patrick Nicodemus (Nov 16 2021 at 05:49):

Zhen Lin commented in a recent post that the free completion of a category C\mathcal{C}C should be [C,Sets]op[\mathcal{C}, \mathbf{Sets}]^{\rm op}[C,Sets]op. In theory opposite categories are somewhat difficult to understand but a famous result of topos theory is that [C,Sets]op[\mathcal{C},\mathbf{Sets}]^{\rm op}[C,Sets]op is monadic over [C,Sets].[\mathcal{C}, \mathbf{Sets}].[C,Sets]. I am wondering if anything has been made of this for the sake of concretely working with the free completion. Are there any papers out there that need the free completion of C\mathcal{C}C for concrete or computational ends and implement this as the category of monad algebras for the "double powerset" monad on [C,Sets][\mathcal{C},\mathbf{Sets}][C,Sets]?

Morgan Rogers (he/him) (Nov 16 2021 at 10:08):

It seems more likely that, when needed, [C,Set]op[\mathcal{C},\mathrm{Set}]^{\mathrm{op}}[C,Set]op is treated as the opposite of a topos. That is, arguments are made in [C,Set][\mathcal{C},\mathrm{Set}][C,Set] and then dualized, since copresheaves are a lot more concrete than algebras for the double powerset monad.

Zhen Lin Low (Nov 16 2021 at 13:34):

Famously, the double powerset monad on the category of sets can be identified with the complete atomic boolean algebras monad, so it's possible to very concrete.

I guess I'm really asking if we can get a sort of concrete description of algebras of the "double powerset" monad in a general topos, as objects equipped with a bunch of operations obeying a bunch of laws.
