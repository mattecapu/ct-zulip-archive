The nLab article lists 5 monoidal structures on [[species]], and @fosco's recent comments here make me want to fit them together more clearly. (There could also be even more.) They might be related to the various monoidal stuctures on [[polynomial functors]], but I'd rather start by considering a simpler analogous situation: the various monoid structures on N[[x]]\mathbb{N}[[x]]N[[x]]. I have a lot say but I'll just get started and say a little.

John Baez (Jan 05 2024 at 11:33):

For any rig RRR, R[[x]]R[[x]]R[[x]] is the rig of formal power series

f^(x)=∑i=0∞fixi \hat{f}(x) = \sum_{i = 0}^\infty f_i x^i f^​(x)=∑i=0∞​fi​xi

where fi∈Rf_i \in Rfi​∈R. Its underlying set is isomorphic to the set of functions

f:N→R f: \mathbb{N} \to R f:N→R

A lot of stuff I'll say will apply to any commutative rig RRR, but I'm mainly interested in R=NR = \mathbb{N}R=N, the initial rig, and traditional mathematicians may prefer R=ZR = \mathbb{Z}R=Z or R=CR = \mathbb{C}R=C or something like that.

John Baez (Jan 05 2024 at 11:40):

Here are some monoid structures on R[[x]]R[[x]]R[[x]].

  1. Addition. This is the usual addition in R[[x]]R[[x]]R[[x]] and also the pointwise addition of functions f:N→Rf: \mathbb{N} \to Rf:N→R.
  2. The Cauchy product. This is the usual multiplication of power series in R[[x]]R[[x]]R[[x]]. I'm giving it this goofy name just to hint that this is related to the monoidal structure on species called the Cauchy product.

John Baez (Jan 05 2024 at 11:41):

John Baez (Jan 05 2024 at 11:43):

John Baez (Jan 05 2024 at 12:17):

N\mathbb{N}N is free monoid on one generator and therefore the initial rig. So it has two monoid structures. So we get two 'convolution' monoid structures on the set of finitely supported functions

f:N→Rf: \mathbb{N} \to Rf:N→R

John Baez (Jan 05 2024 at 12:18):

John Baez (Jan 05 2024 at 12:19):

But also we get two more monoid structures on the set of finitely supported functions f:N→Rf: \mathbb{N} \to Rf:N→R, coming from pointwise addition and multiplication! These are what I called addition and the Hadamard product.

John Baez (Jan 05 2024 at 12:19):

John Baez (Jan 05 2024 at 12:21):

But then there's a final twist of the knife, which is special to N\mathbb{N}N: the set of finitely supported functions f:N→Rf: \mathbb{N} \to Rf:N→R is isomorphic to R[x]R[x]R[x], the free RRR-algebra on one generator. And so it gets the 'composition' product, defined by composing polynomials.

John Baez (Jan 05 2024 at 12:27):

The point here is that since R[x]R[x]R[x] is free on one generator xxx, any element g∈R[x]g \in R[x]g∈R[x] determines a unique homomorphism R[x]→R[x]R[x] \to R[x]R[x]→R[x] sending xxx to ggg. This homomorphism sends any f∈R[x]f \in R[x]f∈R[x] to some element we call f∘gf \circ gf∘g. And this is composition of polynomials!

(f1+f2)∘g=f1∘g+f2∘g(f_1 + f_2) \circ g = f_1 \circ g + f_2 \circ g(f1​+f2​)∘g=f1​∘g+f2​∘g
(f1f2)∘g=(f1∘g)(f2∘g) (f_1 f_2) \circ g = (f_1 \circ g)(f_2 \circ g)(f1​f2​)∘g=(f1​∘g)(f2​∘g)

John Baez (Jan 06 2024 at 10:59):

There are different possible categorifications but let's try this one. Like algebraic geometers use 'ring' to mean 'commutative rig', I'm going to use '2-rig' to mean 'symmetric 2-rig', one where the monoidal structure is symmetric. I'm doing this just to save on typing now!

Say a cocomplete 2-rig is a cocomplete symmetric monoidal category RRR where the tensor product ⊗\otimes⊗ distributes over colimits. Note RRR has two symmetric monoidal structures, ⊗\otimes⊗ and +++, the first distributing over the second.

Define a [[rig category]] following Kelly and Laplaza: it's a category SSS with two symmetric monoidal structures ⊗\otimes⊗ and ⊕\oplus⊕, the first distributing over the second, with the distributivity natural isomorphism obeying a legion of coherence laws.

John Baez (Jan 06 2024 at 11:01):

Suppose RRR is a cocomplete 2-rig and SSS is a small rig category. Let X=RSX = R^SX=RS be the category of all functors from SSS to RRR. Then XXX gets 4 symmetric monoidal structures: 2 coming from the 2 symmetric monoidal structures ⊗\otimes⊗ and +++ on RRR via pointwise operations, and 2 more coming from the 2 symmetric monoidal structures ⊗\otimes⊗ and +++ on SSS via [[Day convolution]].

John Baez (Jan 06 2024 at 11:33):

Now let's see how species are a special case of this. In this example:

  • RRR is the category of sets, made into a cocomplete 2-rig with ⊗=×\otimes = \times⊗=×.
  • SSS is the groupoid of finite sets, which is a [[rig category]] with symmetric monoidal structures ⊗\otimes⊗ and ⊕\oplus⊕ that arise from restricting +++ and ×\times× from the category of finite sets to the groupoid of finite sets.

John Baez (Jan 06 2024 at 11:36):

Then RSR^SRS is the category of species and we get 4 symmetric monoidal structures on it:

  1. +++ is the coproduct of species.
  2. ⊗\otimes⊗ is the Hadamard product, i.e. cartesian product, of species.
  3. ∗+\ast_{+}∗+​ is the Cauchy product of species.
  4. ∗⊗\ast_\otimes∗⊗​ is the Dirichlet product of species.

John Baez (Jan 06 2024 at 11:40):

These fit together to make species into a cocomplete 2-rig in three ways: via the Hadamard product, Cauchy product and Dirichlet product.
