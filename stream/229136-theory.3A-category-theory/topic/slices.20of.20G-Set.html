Morgan Rogers (he/him) (Feb 12 2022 at 07:46):

All geometric morphisms between toposes of the form G-Set are atomic and induced by group homs, and injective homomorphisms induce etale surjections. The former fact is an exercise in Mac Lane & Moerdijk which I can dig up if you need, the latter thing is in the first chapter of my thesis but a better reference will eventually be my joint work with @Jens Hemelaer on factorizing geometric morphisms between toposes of M-sets, assuming I devote some time to finishing it within the next few months.

If it's true there should be a pretty simple explicit 'formula' for the equivalence, right?

I'm reminded of 'Mackey theory', which relates representations of the subgroup H⊆GH \subseteq GH⊆G and equivariant vector bundles over X:=G/HX := G/HX:=G/H, meaning vector bundles p:E→Xp: E \to Xp:E→X with a GGG-action lifting the GGG-action on XXX. This is often studied in a context where everything is a manifold or topological space, which is making it hard for me to find references to how it works in the simpler case of sets. Also, I'm having trouble finding any reference to an equivalence, because people are often in a rush to exploit what's going on, and don't slow down to examine the equivalence that I believe is lurking here.

John Baez (Feb 12 2022 at 17:16):

Anyway:

Given an HHH-set SSS you can form the GGG-set

F(S)=G×S(gh,s)∼(g,hs) \displaystyle{ F(S) = \frac{G \times S}{(gh, s) \sim (g,hs)} }F(S)=(gh,s)∼(g,hs)G×S​

This functor FFF is the left adjoint to restricting GGG-actions to HHH-actions. But F(S)F(S)F(S) is a GGG-set over X:=G/HX := G/HX:=G/H via [g,s]↦[g][g,s] \mapsto [g][g,s]↦[g], where [g][g][g] is the right coset of ggg.

John Baez (Feb 12 2022 at 17:19):

Conversely, given a GGG-set YYY over G/HG/HG/H we can look at the fiber over the coset [1][1][1]. Since HHH fixes [1][1][1] it acts on this fiber, so this fiber becomes an HHH-set.

John Baez (Feb 12 2022 at 17:20):

I think this is the equivalence you want - but yeah, I'm not proving it's an equivalence.

Anyway, this is the essential idea behind 'Mackey theory', translated from the usual Vect context to Set. People call F(S)F(S)F(S), as GGG-set over XXX, an 'induced bundle'. This stuff is widely used in group representation theory.

Jens Hemelaer (Feb 13 2022 at 13:55):

It is indeed the right equivalence. If XXX is an object in a topos Sh(C,J)\mathbf{Sh}(\mathcal{C},J)Sh(C,J), then you can explicitly write down a site for the corresponding slice topos Sh(C,J)/X\mathbf{Sh}(\mathcal{C},J)/XSh(C,J)/X:

  • as category you take the slice category C/X\mathcal{C}/XC/X, i.e. the category of elements of XXX;
  • as covering sieves you take the sieves that are covering sieves after projecting to C\mathcal{C}C.

For the topos PSh(G)\mathbf{PSh}(G)PSh(G) and X=G/HX=G/HX=G/H, the category of elements of XXX is a connected groupoid such that each object has automorphism group isomorphic to HHH, so it is equivalent as a category to HHH. The Grothendieck topology is the presheaf topology. This shows that PSh(G)/X≃PSh(H)\mathbf{PSh}(G)/X \simeq \mathbf{PSh}(H)PSh(G)/X≃PSh(H). Explicitly writing down the equivalence takes some more work, but eventually you get the equivalence that @John Baez wrote down above.

Jens Hemelaer (Feb 13 2022 at 14:14):

More generally, the étale morphisms to a presheaf topos PSh(C)\mathbf{PSh}(\mathcal{C})PSh(C) are precisely the geometric morphisms PSh(D)→PSh(C)\mathbf{PSh}(\mathcal{D}) \to \mathbf{PSh}(\mathcal{C})PSh(D)→PSh(C), induced by a discrete fibration D→C\mathcal{D} \to \mathcal{C}D→C. This is a bit similar to the correspondence between sheaves and étalé spaces. Maybe it is easier to find a reference for this more general statement? I have a feeling that it is a special case of a result in Caramello–Zanfa, but I haven't found a precise reference.

Reid Barton (Feb 13 2022 at 15:57):

In fact this is essentially "the" example of an "etale map", with k⊆Kk \subseteq Kk⊆K a finite separable field extension, G=Gal(k)G = \mathrm{Gal}(k)G=Gal(k) and H=Gal(K)H = \mathrm{Gal}(K)H=Gal(K). Then Spec K→Spec k\mathrm{Spec}\, K \to \mathrm{Spec}\, kSpecK→Speck is etale, the etale topos of kkk is equivalent to GGG-sets with Spec K→Spec k\mathrm{Spec}\, K \to \mathrm{Spec}\, kSpecK→Speck corresponding to G/HG/HG/H, and the etale topos of KKK is HHH-sets. (Though here GGG is really a profinite group and the sets are supposed to have continuous GGG-action. Hopefully an actual algebraic geometer can correct anything I got wrong.)
