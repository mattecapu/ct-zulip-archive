Topic: Slice categories as free lex categories

Sridhar Ramesh (Feb 16 2021 at 23:46):

Hello. This is my first use of Zulip, so forgive me if I am posting this in the wrong place or the wrong way. I am interested in knowing of any reference to cite for the following folklore result: If T is a category with finite limits, and t is an object in T, then the free extension of T (qua category-with-finite-limits) with a morphism from 1 to t is given by the slice category T/t.

(Similar results follow for other notions extending the notion of a category with finite limits which are also suitably preserved by pullback to slice categories; e.g., for elementary toposes. And an analogue result holds for categories with finite products, where we do not use the full slice category but only its full subcategory on slices which are projections from products. And again for notions extending this in the appropriate way; e.g., for cartesian closed categories, where the analogue of this result is sometimes called "functional completeness" and can be cited to Lambek. But I am specifically interested in citing a reference for this result for categories with finite limits.)

dusko (Jun 13 2021 at 17:40):

the statement in terms of extension is in SGA4: that is how it is proved that E/t{\cal E}/tE/t is a topos if E\cal EE is. it is equivalent to the statement that E/t{\cal E}/tE/t is a final resolution of the comonad t×−t\times-t×−. it is funny that grothendieck worked out the proof in detail (expose IV i think), but it became too easy later. i am pretty sure that lambek referred to grothendieck for this, and would expect that it is spelled out in lambek scott, if you read far enough.
