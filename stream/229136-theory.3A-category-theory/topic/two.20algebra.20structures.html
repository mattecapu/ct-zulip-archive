What's the reason Lawvere theories (= finitary monads..?) can always be combined like this?

As I understand it, the tensor operation on theories is very syntactical (Definition 3.2 here is more categorical), the operations symbols and axioms in both theories are put together and we add the appropriate distributivity axioms. There is nothing that prevents it from existing.

The reason (imo) why this does not translate to monads is that while the correspondence theory--monad yields Mod(T,Set)≅SetT\mathrm{Mod}(T,\mathbf{Set}) \cong \mathbf{Set}^{T}Mod(T,Set)≅SetT, we don't have Mod(T,Mod(S,Set))≅(SetS)T\mathrm{Mod}(T,\mathrm{Mod}(S,\mathbf{Set})) \cong (\mathbf{Set}^S)^TMod(T,Mod(S,Set))≅(SetS)T. In fact, with no distributive law, the RHS is not well-defined because TTT is not a monad on SetS\mathbf{Set}^SSetS.

Reid Barton (Jan 18 2022 at 13:06):

It might also be worth noting that the tensor product construction also works when SSS and TTT are multi-sorted theories, and then the sorts of the tensor product theory are the cartesian product of the sorts of SSS and the sorts of TTT (which need not be the same). Mike Shulman (Jan 18 2022 at 17:03):

Fundamentally, the tensor product of theories and the composite monad arising from a distributive law are different things. A T⊗ST\otimes ST⊗S algebra is an object with unrelated TTT-algebra and SSS-algebra structures; hence why it requires no additional data relating TTT and SSS. Whereas if λ\lambdaλ is a distributive law between TTT and SSS, then a TSTSTS-algebra is an object with TTT-algebra and SSS-algebra structures that satisfy a compatibility condition mediated by λ\lambdaλ. In principle one could try to construct the "tensor product of monads" in a similar way to the tensor product of theories, without using a distributive law, and I would expect it to exist in good cases like when the base category is locally presentable and the monads are accessible.
