Topic: Categories of Algebras

Joshua Meyers (Feb 10 2021 at 03:21):

If CCC is a category and FFF is an endofunctor, we can form the category F-AlgF\textbf{-Alg}F-Alg of algebras of FFF.

But it turns out that the mapping Ψ:(C,F)↦F-Coalg\Psi:(C,F)\mapsto F\textbf{-Coalg}Ψ:(C,F)↦F-Coalg is functorial!

The domain of Ψ\PsiΨ is the category of endofunctors. Objects are endofunctors F:C→CF:C\to CF:C→C and a morphism from F:C→CF:C\to CF:C→C to G:D→DG:D\to DG:D→D consists of

  • A functor P:C→DP:C\to DP:C→D
  • A natural transformation p:GP→PFp: GP\to PFp:GP→PF

So this is the oplax arrow category 2Catlax(N,Cat)2\textbf{Cat}_\textbf{lax}(\mathbb{N},\textbf{Cat})2Catlax​(N,Cat), where N\mathbb{N}N is the monoid (N,+,0)(\mathbb{N}, +,0)(N,+,0) considered as a one-object category. Cf. https://ncatlab.org/nlab/show/lax+natural+transformation

The codomain of Ψ\PsiΨ is Cat\textbf{Cat}Cat. The morphism (P,p)(P,p)(P,p) maps to the functor F-Alg→F-AlgF\textbf{-Alg}\to F\textbf{-Alg}F-Alg→F-Alg which sends the algebra (c,x:Fc→c)(c, x:Fc\to c)(c,x:Fc→c) to the algebra (Pc,G(Pc)→pcP(Fc)→PxPc)(Pc,G(Pc)\xrightarrow{p_c} P(Fc) \xrightarrow{Px} Pc)(Pc,G(Pc)pc​​P(Fc)Px​Pc).

Anybody heard of this before, or know what it is a special case of or something?
