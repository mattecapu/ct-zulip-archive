Topic: Surjectivity w.r.t a bifibration

Vincent Moreau (Feb 12 2025 at 14:18):

Hi everyone. Let p:E→Bp : \mathbf{E} \to \mathbf{B}p:E→B be a bifibration, i.e. a functor that is both a Grothendieck fibration and a Grothendieck opfibration. For any object XXX of B\mathbf{B}B, I write EX\mathbf{E}_{X}EX​ for the fiber of XXX, i.e. the subcategory of E\mathbf{E}E containing the objects UUU such that p(U)=Xp(U) = Xp(U)=X and the morphisms u:U→Vu : U \to Vu:U→V such that p(u)=IdXp(u) = \mathrm{Id}_{X}p(u)=IdX​.

Vincent Moreau (Feb 12 2025 at 14:26):

Any morphism f:X→Yf : X \to Yf:X→Y of the base B\mathbf{B}B induces an adjunction f!⊣f∗f_! \dashv f^*f!​⊣f∗ between fiber categories

f!:EX⇆EY:f∗f_! \quad:\quad \mathbf{E}_{X} \leftrightarrows \mathbf{E}_{Y} \quad:\quad f^*f!​:EX​⇆EY​:f∗

given by the bifibration property. This yields a comonad on the fiber EY\mathbf{E}_{Y}EY​.
I say that fff is reflective when the adjunction f!⊣f∗f_! \dashv f^*f!​⊣f∗ is reflective, i.e. equivalently when f∗f^*f∗ is fully faithful or when the counit f!∘f∗→Idf_! \circ f^* \to \mathrm{Id}f!​∘f∗→Id is an isomorphism. (The statement with full faithfulness shows that it makes sense to talk about a reflective morphism even when ppp is merely a fibration, but I don't care about that for the applications I have in mind.)

If we take the bifibration p:SubSet→Setp : \mathbf{SubSet} \to \mathbf{Set}p:SubSet→Set which corresponds to the subset hyperdoctrine, then reflective morphisms are exactly the surjective functions, as in general f!∘f∗f_! \circ f^*f!​∘f∗ sends a subset S⊆YS \subseteq YS⊆Y on S∩f(X)S \cap f(X)S∩f(X), the intersection with the image of fff.

Is this notion of reflective morphisms of a base in a bifibration already in the literature? The reason I consider these morphisms is that I have found a little property relating the Frobenius condition for cartesian closed functors and natural transformations between change of base functors. I thought that it was probably already found.
