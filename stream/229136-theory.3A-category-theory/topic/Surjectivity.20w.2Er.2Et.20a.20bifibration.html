Topic: Surjectivity w.r.t a bifibration

Vincent Moreau (Feb 12 2025 at 14:18):

Hi everyone. Let p:E→Bp : \mathbf{E} \to \mathbf{B}p:E→B be a bifibration, i.e. a functor that is both a Grothendieck fibration and a Grothendieck opfibration. For any object XXX of B\mathbf{B}B, I write EX\mathbf{E}_{X}EX​ for the fiber of XXX, i.e. the subcategory of E\mathbf{E}E containing the objects UUU such that p(U)=Xp(U) = Xp(U)=X and the morphisms u:U→Vu : U \to Vu:U→V such that p(u)=IdXp(u) = \mathrm{Id}_{X}p(u)=IdX​.

Vincent Moreau (Feb 12 2025 at 14:26):

Any morphism f:X→Yf : X \to Yf:X→Y of the base B\mathbf{B}B induces an adjunction f!⊣f∗f_! \dashv f^*f!​⊣f∗ between fiber categories

f!:EX⇆EY:f∗f_! \quad:\quad \mathbf{E}_{X} \leftrightarrows \mathbf{E}_{Y} \quad:\quad f^*f!​:EX​⇆EY​:f∗

given by the bifibration property. This yields a comonad on the fiber EY\mathbf{E}_{Y}EY​.
I say that fff is reflective when the adjunction f!⊣f∗f_! \dashv f^*f!​⊣f∗ is reflective, i.e. equivalently when f∗f^*f∗ is fully faithful or when the counit f!∘f∗→Idf_! \circ f^* \to \mathrm{Id}f!​∘f∗→Id is an isomorphism. (The statement with full faithfulness shows that it makes sense to talk about a reflective morphism even when ppp is merely a fibration, but I don't care about that for the applications I have in mind.)

Jonas Frey (Feb 18 2025 at 15:47):

Interesting question! In case where the bifibration satifsfies the Beck-Chevalley condition, this reminds me of the notion of [[effective descent morphism]]: an effective descent morphism in C\mathbb CC wrt a fibration p:E→Cp:\mathbb E\to \mathbb Cp:E→C is an arrow f:X→Yf:X\to Yf:X→Y in the base sth the canonical comparison arrow pX→Desc(f)p_X\to \mathrm{Desc}(f)pX​→Desc(f) into the category of descent data for fff is an equivalence, and if ppp satisfies BC then Descf≃Alg(Tf)\mathrm{Desc}_f\simeq\mathrm{Alg}(T_f)Descf​≃Alg(Tf​), where Tf=f∗∘f!T_f=f^*\circ f_!Tf​=f∗∘f!​. In other words, fff is a descent morphism if the adjunction f!⊣f∗f_!\dashv f^*f!​⊣f∗ is monadic, which is the case in particular if the adjunction is reflective.

Jonas Frey (Feb 18 2025 at 15:50):

if ppp is a fibration of preorders, then being monadic is equivalent to being a reflection, and we recover your notion of surjectivity eg for the subobject fibration of Set (or any other topos), which satisfies BC.

Jonas Frey (Feb 18 2025 at 15:58):

Another interesting example to look at is the indexed category of sheaves over toposes, which is the contravariant functor Sh(−):Topop→CatSh(-):Top^{op}\to CatSh(−):Topop→Cat which sends every Grothendieck topos to its category of sheaves (ie the topos itself), and every geometric morphism (f∗⊣f∗:E→F)(f^*\dashv f_* : E\to F)(f∗⊣f∗​:E→F) to f∗:F→Ef^*:F\to Ef∗:F→E. This is (the indexed version of) a "co-bifibration", since reindexing maps have right adjoints rather than left adjoints like in a fibration, so the analogous conditions would be to ask when (f∗⊣f∗)(f^*\dashv f_*)(f∗⊣f∗​) is comonadic or a coreflection. It turns out that comonadicity of f∗⊣f∗f^*\dashv f_*f∗⊣f∗​ is precisely the notion of the gm being surjective, whereas being a co-reflection is the stronger notion of being connected surjective.

Thus, here we see that in the non-posetal case, surjectivity is better captured by (co)monadicity rather than (co)reflections in the fibration.

Jonas Frey (Feb 18 2025 at 16:08):

The case of presheaves over Cat can be viewed as a special case of the case of sheaves over toposes by identifying small categories with their toposes of presheaves. As @Vincent Moreau pointed out, the "connected surjective" maps here are the absolutely dense ones, whereas the surjective ones are the "Cauchy surjective functors", ie an adjunction f!⊣f∗f_!\dashv f^*f!​⊣f∗ between presheaf toposes is monadic iff every object in the codomain of fff is a retract of one in the image.

One more thing that is kind of related: if P:Eop→PosP:E^{op}\to PosP:Eop→Pos is a tripos on a topos EEE, then the constant objects functor Δ:E→E[P]\Delta : E \to E[P]Δ:E→E[P] is regular (preserves epis) iff PPP "recognizes epis in EEE" in the sense that e!⊣e∗e_!\dashv e^*e!​⊣e∗ is a reflection for all epis eee in EEE.
