Topic: Fiber object

Jean-Baptiste Vienney (Jan 04 2025 at 22:54):

Has this notion been studied? Is it a special case of a known concept?

Let C\mathcal{C}C be a category together with a distinguished object ⊤∈C\top \in \mathcal{C}⊤∈C (typically the terminal object but in the case of the category of kkk-modules, I would choose ⊤:=k\top :=k⊤:=k). Let f:X→Yf:X \rightarrow Yf:X→Y. We define a fiber object of fff as an object XfX_fXf​ together with an epimorphism πf:X→Xf\pi_f:X \rightarrow X_fπf​:X→Xf​ (requiring πf\pi_fπf​ to be an epimorphism ensures that XfX_fXf​ is unique up to isomorphism if it exists) such that the following universal property is satisfied:

For every object ZZZ and morphism u:X→Zu:X \rightarrow Zu:X→Z if for any two morphisms x,y:⊤→Xx,y:\top \rightarrow Xx,y:⊤→X such that f∘x=f∘yf \circ x=f \circ yf∘x=f∘y, we have that u∘x=u∘yu \circ x=u \circ yu∘x=u∘y, then there exists a unique morphism u~:Xf→Z\tilde{u}:X_f \rightarrow Zu~:Xf​→Z such that u=u~∘πfu=\tilde{u} \circ \pi_fu=u~∘πf​.

In most categories of structured sets and homomorphisms, XfX_fXf​ should be isomorphic to the image of fff. However, I don't see how the definition of fibre object of a morphism could be equivalent to the definition of image of a morphism in general. I rather expect, but I could be wrong, that only under some hypotheses on C\mathcal{C}C, we have can derive a very general first isomorphism theorem which gives that the image of fff is isomorphic to the fibre object XfX_fXf​ of fff.

Mike Shulman (Jan 04 2025 at 22:58):

If you allow x,yx,yx,y to range over all generalized elements of XXX (which is usually what you'd want to do), then your XfX_fXf​ is the coequalizer of the kernel of fff, sometimes called the "regular coimage".

Mike Shulman (Jan 04 2025 at 22:59):

That gives a construction of the usual image (i.e. (regular epi, mono) factorization) in any regular category.
