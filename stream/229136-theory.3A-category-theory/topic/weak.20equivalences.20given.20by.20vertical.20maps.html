Topic: weak equivalences given by vertical maps

Matteo Capucci (he/him) (Jan 02 2024 at 09:39):

Let F:A→BF:A \to BF:A→B be a functor. A map in AAA is FFF-vertical is FFF sends it to an iso.
Clearly: (1) all isos are FFF-vertical and (2) if two out of f,g,fgf,g, fgf,g,fg are FFF-vertical, so is the third.

Let me call ker⁡F\ker FkerF the wide subcategory of AAA spanned by FFF-vertical maps, then (A,ker⁡F)(A,\ker F)(A,kerF) is a [[category with weak equivalences]]. I'm now interested in localizing AAA at ker⁡F\ker FkerF, and in particular in studying when this localization is reflective.

This feels like a very elementary definition and I was wondering if anyone is aware of this being studied before?

fosco (Jan 02 2024 at 10:10):

look at:

  • Cassidy C, Hébert M, Kelly GM. Reflective subcategories, localizations and factorizationa systems. Journal of the Australian Mathematical Society Series A Pure Mathematics and Statistics. 1985;38(3):287-329. doi:10.1017/S1446788700023624 (available here)
  • in particular, the relation between torsion theories and pre-fibrations, explained in Rosicky-Tholen here.
