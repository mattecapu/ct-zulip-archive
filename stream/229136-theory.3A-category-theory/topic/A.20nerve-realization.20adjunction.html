Topic: A nerve-realization adjunction

Patrick Nicodemus (Dec 22 2025 at 20:36):

Let X,YX,YX,Y be topological spaces.
Let f:X→Yf : X \to Yf:X→Y be a continuous function.
Let τ(Y)\tau(Y)τ(Y) denote the topology of YYY, regarded as a poset category.

Write Jf:τ(Y)→Top/XJ_f : \tau(Y) \to \mathbf{Top}/XJf​:τ(Y)→Top/X for the functor which sends the open set UUU to the inclusion f−1(U)⊂Xf^{-1}(U)\subset Xf−1(U)⊂X. Then the nerve-realization adjunction induced by JfJ_fJf​ between Psh(Y)\mathbf{Psh}(Y)Psh(Y) and Top/X\mathbf{Top}/XTop/X restricts to the familiar adjunction between Sh(Y)\mathbf{Sh}(Y)Sh(Y) and Etale(X)\mathbf{Etale}(X)Etale(X) which is usually written as the composition of the equivalance Sh(X)≃Etale(X)\mathbf{Sh}(X) \simeq \mathbf{Etale}(X)Sh(X)≃Etale(X) and an unrelated adjunction Sh(X)  ⟺  Sh(Y)\mathbf{Sh}(X) \iff \mathbf{Sh}(Y)Sh(X)⟺Sh(Y).

I realized this recently and found it interesting. I just wanted to share this.

I have written up a proof here in some personal notes.
https://github.com/patrick-nicodemus/research/blob/main/geometry/manifolds.typ, line 192

An important theorem here, somehow enabling this nice correspondence seems to be that the pullback functor Top/Y→Top/X\mathbf{Top}/Y\to\mathbf{Top}/XTop/Y→Top/X is cocontinuous. As a result, it preserves pointwise left Kan extensions; so the usual functor τ(Y)→Top/Y\tau(Y) \to \mathbf{Top}/Yτ(Y)→Top/Y (whose associated "realization" is the etale space functor for sheaves on Y) can be composed with the pullback functor to give a realization from sheaves on Y to etale spaces on X. (It is interesting that the pullback functor sends etale spaces to etale spaces.)

John Baez (Dec 23 2025 at 19:21):

The nLab says this:

Grammar and spelling

In French, the verb 'étaler' means, roughly, to spread out; '-er' becomes '-é' to make a past participle. So an 'espace étalé' is a space that has been spread out over BBB. On the other hand, 'étale' is a (relatively obscure, distantly related) nautical adjective that can be translated as 'calm' or 'slack'.

To quote from the Wiktionnaire français:

'étale' _qualifie la mer qui ne monte ni ne descend à la fin du flot ou du jusant_
('flot' = 'flow' and 'jusant' = 'ebb').

  • Further reference

Pronunciation

The French pronunciation of "étalé" is three syllables; that of "étale" is two syllables, with the final "e" silent. Many English-speaking mathematicians follow the respective French pronunciations; see for example this nForum thread.

Others use the two-syllable pronunciation (corresponding to French "étale") for both written forms "étale" and "étalé". This group included [[Grothendieck]] himself, when speaking English. (See Colin McLarty, from 4:45 to 6:05 and particularly at 5:26.)
