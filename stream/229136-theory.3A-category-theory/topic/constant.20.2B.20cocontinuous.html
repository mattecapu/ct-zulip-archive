Tom Hirschowitz (Jun 02 2021 at 10:38):

Tough question, @Morgan Rogers (he/him) :sweat:

They pop up in a work in progress with @Ambroise, but the intuition is in progress too, I'm afraid.

The rough idea is that you start with a free monad S=Σ⋆S = \Sigma^\starS=Σ⋆, and you want to define an operation by induction on S(0)S(0)S(0), i.e., a morphism F(S(0))→S(0)F(S(0)) \to S(0)F(S(0))→S(0) for some endofunctor FFF. We take F(X)F(X)F(X) to have the shape G(X,X)G(X,X)G(X,X) for some GGG which is cocontinuous in its first argument, and (cheating a bit) describe the inductive definition as a natural transformation G(Σ(Y),X)→S(G(Y,X)+Y+X)G(\Sigma(Y),X) \to S ( G(Y,X) + Y + X )G(Σ(Y),X)→S(G(Y,X)+Y+X), where the …+Y+X\ldots + Y + X…+Y+X is used for describing base cases. And the functor Y↦G(Y,X)+Y+XY \mapsto G(Y,X) + Y + XY↦G(Y,X)+Y+X is affine, which is used a lot in the development (for now).
