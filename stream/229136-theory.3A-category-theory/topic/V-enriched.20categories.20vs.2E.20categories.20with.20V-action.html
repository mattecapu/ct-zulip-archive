Topic: V-enriched categories vs. categories with V-action

Tim Campion (Apr 22 2022 at 01:10):

It's well-known that if VVV is a monoidal biclosed category, then a category CCC with an action of VVV which is cocontinuous in the VVV variable is the same data as a VVV-enriched category CCC which is tensored over VVV. So in my head, a category with an action of a monoidal category VVV is a special case of a VVV-enriched category.

As pointed out to me recently by Tyler Lawson, it's important that this breaks down when VVV is monoidal but not closed. For instance, if VVV is monoids under cartesian product, then VVV acts on itself via the monoidal product, but this does not correspond to a self-enrichment. And if you start thinking about how close you can get to talking about an internal hom, you end up thinking about centralizers -- a sort of new phenomenon which is kind of invisible in the monoidal biclosed context.

(More generally, EnE_nEn​-algebras in some category CCC admit an important monoidal structure which is not biclosed.)

So... I guess my question is: what are some other examples of monoidal categories VVV which are not biclosed, and how does the notion of a category with VVV-action differ from, and relate to, the notion of a VVV-enriched category?

Mike Shulman (Apr 22 2022 at 03:54):

In the closed case, I think you need not just cocontinuity but that the action has a right adjoint. Of course if the categories are nice enough that follows from the adjoint functor theorem.

Mike Shulman (Apr 22 2022 at 04:01):

In fact I don't think closedness of VVV has much to do with it. If the action has a right adjoint, it should enrich CCC over VVV regardless of whether VVV is closed in its own right; and if the action doesn't have a right adjoint, it doesn't help to know that VVV is closed.

Mike Shulman (Apr 22 2022 at 04:04):

For instance, if GGG is a group, regarded as a discrete monoidal category, then it is biclosed with internal-homs g−1hg^{-1}hg−1h and hg−1h g^{-1}hg−1. But a discrete GGG-actegory is just a GGG-set, while it is GGG-enriched only if it is a pseudo-torsor (i.e. the action is free and transitive).

Dylan McDermott (Apr 22 2022 at 10:12):

Matteo Capucci (he/him) said:

V only needs to be symmetric monoidal for the theorem to work

The usual definition of "tensored" (e.g. in Kelly's book) needs VVV to be closed, because it asks for isomorphisms

C(v∗c,c′)≅v⊸C(c,c′)C(v * c, c') \cong v \multimap C(c, c')C(v∗c,c′)≅v⊸C(c,c′)

where C(c,c′)C(c, c')C(c,c′) is the hom-object, and ⊸\multimap⊸ is the closed structure of VVV.

But VVV doesn't need to be closed if you instead ask for natural bijections

V(v′,C(v∗c,c′))≅V(v′⊗v,C(c,c′)) V(v', C(v * c, c')) \cong V(v' \otimes v, C(c, c'))V(v′,C(v∗c,c′))≅V(v′⊗v,C(c,c′))

(Symmetry isn't needed at all for this.)

Dylan McDermott (Apr 22 2022 at 10:19):

Tim Campion said:

Hm... is there some kind of adjunction between VVV-categories and VVV-actegories?

The way I prefer to think about this is that all of these things embed into Wood's "large V-categories" (which modulo size issues are categories enriched over [Vop,Set][V^{\mathrm{op}}, \mathbf{Set}][Vop,Set] with Day convolution). Wood shows that there is a fully faithful 2-functor from VVV-categories to [Vop,Set][V^{\mathrm{op}}, \mathbf{Set}][Vop,Set]-categories, and the image of this is the [Vop,Set][V^{\mathrm{op}}, \mathbf{Set}][Vop,Set]-categories CCC for which C(c,c′):Vop→SetC(c, c') : V^{\mathrm{op}} \to \mathbf{Set}C(c,c′):Vop→Set is representable for all c,c′c, c'c,c′ (the representations are the hom-objects). There is a similar fully faithful 2-functor from the 2-category of left V\mathrm{V}V-actegories, strong functors and strong natural transformations, and the image is those CCC for which C(c,−)v:C0→SetC(c, {-})v : C_0 \to \mathbf{Set}C(c,−)v:C0​→Set is representable. (C0C_0C0​ is the underlying ordinary category.)

When C(c,−)vC(c, {-})vC(c,−)v and C(c,c′)C(c, c')C(c,c′) are representable (so there is both an enrichment and an actegory), the representations form an adjunction, and this is one way of proving the theorem about closed actegories and tensored VVV-categories.

Alexander Campbell (Apr 22 2022 at 10:42):

@Tim Campion I give a treatment of this comparison from the "enrichment as structure" POV in Section 4 of my paper on Skew-enriched categories. The idea is that you can see V-categories and V-actegories as special kinds of skew V-proactegories. (N.B. To capture V-categories you really do need to consider skew V-proactegories!)

Mike Shulman (Apr 22 2022 at 17:47):

The way I think of it is that the forgetful functor U:NormalVDC→VDCU : \rm NormalVDC \to VDCU:NormalVDC→VDC has a right adjoint Mod\rm ModMod, and therefore the composite Mod∘U{\rm Mod} \circ UMod∘U is a monad on NormalVDC\rm NormalVDCNormalVDC. This monad, call it TTT, is then also essentially the same as the free TTT-algebra functor, which is of course left adjoint to the forgetful functor TAlg→NormalVDCT \rm Alg \to NormalVDCTAlg→NormalVDC.

Mike Shulman (Apr 22 2022 at 17:49):

In other words, Mod\rm ModMod is a right adjoint when defined on VDC\rm VDCVDC, but a left adjoint when defined on NormalVDC\rm NormalVDCNormalVDC. This makes sense because in order to have a unit V→Mod(V)V\to {\rm Mod}(V)V→Mod(V) you need VVV to have units.
