Topic: Pushouts of Monoids

Jade Master (May 07 2020 at 00:11):

Let (C,⊗,I)(C,\otimes,I)(C,⊗,I) be a monoidal category. Under what conditions does the category Mon(C)\mathsf{Mon(C)}Mon(C) have pushouts? This nlab article says that if CCC is cocomplete and ⊗\otimes⊗ is closed then the free monoid construction can be used to construct certain pushouts but not all of them. More specifically, if T:C→Mon(C)T : C \to \mathsf{Mon}(C)T:C→Mon(C) is the free monoid functor (which exists by a theorem of MacLane section VII), then pushouts over maps
T(A)←T(f)T(B)→gX T(A) \xleftarrow{T(f)} T(B) \xrightarrow{g} XT(A)T(f)​T(B)g​X
exist. A more detailed construction of these pushouts can be found in Lemma 5.2 of this paper. Do pushouts of monoids not exist more generally in a cocomplete monoidal closed category? Does anyone know of a counterexample?

Mike Shulman (May 07 2020 at 00:32):

If I had to bet, I would bet that if CCC is merely cocomplete and monoidal, Mon(C)\mathsf{Mon}(C)Mon(C) might not have pushouts, but I don't have any counterexamples immediately to hand, since categories that are cocomplete but not locally bounded are hard to come by.

Mike Shulman (May 07 2020 at 00:37):

However, if CCC is closed monoidal and cocomplete, I'm pretty sure that Mon(C)\mathsf{Mon}(C)Mon(C) is also cocomplete. I think the trick is that under these hypotheses the free-monoid functor preserves reflexive coequalizers, hence monoids are closed under reflexive coequalizers. Now you can build any colimit of monoids out of coproducts of free monoids (which always exist since the free-monoid functor preserves colimits) and reflexive coequalizers.
