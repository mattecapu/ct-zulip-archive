Let A,BA,BA,B be categories, and R:A→BR:A\to BR:A→B a profunctor between them. Define the "arrow category" of [R][R][R] as follows: objects are tuples (a,b,r:R(a,b))(a,b, r:R(a,b))(a,b,r:R(a,b)) and morphisms are (f:a→a′,g:b→b′)(f:a\to a', g:b\to b')(f:a→a′,g:b→b′) such that m′⋅f=g⋅mm'\cdot f = g\cdot mm′⋅f=g⋅m.

Mike Shulman (Jun 05 2022 at 14:38):

A lax colimit, like any kind of (co)limit, is something that makes sense for any shape diagram in any bicategory. The Grothendieck construction of a functor A→CatA\to \rm CatA→Cat is its lax colimit considered as an AAA-shaped diagram in Cat. I think the Grothendieck construction of a normal lax functor A→ProfA \to \rm ProfA→Prof is also its lax colimit as an AAA-shaped diagram in Prof. But the collage of a profunctor R:A↛BR : A \nrightarrow BR:A↛B is its lax colimit considered as a 2\mathbf{2}2-shaped diagram (i.e. an arrow) in Prof\rm ProfProf.

Mike Shulman (Jun 05 2022 at 14:43):

I think in some paper Street (?) used the word "collage" for an arbitrary lax colimit in Prof or in a Prof-like bicategory.

Mike Shulman (Jun 05 2022 at 14:44):

But the point is that the lax colimit of a profunctor R:A↛BR : A \nrightarrow BR:A↛B qua diagram 2→Prof\mathbf{2}\to \rm Prof2→Prof is very different from the lax colimit of any kind of diagram with domain A×BopA\times B^{op}A×Bop.
