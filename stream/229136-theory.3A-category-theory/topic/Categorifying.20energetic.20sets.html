John Baez (Jul 23 2024 at 13:34):

There's a concept of 'energetic set' which generalizes the concept of finite set, and any energetic set has a 'partition function' which generalizes the cardinality of finite sets. I'm looking for useful categorified, or homotopified, or derived versions of these simple concepts. Can you help? First let me explain the basics.

An energetic set is a set XXX equipped with a function E:X→RE : X \to \mathbb{R}E:X→R. Thanks to the physics applications let's call EEE the energy.

There's a category of energetic sets: it's just the slice category Set/R\mathsf{Set}/\mathbb{R}Set/R. Let's focus on the full subcategory of finite energetic sets to avoid worrying about convergence of sum. So, henceforth my energetic sets will be finite by default.

To an energetic set E ⁣:X→RE \colon X \to \mathbb{R}E:X→R we can assign a real-valued function on the real line called its partition function, defined by

Z(β)=∑i∈Xe−βE(i) Z(\beta) = \sum_{i \in X} e^{-\beta E(i)} Z(β)=∑i∈X​e−βE(i)

The minus sign is a convention taken from physics.

Here's how the partition function of an energetic set generalizes the cardinality of a finite set:

Theorem. Two (finite) energetic sets are isomorphic if and only if their partition functions are equal.

Theorem. When β=0\beta = 0β=0, the partition function of an energetic set Z:Z→RZ: Z \to \mathbb{R}Z:Z→R equals the cardinality ∣X∣|X|∣X∣ of its underlying set.

Theorem. There is a functor from finite sets to energetic sets sending each finite set XXX to that finite set equipped with the energy function E=0E = 0E=0. The partition function of the resulting energetic set equals the cardinality ∣X∣|X|∣X∣.

Theorem. There is a way to make the category of (finite) energetic sets into a rig category where the addition ⊕\oplus⊕ is coproduct and the tensor product ⊗\otimes⊗ of energetic sets E:X→R,E: X \to \mathbb{R},E:X→R, E′:X′→RE': X' \to \mathbb{R}E′:X′→R is given by taking the cartesian product X×X′X \times X'X×X′ and equipping it with the energy function

(E⊗E′)(x,y)=E(x)+E′(y) (E \otimes E')(x,y) = E(x) + E'(y)(E⊗E′)(x,y)=E(x)+E′(y)

Theorem. The partition function of energetic sets AAA and BBB obeys

Z(A⊕B)=Z(A)+Z(B) Z(A \oplus B) = Z(A) + Z(B) Z(A⊕B)=Z(A)+Z(B)

and

Z(A⊗B)=Z(A)Z(B) Z(A \otimes B) = Z(A) Z(B)Z(A⊗B)=Z(A)Z(B)

for any (finite) energetic sets XXX and YYY.

For any category CCC there's a functor from its core (the groupoid of objects of CCC and isomorphisms) to the discrete category whose objects are isomorphism classes of objects of CCC. This functor sends any object to its isomorphism class.

When C=SetC = \mathsf{Set}C=Set this functor is called cardinality. When CCC is the category of energetic sets this functor is ZZZ (up to isomorphism).

We think of our finite set XXX as a set of "possible reconstructions of a point". Each element of XXX corresponds to some particular "brightness" of that point (e.g. this "brightness" could correspond to the estimated amplitude of an ultrasound wave that bounced off this point).

David Egolf (Jul 24 2024 at 18:13):

The energy E(x)E(x)E(x) of some x∈Xx \in Xx∈X is some measure of how much we "like" the particular reconstruction xxx. This could be based on some measure of consistency between observed data and some theoretically predicted data (that we would expect to be observed if xxx was present).

(As E(x)E(x)E(x) goes up, how much we like xxx decreases).

David Egolf (Jul 24 2024 at 18:16):

We then want to put a probability distribution on all the different reconstructions available to us. To do this, we introduce a parameter β\betaβ. Using β\betaβ, the probability we assign to the reconstruction x∈Xx \in Xx∈X is proportional to e−βE(x)e^{-\beta E(x)}e−βE(x).

Intuitively, different values of β\betaβ correspond to different ways of using the information about how much we "like" each reconstruction xxx, which is encoded by E(x)E(x)E(x). When β\betaβ is zero, we completely ignore our E(x)E(x)E(x) preference for each xxx. Presumably we take EEE increasingly more into account as β\betaβ becomes larger.

A weighted species is like a species except that the target category is the category of energetic sets (aka weighted sets).

I.e., a weighted species is a functor F:B→B/RF : B \to B/\mathbb{R}F:B→B/R where BBB is the groupoid of finite sets and bijection, B/RB/\mathbb{R}B/R is the category of energetic sets with "energy-preserving bijections".

It was worth reading, because it may be possible to fix the mistake by using the "Dirichlet generating function" of a species instead of the usual "exponential generating function". I've been studying the Dirichlet generating function here:

  • John Baez and James Dolan, Dirichlet speces and the Hasse-Weil zeta function.

Suppose FFF is a species. Then the exponential generating function of FFF is the formal power series

∑n≥0∣F(n)∣n!xn . \displaystyle{ \sum_{n \ge 0} \frac{|F(n)|}{n!} x^n }\, .n≥0∑​n!∣F(n)∣​xn.

On the other hand, the Dirichlet series associated to FFF is

∑n≥1∣F(n)∣n!n−s . \displaystyle{ \sum_{n \ge 1} \frac{|F(n)|}{n!} n^{-s} } \, . n≥1∑​n!∣F(n)∣​n−s.

The most famous of all Dirichlet series is the Riemann zeta function:

ζ(s)=∑n≥1n−s . \displaystyle{ \zeta(s) = \sum_{n \ge 1} n^{-s}} \, . ζ(s)=n≥1∑​n−s.

This comes from a species we call the Riemann species, ZZZ. A ZZZ-structure on a finite set is a way of making it into a semisimple commutative ring - that is, a product of finite fields.

If we're thinking in terms of a partition function we have to think of ln⁡n\ln nlnn as the energy EnE_nEn​ of the number nnn, because the partition function is always defined as

∑ne−βEn \sum_n e^{-\beta E_n}∑n​e−βEn​

and En=ln⁡nE_n = \ln nEn​=lnn makes the partition function equal to the Riemann zeta function:

∑ne−βEn=∑ne−βln⁡n=∑n1nβ=ζ(β)\displaystyle{ \sum_n e^{-\beta E_n} = \sum_n e^{- \beta \ln n} = \sum_n \frac{1}{n^\beta} = \zeta(\beta) }n∑​e−βEn​=n∑​e−βlnn=n∑​nβ1​=ζ(β)

But there's a very nice interpretation of this: each prime number ppp has energy ln⁡p\ln plnp, and the energy of an arbitrary positive integer is the sum of the energy of the primes in its prime factorization!

So, we are to imagine a cylinder of gas where the atoms come in different kinds, one for each prime number. If we allow an arbitrary number of atoms of each different kind, the partition function of this system is the Riemann zeta function.
