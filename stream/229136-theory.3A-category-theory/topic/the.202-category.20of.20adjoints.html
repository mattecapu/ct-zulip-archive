Topic: the 2-category of adjoints

Tim Campion (Jul 24 2022 at 16:30):

Let CCC be a 2-category. Then there is a wide, locally-full sub-2-category Adj(C)⊆CAdj(C) \subseteq CAdj(C)⊆C, whose 1-morphisms are those 1-morphisms of CCC which have a right adjoint in CCC.

The construction C↦Adj(C)C \mapsto Adj(C)C↦Adj(C) is an endofunctor of 2Cat2Cat2Cat, and the inclusion Adj(C)→CAdj(C) \to CAdj(C)→C makes this into a co-pointed endofunctor. I think it's even co-well-pointed. However, I don't think that this co-well-pointed endofunctor is a comonad.

This is a little weird. On the one hand I tend to think of C↦Adj(C)C \mapsto Adj(C)C↦Adj(C) as an "intrinsically interesting construction". On the other hand, I tend to think of endofunctors which are not co/monads as "technical tools" --surely any endofunctor worth its salt will have the decency to arise from some more fundamental adjunction, and thus be a co/monad, right? So I guess I'm wondering

  1. Is there some other construction C↦foo(C)C \mapsto foo(C)C↦foo(C), related to C↦Adj(C)C \mapsto Adj(C)C↦Adj(C), which is more fundamental (and which perhaps is a co/monad or arises from some adjunction)? Whenever I see C↦Adj(C)C \mapsto Adj(C)C↦Adj(C), should I really be thinking about C↦foo(C)C \mapsto foo(C)C↦foo(C)?

  2. If not, then does C↦Adj(C)C \mapsto Adj(C)C↦Adj(C) have a bit more structure than being a co-well-pointed endofunctor, which might make me feel a bit more "at ease" with this construction?

Mike Shulman (Jul 24 2022 at 17:09):

But it is possible to decompose Adj\rm AdjAdj (or something equivalent to it) into a composite of other functors in a way that might be illuminating. For instance, consider the composite Comp∘H∘Sq{\rm Comp}\circ H \circ \rm SqComp∘H∘Sq where:

  1. Sq:2Cat→Dbl\rm Sq : 2Cat \to DblSq:2Cat→Dbl constructs the double category of squares (= quintets) in a 2-category.
  2. H:Dbl→DblH : \rm Dbl \to DblH:Dbl→Dbl constructs the horizontal opposite of a double category.
  3. Comp:Dbl→2Cat\rm Comp : Dbl\to 2CatComp:Dbl→2Cat constructs the 2-category of [[companion pairs]] in a double category.

The composite Comp∘H{\rm Comp}\circ HComp∘H constructs the 2-category of [[conjunctions]] in a double category, and a conjunction in Sq(C){\rm Sq}(C)Sq(C) is precisely an adjunction in CCC. So this composite constructs the 2-category of adjunctions in a 2-category. This is biequivalent to your Adj(C){\rm Adj}(C)Adj(C), but includes the whole adjunction as data, rather than its mere existence as a property.

In addition, we have an adjunction Sq⊣Comp\rm Sq \dashv CompSq⊣Comp, so the composite Comp∘Sq\rm Comp \circ SqComp∘Sq is a monad (though it is biequivalent to the identity). So this modified version of Adj\rm AdjAdj is a "twist" (HHH) away from being a monad.
