I will stop being lazy and I will try to start writing down everything. X⊗X~→C^\mathcal{X} \otimes \widetilde{X} \rightarrow \widehat{C}X⊗X→C over the terminal topos is the same thing as X⊗X~→X⊗C^\mathcal{X} \otimes \widetilde{X} \rightarrow \mathcal{X} \otimes \widehat{C}X⊗X→X⊗C over X\mathcal{X}X (using the universal property of the pullback). So the "correct abstract notion" of a CCC-torsor is a flat morphism from an X\mathcal{X}X-site that presents X⊗C^\mathcal{X} \otimes \widehat{C}X⊗C. But I don't know if that X\mathcal{X}X-site is just Γ∗(C)\Gamma^* (C)Γ∗(C) where Γ\GammaΓ is the global section morphism of X\mathcal{X}X and Γ∗(C)=Γ∗(C1)⇉Γ∗(C0)\Gamma^* (C) = \Gamma^* (C_1) \rightrightarrows \Gamma^* (C_0)Γ∗(C)=Γ∗(C1​)⇉Γ∗(C0​) (or, in the indexed language, it's just post-composition by Γ∗\Gamma^*Γ∗ applied to the associated sheaf of categories).
