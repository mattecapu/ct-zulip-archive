Topic: Categories which admit enrichment in Hausdorff spaces

Eigil Rischel (Nov 04 2025 at 15:41):

Suppose JJJ is some class of limit shapes (for example, all countable limits). Let CCC be a category which has all the limits in JJJ. Suppose we are given an enrichment of CCC over topological spaces (a topology on each homset so that composition is continuous in the product topology). Then we can ask whether the limits in JJJ are compatible with this enrichment, in the sense that the bijection Hom(X,lim⁡iYi)≅lim⁡iHom(X,Yi)Hom(X, \lim_i Y_i) \cong \lim_i Hom(X,Y_i)Hom(X,limi​Yi​)≅limi​Hom(X,Yi​) is a homeomorphism (again, giving the right-hand side the topology of the limit in Top\mathsf{Top}Top. Call this a compatible enrichment.

Clearly every category admits at least one compatible enrichment, namely the indiscrete one. It's not hard to see that (using Zorn's lemma) there is a maximal compatible enrichment (in the sense of having the most open sets). I'm interested in categories whose maximal such enrichment is Hausdorff (that is, every hom-space is Hausdorff). This is clearly equivalent to the existence of just one compatible Hausdorff enrichment.

This has some non-trivial implications about CCC. For example, suppose JJJ is the set of all countable limits. Let fn:A→∏iBif_n: A \to \prod_i B_ifn​:A→∏i​Bi​ be a sequence of maps into a product, writing fni:A→Bif_n^i : A \to B_ifni​:A→Bi​ for the components. Suppose the sequences fnif_n^ifni​ all stabilize at some point, fni=f∞if_n^i = f_\infty^ifni​=f∞i​ when n>Nin > N_in>Ni​. Then these converge, so in any compatible Hausdorff enrichment, fnf_nfn​ also converges to f∞f_\inftyf∞​ (with components f∞if_\infty^if∞i​). Let g:∏iBi→Cg: \prod_i B_i \to Cg:∏i​Bi​→C be any map. Then if gfn=hg f^n = hgfn=h for all nnn (that is, this composite is constant), we must also have gf∞=hg f^\infty = hgf∞=h. But this implication fails in the category of sets.

Clearly one condition that implies this if if every object can be written as a JJJ-limit of JJJ-cosmall objects (i.e those where a map out of a JJJ-limit into them always factors over a projection). But, for example, I think the category of compact Hausdorff spaces has the above property but is not countably cogenerated in this sense.

Does anyone know of previous work studying this property, or an equivalent formulation in terms of "pure" category theory? (I was a bit vague above, but it's fine if JJJ is just given by the limits smaller than some regular cardinal.)
