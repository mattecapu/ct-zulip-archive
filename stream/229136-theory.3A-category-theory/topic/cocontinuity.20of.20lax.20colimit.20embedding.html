Topic: cocontinuity of lax colimit embedding

Tom Hirschowitz (Aug 06 2024 at 13:06):

Sorry for spamming, but here's a second question in a row: if we consider the lax colimit 𝐀𝐀A of a functor F ⁣:𝐂→𝐃F\colon 𝐂 → 𝐃F:C→D in 𝐂𝐚𝐭𝐂𝐚𝐭Cat, we get embeddings in0 ⁣:𝐂→𝐀in₀\colon 𝐂 → 𝐀in0​:C→A and in1 ⁣:𝐃→𝐀in₁\colon 𝐃 → 𝐀in1​:D→A, equipped with a natural transformation λ ⁣:in0∘F→in1λ\colon in₀ ∘ F → in₁λ:in0​∘F→in1​. If I'm not mistaken, the embedding in1 ⁣:𝐃→𝐀in₁\colon 𝐃 → 𝐀in1​:D→A is always cocontinuous. Is this right, and if so do you know a reference for it?

Tom Hirschowitz (Sep 18 2024 at 15:02):

Ok, @Yoann Barszezak and I finally took some time to look into this, sorry for the delay!
First of all, I swapped in0in₀in0​ and in1in₁in1​ in the type of λλλ, sorry about that too, I just fixed the original message.
Also, according to the nlab, a [[sieve]] is necessary fully faithful, so we omit to mention it.
Our conclusions are that:

  • First of all, in1in_1in1​ is a full coreflection, hence in particular a left adjoint, hence cocontinuous. This may be proved purely 2-categorically from the universal property of 𝐀𝐀A. In fact, it is also a sieve, hence it is continuous.
  • Also, in0in₀in0​ is indeed a cosieve, hence it is cocontinuous. This is also elementary, but we don't know how to prove it 2-categorically.

(However, we still don't have any reference...)
