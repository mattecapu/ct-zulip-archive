Topic: Fullness on isomorphisms

Amar Hadzihasanovic (Jan 06 2021 at 14:08):

I have a faithful forgetful functor U:C→DU: C \to DU:C→D and I'm trying to capture the idea that “a DDD-object admits, essentially, at most one structure of CCC-object”, so an isomorphism-robust version of injective-on-objects. The correct notion, in my case, seems to be that, in addition to being faithful, UUU reflects and is full on isomorphisms, that is, any isomorphism f′:Uc→Udf': Uc \to Udf′:Uc→Ud is the image of a (necessarily unique) isomorphism f:c→df: c \to df:c→d.

I have the impression that this kind of forgetful functors is quite common: examples (if I'm not wrong) are the forgetful functor Mnd→SemiGp\mathbf{Mnd} \to \mathbf{SemiGp}Mnd→SemiGp from monoids to semigroups, or the forgetful functor CartMonCat→MonCat\mathbf{CartMonCat} \to \mathbf{MonCat}CartMonCat→MonCat from (cartesian monoidal categories, strong symmetric monoidal functors) to (monoidal categories, strong monoidal functors)...
So I would imagine that the notion deserves a name, but I don't think I've seen it before. Is this familiar to anyone?

Nathanael Arkor (Jan 06 2021 at 14:21):

This is not quite the same, but it is perhaps suitable for your purposes. A monad on a complete category is property-like if the forgetful functor U:T-Alg→CU : T\text{-}\mathrm{Alg} \to \mathscr CU:T-Alg→C is injective-on-objects, or equivalently if it is pseudomonic (i.e. faithful and full on isomorphisms). (This is Theorem 7.1 of Kelly–Lack's On property-like structures.)

Nathanael Arkor (Jan 06 2021 at 14:22):

This just lacks your isomorphism reflection condition.

Amar Hadzihasanovic (Jan 06 2021 at 14:30):

Ah, but pseudomonicity implies isomorphism reflection. If UfUfUf is an isomorphism, then it lifts to some isomorphism ggg, and Uf=UgUf = UgUf=Ug implies f=gf = gf=g by faithfulness, so fff was an iso to begin with.

Amar Hadzihasanovic (Jan 06 2021 at 14:30):

So that's exactly what I was looking for. Thank you Nathanael!
