Reed Mullanix (Nov 16 2024 at 22:03):

There are a couple of angles one can approach them, but this is the direction I'm interested in:

Recall that for CCC, DDD categories and F:C→CatF : C \to \mathrm{Cat}F:C→Cat, the wreath product C≀FDC \wr_{F} DC≀F​D is the fibration associated with the indexed category [F(−),D]:Cop→Cat[ F(-) , D ] : C^{op} \to \mathrm{Cat}[F(−),D]:Cop→Cat. Explicitly, the objects of this fibration consist of pairs (c:C,K:F(c)→D)(c : C, K : F(c) \to D)(c:C,K:F(c)→D) with KKK a functor, and maps between (c,K)(c, K)(c,K) and (c′,K′)(c', K')(c′,K′) consist of pairs (f:C(c,c′),α:K→K′∘F(f)(f : C(c,c'), \alpha : K \to K' \circ F(f)(f:C(c,c′),α:K→K′∘F(f).

A 2-sided wreath product replaces the indexed category F:C→CatF : C \to \mathrm{Cat}F:C→Cat with a 2-sided fibration C←E→CC \leftarrow E \to CC←E→C. The objects of the resulting category are pairs (c:C,K:Ec,c→D)(c : C, K : E_{c,c} \to D)(c:C,K:Ec,c​→D) with KKK a functor, and maps between (c,K)(c, K)(c,K) and (c′,K′)(c', K')(c′,K′) are pairs (f:C(c,c′),α:K∘f∗→K′∘f∗)(f : C(c,c'), \alpha : K \circ f^{*} \to K '\circ f_{*})(f:C(c,c′),α:K∘f∗→K′∘f∗​).

This is quite a nice little object, as it lets us probe DDD with "loop-shaped diagrams" indexed by CCC. Some fun ones to consider:

  • For an endofunctor F:C→CF : C \to CF:C→C, we can wreath with the discrete 2-sided fibrations C(F(−),=)C(F(-),=)C(F(−),=) and C(−,F(=))C(-,F(=))C(−,F(=)), resp. This lets us probe DDD with FFF-algebras and FFF-coalgebras, resp.
  • If CCC is Cat\mathrm{Cat}Cat, then we can wreath by [−,=][-,=][−,=] to probe DDD by endofunctors.
  • In a similar vein, if CCC is Δ\DeltaΔ and Lin:Δ→CatLin : \Delta \to \mathrm{Cat}Lin:Δ→Cat is the functor that sends an object of Δ\DeltaΔ to the corresponding linear order, then we can wreath by [Lin(−),Lin(=)][Lin(-), Lin(=)][Lin(−),Lin(=)] to get a category of "subdivision diagrams" in DDD.
