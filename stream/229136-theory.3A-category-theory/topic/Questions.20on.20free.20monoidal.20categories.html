In ordinary 1-category theory, if you want to form the coproduct of two MMM-algebras (or really a family of MMM-algebras), and if you assume

  • The underlying category has coproducts,
  • The category of algebras has reflexive coequalizers,

then what you can do is follow the construction of Theorem 2.2 here. This is a suitable quotient of M(∑iAi)M(\sum_i A_i)M(∑i​Ai​), and more or less manifests what Kevin was driving at earlier (except that we're in a 2-categorical situation, so you should use a codescent object in place of a reflexive coequalizer, but the basic idea is quite similar).

Todd Trimble (Aug 23 2024 at 21:00):

Reflexive coequalizers turn out to be useful for lots of things.
