Topic: Every double category is a double colimit in a canonical way

Patrick Nicodemus (Aug 13 2023 at 01:42):

We can regard 2-category theory as Cat-enriched category theory. In this setting, if C\mathcal{C}C is a small category, then the weighted colimit of 1:1→Cat1 : 1\to \mathbf{Cat}1:1→Cat with weights in the "presheaf" C:1→Cat\mathcal{C} : 1\to \mathbf{Cat}C:1→Cat is C\mathcal{C}C itself. Thus every category can be written in a canonical way as the colimit of the one-object category. (This is well known but I would like a reference for it if anyone can find one)

Anyway, is there anything similar for double categories? Where every double category is in a canonical way a double colimit of the one object double category, the walking vertical arrow, and the walking composition of two vertical morphisms, or something like this. I am studying a functor on double categories which has a 1-categorical right adjoint and so should preserve colimits, and I am looking for an elegant way to state this, that its behavior on any double category is simply determined by gluing together the "primitive" double categories that make it up.

James Deikun (Aug 13 2023 at 12:33):

The statement in Cat\bold{Cat}Cat-enriched category theory is that the functor 1:1→Cat1 : 1 \to \bold{Cat}1:1→Cat is dense. You should probably look for a dense generator in Cat\bold{Cat}Cat-internal category theory.

Ok, I'll post what I'm thinking so far.

Context/notation: Double categories are composed of two categories EO\mathbb{E}_OEO​, EA\mathbb{E}_AEA​ called "the category of objects" and "the category of vertical arrows", together with domain, codomain, composition, identity functors.
Morphisms in EO\mathbb{E}_OEO​ are called "horizontal arrows." Objects in EA\mathbb{E}_AEA​ are called "vertical arrows." Morphisms in EA\mathbb{E}_AEA​ are called 2-cells.

My guess is that if I work with the 2-category of double categories, double functors, and horizontal natural transformations, I can construct a simple dense 2-functor which would work. So let Dbl\mathbf{Dbl}Dbl refer to the 2-category of strict double categories, double functors and horizontal natural transformations.

Let Δ0,Δ1,Δ2\Delta^0,\Delta^1,\Delta^2Δ0,Δ1,Δ2 be the 0-dimensional, 1-dimensional, and 2-dimensional simplexes respectively, viewed as vertical categories (horizontally discrete). For example, the category ΔA1\Delta^1_AΔA1​ contains three objects: the "walking vertical 1 arrow" vvv as well as identity vertical arrows for the domain and codomain objects of vvv.
The usual face and degeneracy maps in the simplex category do extend to double functors between these double categories. Thus we can assemble this into a diagram in the category of double categories, let me write G=Δ∣[0],[1],[2]G= \Delta\mid_{[0],[1],[2]}G=Δ∣[0],[1],[2]​ for the full subcategory of the usual simplex category Δ\DeltaΔ containing [0],[1],[2][0], [1], [2][0],[1],[2], and R:G→DblR : G\to\mathbf{Dbl}R:G→Dbl for the "free vertical category" functor. The diagram RRR itself does not contain any nontrivial natural transformations, as GGG has no nontrivial natural transformations.

My working conjecture is that RRR is dense in the Cat\mathbf{Cat}Cat-enriched sense? I still have to work out the details on pen and paper.

If E∈Dbl\mathbb{E} \in \mathbf{Dbl}E∈Dbl is a double category then the Cat\mathbf{Cat}Cat-enriched nerve of E\mathbb{E}E with respect to the functor RRR is a Cat\mathbf{Cat}Cat-valued presheaf on RRR.
Let us first look at what RRR looks like when we only consider the underlying presheaf of objects and ignore morphisms. If we look at its category of elements, El⁡(N(R)(E))\operatorname{El}(N(R)(\mathbb{E}))El(N(R)(E)) is a 1-category whose objects are the set of double functors [Δ0,D]⨿[Δ1,D]⨿[Δ2,D][\Delta_0,\mathbb{D}] \amalg[\Delta_1,\mathbb{D}] \amalg [\Delta_2,\mathbb{D}] [Δ0​,D]⨿[Δ1​,D]⨿[Δ2​,D], essentially the set of objects, vertical morphisms and vertical compositions in D\mathbb{D}D. This is fibered over GGG by a projection functor π:El(N(R)(E))→G\pi: \mathrm{El}(N(R)(\mathbb{E})) \to Gπ:El(N(R)(E))→G.

This is a first approximation to the problem of writing E\mathbb{E}E as the colimit of a diagram of simpler objects, the colimit R∘πR\circ \piR∘π would have the same objects and vertical morphisms as E\mathbb{E}E (I think, up to bijection). Moreover we would recover the vertical composition structure of E\mathbb{E}E and the unit arrows. However our construction so far is incomplete as it ignores the horizontal arrows in E\mathbb{E}E and the 2-cells, i.e., it neglects the fact that EO\mathbb{E}_OEO​ and EA\mathbb{E}_AEA​ are categories and not just sets. This is why we need to work 2-categorically.
