Topic: Monads of effective descent type and comonadicity

Brendan Murphy (Jul 08 2024 at 21:28):

Hi all. I'm having trouble understanding the (unstated) proof of Corollary 3.12 in the TAC paper "Monads of effective descent type and comonadicity". It says that if T\mathbf{T}T is a monad on an idempotent complete category A\mathcal{A}A whose underlying functor TTT is a left adjoint then T\mathbf{T}T is of effective descent type iff TTT is conservative. This is a corollary of a proposition that says T\mathbf{T}T is of effective descent type iff TTT is quasi-comonadic, i.e. preserves equalizers of TTT-split pairs in A\mathcal{A}A (which all exist). I don't see how the existence of the right adjoint tells us anything about preserves equalizers. What's going on here? Is the corollary misstated in that it should assume TTT is a right adjoint, so it preserves all limits?
