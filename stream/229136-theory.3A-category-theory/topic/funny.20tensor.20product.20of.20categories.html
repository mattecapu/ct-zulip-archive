Topic: funny tensor product of categories

Jem (May 27 2020 at 19:32):

The funny tensor product C□DC \square DC□D is a monoidal product on CatstrCat_{str}Catstr​ defined by the pushout of C0×D←C0×D0→C×D0C_0 \times D \leftarrow C_0 \times D_0 \to C \times D_0C0​×D←C0​×D0​→C×D0​, where C0C_0C0​ and D0D_0D0​ are the discrete categories on the objects. This forms a symmetric monoidal closed structure on CatstrCat_{str}Catstr​, with the internal hom defined to be the category of functors and unnatural transformations (i.e. transformations without the naturality condition).
Unfortunately, the funny tensor product isn't invariant under equivalence of categories. The unit is 1\mathbf 11 so 1□1≅1\mathbf 1 \square \mathbf 1 \cong \mathbf 11□1≅1, but denoting the indiscrete category on two objects I\mathbf II, we get that I□I≅BZ\mathbf I \square \mathbf I \cong B\mathbb ZI□I≅BZ is a (four object) category equivalent to the delooping of the integers.
Is there a variant of this tensor product which is invariant under equivalence? We could replace C0C_0C0​ with core(C)core(C)core(C) above, but the nlab states that this won't be closed in the 1-category CatstrCat_{str}Catstr​ (see Algebraic categories with few monoidal biclosed structures or none by F. Foltz, G.M. Kelly, and C. Lair.). What happens in the 2-categorical case (or the 1-category of non-strict categories, for that matter)?
