Topic: free cartesian structure over a monoidal category

Matteo Capucci (he/him) (Dec 08 2022 at 15:08):

(crossposting from the nForum)
Fox's theorem famously states that given a monoidal category, the cofree way to make it into a cartesian one is to take the category of comonoids therein. This gives a right adjoint to the forgetful functor CartMonCat→MonCat\bf CartMonCat \to MonCatCartMonCat→MonCat. What about the left adjoint?

Nathanael Arkor (Dec 08 2022 at 16:43):

Matteo Capucci (he/him) said:

Neil Ghani suggested, very plausibly, that it's the functor freely throwing in diagonals/terminal morphisms, and quotienting hom-sets so that both are natural

This sounds right. It's a general fact that any morphism of algebraic theories T→T′T \to T'T→T′ induces a left-adjoint Alg(T)→Alg(T′)\mathrm{Alg}(T) \to \mathrm{Alg}(T')Alg(T)→Alg(T′) which, informally, freely adds the T′T'T′-structure to a TTT-algebra. This fact carries over to 2-algebraic theories. The left adjoint will add the structure which is "missing": which is the natural comonoid structure on each object.

Claudio Pisani (Dec 13 2022 at 11:03):

This is related to the monoidal closed structure on colored operads (that is, symmetric multicategories),
where A⊗BA\otimes BA⊗B is the Boardman-Vogt tensor product and [A,B][A,B][A,B] is the operad of functors A→BA\to BA→B
(see my "sequential multicategories"paper for details).
Indeed, if 111 is the terminal operad, 1⊗A1\otimes A1⊗A and [1,A][1,A][1,A] give respectively the reflection
and the coreflection of AAA in sequential operads (those whose operations are sequences of unary operations).
If we restrict to the "representable" or "monoidal" operads, the sequential ones are the cocartesian monoidal categories, so that we get the reflection and the coreflection of monoidal categories in the cocartesian ones.
While [1,A][1,A][1,A] is the operad of monoids in AAA, 1⊗A1\otimes A1⊗A is obtained, as you said, by adding a commutative monoid structure on each object of AAA and quotienting to make each operation in AAA to commute with these monoid structures.

Since the above result holds in the context of operads, when we restrict to the representable operads
the relevant morphismsare lax functors of monoidal categories.
This puzzles me since, as you mentioned, Fox's result is stated for strong functors.
That is, it seems to me somewhat strange that both the lax form of Fox's theorem
(corollary 3.19 in the cited paper) and the original strong form can be both true.
