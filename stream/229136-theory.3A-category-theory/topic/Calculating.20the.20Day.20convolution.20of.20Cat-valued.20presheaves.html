Topic: Calculating the Day convolution of Cat-valued presheaves

Jesse Sigal (Aug 09 2021 at 20:20):

Summary of my request for references/help :smile:

Let (C,⊗,e)(\mathcal{C}, \otimes, e)(C,⊗,e) be a monoidal category, and consider the category [Cop,Cat][\mathcal{C}^\text{op}, \text{Cat}][Cop,Cat] of Cat\text{Cat}Cat-valued presheaves and natural transformations. I want to:

  1. Find an specialized definition of Day convolution for [Cop,Cat][\mathcal{C}^\text{op}, \text{Cat}][Cop,Cat]; and
  2. Find an explicit/concrete description of it.

Finding a specialized definition

I'm aware of the standard definition of Day convolution in terms of V\mathcal{V}V-enriched categories. I have also seen the coend formula when V=Set\mathcal{V} = \text{Set}V=Set. However, I do not know any enriched category theory and so cannot at the moment make use of the standard definition.

I also know that Cat\text{Cat}Cat-enriched categories are the same as (strict) 222-categories, but I haven't found any definitions of Day convolution for 222-categories (which hopefully would be easier to learn than enriched category theory).

In conclusion, I'm looking for a pre-existing specialized definition to V=Cat\mathcal{V} = \text{Cat}V=Cat (and thus C\mathcal{C}C is given the standard/trivial enrichment as Cat\text{Cat}Cat is cocomplete) or a 222-categorical definition.

Finding an explicit/concrete description

I've successfully applied the definition of Day convolution in the case of Set\text{Set}Set-valued presheaves. I expanded the coend formula to what it means in terms of universal properties. I also wrote it down as an explicit coequalizer.

In the end I had a description of the resulting set in terms of an equivalence relation generated by the two actions of the morphism on the profunctor I took the coend over.

The above is the level of concreteness I'm looking for.

What I'd like

For (1) I'd appreciate one of

  • a link if a definition exists; or
  • a quick working out of the general definition in the special case of Cat\text{Cat}Cat if it's trivial for you; or
  • telling me I should just invest my time in learning enough enriched category theory to understand the standard definition.

For (2) if someone has a definition, an explicit description of the resulting category (objects and morphisms only is fine).

Thanks in advance for any and all help :smiley:

John Baez (Aug 10 2021 at 01:21):

It sounds like you know how Day convolution works for ordinary Set-valued presheaves on a monoidal category. What sort of descriptions do you know for how to convolve two such presheaves F,G:Cop→SetF, G: \mathcal{C}^{\rm op} \to \mathrm{Set}F,G:Cop→Set? Many of these descriptions generalize painlessly to Cat-valued presheaves. Basically, you just write down the same thing but wherever you wrote "set" you now write "category", and wherever you wrote "function" you now write "functor".

fosco (Aug 10 2021 at 08:08):

You should definitely spend a couple of days learning the definition of enriched category and enriched presheaf, to understand that (so to speak) all categorical notions "transport rigidly" from the SetSetSet-world to the V\cal VV-world.

What you ask is "trivial" in the following sense: once you have written down the coend

∫XYFX×GY×hom⁡(_,X⊕Y)\displaystyle \int^{XY} FX \times GY \times \hom(\_, X\oplus Y)∫XYFX×GY×hom(_,X⊕Y)

for SetSetSet-presheaves, interpreted it as a left Kan extension, or as a coequaliser, and found the equivalence relation over ∐XYFX×GY×hom⁡(_,X⊕Y)\coprod_{XY} FX \times GY \times \hom(\_, X\oplus Y)∐XY​FX×GY×hom(_,X⊕Y), you do the same things in CatCatCat; the only problem is that coequalisers in CatCatCat tend to be quite difficult to describe explicitly even though they always exist.

Just to be sure: your functors C→Cat{\cal C} \to {\rm Cat}C→Cat are strict functors, right? Not pseudo.

fosco (Aug 10 2021 at 12:58):

One problem with weaker notions of co/end is that the result of the integral might not be what you expect. For example, the lax coend of the diagram C→Cat{\cal C} \to {\sf Cat}C→Cat sending an object to the slice category over it is the twisted arrow category of C\cal CC; and if you laxify the yoneda lemma with coends, what you get is that there is not an isomorphism F≅∫Ahom⁡(_,A)×FAF \cong \int^A \hom(\_,A)\times FAF≅∫Ahom(_,A)×FA, if FFF is lax, because the RHS is not lax. Instead, you have pseudo-ified F!

Mike Shulman (Aug 10 2021 at 13:12):

Lax things are weird, but pseudo things are much less weird, and often what you want when doing 2-category theory.
