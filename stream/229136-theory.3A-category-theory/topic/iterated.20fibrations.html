Given p:E→Bp:E \to Bp:E→B and q:F→Bq:F \to Bq:F→B [[Grothendieck fibrations]], a morphism ϕ:p→q\phi : p \to qϕ:p→q is a fibration between them (in the sense of [[fibration in a 2-category]]) iff ϕ\phiϕ makes the evident triangle commute and ϕ:E→F\phi : E \to Fϕ:E→F is a Grothendieck fibration itself. That's a well-known theorem proven, for instance in this paper by Hermida.
In particular this theorem tells us that we don't need to check wheter ϕ\phiϕ preserves ppp-cartesian arrows. If it is a fibration, then the following commutes automatically (for any f:b→b′f : b \to b'f:b→b′ in BBB):
