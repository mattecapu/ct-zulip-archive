Topic: Interchange between free products and tensor products

Tobias Fritz (Apr 05 2025 at 14:44):

I've recently been pondering a bit what a good approach to categorical quantum probability should look like. One approach that seems worth pursuing would be to consider both the tensor product and the free product of observable algebras at the same time, and I believe that these two interact quite nicely by forming a normal duoidal category. This is based on the fact that an interchange morphism

(A⊗B)∗(C⊗D)⟶(A∗C)⊗(B∗D)(A \otimes B) \ast (C \otimes D) \longrightarrow (A \ast C) \otimes (B \ast D)(A⊗B)∗(C⊗D)⟶(A∗C)⊗(B∗D)

is straightforward to construct from the universal property of both monoidal structures. (Do this in the category of rings and ring homomorphisms, where the free product ∗\ast∗ is the coproduct, and the tensor product ⊗\otimes⊗ has the analogous universal property but restricted to pairs of homomorphisms with commuting ranges.)

More generally, this looks like just one instance of a very general fact about "free products" and "tensor products", and I imagine that it works likewise in many other cases, such as the following two:

  • With ∗\ast∗ the funny tensor product of categories and ⊗\otimes⊗ the usual product.
  • With ∗\ast∗ the free product of operads and ⊗\otimes⊗ the Boardman-Vogt tensor product.

Nathanael Arkor (Apr 06 2025 at 14:45):

I agree that Commutativity is the right context, but it's less clear to me that Proposition 18 gives you everything you want already. I agree that ⊙\odot⊙ in their notation gives the cartesian product of categories and Boardman–Vogt tensor product of operads. However, I don't see that they show that ⊙\odot⊙ itself forms a duoidal structure on V-Cat\mathcal V\text{-}\mathbf{Cat}V-Cat with respect to another monoidal structure. In the categories example, the funny tensor product is their □\square□. Presumably □\square□ in the example of operads is the free product?

Nayan Rajesh (Apr 07 2025 at 09:52):

Its a general fact that any monoidal category with finite products/coproducts is duoidal (Example 6.19 here) just using the universality of the product/coproduct. The fact that Cat with the funny tensor and the cartesian product is duoidal follows from this; I've never come across the free product of operads, but if it refers to the coproduct then the second example should also follow from this.

Tobias Fritz (Apr 07 2025 at 09:57):
