Topic: The free F-algebra is a T-algebra

Patrick Nicodemus (May 27 2023 at 19:33):

In my research I have been coming across hypotheses and definitions like this:

  • CCC and DDD are categories, FFF is a functor C→DC\to DC→D. TTT is a monad on DDD. An (F,T)(F,T)(F,T) structure on an object XXX in CCC is a TTT-algebra structure on F(X)F(X)F(X). A morphism of (F,T)(F,T)(F,T)-structures is a map f:X→Yf:X\to Yf:X→Y such that F(f)F(f)F(f) is a morphism of TTT-algebras. (Maybe we could also say that XXX is a TTT-algebra relative to FFF.)

  • A special case of the above. CCC is a category. TTT and FFF are both monads on TTT. There is a given distributive law α:T⇒F\alpha :T\Rightarrow Fα:T⇒F so that TTT operates on FFF-algebras in a natural way. An (F,T)(F,T)(F,T) structure on XXX in CCC is a TTT-algebra structure on the free FFF-algebra (FX,μXF)(FX, \mu^F_X)(FX,μXF​).

Replace monad with comonad, etc.

I would like to ask what examples of this situation you can think of and where you tend to observe them. What papers have needed similar hypotheses?

Has anybody ever heard of a theorem of the following form:

Let FFF be a monad on CCC and TTT a monad on DDD. Then both FFF and TTT act on the functor category [C;D][C;D][C;D].

Assume X:C→DX : C\to DX:C→D has the property that X∘FX\circ FX∘F is a TTT-algebra, and moreover the TTT-algebra structure is coherent with that of FFF. Then, (under some additional hypotheses on F,TF,TF,T), the limit of XXX is a TTT-algebra.

Patrick Nicodemus (May 28 2023 at 03:03):

It is a variant of the idea that the limit of algebras is again an algebra but somehow it is much stronger because we do not require that all objects in the diagram XXX are algebras, or all the morphisms in XXX algebra morphisms. Only those which are the images of free FFF-algebras and algebra maps between them. Maybe I need additional hypotheses which involve that the FFF-algebras are somehow "dense" enough in CCC.
