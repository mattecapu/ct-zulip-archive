Morgan Rogers (he/him) (May 15 2026 at 11:10):

I've been thinking about this a little. It's easier for me to think about the induced monad on the slice category (I'll call it MMM) so I'll do that for now.
MMM sends f:X→Af:X\to Af:X→A to π2:X×A→A\pi_2:X \times A \to Aπ2​:X×A→A; the unit is the graph of fff. That means that an algebra is a morphism α:X×A→X\alpha: X \times A \to Xα:X×A→X such that

  • f(α(x,a))=af(\alpha(x,a))=af(α(x,a))=a (condition to be a morphism over AAA)
  • α(x,f(x))=x\alpha(x,f(x))=xα(x,f(x))=x (condition to be a retraction of the unit)
  • α(α(x,a),b)=α(x,b)\alpha(\alpha(x,a),b) = \alpha(x,b)α(α(x,a),b)=α(x,b) (compatibility with multiplication)

The first condition forces fff to be epic, and being a split epi is sufficient for an algebra structure to exist. Not too surprising.

The reason for considering this is that the category of MMM-algebras is the only reconstructible replacement for EEE once we have only the slice and the induced monad. How much of EEE can we recover?

There is a canonical comparison functor from EEE to algebras sending YYY to the free algebra (π2:Y×A→A,π13)(\pi_2:Y \times A\to A, \pi_{13})(π2​:Y×A→A,π13​). This functor need not be faithful (consider a case where AAA is an object of a topos which is not well-supported), but we can still consider whether C≃CM/(idA,π2)C \simeq C^M/(\mathrm{id}_A, \pi_2)C≃CM/(idA​,π2​), where the latter is (isomorphic to) the free algebra on the terminal object of EEE.
