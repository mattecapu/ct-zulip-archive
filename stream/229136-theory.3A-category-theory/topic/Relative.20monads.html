Topic: Relative monads

Bruno Gavranović (Nov 30 2022 at 12:04):

If a monad can be viewed as a lax functor 1→Cat1 \to \mathbf{Cat}1→Cat, what kind of analogous construction can a relative monad be viewed as?

Nathanael Arkor (Nov 30 2022 at 14:40):

I can at least explain why the answer is not obvious. Giving a lax functor 1→K1 \to \mathcal K1→K is equivalent to giving an object A∈KA \in \mathcal KA∈K together with a lax monoidal functor 1→K[A,A]1 \to \mathcal K[A, A]1→K[A,A], hence a monoid in K[A,A]\mathcal K[A, A]K[A,A], which is precisely a monad on AAA in K\mathcal KK.

We can try to go in the opposite direction for relative monads. Assuming the existence of enough pointwise left extensions, (j ⁣:A→E)(j \colon A \to E)(j:A→E)-relative monads can be presented as monoids in skew-monoidal categories [A,E]j[A, E]_j[A,E]j​ (I've made the unit jjj explicit to reduce ambiguity). These are equivalent to lax skew-monoidal functors 1→[A,E]j1 \to [A, E]_j1→[A,E]j​. Notice to generalise from lax monoidal functors to lax functors, in the case of monads, we needed to specify the data of an object in a 2-category. To generalise from a lax skew-monoidal functor to some functor-like structure F ⁣:1→KF \colon 1 \to \mathcal KF:1→K, we therefore need FFF to specify not an object AAA, but rather a 1-cell j ⁣:A→Ej \colon A \to Ej:A→E. However, it is not clear what such a thing should look like in general, particularly if AAA is entirely unrelated to EEE (in an informal sense). Notice that figuring out what FFF should look like requires us first to work out what kind of structure K\mathcal KK should be in this situation, which is also not obvious.

If we have a coherent choice of jA ⁣:A→EAj_A \colon A \to E_AjA​:A→EA​ for each AAA, then there is one potential candidate: we may take K\mathcal KK to be a skew-bicategory in the sense of Lack and Street and FFF to be a "lax skew-functor". In particular, this generalises the "monads as lax functors" perspective, taking jA=1Aj_A = 1_AjA​=1A​. However, it does not permit us to capture arbitrary relative monads (e.g. two relative monads with domain AAA but different codomains).

Nathanael Arkor (Nov 30 2022 at 14:44):

(The story also becomes more complicated when we don't have enough pointwise left extensions, for which skew-monoidal categories do not suffice. For this, one needs to move to some kind of multicategorical structure: cf. my Octoberfest slides.)
