Topic: freely adding comprehensions

Mike Shulman (Jan 15 2022 at 02:02):

If P:Top→CatP:T^{\rm op}\to \rm CatP:Top→Cat is a hyperdoctrine with at least products in the fiber categories, then there is a way of "freely" adding Lawvere-style comprehension to it. The base category T+T^+T+ is the Grothendieck construction of PPP, with objects (x∈T,a∈P(x))(x\in T, a\in P(x))(x∈T,a∈P(x)), and the fiber category P+(x,a)P^+(x,a)P+(x,a) is the Kleisli category of the comonad a×−a\times -a×− on P(x)P(x)P(x): its objects are those of P(x)P(x)P(x), while a morphism from bbb to ccc in P+(x,a)P^+(x,a)P+(x,a) is a morphism a×b→ca\times b\to ca×b→c in P(x)P(x)P(x). The comprehension of bbb in this fiber category is (x,a×b)(x, a\times b)(x,a×b). (If the categories P(x)P(x)P(x) are posets, then P+(x,a)P^+(x,a)P+(x,a) is equivalent to the slice P(x)/aP(x)/aP(x)/a.)

What is the best citation for this construction? The posetal case is described in detail in https://arxiv.org/abs/2108.03415 which says it is "recalled" from https://arxiv.org/abs/1206.0162. The latter in turn attributes it to Jacobs' 1999 book Categorical Logic and Type Theory but without any more specific reference, and I haven't been able to find it therein. Note that the question isn't just about the Grothendieck construction of a hyperdoctrine for a base category, but about the new hyperdoctrine that we can define over it.
