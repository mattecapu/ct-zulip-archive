Clémence Chanavat (Feb 17 2025 at 17:48):

Garner uses an equivalent definition of wfs, where instead of requiring that L=llp(R) L = llp(R) L=llp(R) and R=rlp(L) R = rlp(L) R=rlp(L), he simply asks L⋔R L \pitchfork R L⋔R (i.e. any lifting problem of a morphism in L L L against a morphism in R R R has a solution). If you add that L L L and R R R are closed under retracts, then you can recover back that the two classes L L L and R R R determine each other
