James Deikun (Feb 13 2024 at 18:45):

Well, first of all there are two definitions of nnn-category on offer in Leinster's book, although one is only a "sketch"--let's call that one Baez-Dolan-Leinster (BDL). That one is not algebraic. Then there's the usual "Leinster" (L) definition, based on a globular operad with a (specified) contraction, which is algebraic. Both of them are susceptible to this treatment. The key to doing it in a reasonable amount of work is that FOLDS model equivalence depends only on the signature, not the full content of the theory. The signature for BDL consists of simply the opetopes (there are no predicate symbols except the equality at the top dimension). The signature for L can be taken as the nnn-globes plus one relation symbol for each operation of LnL_nLn​ (9.3.5-6). In the case of the algebraic definition L we are just looking at the restriction of equivalance to subset of FOLDS models where the relations are functional.

It's interesting in the first of thes how Garner repeatedly emphasizes that it's of no concern that the construction of a cofibrant replacement comonad via an AWFS depends on the set JJJ of generating cofibrations, because you usually start with the generating cofibrations (in this case the inclusions of cell boundaries) rather than the cofibrations as a class. James Deikun (Feb 15 2024 at 23:52):

But the dependence actually is super important! You can create an AWFS for the monomorphisms as cofibrations without going out of your way to pick cofibrant generators, as in Section 4.4 of Bourke and Garner, AWFS I--and instead of getting the cofibrant replacement comonad you want, you will get the identity comonad, making your weak functors the same as strict functors! And the same thing seems to apply to the construction of Leinster weak ω\omegaω-categories in the first place, too. This all seems very mysterious to me.

Okay, I think I have an idea how this could all work for Leinster-style globular (n,k)-categories. All of this is pretty speculative but here goes:

Strict (n,k)-categories and their canonical model structure

Let −2≤n,k≤∞-2 \le n,k \le \infty−2≤n,k≤∞, G∞\mathcal{G}_\inftyG∞​ be the category of globes and T∞T_\inftyT∞​ be the monad for strict omega-categories on G∞^\widehat{\mathcal{G}_\infty}G∞​​. Let Tn,kT_{n,k}Tn,k​ be the monad obtained from T∞T_\inftyT∞​ by kkk-truncating the input and then nnn-truncating the output. (This is notably different from what you would normally call a "strict (n,k)(n,k)(n,k)-category" but I think it works here. If it doesn't, probably the normal thing will, though.)

James Deikun (Feb 19 2024 at 15:59):

Now let's consider the class of morphisms of globular sets that are injective on mmm-globes for m<nm < nm<n. Let I\mathcal{I}I be any algebraic cofibrant generator for this class and (LI,RI)(\mathsf{L}_\mathcal{I},\mathsf{R}_\mathcal{I})(LI​,RI​) the corresponding AWFS. We (hopefully) obtain a model structure on G∞^Tn.k\widehat{G_\infty}^{T_{n.k}}G∞​​Tn.k​ where the cofibrations are obtained from right transfer of the above AWFS and all objects are fibrant, and where the weak equivalences are appropriate equivalences of strict (n,k)(n,k)(n,k)-categories, and perhaps even the cofibrant objects are precisely those presented by an (n,k)(n,k)(n,k)-computad and relations purely on nnn-cells.

James Deikun (Feb 19 2024 at 16:00):

Weakening a la Leinster and Garner

Call a strongly Cartesian monad on G∞^\widehat{\mathcal{G}_\infty}G∞​​ an (n,k)-globular operad if it lies over Tn,kT_{n,k}Tn,k​ by a Cartesian monad morphism. The Cartesian monad morphism is determined by its value at 111, which we call the collection of the operad. The forgetful functor from an operad to its underlying collection is monadic; we can left transfer the AWFS generated by I\mathcal{I}I to the slice over Tn,k1T_{n,k}1Tn,k​1 and then right transfer it to (n,k)(n,k)(n,k)-globular operads. Then take a cofibrant replacement of the the terminal (n,k)(n,k)(n,k)-globular operad Tn,kT_{n,k}Tn,k​ to get Tn,k~\widetilde{T_{n,k}}Tn,k​​.

James Deikun (Feb 19 2024 at 16:00):

Define a model structure on Tn,k~\widetilde{T_{n,k}}Tn,k​​-algebras where all objects are fibrant and cofibrations come again from right transfer of the AWFS generated by I\mathcal{I}I; the morphisms out of cofibrant replacements in this model structure are the weak (n,k)(n,k)(n,k)-functors.

Higher structure

Now, there should be a monoidal product for Tn,k~\widetilde{T_{n,k}}Tn,k​​-algebras making this into a monoidal model structure; I have no idea at the moment how to construct it, but this should give all the expected higher transfors as appropriate weak functors t‾:Gm⊗C→D\overline{t} : G_m \otimes \mathfrak{C} \to \mathfrak{D}t:Gm​⊗C→D where GmG_mGm​ is the walking mmm-cell.
