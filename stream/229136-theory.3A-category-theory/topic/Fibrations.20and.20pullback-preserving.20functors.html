Topic: Fibrations and pullback-preserving functors

James Deikun (Oct 17 2024 at 14:29):

Pullback-preserving functors allow one to push forward internal categories. But in some sense an internal category "is just" a small fibration. Is it possible somehow to push forward fibrations along a pullback-preserving functor?

John Baez (Oct 17 2024 at 14:39):

I'm ignorant of how an internal category is a small fibration - is that easy to explain?

James Deikun (Oct 17 2024 at 15:03):

I'd like to link you to the Stacks Project where I learned it but the page seems to be gone. Section B2.3 of the Elephant has a description, but in short:

Given an internal category CCC in B\mathcal BB, for each object III of B\mathcal{B}B, you can get a category in the external sense, by taking as objects the arrows from III to C0C_0C0​, and as arrows the arrows III to C1C_1C1​; composition with the structural arrows of CCC gives the structure of the external category. By composing with arrows of BBB on the other side you get pullback functors; this gives you a split indexed category [C]:Bop→Cat[C] : B^\mathrm{op} \to \mathbf{Cat}[C]:Bop→Cat. You take the Grothendieck construction to get a fibration.

James Deikun (Oct 17 2024 at 15:05):

(And from C0C_0C0​ and C1C_1C1​, you can construct the evidence that the fibration is locally small and has a generic object.)

James Deikun (Oct 17 2024 at 15:21):

The whole thing is a full embedding of 2-categories. The construction above, when you consider every object III, captures all the information about the internal category for Yoneda-like reasons. The interesting thing is that you can end up in some cases with isomorphisms of indexed categories/fibrations when the internal categories they came from were equivalent but not isomorphic! But most things work out very nicely.

James Deikun (May 03 2025 at 16:39):

Answering my own question: Given a fibration p:E→Bp : \mathcal E \to \mathcal Bp:E→B and F:B→B′F : \mathcal B \to \mathcal B'F:B→B′, take the left projection of the comma category B′↓Fp\mathcal B' \downarrow FpB′↓Fp and localize upstairs on the cartesian morphisms of E\mathcal EE with respect to ppp. Basically this construction freely adds cartesian lifts to FpFpFp then forces them to agree with the ones inherited from ppp. If FFF preserves pullbacks then the construction preserves evidence of smallness.
