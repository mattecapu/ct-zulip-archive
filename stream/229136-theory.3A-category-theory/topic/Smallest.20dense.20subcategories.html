Topic: Smallest dense subcategories

Emily (Aug 06 2021 at 20:06):

A subcategory A\mathcal{A}A of a category C\mathcal{C}C is dense if the inclusion functor ι ⁣:A↪C\iota\colon\mathcal{A}\hookrightarrow\mathcal{C}ι:A↪C is so, satisfying any of the following equivalent conditions:

image.png

Now, define the smallest dense full subcategory of C\mathcal{C}C to be, up to equivalence¹, the full subcategory A\mathcal{A}A of C\mathcal{C}C satisfying the following pair of conditions:

  1. The category A\mathcal{A}A is dense in C\mathcal{C}C.
  2. If A′\mathcal{A}'A′ is another dense full subcategory of C\mathcal{C}C, then either A′\mathcal{A}'A′ contains A\mathcal{A}A or it is equivalent¹ to A\mathcal{A}A.

(¹Maybe replacing equivalence by isomorphism might be interesting, too (?))

Question. What are some examples of these? In particular, does C=CRing\mathcal{C}=\mathsf{CRing}C=CRing admit a smallest dense full subcategory (with dense being understood in the enriched category sense in this case)?

That's better. Until we prove otherwise, minimal dense full subcategories may not exist and they may not be unique.

An even better approach, probably, is to admit that we have not only inclusions of subcategories, but also natural isomorphisms between these, so if we really want a poset we should look at equivalence classes of subcategories where two subcategories i:A→Ci: A \to Ci:A→C, i′:A′→Ci': A' \to Ci′:A′→C are equivalent if there's an equivalence j:A→A′j: A \to A'j:A→A′ such that i′ji' j i′j is naturally isomorphic to iii. These form a poset in a natural way.

Fawzi Hreiki said:

I'll wager a guess that C\mathcal{C}C is the smallest dense subcategory of PSh(C)\text{PSh}(\mathcal{C})PSh(C).

C\mathcal{C}C can only be recovered from PSh(C)\text{PSh}(\mathcal{C})PSh(C) up to idempotent completion (and even that only up to equivalence), so any subcategory whose idempotent completion is the same as that of C\mathcal{C}C provides a dense subcategory. In particular, I'm sure it wouldn't be hard to come up with an idempotent-complete category with no smallest generating subcategory.
