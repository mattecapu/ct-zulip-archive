John Baez (May 21 2023 at 19:29):

Proof Sketch.

Consider two monads on Set\mathsf{Set}Set:

  1. Let MMM be the monad for [[semilattices]]: that is, commutative monoids where the monoid operation ⋅\cdot⋅ is idempotent: x⋅x=xx \cdot x = xx⋅x=x.
  2. Let SSS be the monad for vector spaces over the field with 2 elements, F2\mathbb{F}_2F2​. These are equivalent to commutative monoids where the monoid operation +++ obeys x+x=0x+x = 0x+x=0.

John Baez (May 21 2023 at 19:31):

These two algebraic structures are described by Lawvere theories, so they are algebras of finitary monads M,SM,SM,S on Set\mathsf{Set}Set. One might wonder how these two monads both have underlying functors isomorphic to Pf\mathcal{P}_fPf​.

For 1. we need to see how for any set XXX, PfX\mathcal{P}_f XPf​X can be made into the free semilattice on XXX. To do this we think of monoid operation ⋅\cdot⋅ as the union of finite subsets, ∪\cup∪, and the unit of this monoid operation as ∅\emptyset∅. Note ∪\cup∪ is associative, commutative, unital and idempotent: S∪S=SS \cup S = SS∪S=S.

For 2. we need to see how for any set XXX, PfX\mathcal{P}_f XPf​X can be made into the free F2\mathbb{F}_2F2​-vector space over XXX. To do this we think of addition +++ as the symmetric difference of finite sets, Δ\DeltaΔ, and the unit of this monoid operation as ∅\emptyset∅. Note Δ\DeltaΔ is associative, commutative, unital and has SΔS=∅S \Delta S = \emptysetSΔS=∅.
