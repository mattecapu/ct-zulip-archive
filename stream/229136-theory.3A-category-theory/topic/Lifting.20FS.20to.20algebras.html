Topic: Lifting FS to algebras

fosco (Jun 02 2020 at 11:13):

What kind of conditions can one put on a triple (C,T,(E,M))(\mathcal C,T,(\mathcal E,\mathcal M))(C,T,(E,M)), where C\mathcal CC is a category, TTT is a monad on C\mathcal CC, and (E,M)(\mathcal E,\mathcal M)(E,M) is a factorisation system on C\mathcal CC, ensuring that there is a factorisation system (ET,MT)(\mathcal E^T,\mathcal M^T)(ET,MT) on the category of TTT-algebras?

Reid Barton (Jun 02 2020 at 11:28):

Well, if C\mathcal{C}C and CT\mathcal{C}^TCT are locally presentable, and E\mathcal{E}E is generated by a set of maps... is this not the kind of condition you had in mind?

fosco (Jun 02 2020 at 11:32):

Orthogonality shall follow from niceness of C\mathcal CC and TTT, yes; but how can I be sure the factoring object is an algebra?

fosco (Jun 02 2020 at 11:33):

More specifically, I have a precise example in mind: C is Cat, TTT is the free monoid monad; (E,M)(E,M)(E,M) is the bo-ff factorisation: how can I be sure that the factorisation of a strong monoidal functor between monoidal categories is a monoidal category, and the bo and ff parts are strong monoidal?

Reid Barton (Jun 02 2020 at 11:54):

Oh, did you mean ET\mathcal{E}^TET is algebra maps whose underlying map belongs to E\mathcal{E}E, and similarly for M\mathcal{M}M?

fosco (Jun 02 2020 at 12:08):

yes!

Reid Barton (Jun 02 2020 at 12:17):

It looks like maybe it's necessary and sufficient that TTT preserves the class E\mathcal{E}E

Reid Barton (Jun 02 2020 at 12:19):

Necessary because if (ET,MT)(\mathcal{E}^T, \mathcal{M}^T)(ET,MT) is an OFS on CT\mathcal{C}^TCT then the forgetful functor to C\mathcal{C}C preserves the right class, which means the free functor C→CT\mathcal{C} \to \mathcal{C}^TC→CT preserves the left class, which is to say TTT preserves E\mathcal{E}E

Reid Barton (Jun 02 2020 at 12:21):

For sufficiency I'm not so sure but mainly we need to check that we can factor, so suppose A→CA \to CA→C is a map in CT\mathcal{C}^TCT and we factored the underlying map of C\mathcal{C}C as A→B→CA \to B \to CA→B→C as an E\mathcal{E}E-map followed by an M\mathcal{M}M-map. We still have to construct TB→BTB \to BTB→B which fits in a diagram with TA→ATA \to ATA→A and TC→CTC \to CTC→C.

Reid Barton (Jun 02 2020 at 12:22):

If you redraw the diagram with TA→TBTA \to TBTA→TB on the left and B→CB \to CB→C on the right, then TA→TBTA \to TBTA→TB belongs to E\mathcal{E}E (by the assumption on TTT) and B→CB \to CB→C belongs to M\mathcal{M}M

Reid Barton (Jun 02 2020 at 12:22):

so then that gives you a map TB→BTB \to BTB→B, and I imagine the remaining conditions will follow from uniqueness of lifts
