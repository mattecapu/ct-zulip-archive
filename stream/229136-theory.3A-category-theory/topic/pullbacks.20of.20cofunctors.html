Nathaniel Virgo (Aug 25 2024 at 07:24):

Consider the 1-category where the objects are categories and the morphisms are cofunctors, aka [[retrofunctor]] s. Does this category have products or pullbacks? If so, what are they?

Bryce Clarke (Aug 25 2024 at 07:55):

Yes, the 1-category of categories and retrofunctors has all limits. This is Corollary 8.78 in Polynomial Functors: A Mathematical Theory of Interaction.

Todd Trimble (Aug 25 2024 at 16:49):

Is this the category of polynomial comonads on Set\mathsf{Set}Set, in other words the category of comonoids wrt polynomial composition?

If so, then let G:Poly→Cat♯G: \mathsf{Poly} \to \mathsf{Cat}^{\sharp}G:Poly→Cat♯ be the right adjoint to the forgetful functor UUU, which is comonadic. GGG preserves products, so that a product ∏iGPi\prod_i GP_i∏i​GPi​ of cofree comonoids GPiGP_iGPi​ can be formed as G(∏iPi)G(\prod_i P_i)G(∏i​Pi​).

Meanwhile, by comonadicity, a general comonoid CCC is canonically represented as an equalizer of maps between cofree comonoids (formed at the level of underlying polynomial functors):

C→ηCGUC⇉ηGUCGUηCGUGUCC \overset{\eta C}{\to} GUC \overset{GU\eta C}{\underset{\eta GUC}{\rightrightarrows}} GUGUCC→ηCGUCηGUC⇉​GUηC​GUGUC

where η\etaη is the unit of the U⊣GU \dashv GU⊣G adjunction. Given a family of comonoids CiC_iCi​, their product is the equalizer of

∏iGUCi⇉∏iηGUCi∏iGUηCi∏iGUGUCi\prod_i GUC_i \overset{\prod_i GU\eta C_i}{\underset{\prod_i\eta GUC_i}{\rightrightarrows}} \prod_i GUGUC_i∏i​GUCi​∏i​ηGUCi​⇉​∏i​GUηCi​​∏i​GUGUCi​

(This equalizer exists because as an example of a connected limit, it is created/reflected by UUU, as Niu and Spivak remark.) I don't know how satisfying explicit this is for your purposes, but it's the formal dual of the construction recounted as Theorem 2.2 here.
