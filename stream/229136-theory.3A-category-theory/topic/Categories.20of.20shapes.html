Topic: Categories of shapes

James Deikun (Sep 24 2021 at 14:36):

Given a colored planar operad OOO, one can construct what I've been calling its "category of shapes" Shp(O)Shp(O)Shp(O). The objects are the operators of the operad; the morphisms o→po \rightarrow po→p are factorizations o=f(id,…,id,p(g1,…,gn),id,…,id)o = f(id, \ldots, id, p(g_1, \ldots, g_n), id, \ldots, id)o=f(id,…,id,p(g1​,…,gn​),id,…,id).

In the case of the AssocAssocAssoc operad, the resulting category is the augmented simplex category Δ0\Delta_0Δ0​. In the case of a category taken as colored operad, it is the opposite of the twisted arrow category (aka category of factorisations). The associated pages on the nLab don't seem to point to this generalization.

Has anyone heard of this construction in approximately this generality? Is there a nice non-component-based description of it?

Also, Δ0\Delta_0Δ0​ is the walking strict AssocAssocAssoc-category equipped with an AssocAssocAssoc algebra. Does this generalize? It seems to me that it does, but I don't have a nice snappy proof or even a way of phrasing it for the nontrivially-colored case.

John Baez (Sep 24 2021 at 20:03):

Maybe the category you're getting here is what you get when you hit the augmented simplex with an "abelianization" functor?

Let me explain:

There's a morphism from the operad for monoids to the operad for commutative monoids, so any commutative monoid object in a symmetric monoidal category CCC has an underlying monoid object, giving us a functor

U:CommMon(C)→Mon(C)U : \mathrm{CommMon}(C) \to \mathrm{Mon}(C) U:CommMon(C)→Mon(C)

where CommMon(C)\mathrm{CommMon}(C)CommMon(C) is the category of commutative monoid objects in CCC, and similarly for Mon(C)\mathrm{Mon}(C)Mon(C).

When CCC is nice enough - like when it has colimits and the tensor product distributes over colimits, maybe? - UUU should have a left adjoint

L:Mon(C)→CommMon(C) L : \mathrm{Mon}(C) \to \mathrm{CommMon}(C) L:Mon(C)→CommMon(C)

That's what I mean by "abelianization".

So, I'm wildly guessing that when you abelianize the "free monoidal category on a monoid object", you get the "free commutative monoidal category on a commutative monoid object".

James Deikun (Sep 24 2021 at 21:00):

I guess the free strictly-associative symmetric monoidal category has objects the natural numbers again, and its morphisms can be described by a string diagram calculus:

  • Hom(n,m) has n strings in, m out
  • Strings are all the same, no colors
  • Strings can cross and it doesn't matter which is on top; two crossings are the same as none
  • Strings can be capped on their input side, and these caps can be retracted toward the output side
  • There is a joiner piece ▹\triangleright▹ ; it is "symmetric" in the sense that a triangle with a crossing on the input side is the same as one without
  • If one of the inputs of the triangle is capped, the whole thing can be replaced with a simple string.

James Deikun (Sep 24 2021 at 21:03):

Oh, also two triangles feeding into each other can be switched along the piece of string that connects them.
