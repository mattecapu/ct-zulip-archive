Topic: left leg is fibration, right leg is opfibration

Matteo Capucci (he/him) (Nov 08 2022 at 11:35):

Is there a name for an endospan of, say, functors which is a fibration on the left span and an opfibration on the right?
The prime example is given by any finitely complete category C\cal CC, giving C←codC↓→domC\cal C \overset{cod}\leftarrow C^\downarrow \overset{dom}\to \cal CC←codC↓→domC, and more generally, all categories with [[display maps]] D⊆C\cal D \subseteq CD⊆C, including cartesian monoidal categories: C←π2C×C→×C\cal C \overset{\pi_2}\leftarrow C \times C \overset{\times}\to \cal CC←π2​C×C→×C.

Yeah, a two-sided fibration is a span (not necessarily an endo-span) where one leg is a fibration and the other an opfibration. And yes the codomain/domain span is the prototypical one.

a span of a discrete fibration and a discrete opfibration is the category of elements of a profunctor (distributor). the prime example that you mention corresponds to the profunctor homhomhom of the category C\cal CC. benabou presented the bicategory of profunctors using such discrete fibrations...

if you drop the discreteness, then the span lives in a tricategory of (what was it called again?) probifunctors... i think gordon and power and ross street started from such examples to figure out the coherences for tricategories...
