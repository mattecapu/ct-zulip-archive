Topic: pushout gap maps in a commutative cube

Chaitanya Leena Subramaniam (Jan 19 2026 at 13:59):

I realised something interesting over the weekend.

Given a commutative cube in a category with pushouts
image.png

let fff and ggg be the pushout gap maps of the rear face (ABXY) and front face (A'B'X'Y') respectively, and let hhh and kkk be the pushout gap maps of the left face (ABA'B') and right face (XYX'Y') respectively. Then a neat pasting argument (which works equally well in an ∞\infty∞-category) shows that the pushout gap map of the square f→gf\to gf→g (i.e. the square between the front and back pushout gap maps) is isomorphic to the pushout gap map of the square h→kh\to kh→k (the square between the left and right pushout gap maps).

Is this already known to anyone here? And if so, do you have a nice conceptual explanation?

Chaitanya Leena Subramaniam (Jan 20 2026 at 06:36):

I think I’ve got it.

The external pushout-product is a bi-cocontinuous functor S→×C→→C→\mathcal{S}^\to\times\mathcal{C}^\to \to \mathcal{C}^\toS→×C→→C→, and gives C→\mathcal{C}^\toC→ an S→\mathcal{S}^\toS→-module structure (where the internal pushout-product gives S→\mathcal{S}^\toS→ a commutative monoid structure in the symmetric monoidal category of cocomplete categories and cocontinuous functors --- here S\mathcal{S}S is the category of spaces, or sets if we're talking about 1-categories).

The associated cocontinous functor S→⊗C→=(C→)→→C→\mathcal{S}^\to\otimes\mathcal{C}^\to = (\mathcal{C}^\to)^\to \to \mathcal{C}^\toS→⊗C→=(C→)→→C→ is the functor that computes the pushout gap map of a square. Then the module structure on C→\mathcal{C}^\toC→ is what gives us that the gap map of a cube can be computed in two equivalent ways.

This should give the desired result.
