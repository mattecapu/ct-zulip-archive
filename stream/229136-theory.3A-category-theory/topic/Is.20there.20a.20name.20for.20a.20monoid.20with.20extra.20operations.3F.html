Topic: Is there a name for a monoid with extra operations?

Hence, I have (M,e,∗,+,×)(M,e,*,+,\times)(M,e,∗,+,×). My question then is what is this thing called? I'm trying to learn more about such structures, cause I actually have something like this in a program that I'm coding.

Just to give more perspective. The ∗*∗ operator is like a composition (nesting), where m∗nm*nm∗n means something like put nnn inside of $m$ (like the ⊲\vartriangleleft⊲ in Poly\mathbf{Poly}Poly).
The +++ is like an "append", and the ×\times× creates a new object.

Here is an actual example. Consider the category of all sets. Hence, $M$ is the class of all sets. The operator ∗*∗ is the "nesting" operator. For example:

Anyway, there's no simple general name for this structure because your third operation because you're not asserting any equations relating this operation to the other two.

John Baez (Aug 21 2022 at 13:52):

A binary operation ∗:M×M→M\ast : M \times M \to M∗:M×M→M not necessarily obeying any equation is called a [[magma]], so what you have is some structure involving +++ and ×\times×, something similar to a [[near-ring]], together with an unrelated magma structure involving ∗\ast∗.
