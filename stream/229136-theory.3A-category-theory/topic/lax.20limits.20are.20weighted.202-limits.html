Topic: lax limits are weighted 2-limits

Matteo Capucci (he/him) (Nov 12 2020 at 11:36):

On the nlab page about 2-limits it is claimed that

This may look like a different concept, but in fact, for any weight JJJ there is another weight Ql(J)Q_l(J)Ql​(J) such that lax JJJ-weighted limits are the same as Ql(J)Q_l(J)Ql​(J)-weighted 2-limits. Here QlQ lQl is the lax morphism classifier for 2-functors. Therefore, lax limits are really a special case of 2-limits. Similarly, oplax limits, in which we use oplax natural transformations, are also a special case of 2-limits.

Does anyone know how is QlQ_lQl​ defined or a reference to substantiate this claim?

Nathanael Arkor (Nov 12 2020 at 14:29):

I believe the standard reference is Kelly's Elementary Observations on 2-Categorical Limits (see Prop. 5.2).
