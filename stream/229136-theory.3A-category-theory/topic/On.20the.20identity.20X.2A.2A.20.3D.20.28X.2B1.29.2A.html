Hi everyone, let me share a cute observation. I write (−)∗(-)^*(−)∗ for the monad of (set-theoretic) monoids, i.e. X∗X^*X∗ is the set of finite lists/words over the set XXX seen as an alphabet. Writing (−)⋅(−)(-) \cdot (-)(−)⋅(−) for the concatenation, I noticed that the function

(X∗)∗⟶(X+{∙})∗[[w1,…,wn]⟼w1⋅[∙]…[∙]⋅wn\begin{matrix} (X^*)^* & \longrightarrow & (X + \{\bullet\})* \\ [[w_1, \dots, w_n] & \longmapsto & w_1 \cdot [\bullet] \dots [\bullet] \cdot w_n \end{matrix}(X∗)∗[[w1​,…,wn​]​⟶⟼​(X+{∙})∗w1​⋅[∙]…[∙]⋅wn​​

is a bijection natural in the set XXX. Moreover, the multiplication (X∗)∗→X∗(X^*)^* \to X^*(X∗)∗→X∗ corresponds to the unique monoid homomorphism (X+{∙})∗→X∗(X + \{\bullet\})^* \to X^*(X+{∙})∗→X∗ which is the identity on XXX and sends ∙\bullet∙ to the empty word. (Notice however that the natural bijection above is NOT a natural isomorphism for the free monoid structures!)

For those interested, I came across this on my work on higher-order regular languages. Indeed, any finite set Σ\SigmaΣ induces the type

Church⁡(Σ):=(o⇒o)∣Σ∣⇒o⇒o\operatorname{Church}(\Sigma) \quad:=\quad (o \Rightarrow o)^{|\Sigma|} \Rightarrow o \Rightarrow oChurch(Σ):=(o⇒o)∣Σ∣⇒o⇒o

whose closed simply typed λ\lambdaλ-terms correspond exactly to words w∈Σ∗w \in \Sigma^*w∈Σ∗ on the alphabet Σ\SigmaΣ, through the Church encoding. This assignment goes from finite sets to simple types so cannot be composed with itself, but following the natural bijection above the inhabitants of the type

Church⁡(Σ+1)\operatorname{Church}(\Sigma + 1)Church(Σ+1)

correspond exactly to words of words W∈(Σ∗)∗W \in (\Sigma^*)^*W∈(Σ∗)∗.

This led me to consider which endofunctors FFF on the category of sets can be equipped with a natural bijection

F∘F≅F∘((−)+1)F \circ F \quad\cong\quad F \circ ((-) + 1)F∘F≅F∘((−)+1)

For the moment, my only examples of such FFF are the functors (−)∗(-)^*(−)∗, (−)+1(-) + 1(−)+1, and any constant functor. It is easy to take FFF a polynomial functor induced by some family E→BE \to BE→B and to compute what such an isomorphism amounts to on that family, but I want concrete examples.

Also, moving from the category of sets to the category of graphs/quivers, we can see that the free category monad, which I write Path⁡\operatorname{Path}Path, verifies an analog property:

Path⁡(Path⁡(G))≅Path⁡(G+∣∣G∣∣)\operatorname{Path}(\operatorname{Path}(G)) \quad\cong\quad \operatorname{Path}(G + ||G||)Path(Path(G))≅Path(G+∣∣G∣∣)
