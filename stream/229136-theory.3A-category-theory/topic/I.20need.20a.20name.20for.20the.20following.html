Some notes:

  1. In the cases I'm interested in, every nnn-morphism of BBB which is not in AAA is presentable by a doohickey, but this is not true in general. For instance, if ν\nuν is composable with itself, then the composite ν∘iν\nu \circ_i \nuν∘i​ν is not presentable by a doohickey.

  2. Makkai called something very similar a "pre-atom". The difference is that Makkai studies a situation where one is gluing on many nnn-morphisms at once, and he reserves "pre-atom" for the case where a−,a+a_-,a_+a−​,a+​ are less than nnn-dimensional. This is because he will then build up "molecules" from the "atoms" presented by his "pre-atoms". I'm not wild about this terminology because I like my "atoms" to be _indecomposable_, whereas the globes presented by doohickeys are very much decomposable.
