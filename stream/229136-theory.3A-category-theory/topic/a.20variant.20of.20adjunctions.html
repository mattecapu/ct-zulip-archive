Topic: a variant of adjunctions

Mike Shulman (Jun 19 2021 at 17:04):

In a monoidal 2-category (K,⊗,I)(\mathcal{K},\otimes,I)(K,⊗,I), suppose I have morphisms f:I→A⊗Bf:I\to A\otimes Bf:I→A⊗B and g:B⊗A→Ig:B\otimes A \to Ig:B⊗A→I with 2-cells η:1A→(1A⊗g)(f⊗1A)\eta : 1_A \to (1_A \otimes g)(f\otimes 1_A)η:1A​→(1A​⊗g)(f⊗1A​) and ϵ:(g⊗1B)(1B⊗f)→1B\epsilon : (g\otimes 1_B)(1_B\otimes f) \to 1_Bϵ:(g⊗1B​)(1B​⊗f)→1B​, satisfying two evident "triangle" identities. Has this situation been studied? Does it have a name?

Mike Shulman (Jun 19 2021 at 17:08):

Motivation: if K\mathcal{K}K is compact closed with dual objects AoA^oAo, this is a way to re-express an ordinary adjunction Ao⇄BA^o \rightleftarrows BAo⇄B without reference to duals. In particular, a contravariant functor h:Aop→Bh:A^{\mathrm{op}} \to Bh:Aop→B in Cat\rm CatCat induces a structure of this sort in Prof\rm ProfProf, where f(a,b)=B(b,ha)f(a,b) = B(b,h a)f(a,b)=B(b,ha) and g(b,a)=B(ha,b)g(b,a) = B(h a,b)g(b,a)=B(ha,b).
