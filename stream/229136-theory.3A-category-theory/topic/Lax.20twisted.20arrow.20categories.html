Topic: Lax twisted arrow categories

Eigil Rischel (Jun 30 2020 at 11:51):

Is there a good reference about basic properties of "lax twisted arrow categories"?
I am thinking about the following construction: given a 2-category C\mathcal{C}C (strict or not), we can cook up a new 2-category Twlax(C)Tw^{lax}(\mathcal{C})Twlax(C) where

  • Objects are morphisms f:x→yf: x \to yf:x→y in C\mathcal{C}C
  • A map (f:x→y)→(g:a→b)(f: x \to y) \to (g: a \to b)(f:x→y)→(g:a→b) consists of maps i:x→a,j:b→yi:x \to a, j: b \to yi:x→a,j:b→y along with a not necessarily invertible 2-cell jgi⇒fjgi \Rightarrow fjgi⇒f.
  • The 2-cells are defined in a natural way in terms of the 2-cells of C\mathcal{C}C.

It's not that the basic definition is absurdly complicated, but I'm hoping somebody has already worked out the basic properties of these guys.
