Topic: Do we need a full enrichment for day convolution

Brendan Murphy (Mar 16 2024 at 19:17):

The fundmental example of day convolution in my mind is the tensor product of graded objects, where it looks very much like a convolution. And when I'm thinking about graded objects I'm often working in an additive context, so e.g. graded abelian groups. But gr⁡Ab:=Fun⁡(disc⁡(Z),Ab)\operatorname{gr}\mathsf{Ab} := \operatorname{Fun}(\operatorname{disc}(\Z), \mathsf{Ab})grAb:=Fun(disc(Z),Ab) doesn't acquire a day convolution monoidal structure as written, because disc⁡(Z)\operatorname{disc}(\Z)disc(Z) is an ordinary category and what I've written is an ordinary functor category, not an Ab\mathsf{Ab}Ab-enriched one. This isn't really a problem because we can take the free Ab\mathsf{Ab}Ab-enriched category on disc⁡(Z)\operatorname{disc}(\Z)disc(Z). But is there a way to get a day convolution type monoidal structure without this step, e.g. instead using that Ab\mathsf{Ab}Ab is tensored over Set\mathsf{Set}Set?

Brendan Murphy (Mar 16 2024 at 19:19):

Maybe the thing to do here is to note Fun⁡(disc⁡(Z),Ab)\operatorname{Fun}(\operatorname{disc}(\Z), \mathsf{Ab})Fun(disc(Z),Ab) can be identified with the category of cocontinuous functors Fun⁡L(Psh⁡(disc⁡(Z)),Ab)\operatorname{Fun}^L(\operatorname{Psh}(\operatorname{disc}(\Z)), \mathsf{Ab})FunL(Psh(disc(Z)),Ab)? And then observe this is an internal hom of presentable categories and do some magic to transport over the day convolution structure?

Brendan Murphy (Mar 16 2024 at 19:22):

It would be nicer if this were a tensor product of presentable categories, so we could view the monoidal structure as a "base change" from ordinary (monoidal) categories to Ab\mathsf{Ab}Ab-enriched (monoidal) categories

Brendan Murphy (Mar 16 2024 at 19:30):

Ah wait, Fun⁡(disc⁡(Z),Set)op\operatorname{Fun}(\operatorname{disc}(\Z), \mathsf{Set})^{\mathrm{op}}Fun(disc(Z),Set)op is the free completion of disc⁡(Z)\operatorname{disc}(\Z)disc(Z) so Fun⁡R(Fun⁡(disc⁡(Z),Set)op,Ab)\operatorname{Fun}^R(\operatorname{Fun}(\operatorname{disc}(\Z), \mathsf{Set})^{\mathrm{op}}, \mathsf{Ab})FunR(Fun(disc(Z),Set)op,Ab) can be identified with Fun⁡(disc⁡(Z),Ab)\operatorname{Fun}(\operatorname{disc}(\Z), \mathsf{Ab})Fun(disc(Z),Ab). And it's also the tensor product Fun⁡(disc⁡(Z),Set)⊗Ab\operatorname{Fun}(\operatorname{disc}(\Z), \mathsf{Set}) \otimes \mathsf{Ab}Fun(disc(Z),Set)⊗Ab. Well I'm satisfied for now but I'd be happy to hear anyone else's thoughts about day convolution without an enrichment

Nathanael Arkor (Mar 17 2024 at 00:55):

If CCC is a promonoidal category and DDD is a multicategory, then the category of functors [C,D][C, D][C,D] between their underlying categories acquires a multicategory structure, which is monoidal when DDD is representable and monoidally cocomplete. In particular, this captures Day's convolution structure, taking D=SetD = \mathrm{Set}D=Set. The reference for this is §2.8 of Pisani's Sequential multicategories.
