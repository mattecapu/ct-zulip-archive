Suppose M\mathsf{M}M is a monoidal groupoid and N\mathsf{N}N is a monoidal subgroupoid, so the inclusion i:N→Mi : \mathsf{N} \to \mathsf{M}i:N→M is a monoidal functor. For convenience I can assume without loss of generality that N\mathsf{N}N is [[replete]], so any object of M\mathsf{M}M isomorphic to an object of N\mathsf{N}N is already in N\mathsf{N}N.

Then restriction of presheaves from M\mathsf{M}M to N\mathsf{N}N gives a functor

i∗:SetMop→SetNop i^\ast: \mathsf{Set}^{M^{\text{op}}} \to \mathsf{Set}^{N^{\text{op}}}i∗:SetMop→SetNop

i!:SetNop→SetMop i_!: \mathsf{Set}^{N^{\text{op}}} \to \mathsf{Set}^{M^{\text{op}}}i!​:SetNop→SetMop

is "extension of presheaves from N\mathsf{N}N to M\mathsf{M}M, defining them to be 000 on objects not in N\mathsf{N}N", and this is also symmetric monoidal. Furthermore,

i!i∗≅1 i_! i^\ast \cong 1 i!​i∗≅1
