Topic: distributive laws

Jonas Frey (Dec 09 2020 at 02:23):

Given monads S,T:C→CS,T : C \to CS,T:C→C, I'm pretty sure that there is a theorem saying that

  1. distributive laws δ:TS→ST\delta: TS \to STδ:TS→ST

  2. monad structures on STSTST which are compatible with the monad structures on SSS and TTT in a suitable way

  3. "liftings" of SSS to the category CTC^TCT, of TTT-algebras

  4. "extensions" of TTT to the Kleisli category CSC_SCS​

are all equivalent.

Beck's original article "Distributive Laws" and Barr/Well's "Toposes, Triples, and Theories" show the equivalence of 1--3, but don't mention 4 as far as I can see.

Does anybody know where to find a statement of the "full" theorem including 4?

Christian Williams (Dec 09 2020 at 03:09):

Hm, I thought I knew a reference but it seems like it's generally taken for granted. There's an abstract proof using the formal theory of monads, thinking of F:C→CSF:C\to C_SF:C→CS​ as the initial left SSS-module, and pasting with the distributive law. But the down-to-earth reasoning:

given a Kleisli morphism f:A→S(B)f:A\to S(B)f:A→S(B), what structure is necessary to apply TTT? We have T(f):T(A)→T(S(B))T(f): T(A)\to T(S(B))T(f):T(A)→T(S(B)), and then we need a natural transformation λB:T(S(B))→S(T(B))\lambda_B: T(S(B))\to S(T(B))λB​:T(S(B))→S(T(B)) to define Tˉ(f):T(A)→S(T(B))\bar{T}(f):T(A)\to S(T(B))Tˉ(f):T(A)→S(T(B)). The laws for λ\lambdaλ are exactly what you need for this extension of TTT to CSC_SCS​ to be functorial.

I can keep looking for a reference. Surely someone has written it up.

Ralph Sarkis (Dec 09 2020 at 08:15):

Proposition 5.2.5 in Bart Jacobs' Introduction to Coalgebra shows the correspondence between extensions of a functor FFF to the Kleisli category CTC_TCT​ and distributive laws of the functor FFF over the monad TTT (two diagrams are missing from the usual monad distributive law), then they mention in the exercises that you can make this about monads only.

Nathanael Arkor (Dec 09 2020 at 12:31):

Theorem 4.26 of Tanaka's thesis Pseudo-Distributive Laws and a Unified Framework for Variable Binding contains the equivalence of 1, 3 and 4. It also follows from Corollary 6.20 of Distributive laws for relative monads. However, I don't think I've seen an explicit reference to 2 outside of Beck's paper (I have also looked for a unified statement in the past and been unable to find one).

Mike Shulman (Dec 09 2020 at 16:53):

For monads S,TS,TS,T on an object CCC in a 2-category K\mathcal{K}K, an extension of TTT to CSC_SCS​ in K\mathcal{K}K is the same as a lifting of TTT to CSC^SCS in Kop\mathcal{K}^{\mathrm{op}}Kop. So since the proof is purely diagrammatic and works in any 2-category, the equivalence with (3) implies the equivalence with (4). (Note that a distributive law TS→STTS\to STTS→ST in K\mathcal{K}K becomes a distributive law ST→TSST\to TSST→TS in Kop\mathcal{K}^{\mathrm{op}}Kop.)
