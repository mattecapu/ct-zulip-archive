Nima Rasekh (Mar 27 2025 at 15:09):

James Deikun said:

I'm not even sure the definition of double ∞\infty∞-category in the literature is "correct", it should really include vertical completeness, otherwise there's an unnecessary degree of freedom similar to the one in non-complete Segal spaces.

Thanks @Jonas Frey for the shout out!
@James Deikun I think the "correct" notion is somewhat tricky.

  • If you generalize naively from strict to non-strict, then the intuition might be to do \infty-category objects internal to \infty-categories, which unwinds to double complete Segal spaces (i.e. complete in all directions). That has the flaw that the space of objects is over-determined: it is equivalent to the space of horizontal equivalences and vertical equivalences. In particular it will lack a key example like Prof.
  • One solution is to weaken in one direction, which is particularly suitable for double categories that are naturally pseudo double categories (i.e that happen to be strict in one direction). But in general this definition of completeness is annoyingly non-symmetric.
  • A better --symmetric-- analogue might be "gregarious completeness", in the sense we do in the paper. The actual \infty-version is WIP by some people (I think). Here is a talk about it: https://acmbl.github.io/greg_slides.pdf (note Prof is in fact gregarious complete so at least it would be an example)

James Deikun (Mar 27 2025 at 15:30):

It feels like both "vertically complete" and "gregarious complete" are kind of non-comparable in how "good" they are. Vertically complete seems more appropriate for cases where symmetry is not a concern while gregarious complete is better when it is. Fortunately they seem to coincide for equipments!

Nima Rasekh (Mar 27 2025 at 15:32):

Yeah, I think that's the suprising gist of it, unlike categories and bicategories, where there is a "most general" notion of completeness and corresponding equivalence, there isn't one for double categories.
But yes, for many relevant examples that's fine, and they happen to coincide as we prove in the paper.
