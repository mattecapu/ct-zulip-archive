Topic: final functors into filtered categories

Jonas Frey (Feb 08 2023 at 02:37):

If F:C→DF : C \to DF:C→D is a final functor into a filtered category, then is CCC necessarily filtered? If not, does somebody have a counterexample? Thanks!

Jonas Frey (Feb 09 2023 at 01:41):

The statement seems to be wrong after all! The terminal category 1 is filtered, and the terminal projection C→1C\to 1C→1 is final whenever CCC is connected (or weakly contractible for ∞\infty∞-categories), which is weaker than filtered.

Thanks to Mathieu Anel for this counterexample!

Ah, sorry about that! And if C→1C \to 1C→1 is fully faithful, then CCC is indiscrete and hence filtered, so everything checks out.

I suppose Mathieu's counterexample also shows that "fully faithful" can't be weakened to "faithful", since C→1C \to 1C→1 is faithful iff CCC is a preorder, but preorders can have arbitrary homotopy type.

One place this is not the case is when you have a filtered category and look for a cofinal functor from a directed poset. Note that this does not seem to contradict theorem 1.5 of the AR book which you hinted at (where the final functor constructed is moreover fully faithful).
