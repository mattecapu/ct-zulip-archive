Topic: Enriching a monad in a monad

Noah Chrein (Apr 18 2024 at 19:23):

I am looking for a yoneda lemma in an odd situation. I will abstract some details away so they aren't too distracting.

Let (C,M)(\mathbb C,M)(C,M) and (D,N)(\mathbb D,N)(D,N) be monads, that is, 2-functors M,N:[Δ+,Cat]M,N:[\Delta_+, \mathbb C\text{at}]M,N:[Δ+​,Cat] .
[Δ+,Cat][\Delta_+,\mathbb C\text{at}][Δ+​,Cat], as a category, seems to be cartesian closed: there is a monad
(  [Δ+,Cat](M,N)  ,  +1  )(\;[\Delta_+,\mathbb C\text{at}](M,N)\;,\;+1\;)([Δ+​,Cat](M,N),+1) given by N∘F≅+1(F)≅F∘MN\circ F \cong +1(F)\cong F\circ MN∘F≅+1(F)≅F∘M.
I'm looking for a yoneda structure like M→y[Δ+,Cat](Mop,N)M \overset{y}\to [\Delta_+,\mathbb C \text{at}](M^\text{op}, N)M→y[Δ+​,Cat](Mop,N)
so that [Δ+,Cat](Mop,N)(  y(m)  ,  F  )≅F(m)[\Delta_+,\mathbb C \text{at}](M^\text{op},N)(\;y(m)\;,\;F\;)\cong F(m)[Δ+​,Cat](Mop,N)(y(m),F)≅F(m)

this seems to imply M would need to be "enriched" in N, is there a standard notion of a monad enriched in another monad?

As a riff:
I've been playing around with some toy ways of doing this, like enriching (C,M)(\mathbb C, M)(C,M) in (D,N)(\mathbb D, N)(D,N) by enriching C\mathbb CC in D\mathbb DD and having M and N respect a number of things, but this favors the categories over the monads and it feels like there should be a way to enrich M in N without even mentioning the underlying categories. Perhaps this has less to do with monads and more to do with 2-functors in general.
