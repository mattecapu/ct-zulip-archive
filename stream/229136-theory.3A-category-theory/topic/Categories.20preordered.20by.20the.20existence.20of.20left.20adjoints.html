Topic: Categories preordered by the existence of left adjoints

Tobias Fritz (Jun 06 2026 at 19:30):

For categories C\mathsf{C}C and D\mathsf{D}D, let's write C≤D\mathsf{C} \le \mathsf{D}C≤D if there exists a functor C→D\mathsf{C} \to \mathsf{D}C→D which is a left adjoint, or equivalently which has a right adjoint. Since adjunctions compose, this ≤\le≤ relation is transitive, and it's trivially reflexive. Hence the class of all categories becomes preordered.

Tobias Fritz (Jun 06 2026 at 19:30):

What are the properties of this preorder? For example, are there binary infima and suprema? Is it dense? Are there chains and antichains of arbitrarily large cardinality?

Tobias Fritz (Jun 06 2026 at 19:32):

This is just idle curiosity amd I don't have any particular application in mind. But it's intriguing to me because it may reveal a surprisingly intricate combinatorial structure lurking within basic category theory. It's possible that the answers are very simple; I just haven't thought about it at all so far.

fosco (Jun 06 2026 at 20:17):

It might lead somewhere to assume that your categories admit a few co/limits.

If C≤X,YC\le X,YC≤X,Y in your sense, there is a span of left adjoints X←C→YX \leftarrow C \to YX←C→Y, and the functor C→X×YC \to X\times YC→X×Y has itself a right adjoint if CCC has products: say X←f⊣uC→l⊣rYX \overset{f\dashv u}\leftarrow C \overset{l\dashv r}\to YX←f⊣uC→l⊣rY: then the right adjoint of ⟨f,l⟩:C→X×Y\langle f,l\rangle : C\to X\times Y⟨f,l⟩:C→X×Y sends (x,y)(x,y)(x,y) to ux×ryux\times ryux×ry in CCC. So, C≤X×YC\le X\times YC≤X×Y

Jens Hemelaer (Jun 07 2026 at 16:08):

Fun question :) I think that C≤1\mathsf{C} \leq \mathsf{1}C≤1 if and only if C\mathsf{C}C has a terminal object (the right adjoint sends everything to the terminal object), and 1≤C\mathsf{1} \leq \mathsf{C}1≤C if and only if C\mathsf{C}C has an initial object (the left adjoint sends the unique object to the initial object).

Also the empty category (no objects) seems to be in a component on its own, incomparable to any other categories.

Jens Hemelaer (Jun 07 2026 at 16:26):

I see that this is part of a general pattern: if I\mathsf{I}I is a small category, then the diagonal functor C→CI\mathsf{C} \to \mathsf{C}^\mathsf{I}C→CI has a left adjoint if and only if C\mathsf{C}C has I\mathsf{I}I-colimits, and it has a right adjoint if and only if C\mathsf{C}C has I\mathsf{I}I-limits.
