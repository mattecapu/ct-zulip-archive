Topic: Endofunctors of a cartesian monoidal category are opmonoidal

Jean-Baptiste Vienney (Jul 14 2024 at 20:26):

Let C\mathcal{C}C be a category with finite products and F:C→CF:\mathcal{C} \rightarrow \mathcal{C}F:C→C an endofunctor.

Consider the two projections p:A×B→Ap:A \times B \rightarrow Ap:A×B→A and q:A×B→Bq:A \times B \rightarrow Bq:A×B→B. We then obtain F(p):F(A×B)→F(A)F(p):F(A \times B) \rightarrow F(A)F(p):F(A×B)→F(A) and F(q):F(A×B)→F(B)F(q):F(A \times B) \rightarrow F(B)F(q):F(A×B)→F(B). Finally, it gives us a map
⟨F(p),F(q)⟩:F(A×B)→F(A)×F(B)\langle F(p),F(q) \rangle:F(A \times B) \rightarrow F(A) \times F(B)⟨F(p),F(q)⟩:F(A×B)→F(A)×F(B).

We also have a (unique) map F(⊤)→⊤F(\top) \rightarrow \topF(⊤)→⊤.

  1. I think that it makes FFF into an opmonoidal functor.
  2. In the same way I'm wondering if every monad on a category with finite products is not automatically an opmonoidal monad?

Question: Are 1.1.1. and 2.2.2. true or false? Are there any references on this?

Matteo Capucci (he/him) (Jul 15 2024 at 06:26):

Every functor from a monoidal category to a Cartesian category is automatically opmonoidal in the way you describe. The usual way this fact is framed is that Cartesian cats are algebras of the product completion 2-monad on Cat, which is colax idempotent, and one of the main properties of such monads is that every functor between carrier of their algebras is automatically colax.

Jean-Baptiste Vienney (Jul 19 2024 at 13:30):

I think I was still missing something. Here is an updated conjecture.

In the characterizations of cartesian monoidal categories as monoidal categories with extra structure, a structure of comonoid is required on every object, which must be natural and compatible with the tensor product. cf. this blog post where the compatibility with the tensor product is called "uniformity". I think I was missing this compatibility with the tensor product.

If C\mathcal{C}C is a category, call cartesian structure on C\mathcal{C}C the data
(⊤,(A×B,pA,B:A×B→A,qA,B:A×B→B)A,B∈C)(\top,(A \times B, p_{A,B}:A \times B \rightarrow A, q_{A,B}:A \times B \rightarrow B)_{A,B \in \mathcal{C}})(⊤,(A×B,pA,B​:A×B→A,qA,B​:A×B→B)A,B∈C​)
of a terminal object and of a product for every pair of objects.

Every cartesian structure induces a structure of symmetric monoidal category on C\mathcal{C}C. Let's call cartesian monoidal category a symmetric monoidal category C\mathcal{C}C whose structure of symmetric monoidal category is induced by a cartesian structure.

For every category C\mathcal{C}C, we have its category of endofunctors and natural transformations, that I note End(C)\mathrm{End}(\mathcal{C})End(C). If C\mathcal{C}C is a symmetric monoidal category, then we can consider the category SymOpMonEnd(C)\mathrm{SymOpMonEnd}(\mathcal{C})SymOpMonEnd(C) of symmetric opmonoidal functors and opmonoidal natural transformations between them.

For every symmetric monoidal category C\mathcal{C}C, we have a forgetful functor
U(C):SymOpMonEnd(C)→End(C)U(\mathcal{C}):\mathrm{SymOpMonEnd}(\mathcal{C}) \rightarrow \mathrm{End}(\mathcal{C})U(C):SymOpMonEnd(C)→End(C).

We need a bit more than this forgetful functor for our purposes. This is the fix for the missing piece I was talking about before.

If C\mathcal{C}C is a symmetric monoidal category, then End(C)\mathrm{End}(\mathcal{C})End(C) can be made into a symmetric monoidal category End⊗(C)\mathrm{End}_{\otimes}(\mathcal{C})End⊗​(C) where the monoidal structure is induced pointwisely by the one of C\mathcal{C}C. For instance, we define (F⊗G)(A):=F(A)⊗G(A)(F \otimes G)(A):=F(A) \otimes G(A)(F⊗G)(A):=F(A)⊗G(A) and (α⊗β)A:=αA⊗βA(\alpha \otimes \beta)_A := \alpha_A \otimes \beta_A(α⊗β)A​:=αA​⊗βA​ etc...

In the same way, if C\mathcal{C}C is a symmetric monoidal category, then SymOpMonEnd(C)\mathrm{SymOpMonEnd(\mathcal{C})} SymOpMonEnd(C) can be made into a symmetric monoidal category SymOpMonEnd⊗(C)\mathrm{SymOpMonEnd}_{\otimes}(\mathcal{C})SymOpMonEnd⊗​(C). We obtain accordingly a symmetric strict monoidal forgetful functor

Note StrictSymMonCat\mathrm{StrictSymMonCat}StrictSymMonCat the category of symmetric monoidal categories and symmetric strict monoidal functors between them.
