Topic: action on presheaves over a graph

Matteo Capucci (he/him) (Oct 18 2022 at 19:43):

Suppose G=V→sE→tVG=V \overset{s}\rightarrow E \overset{t}\to VG=V→sE→tV is a graph, consider SetG∗\mathbf{Set}^{G^*}SetG∗ where G∗G^*G∗ is the free category on GGG.
There is a canonical endomorphism (−)1:SetG∗→SetG∗(-)^1 : \mathbf{Set}^{G^*} \to \mathbf{Set}^{G^*}(−)1:SetG∗→SetG∗ given by 'pushing forward' data along edges.
That is, given FFF, we define on vertices v∈Vv \in Vv∈V

F1(v)=∑t(e)=vF(s(e))F^1(v) = \sum_{t(e)=v} F(s(e))F1(v)=t(e)=v∑​F(s(e))

and on paths p:v→wp: v \to wp:v→w

F1(p)=λ(e,x:F(s(e))) . (p,F(e)(x))F^1(p) = \lambda (e, x:F(s(e))) \,.\, (p, F(e)(x))F1(p)=λ(e,x:F(s(e))).(p,F(e)(x))

One can show pretty easily this is well-defined on natural transformations too: ϕ1(v):F1(v)→H1(v)\phi^1 (v): F^1(v) \to H^1(v)ϕ1(v):F1(v)→H1(v) sends (e,x)(e,x)(e,x) to (e,ϕs(e)(x))(e, \phi_{s(e)}(x))(e,ϕs(e)​(x)).

Now my questions:

  1. Does anyone know this construction? It seems pretty natural. (Also, it seems to work for any category C\cal CC, not just graphs).
  2. How could I phrase it in purely abstract terms? On the object data, it is given by pull-pushing el(F)0→V\mathrm{el}(F)_0 \to Vel(F)0​→V across the span defining GGG. But on arrows?
  3. Does it remind you of something known in geometry/topology?

Paolo Capriotti (Oct 18 2022 at 20:26):

Is the sum in the definition of F1F^1F1 meant to be over paths? If so, I think this is the left Kan extension along V→G∗V \to G^*V→G∗ of the restriction of FFF to VVV. In other words, the endofunctor (−)1(-)^1(−)1 is the comonad corresponding to the adjunction between SetV\mathbf{Set}^VSetV and SetG∗\mathbf{Set}^{G^*}SetG∗ induced by left Kan extension.
