Topic: p.r.a. monads from adjunctions

Brendan Murphy (Mar 24 2024 at 16:44):

Recall that a functor T:C→DT : \mathcal{C} \to \mathcal{D}T:C→D on a category C\mathcal{C}C with a terminal object 111 is called p.r.a. if the induced functor T1:C→D/T(1)T_1 : \mathcal{C} \to \mathcal{D}/T(1)T1​:C→D/T(1) is a right adjoint, and a monad is called p.r.a. if its underlying functor is p.r.a. and its unit/multiplication are cartesian (have cartesian naturality squares). What conditions on an adjunction F ⁣:C⇄D: ⁣GF \negmedspace: \mathcal{C} \rightleftarrows \mathcal{D}:\negmedspace GF:C⇄D:G tell us that the induced monad on C\mathcal{C}C is p.r.a.?

Brendan Murphy (Mar 24 2024 at 16:47):

If C,D\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D}C,D have all pullbacks, F,GF, GF,G preserve pullbacks, and the adjunction unit & counit are cartesian then we can interpret this as an adjunction in the 222-category of such things and get that the induced monad is again cartesian (which is a necessary condition)

Brendan Murphy (Mar 24 2024 at 16:49):

That the unit/multiplication of the induced monad are cartesian natural transformations means the unit of the adjunction must be cartesian and the "conjugate whiskering" GεFG\varepsilon FGεF of the counit must be cartesian

Kevin Carlson (aka Arlin) (Mar 26 2024 at 18:26):

If C\mathcal CC is cototal then T1T_1T1​ will be a right adjoint if and only if TTT preserves connected limits, so the main thing you want is that FFF preserves connected limits. I'm not sure what to say about the cartesian natural transformations beyond what you've already said.
