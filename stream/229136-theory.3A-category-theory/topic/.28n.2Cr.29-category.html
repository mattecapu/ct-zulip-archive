(By the way, here's a conceptual question: In a quasicategory composition is defined as a relation and not as an operation. That is, two 1-morphisms f ⁣:A→Bf\colon A\to Bf:A→B and g ⁣:B→Cg\colon B\to Cg:B→C don't have a specified composite g∘f ⁣:A→Cg\circ f\colon A\to Cg∘f:A→C. Instead, there is a relation of the form "a 1-morphism h ⁣:A→Ch\colon A\to Ch:A→C is a composite of fff and ggg". This makes sense because such a composite hhh is uniquely determined up to homotopy -- and we only care about a 1-morphism up to homotopy anyway. But why, then, is there a specified identity idA:=s0A\mathrm{id}_A:= s_0AidA​:=s0​A instead of a relation of the form "f ⁣:A→Af\colon A \to Af:A→A is a identity of AAA"?)
