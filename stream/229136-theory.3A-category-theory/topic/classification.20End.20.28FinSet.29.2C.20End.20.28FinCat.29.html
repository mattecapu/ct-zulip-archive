Todd Trimble (Dec 31 2021 at 11:14):

One thing you could say is that past 000, those sequences are nondecreasing, because (letting [n][n][n] denote an nnn-element set) if 0<m,n0 < m, n0<m,n, we have m≤nm \leq nm≤n iff [m][m][m] is a retract of [n][n][n], and (endo)functors preserve retracts.

Another thing you could say is that is that if F([n])=[0]F([n]) = [0]F([n])=[0] for any n>0n > 0n>0, then F([m])=[0]F([m]) = [0]F([m])=[0] for all mmm. This is because for all mmm, there is a morphism f:m→nf: m \to nf:m→n, and thus a morphism F(f):F([m])→[0]F(f): F([m]) \to [0]F(f):F([m])→[0], but this forces F([m])=[0]F([m]) = [0]F([m])=[0] by strictness of [0][0][0].

I don't know any other constraints.
