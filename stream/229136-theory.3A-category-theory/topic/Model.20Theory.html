John Baez (Mar 25 2020 at 20:16):

Yes, a monoid in the category of monoids is exactly a commutative monoid. This is called the Eckmann-Hilton argument.

And as my link shows, you don't even need associativity! A unital magma (a thing with a binary operation having an element that's both a left and right unit) in the category of unital magmas is a commutative unital magma.

Puzzle. Let Σ∗\Sigma^*Σ∗ be the free monoid on the set Σ\SigmaΣ. This is the set of finite sequences of elements of Σ\SigmaΣ, with concatenation as the monoid operation (let's call that operation +++). It has a second semigroup structure − ⁣:Σ∗×Σ∗→Σ∗- \colon \Sigma^* \times \Sigma^* \to \Sigma^*−:Σ∗×Σ∗→Σ∗ that simply discards the second argument: that is, a−b=aa - b = aa−b=a.
