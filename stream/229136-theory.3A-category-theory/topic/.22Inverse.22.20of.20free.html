what examples do you have in mind? the posetal case of this question would be: what monotone endomorphisms FFF generate closure operators MMM whose fixpoints are the subfixpoints of FFF. kelly's paper shows that there is a brutal force answer to such questions. but it is like asking what are the free ordered topological groups over discrete sets. category theory arose from the fact that in real life, such things are ordered objects in the category of topological groups, which are the group objects in the category of topological spaces. monads naturally arise from adjunctions, not directly from endofunctors. and adjunctions naturally arise from profunctors, not from functors. so there is a mismatch.

if the goal is to generate the monad MMM as the algebraic theory over a signature of operations presented by a functor FFF (i extrapolate this from your earlier sketch of quotient monads; this MMM would then be a quotient of the equation-free monad F‾\overline{F}F... sorry if this extrapolation is wrong; but IF that is what you are trying to do) --- THEN the FFF needs to be restricted to polynomial functors. i think that is presented in detail in manes' book "algebraic theories". it mostly goes back to frend linton's thesis and eilenberg.

Ralph Sarkis (Jun 25 2021 at 14:59):

The relevant results for this thread are Theorem 3.3 and 3.4. We show that for any monad MMM on a category with coproducts, there is a monad MsM^{\mathrm{s}}Ms such that the category of semialgebras for MMM (EM algebras but we drop the unit axiom) is isomorphic to the category of algebras for MsM^{\mathrm{s}}Ms.

We call MsM^{\mathrm{s}}Ms the semifree monad on MMM.

Note that when we construct the algebraically free monad on an endofunctor, we add two axioms to the algebras. In our case, we remove one axiom, that is why I wanted some terminology for the "inverse" of algebraically free. Since there was no widely used term, we stuck with our original idea of semifree monad.
