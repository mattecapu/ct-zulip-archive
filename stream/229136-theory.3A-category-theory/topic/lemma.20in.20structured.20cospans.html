John Baez (Apr 11 2021 at 15:53):

Daniel Plácido said:

It seems that the category of morphisms of LX{}_L\mathbb XL​X should be a restriction of X1\mathbb X_1X1​ to morphisms with source/target in the image of LLL.

No. A vertical morphism LX{}_L\mathbb XL​X is a morphism in A\mathsf{A}A, like f:a→a′f: a \to a'f:a→a′ in your notation here. It's not a morphism in X\mathsf{X}X that just happen to be equal L(f):L(a)→L(a′)L(f) : L(a) \to L(a')L(f):L(a)→L(a′) for some fff.

John Baez (Apr 11 2021 at 15:55):

where f:a→bf: a \to bf:a→b and g:b→b′g: b \to b'g:b→b′ are part of the structure of the 2-morphism. We should have said this more clearly: "of the form" is a vague, and that's what confused you! We're talking about a structure here, not a mere property.

John Baez (Apr 11 2021 at 15:57):

It has to be this way, because a 2-morphism knows its source, which in this case is f:a→a′f: a \to a'f:a→a′, and its target, which in this case is g:b→b′g: b \to b'g:b→b′.
