Topic: softened preorder

John Baez (Jan 21 2026 at 22:09):

Thanks to all your help with some questions of mine, I may be ready to prove a theorem. I'd be happy for feedback.

First a definition:

Definition. A softened preorder is a category such that given f,g ⁣:x→yf, g \colon x \to yf,g:x→y, there exists an automorphism α ⁣:y→y\alpha \colon y \to yα:y→y such that g=αfg = \alpha fg=αf.

The idea is that if we can always take α=1y\alpha = 1_yα=1y​ then our category is a preorder, but in general we are weakening or 'softening' the condition that there's at most one morphism from any object xxx to any object yyy. Note that the roles of xxx and yyy are really symmetrical here, since f=α−1gf = \alpha^{-1} gf=α−1g.

Exercise. The opposite of a softened preorder may not be a softened preorder, but it is always an opsoftened preorder: given f,g ⁣:x→yf, g \colon x \to yf,g:x→y, there exists an automorphism α ⁣:x→x\alpha \colon x \to xα:x→x such that g=fαg = f \alphag=fα.

I'm happy to hear suggestions for better names for these kind of categories - and I know category theorists like nothing more than arguing about terminology, so consider this opportunity to argue my big gift to all of you. But I want to move on here....

Theorem. Suppose PPP is a preorder, F:P→GpdF: P \to \mathbf{Gpd}F:P→Gpd is a pseudofunctor, and C=∫FC = \int FC=∫F is the category built via the covariant Grothendieck construction from FFF. Then CCC is a softened preorder. Conversely if CCC is a softened preorder then C≃∫FC \simeq \int FC≃∫F for some preorder PPP and pseudofunctor F:P→GpdF: P \to \mathbf{Gpd}F:P→Gpd.

Let me prove the first part now and try to prove the converse later.

Everything here is a pair, and we're saying

(g0,g1)=(1,g1f1−1)∘(f0,f1)(g_0, g_1) = (1, g_1 f_1^{-1}) \circ (f_0, f_1) (g0​,g1​)=(1,g1​f1−1​)∘(f0​,f1​)

Composition at right is taking place in the Grothendieck construction ∫F\int F∫F. Thus, the first component of each pair composes in the usual way in PPP, and for this we're claiming

Nathanael Arkor (Jan 22 2026 at 10:06):

If the automorphism is assumed unique, then xxx is called prequasi-initial in Tholen's MacNeille completion of concrete categories with local properties. If xxx is also weakly initial, then it is called quasi-initial. There are probably other concepts relevant to what you are studying in this paper.

John Baez (Jan 23 2026 at 01:15):

By the way, I was trying to prove that the category of central finite-dimensional simple algebras over a field kkk is a softened preorder. (Here AAA is central if the center of AAA is kkk.) This example is the only reason why I care about this topic.

I discovered to my delight that this result is true, and it follows right away from the Skolem-Noether theorem.

(I hadn't expected it to be a new result, so I'm not disappointed: I'm just happy that it was discovered by two bigshots.)
