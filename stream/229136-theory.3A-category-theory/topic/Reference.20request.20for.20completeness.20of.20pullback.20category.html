Proposition 1.3 in Classifying Topos, by Giraud. It is stated for finitely complete categories, but the same proof goes through for complete ones.

Mike Shulman (Jun 21 2025 at 15:49):

Oh, of course. Yes, that works. Here's another proof: a continuous fibration B→CB\to CB→C is equivalent to a pseudofunctor Cop→CpltC^{\rm op} \to \rm CpltCop→Cplt to the 2-category of complete categories and continuous functors. Evidently such a pseudofunctor can be precomposed with any functor A→CA\to CA→C.
