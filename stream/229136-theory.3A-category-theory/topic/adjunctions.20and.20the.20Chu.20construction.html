Oscar Cunningham (May 20 2020 at 09:28):

This reminds me of something I talked about here. The category of topological spaces is total, which implies that every limit-preserving functor Topop→Set\mathbf{Top}^\mathrm{op}\to\mathbf{Set}Topop→Set is representable. Meanwhile, (I think) the category of locales has the property that products distribute over colimits.

These two properties between them imply cartesian closedness, because the functor X↦((X×A)→B)X\mapsto ((X\times A)\to B)X↦((X×A)→B) would be limit-preserving and hence representable. But sadly the two pieces of the puzzle have gifted to different categories; pointless topology has one and pointy topology has the other.
