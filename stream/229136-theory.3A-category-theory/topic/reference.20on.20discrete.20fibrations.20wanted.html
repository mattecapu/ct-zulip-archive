Christian Williams (Dec 22 2020 at 23:45):

John Baez (Dec 22 2020 at 23:47):

John Baez (Dec 22 2020 at 23:50):

Alas, it doesn't say much about fibrations. It has an exercise, 2.4viii, which defines "discrete fibration". It tells you to show that given a presheaf on CCC, the forgetful functor from its category of elements to CCC is a discrete fibration.

John Baez (Dec 22 2020 at 23:51):

So that's good, that's half the battle: the other half is taking a discrete fibration and getting a presheaf. In the end you'd want to prove these two procedures give an equivalence of categories between presheaves on CCC and discrete fibrations with base CCC.

Nathanael Arkor (Dec 22 2020 at 23:58):

Loregian–Riehl's Categorical notions of fibration covers both the equivalence between discrete fibrations and presheaves, and the 2-equivalence in the non-discrete setting.

John Baez (Dec 22 2020 at 23:59):

Thanks, I'll look there.
