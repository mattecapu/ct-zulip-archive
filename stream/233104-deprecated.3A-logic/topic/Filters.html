Topic: Filters

সায়ন্তন রায় (Jun 21 2020 at 13:34):

I was recently going through the notion of filters on a set. The definition of an ultrafilter in the book says that an ultrafilter is a maximal proper filter. Then it is claimed that if FFF is a proper filter then FFF is equal to the intersection of all the ultrafilters that contain FFF. How do I show that the intersection of all the ultrafilters that contain FFF is contained in FFF?

Lê Thành Dũng (Tito) Nguyễn (Jun 21 2020 at 14:01):

Suppose that FFF is a proper filter on P(X)\mathcal{P}(X)P(X) and let S⊂XS \subset XS⊂X be a non-empty set that is not in FFF. Consider F′={S′′∣∃S′∈F:(S′∖S)⊂S′′}F ' = \{S'' \mid \exists S' \in F : (S' \setminus S) \subset S''\}F′={S′′∣∃S′∈F:(S′∖S)⊂S′′}. This F′F'F′ is a proper filter, containing the complement of SSS, and it can be extended to some ultrafilter (by https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boolean_prime_ideal_theorem#The_ultrafilter_lemma ). So there is an ultrafilter that contains FFF and not SSS.
