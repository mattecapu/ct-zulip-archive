Henry Story (Jan 04 2021 at 06:40):

Here is how I think the argument goes. As a Category Theorist you accept that a proof in a Category CCC is also an opposite proof in the category CopC^{op}Cop. So if a constructive proof is one that is provable in a Topoi, then any such proof is also one in the complement Topoi of co-constructive logics. And if it can be show that both of those together give first order logic, then there cannot be anything wrong with that either. Note that a lot of research recently seems to be focused on how one can avoid collapse into first order logic and have constructive and co-constructive logics work together. (I am not sure about this but I think I understood that when constructivists and co-constructivists agree - after a process of construction and attempted deconstruction - the result is a first order logic truth).
