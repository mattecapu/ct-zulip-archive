Madeleine Birchfield (Jun 13 2025 at 17:20):

What do (∞,k)(\infty,k)(∞,k)-category theorists call the kkk?

(n,k)(n, k)(n,k)-categories are nnn-truncated (∞,k)(\infty,k)(∞,k)-categories, and I think in this case nnn is called the "truncation level", see e.g. section 11.3 of Cisinki et al's Formalization of Higher Categories.
