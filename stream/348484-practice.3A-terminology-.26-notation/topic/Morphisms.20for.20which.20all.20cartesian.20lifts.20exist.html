Topic: Morphisms for which all cartesian lifts exist

Max New (Nov 03 2025 at 16:05):

Let F:C→DF : C \to DF:C→D be a functor (not assumed to be a fibration). Is there a common term for a morphism f:x→yf : x \to yf:x→y in DDD such that for all F(y^)=yF(\hat y) = yF(y^​)=y the cartesian lift f∗y^f^*\hat yf∗y^​ exists?

Nathan Corbyn (Nov 03 2025 at 17:04):

I don’t know of one in general, but in case F=cod:D→→DF = \mathrm{cod} : \mathcal{D}^\to \to \mathcal{D}F=cod:D→→D we would say fff is carrable. So maybe FFF-carrable?

Max New (Nov 03 2025 at 17:40):

carrable seems fine. Any explanation for why it's called that?

Nathanael Arkor (Nov 03 2025 at 17:58):

It's a synonym of "quarrable" in French, which means "quadrable", presumably because if a morphism is such, then we can form universal squares?

Mike Shulman (Nov 08 2025 at 18:50):

When writing in English, I think we should generally translate words into English. So maybe "FFF-quadrable"?

John Baez (Nov 09 2025 at 14:22):

(When I see "quadrable" it reminds of expressions like "solvable with quadratures", which means "integrable".)
