Here we prove a theorem, the result of which was relayed by Tom Leinster. (Baez, 2023) Specifically that a monoid object internal to a category is a group object when its association square is a pullback. Our proof proceeds by exploiting a symmetry of the pullback diagram.

There's a general principle that for any statement that can be expressed in the language of limits, which applies in this case, the statement is true iff it holds in the category of sets. The idea is that the Yoneda embedding preserves and reflects limits, i.e., for any category CCC in which the statement is interpreted, the collection of representable functors

C⟶C(c,−)SetC \overset{C(c, -)}{\longrightarrow} \mathsf{Set}C⟶C(c,−)​Set

jointly preserves and reflects limits, so verification in sets suffices. This can be seen as a basic "completeness theorem".
