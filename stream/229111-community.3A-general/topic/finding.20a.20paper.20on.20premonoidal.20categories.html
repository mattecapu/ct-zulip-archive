Cole Comfort (Nov 01 2020 at 12:54):

(Cospan(FinSet),+)({\sf Cospan}({\sf FinSet}), +) (Cospan(FinSet),+) gives a semantics for equivalence relations with scalars.

(Span(FinSet),+)({\sf Span}({\sf FinSet}), +) (Span(FinSet),+) gives a semantics for nondeterministic circuits weighted by natural numbers, because it is a presentation for natural number matrices under the disjoint union.

(Span(FinSet),×)({\sf Span}({\sf FinSet}), \times) (Span(FinSet),×) is like the nonlinear version of (Span(FinSet),+)({\sf Span}({\sf FinSet}), +)(Span(FinSet),+), because you have the multiplication gate and its unit which is like an affine shift; as well as the transpose of these things. Although, I only really have intuition about the full subcategory of this category generated by [2n][2^n][2n].

Because of this result that premonoidal categories embed in the Kleisli category of a state monad, I imagine that perhaps this exotic category gives a semantics for something like stateful equivalence relations? I am not so sure.

I am not sure if (Corel(FinSet),×)({\sf Corel}({\sf FinSet}), \times) (Corel(FinSet),×) is monoidal, and I don't have any reason to believe it is. In the case of (Span(FinSet),+)({\sf Span}({\sf FinSet}), +) (Span(FinSet),+), the quotient taking you to relations imposes the equation 2=1 2=12=1, giving a semantics for nondeterministic circuits. Similarly the quotient on (Cospan(FinSet),+)({\sf Cospan}({\sf FinSet}), +) (Cospan(FinSet),+) taking you to corelations just removes the extra scalars, and gives you actual equivalence relations. The case of (Span(FinSet),×)({\sf Span}({\sf FinSet}), \times) (Span(FinSet),×) is a bit more interesting. At least in the full subcategory generated by objects [2n][2^n][2n], then the quotient involves some nontrivial identity involving the multiplication gate, which doesn't really have a very nice categorical interpretation, as well as the extraness of one of the Frobenius algebras (the latter, as in (Corel(FinSet),+)({\sf Corel}({\sf FinSet}), +)(Corel(FinSet),+)). This other quotient is like saying that t2=t t^2=tt2=t, where t tt is the 2×22\times 22×2 upper triangular matrix containing 1s. I guess this is the bit that removes the proof relevance.
