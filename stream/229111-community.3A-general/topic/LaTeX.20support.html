John Baez (Sep 05 2020 at 18:29):

Another type of question such a doc could answer would be: should I use ⇒\Rightarrow⇒ for functors?

No, that would terribly confusing! Most importantly, nobody ever does it: they use →\to→. Second, functors are an 1-morphisms in Cat and 1-morphisms are denoted →\to→. Natural transformations are 2-morphisms in Cat so it's good to use ⇒\Rightarrow⇒ for them, e.g. α:F⇒G\alpha : F \Rightarrow Gα:F⇒G where F,G:C→DF,G : C \to DF,G:C→D are functors.
