Martti Karvonen (Jun 06 2020 at 13:35):

I haven't checked all of the details (of the paper or of what I'm about to say), but I think one can follow a similar proof strategy while assuming ordinary AC instead of global AC: Given KKK (whose objects are now just elements of VVV), define LLL with the same objects, but maps X→YX\to YX→Y in LLL are given by factorizations X→Z→YX\to Z\to YX→Z→Y of maps in KKK with the property that the rank of ZZZ is at most that of X,YX,YX,Y. Composition of two such factorizations is given by choosing the middle object to be the one of the middle objects with the smaller rank, or if the ranks agree, the rightmost one. Define F(X)F(X)F(X) as the set of all morphisms of KKK into XXX whose domain has rank at most that of XXX. Given a morphism (f,g) ⁣:X→Y(f,g)\colon X\to Y(f,g):X→Y in LLL, define F(f,g)kF(f,g)kF(f,g)k by doing the composition (f,g)(k,id)(f,g)(k,id)(f,g)(k,id) in LLL and taking the map from the resulting middle object to YYY. GGG is done similarly with maps out of XXX. The rest follows the original proof, i.e. "The details are left to the reader."

Martti Karvonen (Jun 08 2020 at 03:57):

for a category CCC, one wants a total preorder on objects such that the down-class of every object is in fact a set, and any morphism factors through something in the downset of the domain and the codomain.

The second condition is vacuous as at least one of (f,id)(f,id)(f,id), (id,f)(id,f)(id,f) will do. Instead of a structure on objects, one could think of the argument in terms of a structure on morphisms. I believe the following is sufficient for the argument to go through: having two classes LLL and RRR of morphisms such that (i) any morphism factors as r∘lr\circ lr∘l with r∈Rr\in Rr∈R and l∈Ll\in Ll∈L, (ii) for any object XXX, the class of LLL-morphisms (RRR-morphisms) with domain (codomain) XXX is a set and (iii) given a morphism r2l2r1l1r_2l_2r_1l_1r2​l2​r1​l1​ we have r2l2r1∈Rr_2l_2r_1\in Rr2​l2​r1​∈R or l2r1l1∈Ll_2r_1l_1\in Ll2​r1​l1​∈L (or both). Having such a preorder then gives LLL and RRR by considering maps to/from an object lower in the preorder, but perhaps one could find LLL and RRR through some other means. Roughly: (iii) gives a simple of way of defining an associative composition when morphisms are taken to be such factorizations (plus formal identities), (ii) ensures that the resulting category is concrete and (i) that the functor (r,l)↦rl(r,l)\mapsto rl(r,l)↦rl is full.

Martti Karvonen (Jun 09 2020 at 15:03):

A particularly succinct sufficient condition for being the quotient of a concrete category (slightly stronger than (i)-(iii) above) amounts to saying the smallness-condition (ii) above and "any fff is in L∪RL\cup RL∪R". This behaves quite nicely: such a structure pulls back along small-fibred functors and pushes forward along surjective-on-objects and full small-fibred functors. Thus if our SetSetSet has a such a structure, so will any quotient of a small-fibred concrete category, and the converse is also true as discussed above.
