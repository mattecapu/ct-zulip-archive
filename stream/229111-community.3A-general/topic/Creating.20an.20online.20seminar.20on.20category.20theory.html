I would be interested into inviting people to make tutorials on lots of current research topics which are either in category theory or at least loosely linked to category theory such as:

  • Probability/satistics with category theory
  • Differential categories
  • Formal category theory
  • Curry-Howard-Lambek correspondence
  • Categorification
  • Categorical database theory
  • Algebraic geometry (in particular category-theoretic approaches)
  • Model theory (for instance Makkai's work)
  • Theory of programming languages
  • Uses of category theory in current research in abstract algebra
  • Homotopy theory
  • Toposes
  • Category theory in theoretical physics (for instance Schreiber's and co kind of work)
  • etc...
    The goal is to make them easier to learn by anyone interested.

Claudio Pisani (Mar 23 2024 at 06:53):

Jean-Baptiste Vienney said:

Ok, so no one wants to talk :laughing:

I would like to talk about my latest research on "unbiased symmetric multicategories"
(which could be the title)
showing how symmetric multicategories (in their unbiased form, with sequences replaced by families) are double categories with finite sums and with a sum preserving discrete fibration to the double category of pullback squares in finite sets
M:M→PbM: \rm\mathbb M \to \mathbb PbM:M→Pb
If the "loose" component of MMM is an opfibration (respectively, a discrete opfibration) we get unbiased symmetric monoidal categories (respectively, commutative monoids).
If we replace the "indexing" base Pb\rm\mathbb PbPb with Tot\rm\mathbb TotTot suitably defined, we get planar multicategories.
If we consider Bij\rm\mathbb BijBij (commuting squares in finite sets with two bijective parallel sides) the inclusion Bij→Pb\rm\mathbb Bij \to \rm\mathbb PbBij→Pb can be seen as the multicategory with just an arrow, and taking Bij\rm\mathbb BijBij as the indexing base, we get categories in the form of unary multicategories.
Another interesting instance is when M\rm\mathbb MM is the double category associated to the family fibration of a category CCC; this gives the unbiased version of a [[sequential multicategory]]...

I would be happy to talk on these (and on many other related) topics!
