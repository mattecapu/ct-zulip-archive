Topic: Adjoint School 2026

Aven Dauz (Dec 04 2025 at 12:38):

Dear all,

We're pleased to announce that applications for Adjoint School 2026 are currently open! Adjoint School is a great opportunity for anyone interested in seeing what applied category theory is all about, and collaborate with other researchers on some exciting topics such as quantum computation, Markov processes, and partiality!

Applications are open until December 14th, you can find the application form here

Overview of the school

As a student of the Adjoint School, you will be in groups of 4-5 people led by a mentor and a few TAs. You can look at each of the four research project descriptions and the mentors here. The focus of the school is to give students an environment to explore the proposed project topic, develop research skills, and network with other researchers in the applied category theory community.

The school is divided into two sections:

  1. An online learning seminar period from February - June 2026
  2. An in-person research week from 29 June to 6 July, 2026 in Talinn, Estonia. The following week will be the ACT2026 conference.

During the online seminar period, students read and discuss two assigned papers with their groups, and give presentations to the rest of the school. Up through the in-person research week, students will finalize their research as a blog-post on the n-Category Cafe and give a presentation at the ACT conference. During the in-person research week, students will meet regularly with their groups to further develop their research, practice presentations, and take some time to enjoy the sights in Talinn!

Who should apply?

If you're interested in learning about the many applications of category theoretic methods in other disciplines beyond pure math, please apply! We will consider applications from strong undergraduate, masters, PhDs, post-docs, or people outside of academia in a relevant discipline. We recommend some familiarity with basic category theory, but students will be in an interdisciplinary environment with people from computer science, math, engineering, physics, and other applied fields.

Feel free to message @Aven Dauz or @Diana Kessler if you have any questions!
