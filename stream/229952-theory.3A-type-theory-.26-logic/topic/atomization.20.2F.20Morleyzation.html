Topic: atomization / Morleyzation

Matteo Capucci (he/him) (Mar 07 2026 at 11:37):

Atomization (see §3 of Hodge's big red book) is the process whereby we replace a theory TTT with TatmT^{atm}Tatm which is built from TTT by adding, for all sentences φ\varphiφ, a relation symbol RφR_\varphiRφ​ and an axiom Rφ↔φR_\varphi \leftrightarrow \varphiRφ​↔φ.
I have a feeling this has a neat categorical description: if we take TTT to be an algebra DA→ADA \to ADA→A for some doctrine (= 2-monad) DDD, then its atomization is the equivalent algebra DA/≅DA / \congDA/≅ obtained as the strict [[coisoinserter]] (aka pseudocoequalizer)
image.png
In fact I'm not sure precomposing by iii is even necessary. Without that, that is a categorified version of the usual way of presenting algebras as quotients of free ones.

Anyway, does anyone know if this has been noticed before?
