Not sure where to ask this, but here we go.

We can define a monoid operation over the set of nondeterministic finite-state automata (NFA) up to bisimilarity (reminiscent of the sum of regular expressions/automata up to language equivalence): given two automata A1,A2A_1,A_2A1​,A2​, let A1+A2A_1+A_2A1​+A2​ be the automata whose (final) states are the disjoint sum of the (final) states of A1A_1A1​ and A2A_2A2​, with the two initial states identified, and whose transition relation is also the disjoint union of the two transition relations, except on the initial state, where it behaves as you'd expect. This operation is a congruence for bisimilarity, so we can lift it to equivalence classes.

Moreover, because A+A≅AA+A\cong AA+A≅A, we can define a partial order on NFA up to bisimilarity: A≤BA\leq BA≤B iff A+B≅BA +B\cong BA+B≅B.

The sum is the join for this partial order, but does it also have meets? I have not checked this carefully, but the operation seems to lift to bisimilarity classes and to define a valid meet, making automata up to bisimilarity a lattice.

Ralph Sarkis (Dec 14 2022 at 18:00):

Not an answer (but maybe a helpful discussion). Just to make sure I understand, we have A≤BA \leq BA≤B implies the language recognized by AAA (denoted by L(A)L(A)L(A)) is included in L(B)L(B)L(B), but not necessarily the converse. That is, we might have L(A)⊆L(B)L(A) \subseteq L(B)L(A)⊆L(B) but not A≤BA\leq BA≤B.

So we know the language recognized by the meet of A1A_1A1​ and A2A_2A2​ (if it exists) is included in the intersection of the languages. This suggests your construction, but the failure of the converse above does not help confirming your construction is the right one.

Todd Schmid (he/they) (May 29 2023 at 19:15):

It took me a bit to realize which precise order you mean! You literally mean the sum operator in classic process algebra (nondeterministic choice). If you are in need of a meet operation for this join that is defined on the final coalgebra, you can use Lambek's lemma: the final Pf\mathcal P_fPf​-coalgebra (or final Pf(A×−)\mathcal P_f(A\times-)Pf​(A×−)-coalgebra, if you want labels) is a free semilattice with bottom, so you can indeed take finite intersections.
