Topic: Quasi-Derivations?

Carlos Zapata-Carratala (Sep 22 2023 at 23:49):

Hi everyone!

Let RRR be a ring. An additive map Δ:R→R\Delta: R\to RΔ:R→R is (provisionally) called a quasi-derivation when

Δ(a⋅b)=Δ(a)⋅b+a⋅Δ(b)+Δ(a)⋅Δ(b)\Delta(a\cdot b) = \Delta(a)\cdot b + a \cdot \Delta(b) + \Delta(a)\cdot \Delta(b)Δ(a⋅b)=Δ(a)⋅b+a⋅Δ(b)+Δ(a)⋅Δ(b).

Quasi-derivations do not close under addition, they do not form a RRR-module in the usual way and they do not close under commutator bracket (in fact they don't seem to carry any obvious Lie algebra structure).

Short simple question: has anyone seen these guys before? They appear naturally when defining finite difference operators, and particularly in discrete analogues of directional derivatives.

John Baez (Sep 23 2023 at 09:11):

Say we define a finite difference operator on functions f:R→Rf: \mathbb{R} \to \mathbb{R}f:R→R in the usual way:

(Δ(f))(x)=f(x+1)−f(x) (\Delta(f))(x) = f(x+1) - f(x) (Δ(f))(x)=f(x+1)−f(x)

John Baez (Sep 23 2023 at 09:13):

Δ\DeltaΔ is not a derivation but it's a twisted derivation:

(Δ(fg))(x)=f(x+1)g(x+1)−f(x)g(x) (\Delta(f g))(x) = f(x+1)g(x+1) - f(x) g(x) (Δ(fg))(x)=f(x+1)g(x+1)−f(x)g(x)
=(f(x+1)−f(x))g(x+1)+f(x)(g(x+1)−g(x))= (f(x+1) - f(x)) g(x+1) + f(x)(g(x+1) - g(x)) =(f(x+1)−f(x))g(x+1)+f(x)(g(x+1)−g(x))
=Δ(f)α(g)+fΔ(g) = \Delta(f) \alpha(g) + f \Delta(g) =Δ(f)α(g)+fΔ(g)

John Baez (Sep 23 2023 at 09:17):

where α\alphaα is the automorphism of the ring of functions f:R→Rf : \mathbb{R} \to \mathbb{R}f:R→R given by

(α(g))(x)=g(x+1) (\alpha(g))(x) = g(x+1) (α(g))(x)=g(x+1)

John Baez (Sep 23 2023 at 09:17):

So we say Δ\DeltaΔ is a derivation twisted by the automorphism α\alphaα.

John Baez (Sep 23 2023 at 09:18):

Twisted derivations are in turn a special case of derivations on a ring taking values in a bimodule of that ring. I've been thinking about those lately. Given a ring RRR and a bimodule MMM, a derivation on RRR with values in MMM is a function

Δ:R→M \Delta : R \to MΔ:R→M

such that

Δ(1)=0 \Delta(1) = 0 Δ(1)=0
Δ(r+r′)=Δ(r)+Δ(r′) \Delta(r + r') = \Delta(r) + \Delta(r') Δ(r+r′)=Δ(r)+Δ(r′)
Δ(rr′)=Δ(r)r′+rΔ(r′) \Delta(r r') = \Delta(r) r' + r \Delta(r') Δ(rr′)=Δ(r)r′+rΔ(r′)

John Baez (Sep 23 2023 at 09:21):

where the last formula the multiplication is defined using the fact that MMM is a bimodule of RRR. Taking M=RM = RM=R with various interesting bimodule structures we get various kinds of 'twisted' derivations on RRR.

John Baez (Sep 23 2023 at 09:22):

Derivations of this generalized sort are connected to 'Hochschild cohomology'.

This is called a derivation of weight 1 and it is the differentiation operator for weighted differential algebras

JS PL (he/him) (Sep 25 2023 at 21:43):

So in general, for a base commutative ring kkk and a kkk-algebra RRR and λ∈k\lambda \in kλ∈k, a derivation of weight λ\lambdaλ on RRR is a kkk-linear map D:R→RD: R \to RD:R→R such that:
D(a⋅b)=D(a)⋅b+a⋅D(b)+λ⋅D(a)⋅D(b)D(a \cdot b) = D(a) \cdot b + a \cdot D(b) + \lambda \cdot D(a) \cdot D(b)D(a⋅b)=D(a)⋅b+a⋅D(b)+λ⋅D(a)⋅D(b)

Oh yes they are very much related to Rota-Baxter stuff! Briefly, a Rota-Baxter algebra is the "intergration" analogue of a differential algebra. Then a differential Rota-Baxter algebra is one with a differential and an integral operation which satisfy versions of the Fundamental theorems of Calculus in nice ways. In one of the references I posted above, they explain how to construct cofree differential Rota-Baxter algebras of any weight, and how the free Rota-Baxter algebra monad and cofree differential algebra comonad distribute over one another.
