In Theorem 5.2 he shows that given symmetric pseudomonoids M and M′ in a symmetric monoidal bicategory (C, ⊗), there is a natural way to make M ⊗ M′ into a symmetric pseudomonoid, of which the tensor product of 2-rigs is an example. I believe a binary operation 'commutes with itself' in the sense of commutative monads if

(g1g2)(h1h2)=(g1h1)(g2h2)⋆ (g_1 g_2) (h_1 h_2) = (g_1 h_1) (g_2 h_2) \qquad \qquad \star (g1​g2​)(h1​h2​)=(g1​h1​)(g2​h2​)⋆

Expanding on the above: take any number nnn and any number k≤nk \le nk≤n. Create a simplex with nnn ordered vertices. Label the first kkk vertices with objects of J\mathbb{J}J and the rest with objects of K\mathbb{K}K. Edges are typed: an edge between two J\mathbb{J}J-objects is a backwards vertical arrow of J\mathbb{J}J, an edge between two K\mathbb{K}K-objects is a forwards vertical arrow of K\mathbb{K}K, and an edge from a J\mathbb{J}J-object to a K\mathbb{K}K-object is an arrow of C\mathcal{C}C.

First, freely pick edges along the spine of the simplex. There will be either 0 or 1 arrows of C\mathcal{C}C along the spine. If there is one, you also get to freely pick the edge from 0 to nnn. Then fill in the rest of the simplex. First fill in facets from the nerves of the vertical categories of J\mathbb{J}J and K\mathbb{K}K. Then fill in facets which look like: a simplex from J\mathbb{J}J and one from K\mathbb{K}K glued to one from C\mathcal{C}C along U:J→Sq(C)U : \mathbb{J} \to \mathbb{S}\mathrm{q}(\mathcal{C})U:J→Sq(C) and V:K→Sq(C)V : \mathbb{K} \to \mathbb{S}\mathrm{q}(\mathcal{C})V:K→Sq(C). Then fill in the facets which represent lifts: if they are tetrahedra fill in directly using the lifting structure, if they are higher-dimensional repeat this whole process on a smaller scale. If the lifting structure is well-behaved you should get the same results no matter which order you fill in facets.

Maps between simplices with the same (n,k)(n,k)(n,k) consist of simplices built the same way, but with backwards horizontal arrows instead of objects of J\mathbb{J}J, forwards horizontal arrows instead of objects of K\mathbb{K}K; for edges, inverted squares of J\mathbb{J}J, normal squares of K\mathbb{K}K, and twisted squares of C\mathcal{C}C; and higher simplices the most important of which represent the naturality squares of the lifting structure.
