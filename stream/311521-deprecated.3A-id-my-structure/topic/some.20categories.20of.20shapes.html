Mike Shulman (Jan 22 2023 at 17:55):

Let SkS_kSk​ be the strict monoidal category freely generated by an object GGG and kkk morphisms I→GI\to GI→G, where III is the monoidal unit. What is a good name for presheaves on SkS_kSk​ for general kkk?

  • When k=1k=1k=1, this is the category of augmented semisimplicial sets. For a presheaf XXX, we have X(Gn)==Xn−1X(G^n) == X_{n-1}X(Gn)==Xn−1​, the set of (n−1)(n-1)(n−1)-simplices; thus X(I)=X(G0)=Xn−1X(I) = X(G^0) = X_{n-1}X(I)=X(G0)=Xn−1​ is a set of (−1)(-1)(−1)-simplices.

  • When k=2k=2k=2, this is the category of semicubical sets, where X(Gn)=XnX(G^n) = X_nX(Gn)=Xn​ is the set of nnn-cubes. Note the interesting dimension-shift.

I'd also be interested in names for the analogues where the category is symmetric or semicartesian monoidal, although the former could presumably be obtained by adding the adjective "symmetric", and the latter by removing the prefix "semi-" if it's present in the general name.

Amar Hadzihasanovic (Jan 23 2023 at 08:21):

I guess one way of seeing SkS_kSk​ is as the monoidal subcategory of the augmented simplex category generated by the facets (codimension-1 faces) of the (k−1)(k-1)(k−1)-simplex. What I mean is: you “black-box” the (k−1)(k-1)(k−1)-simplex and (k−2)(k-2)(k−2)-simplex.

The morphisms are generated under joins by the identities on the (k−1)(k-1)(k−1) and (k−2)(k-2)(k−2)-simplices, and by the kkk-many face inclusions of the (k−2)(k-2)(k−2)-simplex into the (k−1)(k-1)(k−1)-simplex.

James Deikun (Jan 23 2023 at 12:57):

Think of the "square" in the 3-case being like a window pane in a child's drawing of a house: a square with a cross in the middle. The choice of which edges are "middle" is arbitrary because there's no structure that distinguishes which points are the "corners", but you could embed this in a 2-category where the kkk generating morphisms are ordered and then everything would turn out like this precisely: for k+2k+2k+2, GnG^nGn would be an nnn-cube divided along each axis by kkk internal hyperplanes. You could even see the case for k=1k = 1k=1 as being about cubes where you only care about the vicinity of one corner, and then you cut near that corner with a diagonal hyperplane to get the simplex view (and the cutting causes the "curious" dimension shift).
