Topic: A generalised category of zig-zags

Amar Hadzihasanovic (Mar 22 2023 at 11:40):

I am looking for a name or reference for the following construction, which generalises the [[localization]] of a category.

Suppose we have a category C\mathcal{C}C and two wide subcategories B\mathcal{B}B and F\mathcal{F}F.
Then I can define a category, call it B∗F\mathcal{B}^*\mathcal{F}B∗F, whose

  • objects are the objects of C\mathcal{C}C,
  • morphisms from xxx to yyy are zig-zags ([b0],f0,…,bn,[fn]):x←x1→x2←…→y([b_0], f_0, \ldots, b_n, [f_n]): x \leftarrow x_1 \rightarrow x_2 \leftarrow \ldots \rightarrow y([b0​],f0​,…,bn​,[fn​]):x←x1​→x2​←…→y, starting and ending with either a backward-facing or forward-facing morphism, where the backward-facing morphisms bib_ibi​ are in B\mathcal{B}B and the forward-facing morphisms fif_ifi​ are in F\mathcal{F}F,

composing by concatenation + composition in B\mathcal{B}B and F\mathcal{F}F when the final morphism of one zig-zag and the initial morphism of the other have the same direction, and modulo the following identifications:

  • backward-facing identities are equal to forward-facing identities,
  • a zigzag (b0,f0):x←y0→z(b_0, f_0): x \leftarrow y_0 \rightarrow z(b0​,f0​):x←y0​→z is equal to (f1,b1):x→y1←z(f_1, b_1): x \rightarrow y_1 \leftarrow z(f1​,b1​):x→y1​←z whenever b0;f1=f0;b1b_0;f_1 = f_0;b_1b0​;f1​=f0​;b1​.

When F=C\mathcal{F} = \mathcal{C}F=C, then this should be precisely the localisation of C\mathcal{C}C at the morphisms in B\mathcal{B}B, and more in general any morphism which is in the intersection of F\mathcal{F}F and B\mathcal{B}B will be invertible in B∗F\mathcal{B}^*\mathcal{F}B∗F.
However one can, for example, consider examples where F\mathcal{F}F and B\mathcal{B}B only intersect at isomorphisms, and in that case the result has a different flavour compared to a localisation.

I guess that

  • if pullbacks of F\mathcal{F}F-morphisms along B\mathcal{B}B-morphisms exist and are in F\mathcal{F}F, and symmetrically, then B∗F\mathcal{B}^*\mathcal{F}B∗F is a quotient of the bicategory of spans in C\mathcal{C}C where the left leg is in B\mathcal{B}B and the right leg in F\mathcal{F}F,
  • dually if pushouts of F\mathcal{F}F-morphisms along B\mathcal{B}B-morphisms exist and are in F\mathcal{F}F, and symmetrically, then B∗F\mathcal{B}^*\mathcal{F}B∗F is a quotient of the bicategory of cospans in C\mathcal{C}C where the right leg is in B\mathcal{B}B and the left leg in F\mathcal{F}F...

Amar Hadzihasanovic (Mar 22 2023 at 12:45):

Oh, actually I think the following is the case, more precisely to my previous guess.

Suppose that C\mathcal{C}C has pullbacks, and

  • pullbacks of F\mathcal{F}F-morphisms along B\mathcal{B}B-morphisms are in F\mathcal{F}F,
  • pullbacks of B\mathcal{B}B-morphisms along F\mathcal{F}F-morphisms are in B\mathcal{B}B;
    then there is a bicategory of spans in C\mathcal{C}C whose left leg is in B\mathcal{B}B and whose right leg is in F\mathcal{F}F.

I believe that B∗F\mathcal{B}^*\mathcal{F}B∗F is exactly the 1-truncation of this bicategory, i.e. the category with the same objects, and as morphisms classes of 1-morphisms under the equivalence relation generated by “f∼gf \sim g f∼g if there exists a 2-morphism between fff and ggg”;

Amar Hadzihasanovic (Mar 22 2023 at 12:49):

The cospan zig-zag (f1,b1)(f_1, b_1)(f1​,b1​) is equal to the composite of spans (idx,f1)(\mathrm{id}_x, f_1)(idx​,f1​) and (b1,idy)(b_1, \mathrm{id}_y)(b1​,idy​), which is exactly the pullback span produced by (f1,b1)(f_1, b_1)(f1​,b1​), and there is a morphism from the span (b0,f0)(b_0, f_0)(b0​,f0​) to this pullback precisely when b0;f1=f0;b1b_0;f_1 = f_0; b_1b0​;f1​=f0​;b1​.
