this is very close to what is called a "weighted" cone, and the analogous notion of limit is called a weighted limit.

James points out that this is a subfunctor m:P→HomC(c,D(−)m: P \to Hom_C(c,D(-)m:P→HomC​(c,D(−). In general, for a weighted cone, PPP need not be a subfunctor, but it can be an arbitrary presheaf, and mmm can be an arbitrary natural transformation.

Or in other words, instead of Wi⊂HomC(c,Di)W_i\subset Hom_C(c,D_i)Wi​⊂HomC​(c,Di​), we have a map fi:Wi→HomC(c,Di)f_i : W_i \to Hom_C(c,D_i)fi​:Wi​→HomC​(c,Di​), and this reduces to your situation when we have fif_ifi​ injective.

The notion of weighted limit is essential in enriched category theory, where ordinary limits often cannot be defined.
