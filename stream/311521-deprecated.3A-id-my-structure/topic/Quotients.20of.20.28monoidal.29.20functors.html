Topic: Quotients of (monoidal) functors

Martti Karvonen (May 01 2023 at 17:41):

I'm in a situation where I have a (lax monoidal) functor FFF into Set and equivalence relations ≈A\approx_A≈A​ on each F(A)F(A)F(A). I can write down conditions under which F/≈F/\approxF/≈ becomes a lax monoidal functor (so that the quotient maps F(A)→F(A)/≈AF(A)\to F(A)/\approx_AF(A)→F(A)/≈A​ form a monoidal natural transformation), but I'm wondering if there's some standard terminology for this kind of thing. Has anyone seen this before? What would you call it?

John Baez (May 01 2023 at 18:09):

I don't know, but it sounds like your functor might actually be a functor into [[setoids]], where a setoid is a set with an equivalence relation.

John Baez (May 01 2023 at 18:10):

(As you can see from the link, people are still fighting a bit about what setoids actually are.)

Morgan Rogers (he/him) (May 02 2023 at 09:39):

An alternative take is that you can view FFF as a (co)presheaf and ≈A{\approx}_A≈A​ as an equivalence relation on it in the category of (co)presheaves. I don't know what the monoidal product is doing, though (which monoidal structure on Set\mathrm{Set}Set is it hitting?) but Day convolution might be useful to handle the monoidal structure.

Martti Karvonen (May 02 2023 at 14:24):

I'm using the cartesian monoidal structure on Set. I guess in this take FFF becomes an internal monoid (wrt Day convolution), and ≈\approx≈ is then a congruence on this monoid.
