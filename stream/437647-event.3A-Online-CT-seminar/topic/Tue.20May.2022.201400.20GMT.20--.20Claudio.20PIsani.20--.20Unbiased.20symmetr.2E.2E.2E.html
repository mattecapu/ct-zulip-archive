Claudio Pisani (May 16 2024 at 21:01):

Here is the abstract:

We present an unbiased theory of symmetric multicategories, where sequences are replaced by families.
To be effective, this approach requires an explicit consideration of indexing and reindexing of objects and arrows,
handled by the double category Pb\mathbb{P}\rm{b}Pb of pullback squares in finite sets.
A symmetric multicategory is then a finite sum preserving discrete fibration of double categories
M:M→PbM: \mathbb{M}\to\mathbb{P}\rm{b}M:M→Pb.

The idea is that

  • M0\mathbb{M}_0M0​ is the category of families of objects of MMM with their reindexing;
  • M1\mathbb{M}_1M1​ is the category of families of arrows of MMM with their reindexing;
  • loose composition corresponds to composition in MMM.

If the loose part of MMM is an opfibration (with opcartesian arrows stable with respect to reindexing) we get unbiased symmetric monoidal categories.

As a first immediate generalization, we can remove the finiteness condition to obtain infinitary symmetric multicategories.

More interestingly, we can replace the indexing base Pb\mathbb{P}\rm{b}Pb with another "double prop" P\mathbb{P}P, obtaining the category of P\mathbb{P}P-multicategories as the slice DP/P\cal DP/\mathbb{P}DP/P of the category of double props.
For instance, we can enhance Pb\mathbb{P}\rm{b}Pb by totally ordering each fiber of its loose arrows to obtain the prop Tot\mathbb{T}\rm{ot}Tot for plain multicategories.
The morphism of props Tot→Pb\mathbb{T}\rm{ot} \to \mathbb{P}\rm{b}Tot→Pb, which is itself a symmetric multicategory, induces then a functor L:Mlt→sMltL: \bf Mlt \to \bf sMltL:Mlt→sMlt between the corresponding slices and the adjunction L⊣R:sMlt→MltL\dashv R: \bf sMlt \to \bf MltL⊣R:sMlt→Mlt arises as an instance (when B=DP\cal B = \cal DPB=DP) of the general adjunction between the fibers in the codomain bifibration on a category with pullbacks B\cal BB.

We present other instances and show how several concepts and properties find a natural setting in this framework.

We also consider cartesian multicategories as the algebras for a monad (−)cart(-)^{\rm cart}(−)cart on sMlt\bf sMltsMlt,
where the loose arrows of Mcart\mathbb{M}^{\rm cart}Mcart are "spans" formed by a tight and a loose arrow in M\mathbb{M}M.
