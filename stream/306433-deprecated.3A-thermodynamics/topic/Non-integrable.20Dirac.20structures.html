John Baez (Feb 15 2022 at 21:16):

That's interesting. "Non-holonomic constraint" means precisely that some integrability conditions fail: you can move along a long path obeying the constraints to get from a state xxx to some arbitrarily nearby state x′x'x′ that you can't get to using a short path that obeys the constraints. So, this makes sense.
