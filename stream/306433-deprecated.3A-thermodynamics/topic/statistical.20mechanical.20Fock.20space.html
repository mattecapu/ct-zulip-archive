But you are right, there should be a construction like this.

If you think of Fock space as just a vector space and forget the Hilbert structure for a minute, the Fock space on a vector space VVV is just the free commutative monoid object on VVV:

∑n∈NV⊗nSn \displaystyle{ \sum_{n \in \mathbb{N}} \frac{V^{\otimes n}}{S_n} } n∈N∑​Sn​V⊗n​

where the symmetric group SnS_nSn​ acts on the n-fold tensor product V⊗nV^{\otimes n}V⊗n by permuting factors.

Similarly given a convex space XXX we should have a free commutative monoid on XXX in the category of convex spaces, given by

∑n∈NXnSn \displaystyle{ \sum_{n \in \mathbb{N}} \frac{X^n}{S_n} } n∈N∑​Sn​Xn​

By the way, this construction is quite general: whenever you have a symmetric monoidal category with countable colimits, and the tensor product distributes over those colimits, the free commutative monoid on an object xxx is
