Owen and I are thinking of Dirac manifolds (= manifolds with Dirac structure) as a simultaneous generalization of presymplectic and Poisson manifolds. John Baez (Feb 22 2022 at 22:38):

Markus Lohmayer said:

So, do you know what a Lagrangian submanifold in symplectic geometry means in terms of a physical example?

Yes. Most importantly for us perhaps, given symplectic manifolds XXX and YYY thought of as phase spaces, a Lagrangian submanifold of X‾×Y\overline{X} \times YX×Y describes a physically realistic relation between states of XXX and states of YYY. Here X‾\overline{X}X is XXX with its symplectic structure multiplied by −1-1−1.

For example XXX could be the space of position-momentum pairs of a particle at time 0, and YYY could be the space of position-momentum pairs of a particle at time 1. Then time evolution via a Hamiltonian gives a relation between points in XXX and points in YYY, and this relation is a Lagrangian submanifold L⊆X‾×YL \subseteq \overline{X} \times YL⊆X×Y.

John Baez (Feb 22 2022 at 22:40):

Another example is studied a lot in

  • Baez and Fong, A compositional framework for passive linear networks.

where we show a large class of electrical circuits give rise to a Lagrangian relation between voltage-current pairs on the input wires and voltage-current pairs on the output wires.
