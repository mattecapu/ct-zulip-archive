Topic: Semi-direct monoidal product on Set²

Vincent Moreau (Feb 08 2024 at 09:06):

Dear category theorists,

In the last few days, I found a cute construction. The category Set2\mathbf{Set}^2Set2 can be endowed with a monoidal structure given by the set-level semi-direct product

(Q,A)⋊(R,B)=(Q+(A×R),A×B)(Q, A) \rtimes (R, B) = (Q + (A \times R), A \times B)(Q,A)⋊(R,B)=(Q+(A×R),A×B)

whose unit is the object (0,1)(0, 1)(0,1). Then, if I am not mistaken, the category of monoids in this monoidal category Set2\mathbf{Set}^2Set2 is isomorphic to the category of pairs (Q,M)(Q, M)(Q,M) where MMM is a (usual) monoid with a left action on the set QQQ.

Is this tensor product over Set2\mathbf{Set}^2Set2 already known, and if yes, what about the identification of its monoids?

In the extended version of "Differential 2-rigs", which unfortunately does not appear anywhere yet, @Todd Trimble and I give the following definition:

fosco (Feb 09 2024 at 09:36):

Let M\mathcal{M}M be an A\mathcal{A}A-bimodule (i.e. an actegory for A\mathcal{A}A, with actions on both sides); one can define the square-zero extension of M\mathcal{M}M to be the 2-rig A⋉M\mathcal{A}\ltimes \mathcal{M}A⋉M whose underlying category is A×M\mathcal A \times \mathcal MA×M and a 2-rig structure determined by

  • componentwise coproducts (i.e. the sum (A,M)∪(B,N)(A,M)\cup (B,N)(A,M)∪(B,N) is defined as (A∪B,M∪N)(A\cup B, M\cup N)(A∪B,M∪N) for A,B∈AA,B\in\mathcal{A}A,B∈A and M,N∈MM,N\in\mathcal{M}M,N∈M)

  • multiplication defined as follows using the left and right action:

    (A,M)⊠(B,N):=(A⊗B,A⊲N∪M⊳B).(A,M)\boxtimes(B,N) := (A\otimes B, A\lhd N \cup M\rhd B).(A,M)⊠(B,N):=(A⊗B,A⊲N∪M⊳B).

Thanks, Fosco. No, this is pretty much what I might have said myself. I might have added that maps into this square zero extension correspond to monoidal functors B→A\mathcal{B} \to \mathcal{A}B→A together with a functor D:B→MD: \mathcal{B} \to \mathcal{M}D:B→M carrying a suitable Leibniz constraint D(b⊗b′)≅ϕ(b)D(b′)∪D(b)ϕ(b′)D(b \otimes b') \cong \phi(b) D(b') \cup D(b) \phi(b')D(b⊗b′)≅ϕ(b)D(b′)∪D(b)ϕ(b′) where the right side refers to the actegory action. (There are some ideas in gestation about fruitful ways to extend this further, but this is good for now.)
