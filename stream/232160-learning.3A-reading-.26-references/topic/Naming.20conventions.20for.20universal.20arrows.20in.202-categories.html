Topic: Naming conventions for universal arrows in 2-categories

Patrick Nicodemus (Apr 16 2025 at 22:26):

Let G:D→CG : D\to CG:D→C be a functor between categories. Let ccc be an object in CCC. The notion of a "universal arrow" (d,η:c→Gd)(d, \eta : c \to G d)(d,η:c→Gd) from ccc into DDD is standard.

Is there a special term for the case where we require that G(d)=cG(d)=cG(d)=c and η\etaη is the identity homomorphism? Such a thing would be useful in the case that we view DDD as fibered over CCC by GGG, and are looking for ddd "over" ccc.

If C,DC, DC,D are 2-categories or bicategories, one can consider other notions of universal arrow from ccc into GGG:

  • (d,η:c→Gd)(d, \eta : c \to G d)(d,η:c→Gd) such that for every (d′,f:c→Gd′)(d', f : c\to G d')(d′,f:c→Gd′) there is a pair (s:d→d′,α:f⇒G(s)∘η)(s : d\to d', \alpha : f\Rightarrow G(s)\circ \eta)(s:d→d′,α:f⇒G(s)∘η) which is initial among such pairs (we could call this a "lax universal arrow" as it seems to correspond to this https://ncatlab.org/nlab/show/lax+2-adjunction)
  • (d,η:c→Gd)(d, \eta : c \to G d)(d,η:c→Gd) such that for every (d′,f:c→Gd′)(d', f : c\to G d')(d′,f:c→Gd′) there is an essentially unique pair (s:d→d′,α:f⇒G(s)∘η)(s : d\to d', \alpha : f\Rightarrow G(s)\circ \eta)(s:d→d′,α:f⇒G(s)∘η) up to isomorphism (would seem to correspond to a biadjunction)
    What do we call these notions of universal arrow?
