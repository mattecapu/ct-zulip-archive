⊤M=⊤∘ !:M→Ω\top_M=\top\circ\,!:M\to\Omega⊤M​=⊤∘!:M→Ω is pre-composition with the unique terminal map !:M→1!:M\to 1!:M→1.

Then you can rewrite congruence in an appealing way. Here   ⟹  α\overset{\alpha}{\implies}⟹α​ is a not-necessarily invertible 2-morphism (which acts as ⋀\bigwedge⋀ on the codomain of all its argument morphisms), Δ\DeltaΔ doubles the argument, and (12),(23)(12),(23)(12),(23) are transpositions of arguments:

  • transitive ∧∘(∼×∼)∘(idM×Δ×idM)  ⟹  α∧∘ (∼×⊤M)∘(23)\land\circ(\sim\times\sim)\circ(id_M\times\Delta\times id_M)\overset{\alpha}{\implies}\land\circ\,(\sim\times\top_M)\circ(23)∧∘(∼×∼)∘(idM​×Δ×idM​)⟹α​∧∘(∼×⊤M​)∘(23)

Eric M Downes (May 29 2024 at 06:41):

During our last session, IIRC Morgan wanted to know more about example 4.9.10, which I happen to have experience with. I figure I'd summarize a few facts about it and Morgan can challenge anything he wants more detail on or doesn't believe. I'l close by rambling incoherently, as I am wont to do.

We have a monoid μ:MM→M\mu:MM\to Mμ:MM→M,
-- so its direct image μ⊢:2M2M→2M\mu^\vdash:2^M2^M\to2^Mμ⊢:2M2M→2M is also a monoid.
-- this makes 2M2^M2M into a residuated lattice; μ\muμ distributes over ∩,∪\cap,\cup∩,∪.

... acting on AAA via a curried action α:M→End(A)\alpha:M\to End(A)α:M→End(A).

  • so 2End(A)2^{End(A)}2End(A) is a residuated lattice using function composition as the product.
  • with a closure operator on both ends taking a subset to the monoid it generates
  • all magma-actions** can be realized as maps a:X→AAa: X\to A^Aa:X→AA from possibly not-moore-closed X⊆MX\subseteq MX⊆M.
  • the moore closure on both sides then gives the enveloping monoid action (adjoint monoid in Lie Algebra); the smallest associative monoid with the magma as a retract.

** -- that is, not necessarily associative maps XA→AXA\to AXA→A.

Now time for the crazy-person ranting you have all come to expect from my communications.

The general functor-category in which the magma action lives in that last point, expressing its closure, would be [discrete(X),X∗]⨾[X∗,cl∘a⊢(X)]\sf [discrete(X), X^*] ⨾ [X^*, cl\circ a^\vdash(X)][discrete(X),X∗]⨾[X∗,cl∘a⊢(X)], where the final target category is the enveloping monoid, and the intermediate monoid is the Kleene-star, and the second functor is the unique monoid congruence. The discrete category needs in the general case, at least one non-identity morphism per generator. The composition data is then specified in the fibration.

This is sort of formal and non-constructive in the general case. In contrast, the powerset picture is constructive, and for magmas of order ≲\lesssim≲ 1000, even computable on a decade-old computer. :)

If you have a magma with more structure, you can be less abstract and have the source morphisms carry more of the weight; I think for instance discrete(O)discrete(\mathbb{O})discrete(O) should be the discrete groupoid containing three copies of the quaternions H\mathbb{H}H.

But even in the non-constructive case, this also suggests interesting intermediate concepts of closures for magmas. Like, maybe you don't immediately project down every object to a single object... you could have a whole series of intermediate steps to categories with fewer and fewer objects. I believe this series of fibrations just recapitulates the ascending chains of the submonoid lattice that contain the magma. But where Morgan might be able to see if this is generally true is working with functors between topoi, so instead of a residuated powerset lattice, we have presheaves to the subobject classifier.
