Topic: Bicategory(/double category) of spans with lax 2-cells

Harrison Grodin (Jan 02 2025 at 23:31):

For a category C\mathcal{C}C, the bicategory of spans has

  • objects from C\mathcal{C}C,
  • morphisms X→YX \to YX→Y are spans X←fZ→gYX \xleftarrow{f} Z \xrightarrow{g} YXf​Zg​Y
  • a 2-cell from X←fZ→gYX \xleftarrow{f} Z \xrightarrow{g} YXf​Zg​Y to X←f′Z′→g′YX \xleftarrow{f'} Z' \xrightarrow{g'} YXf′​Z′g′​Y is a map Z→hZ′Z \xrightarrow{h} Z'Zh​Z′ such that f=h;f′f = h \mathbin{;} f'f=h;f′ and g=h;g′g = h \mathbin{;} g'g=h;g′

Replacing category C\mathcal{C}C with a bicategory B\mathcal{B}B, we can get a similar construction with "(co?)lax" 2-cells:

  • a 2-cell is a map Z→hZ′Z \xrightarrow{h} Z'Zh​Z′ equipped with B\mathcal{B}B-2-cells f⇒h;f′f \Rightarrow h \mathbin{;} f'f⇒h;f′ and g⇒h;g′g \Rightarrow h \mathbin{;} g'g⇒h;g′

(An analogous story should work for double categories, too.)

This seems like a rather natural construction (and I have a use case for it!), but I haven't been able to find a reference. (I expected to see it near discussions of spans and bi/double categories, but nothing so far afaict.) Does anyone know where I might look?
