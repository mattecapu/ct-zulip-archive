Topic: A simple adjunction in probability theory

Let F:R∗→[0,1]F:\mathbb{R}^*\to [0,1]F:R∗→[0,1] be a weakly monotonic, right-continuous function from the extended reals to the unit interval, satisfying F(−∞)=0F(-\infty)=0F(−∞)=0. In other words, FFF is a limit-preserving functor. By the adjoint functor theorem for preorders, FFF has a right adjoint X:[0,1]→R∗X:[0,1]\to\mathbb{R}^*X:[0,1]→R∗.
