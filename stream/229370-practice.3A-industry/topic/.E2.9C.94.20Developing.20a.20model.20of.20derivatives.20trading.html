Topic: ✔ Developing a model of derivatives trading

Julius Hamilton (Jul 01 2024 at 15:22):

This is kind of an experiment in summarizing the content of the conversation and then if there are further questions they can be opened in new threads.

  • I wanted to make a categorical model of momentum trading in order to design a momentum trading algorithm for cryptocurrency trading
  • I personally decided that going forward, when making ologs, I will always consider the objects to be “sets of real-world things”, until I think of a clear alternative
  • People suggested I read papers on the axiomitization of blockchains and the formalization of ledgers
  • Patterson’s article on relational ologs seems really elucidating
  • ologs can be the domains of functors, where the text labels are the domain, being mapped to mathematical structures
  • ologs can be thought of as the domain being text labels and the codomain being data points, like the name of a column in a database.
  • I think a table in a database can be understood as a collection of morphisms to an object (a cospan) because tables are like functions, the headers are indices which return a value
  • I think Remy says that seeing an olog as a functor from text labels to mathematical structures is analogous to seeing it as a mapping from a language to a structure in model theory
  • I think Remy says if my model includes power sets, I should use a category of monoids. In this model, I think the objects are not just sets of things, but monoids of things, where I think the monoid operation tends to be the disjoint union.
  • I think Remy says that to add time as a factor, I could have a functor from a category of time intervals to ICM, which I think would serve to index everything temporally
  • Remy said to design ologs so that they can decompose information
  • I have some questions about choices for modeling “bundles of things” that I want to open a new thread about

Thank you
