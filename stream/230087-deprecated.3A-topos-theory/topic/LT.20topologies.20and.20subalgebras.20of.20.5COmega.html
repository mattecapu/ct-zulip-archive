Topic: LT topologies and subalgebras of \Omega

Matteo Capucci (he/him) (Jan 19 2021 at 10:30):

Slightly dumb question: are Lawvere-Tierney topologies on a topos E\mathcal EE in 1-to-1 correspondence with complete Heyting sub-algebras of Ω\OmegaΩ?
The idea is that given j:Ω→Ωj: \Omega \to \Omegaj:Ω→Ω, one gets a complete Heyting subalgebra Ωj\Omega_jΩj​ of Ω\OmegaΩ, i.e. the object of jjj-closed truth values. On the other hand, given ι:Ω′↪Ω\iota: \Omega' \hookrightarrow \Omegaι:Ω′↪Ω complete Heyting subalgebra it seems this has a right adjoint κ\kappaκ which post-composed with the inclusion yields a map j:=ικ:Ω→Ωj:=\iota\kappa:\Omega \to \Omegaj:=ικ:Ω→Ω with the following properties:

  1. j(⊤)=⊤j(\top) =\topj(⊤)=⊤ since right adjoints preserve limits and ι\iotaι is a morphism of complete Heyting algebras,
  2. jj=jjj = jjj=j because jjj is extensive (by (1)) and a comonad,
  3. jjj is order preserving since both ι\iotaι and κ\kappaκ are (or, alternatively: jjj is left exact since both ι\iotaι and κ\kappaκ are).

Fawzi Hreiki (Jan 19 2021 at 12:13):

Invoking completeness somehow feels wrong because it's not a first-order property. A better approach (imo) is just to talk about co-reflective subalgebras, in which case its obviously true since every co-localisation induces an idempotent co-monad and vice versa.

Fawzi Hreiki (Jan 19 2021 at 12:58):

Apart from some axiom of choice stuff, categories internal to toposes are not so different from small categories. In this case in particular, using internal categories seems totally natural since there is a correspondence between sites in E\mathscr{E}E and internal categories in E\mathscr{E}E equipped with modalities on their presheaf category.
