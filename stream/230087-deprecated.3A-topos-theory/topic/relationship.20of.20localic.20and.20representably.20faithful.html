What does "representably faithful" mean?

My first guess was: a geometric morphism X→YX \to YX→Y is representably faithful if Geom(A,X)→Geom(A,Y)Geom(A, X) \to Geom(A,Y)Geom(A,X)→Geom(A,Y) is faithful for every topos AAA, where Geom(A,Z)Geom(A,Z)Geom(A,Z) is the category of geometric morphisms from A→ZA \to ZA→Z, where a morphism is a natural transformation between the left adjoints. But then if XXX is a localic topos, and Y=SetY = SetY=Set is sheaves on a point, then taking A=SetA = SetA=Set as well, we see that for the localic morphism Y→XY \to XY→X to be representably faithful, the specialization order on YYY must be codiscrete. So for lots of YYY this fails.

My next guess was: a geometric morphism X→YX \to YX→Y is representably faithful if Geom(Y,A)→Geom(X,A)Geom(Y,A) \to Geom(X,A)Geom(Y,A)→Geom(X,A) is faithful for every topos AAA. Now taking A=Fun(FinSet,Set)A = Fun(FinSet, Set)A=Fun(FinSet,Set) to be the object classifier, we see this condition implies that the left adjoint to X→YX \to YX→Y must be faithful. Now, a left or right exact functor between complete / cocomplete categories is faithful if and only if it is conservative, so we see that the left adjoint to X→YX \to YX→Y must be conservative, i.e. X→YX \to YX→Y must be a surjective geometric morphism. So if X→YX \to YX→Y is a geometric morphism between localic toposes which is not surjective, it is not representably faithful in this sense.

Do you know whether every representably fully faithful morphism is an inclusion?

If this is the case, then we can maybe prove that representably faithful implies localic as follows.
Take a geometric morphism f:F→Ef : \mathcal{F} \to \mathcal{E}f:F→E. We already know that Δf\Delta_fΔf​ is localic (Elephant B3.3.8(i)), in particular representably faithful. It remains to show that Δf\Delta_fΔf​ is representably full. Morgan Rogers (he/him) (Apr 21 2022 at 15:14):

In case it helps to indicate why finding counter-examples is hard, the equivalence of localic and representably faithful does hold for the global sections morphism of presheaf toposes, since being representably faithful means that for any theory T\mathbb{T}T classified by such a topos, there is at most one morphism between any pair of models in any topos. In particular, since we can recover the category over which we're taking presheaves up to idempotent completion as the category of finitely presentable models in Set\mathrm{Set}Set, we conclude that the topos is equivalent to the category of presheaves on a preorder, which is necessarily localic.

The classifying topos perspective is interesting here. For a theory to be classified by a topos which is representably faithful over Set, we in particular need rigidity, which is to say that the only endomorphism of any model is the identity. I don't know of a systematic way to produce examples of such theories, but maybe a logician can chime in ;)
