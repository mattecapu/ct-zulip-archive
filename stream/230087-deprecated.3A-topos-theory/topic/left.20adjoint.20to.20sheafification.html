I think this is the key to explicitly write down the left adjoint to sheafification.

Suppose that the complete atomic boolean algebra BBB is given as B=P(S)B = \mathcal{P}(S)B=P(S). Note that PSh(S)≃Sh(B)\mathbf{PSh}(S) \simeq \mathbf{Sh}(B)PSh(S)≃Sh(B). Now consider the presheaf on SSS which is a singleton over some element a∈Sa \in Sa∈S and the empty set elsewhere. Then the corresponding sheaf on BBB is the representable presheaf y{a}\mathbf{y}\{a\}y{a} where y\mathbf{y}y denotes the Yoneda embedding.

I'll write i∗i^*i∗ for the sheafification and i!i_!i!​ for its left adjoint. To compute i!i_!i!​ it is enough to compute it on the sheaves of the form y{a}\mathbf{y}\{a\}y{a}, because all others are colimits (even coproducts) of these. Now:
Hom(i!(y{a}),F)≃Hom(y{a},i∗F)≃(i∗F)({a})\mathrm{Hom}(i_!(\mathbf{y}\{a\}), \mathcal{F}) \simeq \mathrm{Hom}(\mathbf{y}\{a\}, i^*\mathcal{F}) \simeq (i^*\mathcal{F})(\{a\})Hom(i!​(y{a}),F)≃Hom(y{a},i∗F)≃(i∗F)({a})
Further, as @sarahzrf said, we have (i∗F)({a})=F({a})(i^*\mathcal{F})(\{a\}) = \mathcal{F}(\{a\})(i∗F)({a})=F({a}).
It follows that i!(y{a})=y{a}i_!(\mathbf{y}\{a\}) = \mathbf{y}\{a\}i!​(y{a})=y{a}.

Jens Hemelaer (Jan 08 2021 at 09:53):

I think the problem here is that the coproduct of two sheaves is not computed by taking the coproduct of underlying presheaves.

Take the natural inclusion of presheaves j:y{a}⊔y{b}→y{a,b}j : \mathbf{y}\{a\} \sqcup \mathbf{y}\{b\} \to \mathbf{y}\{a,b\}j:y{a}⊔y{b}→y{a,b}. This is not a bijection, because the domain has no sections over {a,b}\{a,b\}{a,b} and the codomain does. But after sheafification, this map becomes an isomorphism of sheaves. To see this, it is enough to show that it becomes a bijection at each stalk (because the topos has enough points). Each stalk is given by taking the sections over {c}\{c\}{c} for some element c∈Sc \in Sc∈S. The map jjj is given as follows on each stalk:

  • If c∉{a,b}c \notin \{a,b\}c∈/{a,b}, then both stalks are empty.
  • If c=ac = ac=a or c=bc = bc=b, then both stalks are a singleton and jjj restricts to a bijection.

In the same way, you can show that every sheaf on BBB is a coproduct of sheaves of the form y{x}\mathbf{y}\{x\}y{x} (in the category of sheaves, not in the category of presheaves). But there is an easier proof using Sh(B)≃PSh(S)\mathbf{Sh}(B) \simeq \mathbf{PSh}(S)Sh(B)≃PSh(S). First prove that every presheaf in PSh(S)\mathbf{PSh}(S)PSh(S) is a coproduct of representable presheaves. Then show that the representable presheaves in PSh(S)\mathbf{PSh}(S)PSh(S) correspond to the sheaves of the form y{x}\mathbf{y}\{x\}y{x} in Sh(B)\mathbf{Sh}(B)Sh(B).
