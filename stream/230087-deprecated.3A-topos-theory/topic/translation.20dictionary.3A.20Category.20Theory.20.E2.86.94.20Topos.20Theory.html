Mike Shulman (Mar 16 2022 at 21:03):

Let's see. Both kinds of 2-categorical duality reverse the handedness of internal adjoints, i.e. a left adjoint in CCC is a right adjoint in CopC^{op}Cop and CcoC^{co}Cco, and a left adjoint in CcoopC^{coop}Ccoop. Thus, if K→MK\to MK→M is a proarrow equipment, then Kop→MopK^{op} \to M^{op}Kop→Mop and Kco→McoK^{co} \to M^{co}Kco→Mco are not, but Kcoop→McoopK^{coop} \to M^{coop}Kcoop→Mcoop is.

On the other hand, if (−)∗:K→M(-)_* : K\to M(−)∗​:K→M is an equipment, i.e. a bo+lff functor such that each f∗f_*f∗​ has a right adjoint f∗f^*f∗, then since adjoints point in the opposite direction and passage to adjoints reverses 2-cells, we also have a functor (−)∗:K→Mcoop(-)^* : K\to M^{coop}(−)∗:K→Mcoop. By the above, each f∗f^*f∗ is still a right adjoint in McoopM^{coop}Mcoop, so this is not an equipment. But applying one more duality of either sort fixes that, so (−)∗:Kop→Mco(-)^* : K^{op} \to M^{co}(−)∗:Kop→Mco and (−)∗:Kco→Mop(-)^* : K^{co} \to M^{op}(−)∗:Kco→Mop are both equipments (and, of course, one is the coop of the other).

In other words, the Klein 4-group acts on equipments, but the action isn't simply lifted from the action on 2-categories: it acts differently on KKK and MMM.

Now there is a "tautological" equipment Adj→Cat\rm Adj \to CatAdj→Cat, where Adj\rm AdjAdj is the 2-category of categories and adjunctions pointing in the direction of their left adjoints. Thus, we also have equipments Adjcoop→Catcoop\rm Adj^{coop} \to Cat^{coop}Adjcoop→Catcoop, Adjop→Catco\rm Adj^{op} \to Cat^{co}Adjop→Catco, and Adjco→Catop\rm Adj^{co} \to Cat^{op}Adjco→Catop, where the first two send an adjunction to its left adjoint, and the second two send an adjunction to its right adjoint.

It sounds like in this conversation we're defining Top\rm TopTop as a subcategory of AdjopAdj^{op}Adjop, with geometric morphisms pointing in the direction of their right adjoint, but with 2-cells defined as going between their left adjoints. Thus, we have equipments Topop→TopLex\rm Top^{op} \to TopLexTopop→TopLex, Topco→TopLexcoop\rm Top^{co} \to TopLex^{coop}Topco→TopLexcoop, Top→TopLexco\rm Top \to TopLex^{co}Top→TopLexco, and Topcoop→Catop\rm Top^{coop} \to Cat^{op}Topcoop→Catop. Since they constitute an orbit of the Klein-4 action, they all carry the same information.
