David Michael Roberts (Jan 17 2021 at 09:33):

There are a bunch! Here are some that in some cases are only equivalent to the usual open cover topology for a subcategory TTT of TopTopTop. Here "cover" means a collection of subsets whose union is the whole space

  1. Numerable open covers (TTT = paracompact spaces)
  2. Finite open covers (TTT = compact spaces)
  3. Countable open covers (TTT = Lindelöf spaces)
  4. Something about point-finite open covers (chosen so that TTT=normal spaces)
  5. Covers by regular closed sets (=closures of their interiors) such that their interiors form a cover (T=?T=?T=?)
  6. Covers by arbitrary subsets whose interiors form a cover (T=?T=?T=?)

We can give up equivalence to the usual topology at all, and ask for the extensive topology, where a covering family consists of a bunch of disjoint clopen subsets that cover.

We can drop the condition that we are using subsets and consider

  1. Jointly surjective open maps
  2. Jointly surjective universally closed maps

Then there are things like the topology generated by the extensive topology and the biquotient maps, and the same with biquotient replaced by triquotient.

Extra pullback-stable conditions can be added like separatedness (https://stacks.math.columbia.edu/tag/0CY0), which is probably superfluous when dealing with TTT = Hausdorff spaces.

And so on.

Jens Hemelaer (Jan 17 2021 at 12:20):

If you take as covering sieves on Top\mathsf{Top}Top the sieves that contain a finite jointly surjective family, then you get condensed/pyknotic sets.
This is the same thing as presheaves on C\mathcal{C}C that send finite coproducts to finite products, where C\mathcal{C}C is the full subcategory of Top\mathsf{Top}Top consisting of those spaces βS\beta SβS that are Stone–Čech compactifications of sets SSS.
(Here the explanation by Clausen).

If I understand correctly, this gives another item on @David Michael Roberts's list above.

  1. Finite disjoint open covers (TTT consists of the spaces βS\beta SβS or more generally Stonean spaces).

Fawzi Hreiki (Jan 17 2021 at 13:26):

@John Baez I think the idea is that while presheaves in general have the ‘wrong’ colimits (geometrically speaking), product preserving presheaves on an extensive category (ie sheaves with respect to the extensive topology) will at least have the ‘correct’ sums.

John Baez (Jan 17 2021 at 16:45):

Hmm, I have no intuition for this, so I don't know what the 'wrong' or 'correct' sums are.

Fawzi Hreiki (Jan 17 2021 at 16:59):

Well 'correct' and 'wrong' in the sense that the Yoneda embedding preserves limits but not colimits (which, when dealing with sites consisting of spaces, often express some sort of geometric content). Extensive categories have fairly 'geometric' coproducts (a bundle over a sum E→A+BE \rightarrow A + BE→A+B decomposes nicely into a pair of bundles over the summands E1→AE_1 \rightarrow AE1​→A and E2→BE_2 \rightarrow BE2​→B). The extensive topology basically preserves (at least) this geometry.
