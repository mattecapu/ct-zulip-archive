Topic: Every elementary topos concretizable?

Jens Hemelaer (Nov 02 2020 at 09:16):

Does every elementary topos admit a faithful functor to the category of sets?

This is the case for every Grothendieck topos, and also for the few examples of non-Grothendieck toposes that I know (like the topos of finite sets or the topos of MMM-sets where MMM is a "large" category with one object).

Maybe there are even elementary toposes that are not locally small?

Reid Barton (Nov 02 2020 at 15:09):

I'm not sure whether the product of a class-sized family of copies of Set really makes sense at all in ZFC, but it's definitely not locally small, as Morgan said. That's why I suggested working with small (pre)sheaves, which we can regard as formal small colimits of representables. They form an ordinary, class-sized locally small category which is however not locally presentable.

Reid Barton (Nov 02 2020 at 15:11):

I'm not sure exactly when the category of small presheaves on CCC is an elementary a topos. At a minimum it has to have finite limits, which is a nontrivial condition on CCC. For example if CCC is a class-sized discrete category then the terminal object of the presheaf category is not a small presheaf.

Reid Barton (Nov 02 2020 at 15:15):

I think the condition on CCC is that every finite diagram has a set of weakly universal cones (i.e., a set of cones such that every cone factors through one of them in at least one way). For example, CCC could itself have finite limits.

Reid Barton (Nov 02 2020 at 15:16):

The rest of the Giraud axioms seem fine (the category of presheaves on CCC in a bigger universe is definitely a topos, and things like small coproducts and coequalizers and pullbacks don't leave the full subcategory of small presheaves) so I guess the above condition on CCC is enough for the presheaf category to be an elementary topos.

Reid Barton (Nov 02 2020 at 15:22):

I also don't know when exactly the small sheaves for a topology on such CCC form what we could call a "Giraud topos" (= Giraud axioms minus a set of generators), but I understand that it's supposed to be true for condensed sets, for example.

David Michael Roberts (Nov 03 2020 at 04:56):

A Giraud topos is a cocomplete locally small pretopos, no? This is rather easy to arrange, and we can do better. Say Rop→ToposbR^{op} \to Topos_bRop→Toposb​ is a cofiltered diagram with RRR well-founded (maybe we don't need this bit). Then the colimit of R→Toposbop→LEXcocontR\to Topos_b^{op} \to LEX_{cocont}R→Toposbop​→LEXcocont​ (lextensive categories with finite-limit- and colimit-preserving functors) is a cocomplete Heyting pretopos with a subobject classifier and finitary W-types. It might not be locally small, but if the diagram of inverse image functors is eventually full it will be. Further, if powerobjects are eventually constant, it will be an unbounded Set-topos.

David Michael Roberts (Nov 03 2020 at 04:57):

I believe if the diagram arises from a diagram of (small) sites, then the colimit looks something like small sheaves on a large site. Compare with Moerdijk's _Continuous fibrations and inverse limits of toposes_ http://www.numdam.org/item/CM_1986__58_1_45_0/
