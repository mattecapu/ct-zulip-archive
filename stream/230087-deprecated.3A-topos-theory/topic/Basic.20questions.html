Jens Hemelaer (Nov 24 2020 at 09:49):

The corresponding closure operator on Sub(1)\mathrm{Sub}(1)Sub(1) is called a nucleus. For localic Grothendieck toposes, Lawvere–Tierney topologies (equivalently, Grothendieck topologies on Dop\mathbf{D}^\mathrm{op}Dop) correspond bijectively to nuclei on the frame Sub(1)\mathrm{Sub}(1)Sub(1). This is also the case here, because SetD\mathbf{Set}^\mathbf{D}SetD is a localic topos for any poset.

Jens Hemelaer (Nov 24 2020 at 09:57):

In the case where D\mathbf{D}D is a finite poset, these correspond precisely to the subsets of D\mathbf{D}D.

In "Grothendieck topologies on a poset" by @Bert Lindenhovius it is shown that more generally the Grothendieck topologies on any Artinian poset P\mathbf{P}P correspond to the subsets of P\mathbf{P}P.

Jens Hemelaer (Nov 24 2020 at 10:03):

As a reference:
In Maclane–Moerdijk, "Sheaves in Geometry and Logic", IX.5, Corollary 6, it is shown that nuclei on a locale XXX correspond to Lawvere–Tierney topologies on the topos Sh(X)\mathbf{Sh}(X)Sh(X).

For localic toposes, it's more or less the same thing.
