Ralph Sarkis (May 25 2023 at 17:38):

I am unrolling all symbols one by one to get something that I find as simple as I want. We start with the statement
5∉{x∈R∣13−2x>c}5 \not\in \{x \in \R | 13 - 2x > c\}5∈{x∈R∣13−2x>c}
This is the same thing as (by looking at the definition of ∉\not\in∈:
NOT(5∈{x∈R∣13−2x>c})\mathsf{NOT} \left(5 \in \{x \in \R | 13 - 2x > c\}\right)NOT(5∈{x∈R∣13−2x>c})
I can simplify what is inside the NOT by replacing xxx by 555 in the right part (because that is what set-builder notation means):
NOT(13−2⋅5>c)\mathsf{NOT} \left( 13 - 2\cdot 5 > c\right)NOT(13−2⋅5>c)
A bit of algebra gives me:
NOT(3>c)\mathsf{NOT} \left( 3 > c\right)NOT(3>c)
Finally, I put the NOT back insider: (EDIT: I fixed my arithmetic mistake noted by Jean-Baptiste)
3≤c3 \leq c3≤c
