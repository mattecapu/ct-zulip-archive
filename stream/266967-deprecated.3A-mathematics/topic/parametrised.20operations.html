Topic: parametrised operations

Matteo Capucci (he/him) (Sep 11 2021 at 09:44):

I stumbled upon a structure I'd call a 'parametrised monoidal operation' and I was wondering if someone has ever thought about it before (googling is not satisfying)
In its most simplified form, it's an operation μ:P×Q×Q→Q\mu : P \times Q \times Q \to Qμ:P×Q×Q→Q on a set QQQ, together with a map η:P→Q\eta : P \to Qη:P→Q, that satisfies parametrised associativity and unitality:

μ(p,μ(p,q1,q2),q3)=μ(p,q1,μ(p,q2,q3))μ(p,η(p),q)=μ(p,q,η(p))=q\mu(p, \mu(p, q_1,q_2),q_3) = \mu(p,q_1,\mu(p,q_2,q_3))\\ \mu(p,\eta(p),q)=\mu(p,q,\eta(p))=qμ(p,μ(p,q1​,q2​),q3​)=μ(p,q1​,μ(p,q2​,q3​))μ(p,η(p),q)=μ(p,q,η(p))=q

The more astute among you might have noticed this is secretly assuming a comonoidal structure on PPP, which comes for free in a Cartesian setting. Otherwise, to generalize this structure to an arbitrary monoidal category one has to esplicitly ask for it (but notice: this pair of comonoidal-monoidal structure, even assuming P=QP=QP=Q, is weaker than an Hopf algebra structure, since no compatibility between the operations is asked)
The even more astute among you might have noticed this is a comonoid in lenses, and its monoidal generalization is a comonoid in optics.

Examples of such structures:

  1. Any monoid, with P=1P=1P=1, or simply with μ\muμ ignoring PPP altogether
  2. Any ring, with P=2={+,×}P=2=\{+,×\}P=2={+,×} selecting either of the two monoidal structures and η\etaη mapping each operation to its identity
  3. I suspect that Lie groups might provide interesting examples? I picture a group having a continuous family of group structures indexed by PPP. Something like that is going on in deformation theory but iirc the deformed operations are not associative and unital throughout

John Baez (Sep 11 2021 at 12:41):

In the deformation theory of associative algebras we do ask that the multiplication be associative and unital (and give us an algebra) throughout the deformation process. So there are lots of R\mathbb{R}R-parametrized monoids running around in algebra, e.g. in "deformation quantization" where we deform a commutative product into a noncommutative one.
