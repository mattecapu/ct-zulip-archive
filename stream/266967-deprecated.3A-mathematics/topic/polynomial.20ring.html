Hugo Jenkins said:

  • A[X]⊗AA[X]≅A[X,Y]A[X]\otimes_A A[X]\cong A[X,Y]A[X]⊗A​A[X]≅A[X,Y]

So if CALGA\mathsf{CALG}_ACALGA​ is the category of commutative AAA-algebras. In this category, ⊗A\otimes_A⊗A​ is the coproduct. What this identity is saying is that PP(A)PP(A)PP(A) is the coproduct of P(A)P(A)P(A) and P(A)P(A)P(A) in CALGA\mathsf{CALG}_ACALGA​.
But if you want to stay within CRING\mathsf{CRING}CRING then ⊗A\otimes_A⊗A​ is a coequalizer. for CRing \mathbf{CRing} CRing, ⊗ \otimes ⊗ is the coproduct, and the free ring on a set of elements (the corresponding polynomial ring Z[S] \mathbb{Z}[S] Z[S]) is a left adjoint, so we get coproduct preservation. You can probably derive everything from just this.
