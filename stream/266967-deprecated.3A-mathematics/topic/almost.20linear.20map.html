I think you can equip any ripped monoid with a partial order given by x≥yx\geq yx≥y iff the exists aaa with y+a=xy+a = xy+a=x. Note that if x=y+ax = y+ax=y+a and y=x+by = x+by=x+b then we can evaluate x+a+b+a+b+…x + a + b + a + b+\dotsx+a+b+a+b+… in two ways to prove x=yx=yx=y. So this ordering doesn't make anything equivalent that wasn't already equal.

Then instead of 'almost linear' we can say 'sublinear'.

John Baez (Apr 14 2022 at 15:28):

I think there's a significant difference between an algebraic structure with operations of arity α\alphaα for all cardinals less than some cardinal κ\kappaκ, and algebraic structures with operations of every arity. The former can be handled by an bounded [[infinitary Lawvere theory]].
