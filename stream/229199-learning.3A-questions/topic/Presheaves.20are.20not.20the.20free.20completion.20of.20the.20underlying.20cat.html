Topic: Presheaves are not the free completion of the underlying cat

Peva Blanchard (Jun 24 2024 at 12:05):

Given a category CCC, it is known that the category of presheaves Psh(C)=[Cop,Set]\text{Psh}(C) = [C^{op}, \text{Set}]Psh(C)=[Cop,Set]

  • is the free co-completion of CCC
  • and is also complete

But, is there a specific example that shows that Psh(C)\text{Psh}(C)Psh(C) is not the free completion of CCC?

John Baez (Jun 24 2024 at 12:19):

C=1C = 1C=1 should do it. If you believe the free cocompletion of the terminal category is Set\mathsf{Set}Set, you should also believe the free completion of the terminal category is Setop\mathsf{Set}^{\rm{op}}Setop, so all you need to show is that Setop\mathsf{Set}^{\rm{op}}Setop is not equivalent to Set\mathsf{Set}Set. For this, you can note that binary products distribute over binary coproducts in Set\mathsf{Set}Set, but not the other way around.

This is a somewhat confusing question because there's another way something can fail to be "free on X": it can have a bunch of extra stuff. And depending on what you're doing, that may be happening here.

We don't get Set\mathsf{Set}Set simply by first taking the free completion of the terminal category and then imposing extra equations (isomorphisms): from this viewpoint it also has a lot of extra objects that didn't arise as limits from the first object you put in, the object coming from the terminal category, which I assume you are calling 111.

But anyway, if we were trying to form the free category with limits on one object xxx, we would not make xxx be terminal, so we would not have xÃ—xâ‰…xx \times x \cong xxÃ—xâ‰…x. So maybe the answer to your question is "yes".
