Topic: Misunderstanding on Invariant Basis Number

Jean-Baptiste Vienney (Jun 14 2024 at 21:55):

The following is driving me absolutely crazy. I'm misunderstanding something but I don't find what it is.

Let RRR be a ring. In these notes (corrected the link), they say that:
(1) if III is a proper ideal of RRR, and if R/IR/IR/I has the invariant basis number (IBN), then RRR also has the IBN,
(2) every division ring has the IBN.

With my understanding, it would follow that every nonzero ring has the IBN (which is false).

Here is my reasoning: Let RRR be a nonzero ring. Let III be a maximal ideal of RRR, which exists by Krull's theorem. Then R/IR/IR/I is a division ring, therefore R/IR/IR/I has the IBN. Hence RRR has the IBN.

Where is the mistake??

Todd Trimble (Jun 14 2024 at 22:05):

It's probably in assuming that a ring modulo a maximal two-sided ideal is a division ring, as opposed to a simple ring.

For example, the maximal two-sided ideal of an n×nn \times nn×n matrix algebra is the zero ideal.

Jean-Baptiste Vienney (Jun 14 2024 at 22:12):

Oh thanks, it should be that. They say here that the quotient of a ring RRR by a two-sided ideal III is a division ring iff III is maximal as a left ideal or as a right ideal.
