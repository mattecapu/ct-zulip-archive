Topic: construction of left adjoint by generators and symm. topos

Jonas Frey (Aug 30 2025 at 18:11):

Is the following principle stated anywhere? Or am I confused?

Let U:B→AU : B \to AU:B→A be a functor between cocomplete categories. An object a∈Aa\in Aa∈A is called UUU-adjointable, if hom⁡(a,U−)\hom(a,U-)hom(a,U−) is representable. Then the class of UUU-adjointable objects is closed under colimits in AAA. In particular, to prove that UUU has a left adjoint, it is sufficient to exhibit "free" objects for a class of objects which generate AAA under colimits.

Proof: If aaa is the colimit of a diagram DDD of UUU-adjointable objects, then hom⁡(a,U−)\hom(a,U-)hom(a,U−) is represented by colim(FD)\mathsf{colim}(FD)colim(FD), where FFF is the partial left adjoint on the category of adjointable objects.

(I'm asking since Johnstone uses something like this to show that the "symmetric monad" exists, and I was wondering why he didn't have to prove well-definedness. But it seems to be a general thing?)

Jonas Frey (Aug 30 2025 at 18:46):

Just for completeness, let me give Johnstone's argument: we want to show that the forgetful 2-functor

U:Logos→PresU:\mathsf{Logos}\to\mathsf{Pres}U:Logos→Pres

has a left biadjoint, where Logos\mathsf{Logos}Logos is the 2-category of Grothendieck toposes and lex cocontinuous functors, ie inverse image parts of geometric morphisms, and Pres\mathsf{Pres}Pres is the 2-category of locally presentable categories and cocontinuous functors. It is easy to see that free logoses over presheaf categories P(C)P(C)P(C) (viewed as objects of Pres\mathsf{Pres}Pres) exist: the free logos on P(C)P(C)P(C) is P(Lex(C))P(\mathsf{Lex}(C))P(Lex(C)) -- the category of presheaves on the free finite-limit category on CCC. The existence of the left biadjoint now follows from the above principle since every presentable category is presentable as a coinverter of a 222-cell between cocontinuous functors between presheaf categories.

Jonas Frey (Aug 30 2025 at 18:49):

Actually the left adjoint to U:Logos→PresU:\mathsf{Logos}\to\mathsf{Pres}U:Logos→Pres is closely related to (almost and instance of) the use of the adjoint lifting theorem discussed here in the context of distributive laws a while ago. So the above principle seems to be a good way to think about the extension of left adjoints from Kleisli categories to categories of algebras in general.

Nathanael Arkor (Aug 31 2025 at 11:01):

In this specific instance, the proof in the bicategorical case really does just proceed by replacing isomorphisms with equivalences everywhere (and it is clear this works, because the proof is given simply by constructing a chain of isomorphisms). So I don't think I would have a problem with authors simply pointing to the one-dimensional argument. On the other hand, I do not find it reasonable for authors simply to refer to a one-dimensional argument that involves equational reasoning (and hence requires dealing with coherence in two dimensions), because these do not extend from one dimension so evidently.
