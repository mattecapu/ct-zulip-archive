Todd Trimble (May 17 2024 at 13:29):

To discuss these things cleanly, it's preferable to work with the "algebraist's simplex category", consisting of finite ordinals (including the empty ordinal) and order-preserving maps between them. I'll denote this as Δa\Delta_aΔa​. It has one more object than the topologist's Δ\DeltaΔ, which omits the empty ordinal. Under ordinal sum, Δa\Delta_aΔa​ is a strict monoidal category whose monoidal unit is the empty ordinal 0\mathbf{0}0. The terminal object 1\mathbf{1}1, the one-element ordinal, is a monoid object, as terminal objects always are. Δa\Delta_aΔa​ considered together with the monoid 1\mathbf{1}1 is sometimes called "the walking monoid": given a (strict) monoidal category M\mathbf{M}M together with a monoid object MMM, there is a unique strict monoidal functor Δa→M\Delta_a \to \mathbf{M}Δa​→M that carries the monoid structure on 1\mathbf{1}1 to the monoid structure on MMM. Similarly, Δaop\Delta_a^{op}Δaop​ is the "walking comonoid".

Todd Trimble (May 17 2024 at 13:30):

If A\mathbf{A}A is a category and CCC is a comonad on A\mathbf{A}A, then we may regard CCC as a comonoid object in the category of endofunctors [A,A][\mathbf{A}, \mathbf{A}][A,A], regarding the latter as a strict monoidal category under endofunctor composition. Since Δaop\Delta_a^{op}Δaop​ is the "walking comonoid", we get a unique monoidal functor

Δop→[A,A]\Delta^{op} \to [\mathbf{A}, \mathbf{A}]Δop→[A,A]

that carries the comonoid 1\mathbf{1}1 to the comonoid CCC. If, further, we have an object AAA of A\mathbf{A}A, then we have a functor evA:[A,A]→A\mathrm{ev}_A: [\mathbf{A}, \mathbf{A}] \to \mathbf{A}evA​:[A,A]→A which evaluates an endofunctor FFF at AAA, i.e., sends FFF to F(A)F(A)F(A). The evident composite

Δaop→[A,A]→evAA\Delta_a^{op} \to [\mathbf{A}, \mathbf{A}] \overset{\mathrm{ev}_A}{\to} \mathbf{A}Δaop​→[A,A]→evA​A

then defines a simplicial object in A\mathbf{A}A, and this is the basic construction we are discussing.

Todd Trimble (May 17 2024 at 13:30):

In simplicial language, it's actually what one calls an "augmented" simplicial object. It's augmented because we have this extra object 0\mathbf{0}0 in Δa\Delta_aΔa​, where the functor Δaop→A\Delta_a^{op} \to \mathbf{A}Δaop​→A takes 0\mathbf{0}0 to AAA, and in general takes the nnn-element ordinal n\mathbf{n}n to Cn(A)C^n(A)Cn(A). We also have this extra map 0→1\mathbf{0} \to \mathbf{1}0→1, and the functor Δaop→A\Delta_a^{op} \to \mathbf{A}Δaop​→A takes this map to an extra face map of the form C(A)→AC(A) \to AC(A)→A, and this is referred to as the augmentation. Sometimes the experts refer to the receiving object AAA of the augmentation as being in (simplicial) degree −1-1−1, so that the face maps in low degree end with the shape

…C2(A)⇉C(A)→A.\ldots C^2(A) \rightrightarrows C(A) \to A.…C2(A)⇉C(A)→A.

Now suppose we have a monad MMM on a category E\mathbf{E}E, and an MMM-algebra AAA. Let A=EM\mathbf{A} = \mathbf{E}^MA=EM be the category of MMM-algebras. There's a forgetful functor U:EM→EU: \mathbf{E}^M \to \mathbf{E}U:EM→E, with a free functor F:E→EMF: \mathbf{E} \to \mathbf{E}^MF:E→EM that is left adjoint to it. Todd Trimble (May 17 2024 at 13:31):

In my idiolect, I make a distinction between this and what I would (nowadays) call the bar resolution on AAA. The bar resolution is the augmented simplicial object in E\mathbf{E}E that we get by appending to this construction the forgetful functor UUU:

Δaop→[EM,EM]→evAEM→UE\Delta_a^{op} \to [\mathbf{E}^M, \mathbf{E}^M] \overset{\mathrm{ev}_A}{\to} \mathbf{E}^M \overset{U}{\to} \mathbf{E}Δaop​→[EM,EM]→evA​EM→UE

and this is what I would denote by B(M,M,A)B(M, M, A)B(M,M,A). The object in simplicial degree n−1n-1n−1 is UCnA=UFU…FUAU C^n A = UFU\ldots FUAUCnA=UFU…FUA, but this is the same as MnUAM^n UAMnUA.

Todd Trimble (May 17 2024 at 13:31):

The reason for further composing with UUU is that it allows us to pick up some extra degeneracy maps that play the role of a contracting homotopy, one that witnesses a homotopy equivalence between this augmented simplicial object, and the constant augmented simplicial object (that is constantly AAA in every simplicial degree). It's this contracting homotopy that I think of as putting the "r" in "resolution". Namely, the contracting homotopy, given by maps

B(M,M,A)n−1→B(M,M,A)n,B(M, M, A)_{n-1} \to B(M, M, A)_n,B(M,M,A)n−1​→B(M,M,A)n​,

is given by components of the unit of the monad MMM:

uMnUA:MnUA→Mn+1UA.u_{M^n UA}: M^n UA \to M^{n+1} UA.uMnUA​:MnUA→Mn+1UA.

Notice that these unit maps live in E\mathbf{E}E, not in EM\mathbf{E}^MEM (unit maps are not algebra maps!). That's why we had to map down to E\mathbf{E}E.

For now, I am going to take a break for a few days to recover and get my thoughts together. But first I wanted to write a summary of all I have learned on this thread:

  • You can construct a simplicial object (a "comonadic simplicial object") from any comonad due to the amazing properties of the opposite augmented simplex category being the "walking comonoid" (and dually for monads and cosimplicial objects)
  • In the very special case that the comonad just so happens to be on an EM category for a monad T, AND that monad T is the "flip" of the comonad when it comes to their composite adjunctions, then the above construction reduces to a "bar construction". This is a very special case because not every comonad arises in this way!
  • A simplicial resolution is when you have an object you can interpret as being the colimit of a simplicial object; together they form an augmented simplicial object
  • A bar construction is a special case of BOTH the comonadic simplicial object AND a simplicial resolution. However, comonadic simplicial objects and simplicial resolutions are NOT special cases of one another (thus warranting a correction to the nlab page for "simplicial resolution" under the Examples section)!
  • A canonical resolution is usually just the same as the bar construction, but in some cases one can find a "more efficient" way to present an object via colimits than an entire simplicial object. So in other words, the coequalizer at the level of algebras is essentially never a split coequalizer, which is what the acyclic structure would give you.
