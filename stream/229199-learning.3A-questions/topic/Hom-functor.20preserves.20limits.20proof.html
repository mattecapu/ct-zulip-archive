However, I'm not entirely convinced this is the result we want to show. The page on preservation of limits emphasises that, for a functor FFF to preserve a limit, it is not enough to show that F(lim⁡iXi)≅lim⁡iF(Xi)F(\lim_i X_i) \cong \lim_i F(X_i)F(limi​Xi​)≅limi​F(Xi​). Instead, the point is to preserve the entire universal cone. Another way to say this is that there is a canonical comparision map F(lim⁡iXi)→lim⁡iF(Xi)F(\lim_i X_i) \to \lim_i F(X_i)F(limi​Xi​)→limi​F(Xi​) obtained by applying FFF to the legs of the limit cone; to preserve the limit, this comparison map must be an isomorphism.

Taking F=HomC(Y,−)F = \text{Hom}_\mathcal{C}(Y, -)F=HomC​(Y,−), shouldn't the proof involve explicitly applying FFF to the legs of the limit cone, and showing that the resulting cone is a limit cone in Set\mathbf{Set}Set? Otherwise it seems like we're proving a weaker version of limit preservation than we actually want.

Here's an example of the phenomenon I mean. In Set\mathbf{Set}Set, we have that N×N≅N\mathbb{N} \times \mathbb{N} \cong \mathbb{N}N×N≅N as sets, by a standard "snaking" argument. Consider the usual limit cone diagram for N×N\mathbb{N} \times \mathbb{N}N×N over two copies of N\mathbb{N}N with the usual cartesian product projections. Then the constant functor ΔN ⁣:Set→Set\Delta_\mathbb{N} \colon \mathbf{Set} \to \mathbf{Set}ΔN​:Set→Set sending every set to N\mathbb{N}N (and every morphism to the identity) preserves this limit at the object level, since ΔN(N×N)=N≅N×N≅ΔN(N)×ΔN(N)\Delta_\mathbb{N}(\mathbb{N} \times \mathbb{N}) = \mathbb{N} \cong \mathbb{N} \times \mathbb{N} \cong \Delta_\mathbb{N}(\mathbb{N}) \times \Delta_\mathbb{N}(\mathbb{N})ΔN​(N×N)=N≅N×N≅ΔN​(N)×ΔN​(N). However, the limit cone with projections π1,π2 ⁣:N×N→N\pi_1, \pi_2 \colon \mathbb{N} \times \mathbb{N} \to \mathbb{N}π1​,π2​:N×N→N gets mapped to an "identity" cone idN,idN ⁣:N→N\text{id}_\mathbb{N}, \text{id}_\mathbb{N} \colon \mathbb{N} \to \mathbb{N}idN​,idN​:N→N. So in general, merely having an isomorphism F(lim⁡iXi)≅lim⁡iF(Xi)F(\lim_i X_i) \cong \lim_i F(X_i)F(limi​Xi​)≅limi​F(Xi​) is not enough to show FFF preserves limits. Does the proof at the nlab page need to be updated, then?

Rémy Tuyéras (Feb 11 2026 at 00:44):

@Ruby Khondaker (she/her) The fact that hom-sets preserve limits does not follow from a proof, but rather directly from the definition of limits. See below.

Definition 1. Let F:I→CF:I \to \mathcal{C}F:I→C be a functor and ΔI\Delta_IΔI​ be the obvious constant functor C→CI\mathcal{C} \to \mathcal{C}^IC→CI. A limit for a functor FFF is a natural transformation ηF:ΔI(L)⇒F\eta_F: \Delta_I(L) \Rightarrow FηF​:ΔI​(L)⇒F such that every natural transformation d:ΔI(X)⇒Fd:\Delta_I(X) \Rightarrow Fd:ΔI​(X)⇒F in C\mathcal{C}C factorizes uniquely as d=ηF∘ΔI(f)d = \eta_F \circ \Delta_I(f)d=ηF​∘ΔI​(f) where f:X→Lf: X \to Lf:X→L is an arrow in C\mathcal{C}C.

Definition 2. Given ηF\eta_FηF​, this is equivalent to requesting that the function C(X,L)→CI(ΔI(X),F)\mathcal{C}(X,L) \to \mathcal{C}^I(\Delta_I(X),F)C(X,L)→CI(ΔI​(X),F) is a bijection.

Remark 1. Now, it is quite straightforward to show that CI(ΔI(X),F)\mathcal{C}^I(\Delta_I(X),F)CI(ΔI​(X),F) is exactly the limit lim CI(X,F(−))\mathsf{lim}\,\mathcal{C}^I(X,F(-))limCI(X,F(−)) in Set\mathbf{Set}Set (hint: just describe the natural transformations as families of compatible arrows)
