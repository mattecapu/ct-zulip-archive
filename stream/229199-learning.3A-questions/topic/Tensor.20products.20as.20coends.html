fosco (Jan 06 2026 at 09:48):

Yes, it is a coend. A high-level explanation is that a module RMS{}_R M_SR​MS​ is a profunctor M:Sop⊗R→AbM : S^{op}\otimes R \to AbM:Sop⊗R→Ab and tensor product with SNT{}_S N_TS​NT​ coincides with composition in the appropriate bi-/double-category

fosco (Jan 06 2026 at 09:51):

If you don't like this explanation, however, there's a more analytic one. The tensor product is a quotient of M⊗ZNM\otimes_{\mathbb Z} NM⊗Z​N (the tensor product as abelian groups, which is a way bigger module) by the relation that balances scalars in SSS imposing (m.s)⊗n=m⊗(s.n)(m.s)\otimes n = m \otimes (s.n)(m.s)⊗n=m⊗(s.n).

fosco (Jan 06 2026 at 09:52):

As such, it's an instance of the functor tensor product https://ncatlab.org/nlab/show/tensor+product+of+functors of this nLab page when C\cal CC has a single object.
