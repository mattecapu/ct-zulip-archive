Mike Shulman (Dec 03 2023 at 19:29):

Another quick comment is that all mathematical structure can be transported across any isomorphism. So if you have an isomorphism X≅YX\cong YX≅Y and XXX has some structure, whether it's a monoid or an abelian group or a topological space or what-have you, then you can transport it across that isomorphism to give YYY the same structure. In particular, therefore, you can do this when XXX and YYY are hom-sets and the isomorphism comes from an adjunction.
