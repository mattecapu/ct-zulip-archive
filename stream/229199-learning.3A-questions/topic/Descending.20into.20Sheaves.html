John Baez (Jan 21 2025 at 17:46):

Let me try to state the general pattern for 'descent' and 'descent data'. It's very general so what I say may sound a bit vague. When you have a map p:Y→Xp: Y \to Xp:Y→X of some sort, you can often pull things over XXX back to get things over YYY.

The first crude descent question is: given a thing over YYY, can you get it by pulling back a thing over XXX?

If you can do it, we say that thing descends to XXX.

A better descent question is: what extra structure must we give an thing over YYY, to specify how it comes from pulling back a particular thing over XXX? (Different things over XXX can give the same thing when you pull them back to YYY.)

The answer to the better descent question is to describe descent data for a thing over YYY, and prove: the (n-)category of things over XXX is equivalent to the (n-)category of things over YYY equipped with descent data.

Example. For a simple example of this pattern, let p:Y→Xp: Y \to Xp:Y→X be an open cover of XXX by contractible open sets

Y=⨆iUiUi open in X Y = \bigsqcup_i U_i \qquad U_i \text{ open in } XY=⨆i​Ui​Ui​ open in X

and say that a thing is a principal GGG-bundle. Then the descent question is: what extra data do we need to give a principal GGG-bundle over YYY (wlog trivial) for it to descend to a principal GGG-bundle over XXX? And the answer is: transition functions

gij:Ui∩Uj→G g_{ij} : U_i \cap U_j \to Ggij​:Ui​∩Uj​→G

obeying the cocycle condition

gijgjk=gik g_{ij} g_{jk} = g_{ik}gij​gjk​=gik​

So in this case descent data consists of transition functions obeying the cocycle condition.

We have already seen, in earlier conversations, how in this example descent data is connected to a certain monad, and to a certain simplicial object. This is typical! Indeed here's a pretty general descent question:

Q: Given a right adjoint functor U:D→CU: \mathsf{D} \to \mathsf{C}U:D→C that is monadic, what data do we need to give an object of c∈Cc \in \mathsf{C}c∈C to specify an object of D\mathsf{D}D that it came from?

And the answer is:

A: ccc must be equipped with a map α:Tc→c\alpha: Tc \to cα:Tc→c making it into an algebra of the monad T=RLT = R LT=RL, where LLL is the left adjoint of RRR.

This is called monadic descent. For more try these:

  • nLab, Monadic descent.
  • MathOverflow, Why are monadicity and descent related?

The first one features the example of principal bundles that I just gave. The second features a classic example from algebra, where some arrows are turned around - as usual when we go from topology to algebra. Here the descent question is: given a ring homomorphism ϕ:A→B\phi: A \to Bϕ:A→B, what data do we need to equip a BBB-module with for it to be of the form B⊗AMB \otimes_A MB⊗A​M for some AAA-module?

I became enamored with the second question in the special case where AAA is a field and BBB is a field containing AAA. John Onstead (Feb 19 2025 at 01:44):

Given an object AAA of some site, the co-Yoneda lemma tells us that the data a sheaf SSS assigns to AAA can simply be thought of as copies of AAA itself (viewed as a representable) that will be involved in the colimit representation of SSS.
