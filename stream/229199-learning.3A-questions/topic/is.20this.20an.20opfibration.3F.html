Topic: is this an opfibration?

John Baez (Mar 20 2022 at 20:03):

I've got a question. Let Graph\mathsf{Graph}Graph be the usual category theorist's category of graphs, or [[quivers]]: the category of functors from the category s,t:E→Vs,t: E \to Vs,t:E→V to Set\mathsf{Set}Set.

John Baez (Mar 20 2022 at 20:03):

John Baez (Mar 20 2022 at 20:04):

Mike Shulman (Mar 20 2022 at 20:55):

Let's see, a graph E⇉VE\rightrightarrows VE⇉V is equivalently a single function E+E→VE+E \to VE+E→V. If we have a function f:E→E′f:E\to E'f:E→E′, then we can take the pushout of the span V←E+E→E′+E′V \leftarrow E+E \to E'+E'V←E+E→E′+E′ to get a set V′V'V′, and hence a graph E′⇉V′E' \rightrightarrows V'E′⇉V′ with a map from our original one. It looks to me like this would be an opcartesian morphism; does something go wrong?

I'm actually trying to settle more general question, maybe I should ask that:

Given a functor ϕ:C→D\phi: C \to Dϕ:C→D between small categories, is the precomposition-with-ϕ\phiϕ between presheaf categories ϕ∗:D^→C^\phi^\ast: \hat{D} \to \hat{C}ϕ∗:D^→C^ always an opfibration?

John Baez (Mar 20 2022 at 21:07):

For some stupid reason I think the answer is no, so I was looking for a counterexample.

Mike Shulman (Mar 20 2022 at 21:47):

There's a general fact (which doesn't seem to be very widely known) that any pushout-preserving isofibration with a fully faithful left adjoint is an opfibration. The proof is kind of follow-your-nose once you suspect that such a thing might be true, and the case of edge\rm edgeedge is an instance of that. So the answer to your more general question will be yes if Lanϕ\rm Lan_\phiLanϕ​ is fully faithful, which is true in particular whenever ϕ\phiϕ is fully faithful.

Mike Shulman (Mar 20 2022 at 21:48):

So I would look for a counterexample where ϕ\phiϕ is not fully faithful. Perhaps ϕ:2→1\phi : 2\to 1ϕ:2→1, so that ϕ∗\phi^*ϕ∗ is the diagonal functor Set→Set×Set\rm Set \to Set\times SetSet→Set×Set?

Morgan Rogers (he/him) (Mar 20 2022 at 22:35):

Mike Shulman said:

So I would look for a counterexample where ϕ\phiϕ is not fully faithful. Perhaps ϕ:2→1\phi : 2\to 1ϕ:2→1, so that ϕ∗\phi^*ϕ∗ is the diagonal functor Set→Set×Set\rm Set \to Set\times SetSet→Set×Set?

Yep, this isn't an opfibration, since we can take the inclusion of the initial object into either of the representables to get a morphism with no lift.
