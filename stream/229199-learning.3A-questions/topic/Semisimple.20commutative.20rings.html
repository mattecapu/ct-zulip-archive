In other words, if RRR is von Neumann regular, then so is the matrix algebra Mn(R)M_n(R)Mn​(R).

I think this fact could be waggled into a proof of one of the facts mentioned in the Wikipedia article, that a finitely generated submodule NNN of a finitely generated free module RnR^nRn is a direct summand of RnR^nRn. In other words, that the inclusion N↪RnN \hookrightarrow R^nN↪Rn splits. And from here, one can argue that every (let's say left) module of a von Neumann regular ring RRR is a flat RRR-module, as follows:

Any module is a filtered colimit of finitely presented modules -- this is something basic and general, falling under the basic framework of Gabriel-Ulmer duality or locally finitely presentable categories or what have you. And a filtered colimit of flat modules is again flat. So it would suffice to prove that a finitely presented RRR-module MMM is flat. So suppose we have an exact sequence

0→N→Rn→M→00 \to N \to R^n \to M \to 00→N→Rn→M→0

where NNN is finitely generated. This exact sequence splits, by the previous paragraph. So then MMM is a direct summand of RnR^nRn, hence is projective. But projective modules are flat. Done!

Somewhere deep in the von Neumann paper, around lemmas 15 and 16, it is shown that principal left (or right) ideals of von Neumann regular rings are closed under meets and joins in the lattice of all left ideals. They're closely linked: a variety is the same as the category of 'models' of an algebraic theory of the kind called a Lawvere theory.

Simply put, a Lawvere theory is a category TTT with products whose objects are x0,x1,x2,…x^0, x^1, x^2, \dotsx0,x1,x2,… for some xxx, and a model of this theory is a product-preserving functor f:T→Setf: T \to \mathsf{Set}f:T→Set. A morphism of models is a natural transformation between such functors. The category of models of a Lawvere theory is called a variety.

This is not a definition of 'variety' that you'd ever usually see people give in the field of 'universal algebra', but it winds up being equivalent.

For example: there's a Lawvere theory TTT of rings, where the morphisms xn→xx^n \to xxn→x are all the nnn-ary operations you can define in the language of rings, with all the usual equations between them. A model of TTT is a ring, and morphism of models is a ring homomorphism. The category of models is the category of rings, and this is a variety.
