Topic: Question about a perspective on fibrations

Patrick Nicodemus (Jan 07 2024 at 01:44):

Consider the 2-category Cat\mathbf{Cat}Cat of small categories. There are multiple 2-category structures on Cat/B\mathbf{Cat}/\mathbb{B}Cat/B for a choice of small category B\mathbb{B}B; there is the obvious "strict" one or we could also consider the 2-category where morphisms p⇒qp\Rightarrow qp⇒q are given by oplax cells. A Grothendieck fibration ppp over B\mathbb{B}B is an object in Cat/B\mathbf{Cat}/\mathbb{B}Cat/B with the property that every oplax cell into ppp can be strengthened to a strict cell. I think this should make the category Cat/Bstrict(q,p)\mathbf{Cat}/\mathbb{B}_{\mathrm{strict}}(q,p)Cat/Bstrict​(q,p) into a coreflective subcategory of Cat/Boplax(q,p)\mathbf{Cat}/\mathbb{B}_{\mathrm{oplax}}(q,p)Cat/Boplax​(q,p) when ppp is a fibration, and I was wondering if there were other interesting situations where you have two reasonable 2-category structures on the same set of objects, with the first one being a sub-2-category of the second one, and a class of fibrant objects characterized by the fact that the inclusion functor on homs admits a right adjoint.
I think this is maybe related to the fact that fibrations are algebras of Kock-Zoberlein monads but I really don't know anything about those, I just learned about them the other day.
