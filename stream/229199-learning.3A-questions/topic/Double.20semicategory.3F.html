Topic: Double semicategory?

Adittya Chaudhuri (Mar 20 2026 at 07:44):

There is a notion called semicategory, which is exactly like a category but without the requiremnent for the existence of the identity morphisms.

Recently, I came across the following situation:

I was able to construct a structure like a double category D=[D1⇉D0]\mathbb{D}=[\mathsf{D}_{1} \rightrightarrows \mathsf{D}_{0} ]D=[D1​⇉D0​], where D1\mathsf{D}_{1} D1​ is the category of arrows and D0 \mathsf{D}_{0}D0​ is the category of objects, where I could not find a natural way to define the identity assigning functor U ⁣:D0→D1\mathsf{U} \colon \mathsf{D}_{0} \to \mathsf{D}_{1} U:D0​→D1​. But, the composition functor ∘ ⁣:D1×D0D1→D1\circ \colon \mathsf{D}_{1} \times_{\mathsf{D}_{0} } \mathsf{D}_{1} \to \mathsf{D}_{1} ∘:D1​×D0​​D1​→D1​ makes the composition of horizontal 1-cells associative.

The above situation naturally led me to ask whether there already exists a notion of double semicategory in the literature with the same spirit as a semicategory is to a category.
