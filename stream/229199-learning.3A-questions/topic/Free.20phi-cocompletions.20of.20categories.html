Topic: Free phi-cocompletions of categories

John Onstead (Jul 15 2025 at 11:16):

A large class of subcategories of the presheaf category are given by cocompleting the category freely under some class of colimits. Examples of this include Cauchy completion (absolute colimits), coproduct completion, Ind completion (filtered colimits), and sifted colimit completions. There is an explicit representation of these cocompletion: given some class ϕ\phiϕ, the free cocompletion under those colimits is Flatϕc[Cop,Set]\mathrm{Flat}_{\phi^c}[C^{op}, \mathrm{Set}]Flatϕc​[Cop,Set], where ϕc\phi^cϕc is a certain "conjugate/complement" class to ϕ\phiϕ and "flat" denotes a ϕc\phi^cϕc-limit preserving functor that also preserves these limits even when they don't exist. If CCC already has all ϕc\phi^cϕc colimits, then the free ϕ\phiϕ-cocompletion is just Limϕc[Cop,Set]\mathrm{Lim}_{\phi^c}[C^{op}, \mathrm{Set}]Limϕc​[Cop,Set].

John Onstead (Jul 15 2025 at 11:17):

For instance, for ind completion, ϕ\phiϕ is filtered colimits and ϕc\phi^cϕc is finite limits, so the notion of ϕc\phi^cϕc flatness is just the usual notion of flatness. For sifted colimits, ϕc\phi^cϕc is finite products. The free cocompletion itself is also a special case- if ϕ\phiϕ is all colimits, then ϕc\phi^cϕc must be absolute limits, and any presheaf preserves those (even the absolute limits that don't exist). But in all examples I have with explicit characterizations of ϕc\phi^cϕc, ϕ\phiϕ-colimits both distribute AND commute with ϕc\phi^cϕc-limits in Set\mathrm{Set}Set, making it difficult to determine which one of these two is the actual correct characterization of the class ϕc\phi^cϕc, since commutativity and distributivity diverge in general.

John Onstead (Jul 15 2025 at 11:18):

So my question is: what is the correct characterization of ϕc\phi^cϕc: is it the class of limits that commute with ϕ\phiϕ-colimits in Set\mathrm{Set}Set, or those that distribute with them? Thanks!

John Onstead (Jul 16 2025 at 05:53):

After doing some more work with help from your hints above, I've derived a relatively simple counter-example to my claim and therefore showed it to be false. Here's the basics. By a partial result in the paper, if CCC embeds fully faithfully into the compact objects of DDD a category with filtered colimits, then the canonical functor Ind(C)→D\mathrm{Ind}(C) \to DInd(C)→D via the universal property of Ind is fully faithful. By density of CCC we derive a chain of inclusions: Ind(C)→D→Psh(C)\mathrm{Ind}(C) \to D \to \mathrm{Psh}(C)Ind(C)→D→Psh(C). So DDD is just an enlargement of the ind completion in presheaves, therefore, if we find such an enlargement that doesn't change the compact objects of the ind completion (which are already precisely the retracts of representables), we have an example of a non-equivalent category to ind which satisfies my criteria.

John Onstead (Jul 16 2025 at 05:53):

Your suggestion above made me remember that the compact objects of presheaves are the finite colimits of representables. Thus, if DDD includes any of those, we've added in a new compact object and so we indeed change the compact objects of the category. But the solution is simple: take the ind completion and add in just a single infinite colimit of representables, and let that be DDD. This isn't a compact object in presheaves, and therefore adding it does not in fact change the compact objects of CCC. Hence, it is a counterexample and the above "conjecture" is false.

John Onstead (Jul 18 2025 at 11:51):

In the free coproduct construction of CCC, the objects can be thought of as families of objects of CCC. So there might be a relation between Coprod(C)\mathrm{Coprod}(C)Coprod(C) and the comma category (Set/C)(\mathrm{Set}/C)(Set/C), with Set\mathrm{Set}Set the subcategory of Cat\mathrm{Cat}Cat on discrete categories. In another case, the free filtered colimit construction of CCC has objects that you can think of as filtered diagrams in CCC. So there's some relation between Ind(C)\mathrm{Ind}(C)Ind(C) and (Filt/C)(\mathrm{Filt}/C)(Filt/C) with Filt\mathrm{Filt}Filt being the subcategory of Cat\mathrm{Cat}Cat on filtered categories.

When generalizing to the free cocompletion under all diagrams, we thus expect there to be a relation between [Cop,Set][C^{op}, \mathrm{Set}][Cop,Set] and (Cat/C)(\mathrm{Cat}/C)(Cat/C). While these aren't equivalent, this relation can actually be restricted to an equivalence between presheaves on CCC and discrete fibrations into CCC using the Grothendieck construction. This is a reflection of how the canonical colimit describing a presheaf is a colimit over its category of elements, the discrete fibration.
