Todd Trimble (Oct 28 2023 at 09:43):

It seems to me that all monads MMM (let's say on Set\mathrm{Set}Set) bear this type of structure, insofar as all monads on Set\mathrm{Set}Set can be construed as infinitary Lawvere theories.

M(j)M(j)M(j) can be thought of as codifying the set of jjj-ary operations of the monad qua theory, in the sense that if XXX is an MMM-algebra, then the algebra structure on XXX induces (via what the nLab calls the "coYoneda lemma") a map

∫jM(j)⋅Xj≅M(X)→X\int^j M(j) \cdot X^j \cong M(X) \to X∫jM(j)⋅Xj≅M(X)→X

hence a map M(j)⋅Xj→XM(j) \cdot X^j \to XM(j)⋅Xj→X dinatural in jjj, hence a map M(j)→Set(Xj,X)M(j) \to \mathrm{Set}(X^j, X)M(j)→Set(Xj,X) natural in jjj which interprets elements in M(j)M(j)M(j) as operations on XXX. And so it would go when we correspondingly think of MMM itself as an MMM-module via the monad multiplication:

∫jM(j)⋅M(−)j≅M∘M→M\int^j M(j) \cdot M(-)^j \cong M \circ M \to M∫jM(j)⋅M(−)j≅M∘M→M

which is what you're describing.

Todd Trimble (Nov 04 2023 at 02:39):

Something we can say: suppose MMM is a strong monad on a cartesian category C\mathcal{C}C, and (X,α:M(X)→X)(X, \alpha: M(X) \to X)(X,α:M(X)→X) is an MMM-algebra. Then, if JJJ is an object such that XJX^JXJ exists (i.e., if there exists a representing object for the functor C(J×−,X)\mathcal{C}(J \times -, X)C(J×−,X)), then for each morphism t:1→M(J)t: 1 \to M(J)t:1→M(J), we have an accompanying operation XJ→XX^J \to XXJ→X formed as the composite

XJ≅1×XJ→t×idM(J)×XJ→M(J×XJ)→M(X)→αX.X^J \cong 1 \times X^J \overset{t \times \mathrm{id}}{\to} M(J) \times X^J \to M(J \times X^J) \to M(X) \overset{\alpha}{\to} X.XJ≅1×XJ→t×idM(J)×XJ→M(J×XJ)→M(X)→αX.

where the first unlabeled non-isomorphism comes from the strength, and the second is MMM applied to the universal arrow J×XJ→XJ \times X^J \to XJ×XJ→X.

Todd Trimble (Nov 04 2023 at 02:39):

For example, suppose C\mathcal{C}C has finite coproducts and cartesian product distributes over these coproducts. By abuse of notation, let jjj denote the jjj-fold coproduct of copies of the terminal object 111. Then the ordinary cartesian power XjX^jXj gives the above representing object, and for every formal "operation" t:1→M(j)t: 1 \to M(j)t:1→M(j) we can form an actual operation Xj→XX^j \to XXj→X as above, for every MMM-algebra XXX. This applies in particular to algebras of the form M(c)M(c)M(c), and we get operations M(c)j→M(c)M(c)^j \to M(c)M(c)j→M(c) that are natural in ccc, hence a natural operation Mj→MM^j \to MMj→M.
