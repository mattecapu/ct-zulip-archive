Topic: Hom-set proof of postcomposition adjunction

Ali Caglayan (Aug 22 2021 at 14:23):

Hi, I'm looking for a proof that postcomposition preserves adjunctions: F⊣G  ⟹  F∗⊣G∗F \dashv G \implies F_* \dashv G_*F⊣G⟹F∗​⊣G∗​. I know a few proofs of this, but I am looking for one that only uses the hom-set definition of an adjunction.
Here is what I know:

  • Using the unit-counit characterization of an adjunction, you can easily reassemble the parts of the postcomposition adjunction. But I don't want to use the unit-counit definition.
  • The postcomposition functor [X,−]:Cat→CAT[X,-] : \mathsf{Cat} \to \mathsf{CAT}[X,−]:Cat→CAT clearly preserves adjoint morphisms inside a 2-category, but this proof strays from the hom-set definition of an adjunction for the same reason as above.
  • The closest I have found uses ends: [X,C](F∘x,y)≃∫i∈XC(Fxi,yi)≃∫i∈XD(xi,Gyi)≃[X,D](x,G∘y)[X,C](F \circ x, y) \simeq \int_{i \in X} C(Fxi,yi) \simeq \int_{i \in X} D(xi,Gyi) \simeq [X,D](x,G \circ y)[X,C](F∘x,y)≃∫i∈X​C(Fxi,yi)≃∫i∈X​D(xi,Gyi)≃[X,D](x,G∘y) but I am not quite sure this gives me anything. It seems to me that it requires the same amount of work as defining the original hom-set bijection directly and showing that is natural.

Does anybody know an easier proof, or do I have to check the naturality by hand?
