Topic: Categorical treatments for Palmgren & Vickers' Cartϖ

Yuto Ikeda (Dec 20 2025 at 12:49):

In Section 8.2 in Palmgren and Vickers' "Partial Horn logic and cartesian categories", they consider a unary operation Cartϖ\mathrm{Cart\varpi}Cartϖ on quasi-equational theories (which are equiavelnt to essentially algebraic theories). For a quasi-equational theory T\mathbb{T}T, another, denoted CartϖT\mathrm{Cart\varpi} \mathbb{T}CartϖT, is designed as follows: models of CartϖT\mathrm{Cart\varpi} \mathbb{T}CartϖT in Set\mathbf{Set}Set are pairs (C,M)(\mathcal{C}, M)(C,M) of a cartesian category C\mathcal{C}C and a model MMM of the theory T\mathbb{T}T in C\mathcal{C}C. (Here, cartesian categories refers to categories with finite limits.) In short, CartϖT\mathrm{Cart\varpi} \mathbb{T}CartϖT is "the theory of cartesian categories equipped with a model of T\mathbb{T}T".

Although their motivation for introducing CartϖT\mathrm{Cart\varpi} \mathbb{T}CartϖT is to obtain a simple construction of classifying (syntactic) categories, if we abstract away from this motivation and identify quasi-equational theories with their classifying categories, Cartϖ\mathrm{Cart\varpi}Cartϖ can be viewed as an operation on cartesian categories. I wonder if there are any categorical treatments (characterization or known properties) on this operation Cartϖ\mathrm{Cart\varpi}Cartϖ.

For instance, Cartϖ\mathrm{Cart\varpi}Cartϖ seems to naturally form an endofunctor on the (2-)category of cartesian categories. Moreover, it seems to be a pointed endofunctor (i.e., there is a natural transformation id⇒Cartϖ\mathrm{id} \Rightarrow \mathrm{Cart\varpi}id⇒Cartϖ) via an "externalization" process: for pairs (C,M)(\mathcal{C}, M)(C,M) as described above, we obtain a Set\mathbf{Set}Set-model ΓC[M]\Gamma_\mathcal{C} [M]ΓC​[M] of T\mathbb{T}T by sending MMM along the global section functor ΓC=C(1,−) ⁣:C→Set\Gamma_\mathcal{C} = \mathcal{C} (1, -) \colon \mathcal{C} \to \mathbf{Set}ΓC​=C(1,−):C→Set. This sends Set\mathbf{Set}Set-models of CartϖT\mathrm{Cart\varpi} \mathbb{T}CartϖT to Set\mathbf{Set}Set-models of T\mathbb{T}T, which suggests it is induced by a morphism of theories T→CartϖT\mathbb{T} \to \mathrm{Cart\varpi} \mathbb{T}T→CartϖT. However, I don't know if Cartϖ\mathrm{Cart\varpi}Cartϖ forms a (2-)monad.

Thanks for any suggestions!

If so, it seems that a pair (C,M)(\mathcal{C}, M)(C,M) of the sort you're talking about - a finite limits category C\mathcal{C}C and a model MMM of the theory T\mathbb{T}T in C\mathcal{C}C - is the same as an object in T↓Cart\mathcal{T} \downarrow \mathbf{Cart}T↓Cart, the 2-category of finite limits categories equipped with a finite-limits-preserving functor from T\mathcal{T}T. Morgan Rogers (he/him) (Dec 25 2025 at 08:52):

@Yuto Ikeda I don't know why you brought Set into the picture (also, the equivalence in your last message doesn't look right to me, since the LHS is a 1-category while the second looks more naturally like a 2-category) but the size issue you observe applies nonetheless. Nonetheless, supposing you circumvent that by putting some constraints in, it's fun to consider whether this might be a monad (or perhaps a relative pseudomonad?)
The result of applying your functor is a finite limit category classifying internal categories containing a model of T\mathbb{T}T. So iterating, we have a model in an internal category in an internal category in some (external) category C\mathcal{C}C. So a "multiplication" to make this into a monad would need to take an internal category in an internal category and externality it in a way that preserves models of theories.
I think this amounts to an internal version of the Grothendieck construction and that the preservation of models of finite limit theories will be automatic BUT I think the existence of the internal Grothendieck construction depends on the existence of some colimits which are not assumed to exist, so I don't know how things will look for a generic finite limit category.
Perhaps someone else can weigh in :innocent:
