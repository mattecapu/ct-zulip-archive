If you have a commutative ring RRR that's an integral domain, you can form its field of fractions Frac(R)\mathrm{Frac}(R)Frac(R). Since it contains RRR as a subring, Frac(R)\mathrm{Frac}(R)Frac(R) becomes an RRR-module, and any submodule of this is called a fractional ideal of RRR. These are really important in number theory.

The set of fractional ideals of RRR becomes a monoid because given two fractional ideals I,JI,JI,J we can define a new one
