Topic: composing universal property morphisms

David Egolf (Dec 05 2023 at 20:51):

I'm currently contemplating this rather impressive diagram (from "Categorical logic from a categorical point of view" by Michael Shulman):
diagram

In this diagram, many of the morphism pictured are induced by using the universal property of products. To try and show that different polygons in this diagram commute I think involves calculating the composite of these "universal property morphisms". For example, we'd like to be able to show that (i×1×1)∘(Δ×1)∘(1,j)=(i,1,j)(i \times 1 \times 1) \circ (\Delta \times 1) \circ (1,j) = (i,1,j)(i×1×1)∘(Δ×1)∘(1,j)=(i,1,j).

I think I've figured out an approach that works, but I would like to try and formalize it a bit so that I can use it more easily. I drew this rough diagram to illustrate the approach:
rough diagram

David Egolf (Dec 05 2023 at 20:53):

In this diagram AAA, BBB and B′B'B′ are objects, while DDD and D′D'D′ are diagrams. The fancy wider arrows are supposed to indicate that there are potentially multiple parallel morphisms present. Since we want to use the universal property of a limit, the idea is that BBB and B′B'B′ (together with their projecting morphisms) are limits over the diagrams DDD and D′D'D′, respectively.

David Egolf (Dec 05 2023 at 20:53):

For each object ddd in DDD, there is exactly one morphism in fff from AAA to ddd. In addition, the morphisms ggg from DDD to D′D'D′ are such that when composed with the morphisms π\piπ from BBB to DDD, there is exactly one morphism in the composite to each object of D′D'D′ from BBB.

David Egolf (Dec 05 2023 at 20:53):

The dashed arrows indicate morphisms induced by the universal property of the limits involved. The dashed arrow ufu_fuf​ indicates the morphism induced by by the morphisms fff, and ugu_gug​ is the morphism induced by the morphisms g∘πg \circ \pi g∘π.

Since each path in the left triangle commutes (we might say π∘uf=f\pi \circ u_f = fπ∘uf​=f) and each path in the right rectangle commutes (we might say g∘π=π′∘ugg \circ \pi = \pi' \circ u_gg∘π=π′∘ug​) I think each path in the larger "outside" diagram commutes (so g∘f=π′∘ug∘ufg \circ f = \pi' \circ u_g \circ u_fg∘f=π′∘ug​∘uf​). I think this implies that ug∘ufu_g \circ u_fug​∘uf​ is the universal property morphism ug∘fu_{g \circ f}ug∘f​ induced by g∘fg \circ fg∘f.

David Egolf (Dec 05 2023 at 20:54):

So, to form the composite of two morphisms ufu_fuf​ and ugu_gug​ induced by universal properties, I think one takes the universal property morphism induced by the "composite" of their inducing sets of morphisms fff and ggg. (Although, note that π\piπ was involved in induced ugu_gug​, not just the morphisms ggg.)

Does this seem like it should work? I'd also be interested in learning how to formalize this cleanly. I'm not used to thinking about "composing" collections of parallel morphisms!

David Egolf (Dec 05 2023 at 21:19):

I guess one limitation of this approach is that it assumes the inducing morphisms for the second universal morphism (from BBB to B′B'B′) all "factor through" the diagram DDD. I don't think is necessarily always the case.

To handle a more general situation, probably a relevant diagram would look more like this:
more general diagram

In this diagram we don't assume that the morphisms inducing the second universal morphism factor through DDD.

In this more general case we'd have π′∘ug∘uf=g∘uf\pi' \circ u_g \circ u_f = g \circ u_fπ′∘ug​∘uf​=g∘uf​. I guess that in this case ug∘ufu_g \circ u_fug​∘uf​ is the universal property morphism induced by g∘ufg \circ u_fg∘uf​.
