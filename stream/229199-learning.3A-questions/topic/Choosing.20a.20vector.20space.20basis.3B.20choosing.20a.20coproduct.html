"An isomorphism R⊕...⊕R→E\mathbb{R} \oplus ... \oplus \mathbb{R} \rightarrow ER⊕...⊕R→E"

John Baez (Sep 02 2023 at 21:36):

I like to think about a basis of a vector space VVV as an isomorphism α:V→FS\alpha : V \to FSα:V→FS where F:Set→VectF : \mathsf{Set} \to \mathsf{Vect}F:Set→Vect is the "free vector space on a set functor". In this blog article I basically described the essential image of this functor FFF. Roughly, this means to describe in purely linear algebraic terms what extra structure a vector space gets from having a basis.

I also did it for finite-dimensional vector spaces. The answer for finite-dimensional vector spaces is different in flavor from the answer for vector spaces.

Most of what I was describing was work of Aurelio Carboni, but the case of arbitrary vector spaces, as opposed to finite-dimensional ones, involves a conjecture that was proved by Theo Johnson-Freyd in a comment to my article.

A minor comment: given a fin. dim. vector space VVV and choosing an isomorphism to a specified Rn\mathbb{R}^nRn gives an ordered basis, but choosing an isomorphism to the vector space of functions RS\mathbb{R}^SRS, where SSS is a finite set, gives an unordered (in the sense of not-necessarily ordered) basis.
