Oscar Cunningham (Oct 06 2025 at 09:07):

The Graphical Linear Algebra site works in the monoidal category with ⊕\oplus⊕ as the monoidal product, which is not at all what we want here. We want strings next to each other to represent the tensor product ⊗\otimes⊗.

I think the search we want to do is for 'vector calculus' along with 'Penrose graphical notation'. Indeed the Wikipedia page for Penrose's notation has a picture that essentially explains the whole thing:

Oscar Cunningham (Oct 06 2025 at 09:15):

On the other hand, I kinda hate these stupid 'bubbles' representing the derivative. I think you should be able to do the whole thing without them. Write C(R3)C(\mathbb{R}^3)C(R3) for the real vector space of sufficiently nice functions on R3\mathbb{R}^3R3. Then have one kind of string in your diagram for R3\mathbb{R}^3R3, and one for C(R3)C(\mathbb{R}^3)C(R3). You can think of a vector field as an element of C(R3)⊗R3C(\mathbb{R}^3)\otimes\mathbb{R}^3C(R3)⊗R3 and represent it as a box in the string diagram with one of each kind of leg. Then the derivative is just a linear map C(R3)→C(R3)⊗R3C(\mathbb{R}^3)\to C(\mathbb{R}^3)\otimes\mathbb{R}^3C(R3)→C(R3)⊗R3, and everything becomes normal string diagram manipulations with no bubbles.
