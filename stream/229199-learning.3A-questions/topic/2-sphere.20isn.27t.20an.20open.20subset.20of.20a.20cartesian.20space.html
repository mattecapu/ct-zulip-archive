Ok, if UUU is a clopen set in Rn\mathbb{R}^nRn which is neither ∅\emptyset∅ nor Rn\mathbb{R}^nRn, then Rn\U\mathbb{R}^n \backslash URn\U is also clopen. Jean-Baptiste Vienney (Jun 30 2024 at 01:10):

To sum up:

  • The nnn-sphere is compact as a bounded closed subset of Rn+1\mathbb{R}^{n+1}Rn+1.
  • If the nnn-sphere is homeomorphic to an open subset UUU of an Rp\mathbb{R}^pRp, then UUU is compact, thus closed, thus a clopen. So UUU is either Rp\mathbb{R}^pRp or the empty set. The cardinality is preserved by homeomorphism, thus UUU is necessarily Rp\mathbb{R}^pRp.

Now, we have reduced the problem to the one of proving that the nnn-sphere isn't homeomorphic to an Rp\mathbb{R}^pRp.

Jean-Baptiste Vienney (Jun 30 2024 at 01:42):

Ok, so you just have to say this:

Let n≥1n \ge 1n≥1.

  • The nnn-sphere is compact as a bounded closed subset of Rn+1\mathbb{R}^{n+1}Rn+1.
  • If the nnn-sphere is homeomorphic to an open subset UUU of an Rp\mathbb{R}^pRp, then UUU is compact, thus closed, thus a clopen. So UUU is either Rp\mathbb{R}^pRp or the empty set. The cardinality is preserved by homeomorphism, thus UUU is necessarily Rp\mathbb{R}^pRp. Thus Rp\mathbb{R}^pRp is compact. But Rp\mathbb{R}^pRp is not compact if p≥1p \ge 1p≥1 because it is a subset of Rp\mathbb{R}^pRp which is not bounded. Hence, p=0p=0p=0. Thus SnS^nSn is homeomorphic to a point which is false because it is an infinite set.
