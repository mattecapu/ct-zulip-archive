John Baez (Sep 15 2023 at 18:18):

Hmm. I can never remember this stuff, but I read that strong epimorphisms are precisely those left orthogonal to any monomorphism. Furthermore I read that they obey the left two-out-of-three property: if ggg and gfg fgf are strong epis then so is fff. (In fact something stronger is true.)

David Jaz Myers has a paper in which he defines a cartesian factorization system to be an orthogonal factorization system in which the left class (which are like the monos) satisfies the left two-out-of-three and is closed under pullback along the right class (which are like the strong epis). He proves that any such factorization gives a Street fibration (which is the non-evil version of a fibration). So he may be doing what you want, @Jules Hedges! See Proposition 2.4 and the diagram underneath.

Dylan McDermott (Sep 15 2023 at 18:54):

If (E,M)(\mathcal{E}, \mathcal{M})(E,M) is an orthogonal factorization system on C\mathcal{C}C, then the codomain functor M→C\mathcal{M} \to \mathcal{C}M→C is always an opfibration, nothing else is needed. So if I've dualized correctly, this implies the domain functor E→C\mathcal{E} \to \mathcal{C}E→C is a fibration. The square on the right of @Kevin Arlin's picture is an instance of the diagonal fill-in property.
