Topic: equivalence relationships preserved by endofunctors

David Egolf (Jan 05 2022 at 02:25):

Let F:C→CF: \mathsf{C} \to \mathsf{C}F:C→C be a functor. Let ppp be an equivalence relationship on the objects of C\mathsf{C}C. We say that FFF preserves ppp when a∼pb  ⟹  F(a)∼pF(b)a \sim_p b \implies F(a) \sim_p F(b)a∼p​b⟹F(a)∼p​F(b).
Two questions:

  1. Given an endofunctor FFF, how can one discover which equivalence relationships it preserves?
  2. Given an equivalence relationship ppp, how can one discover the endofunctors that preserve ppp?

David Egolf (Jan 05 2022 at 22:53):

Here's another one, assuming I didn't mix up something:
Let a∼b  ⟺  F(a)≅F(b)a \sim b \iff F(a) \cong F(b)a∼b⟺F(a)≅F(b).
First we show this is an equivalence relationship.
F(a)≅F(a)  ⟹  a∼aF(a) \cong F(a) \implies a \sim aF(a)≅F(a)⟹a∼a for any aaa.
If a∼ba \sim ba∼b, then F(a)≅F(b)  ⟹  F(b)≅F(a)  ⟹  b∼aF(a) \cong F(b) \implies F(b) \cong F(a) \implies b \sim aF(a)≅F(b)⟹F(b)≅F(a)⟹b∼a, with a,ba,ba,b any objects in CCC.
If a∼ba \sim ba∼b and b∼cb \sim cb∼c for any objects a,b,ca,b,ca,b,c in CCC, then we have F(a)≅F(b)F(a) \cong F(b)F(a)≅F(b) and F(b)≅F(c)F(b) \cong F(c)F(b)≅F(c) and so F(a)≅F(c)F(a) \cong F(c)F(a)≅F(c), which implies a∼ca \sim ca∼c.

Now we show the equivalence relationship is preserved by FFF.
Let a∼ba \sim ba∼b. Then we have F(a)≅F(b)F(a) \cong F(b)F(a)≅F(b), which implies F(F(a))≅F(F(b))F(F(a)) \cong F(F(b))F(F(a))≅F(F(b)), because functors send isomorphic objects to isomorphic objects. But F(F(a))≅F(F(b))F(F(a)) \cong F(F(b))F(F(a))≅F(F(b)) implies that F(a)∼F(b)F(a) \sim F(b)F(a)∼F(b), by definition of our equivalence relationship. So, a∼b  ⟹  F(a)∼F(b)a \sim b \implies F(a) \sim F(b)a∼b⟹F(a)∼F(b).

I suppose this procedure always works? Say ppp is an equivalence relationship preserved by FFF. Then let qqq be the equivalence relationship defined by a∼qb  ⟺  F(a)∼pF(b)a \sim_q b \iff F(a) \sim_p F(b)a∼q​b⟺F(a)∼p​F(b). We want to show qqq is preserved by FFF.
Let's check that qqq really is an equivalence relationship.

  • F(a)∼pF(a)  ⟹  a∼qaF(a) \sim_p F(a) \implies a \sim_q aF(a)∼p​F(a)⟹a∼q​a.
  • a∼qb  ⟹  F(a)∼pF(b)  ⟹  F(b)∼pF(a)  ⟹  b∼paa \sim_q b \implies F(a) \sim_p F(b) \implies F(b) \sim_p F(a) \implies b \sim_p aa∼q​b⟹F(a)∼p​F(b)⟹F(b)∼p​F(a)⟹b∼p​a
  • a∼qba \sim_q ba∼q​b and b∼qcb \sim_q cb∼q​c implies F(a)∼pF(b)F(a) \sim_p F(b)F(a)∼p​F(b) and F(b)∼pF(c)F(b) \sim_p F(c)F(b)∼p​F(c), and this means F(a)∼pF(c)F(a) \sim_p F(c)F(a)∼p​F(c), which implies a∼qca \sim_q ca∼q​c

Let's check that qqq is preserved by FFF.
a∼qb  ⟹  F(a)∼pF(b)  ⟹  F(F(a))∼pF(F(b))a \sim_q b \implies F(a) \sim_p F(b) \implies F(F(a)) \sim_p F(F(b))a∼q​b⟹F(a)∼p​F(b)⟹F(F(a))∼p​F(F(b)), where we use the fact that ppp is preserved by FFF.
Then F(F(a))∼pF(F(b)  ⟹  F(a)∼qF(b) F(F(a)) \sim_p F(F(b) \implies F(a) \sim_q F(b)F(F(a))∼p​F(F(b)⟹F(a)∼q​F(b) as desired.

David Egolf (Jan 06 2022 at 17:00):

Can we get more equivalence relationships using the strategy above?
Let ppp be an equivalence relationship on CCC preserved by FFF.
We saw above that the equivalence relationship qqq defined by a∼qb  ⟺  F(a)∼pF(b)a \sim _q b \iff F(a) \sim_p F(b)a∼q​b⟺F(a)∼p​F(b) is also an equivalence relationship preserved by FFF.
Let us call the equivalence relationship generated from ppp in this way by the name F(p)F(p)F(p).

If ppp is an equivalence relationship preserved by FFF, then so is F(p)F(p)F(p).
This means that F(F(p))=F2(p)F(F(p)) = F^2(p)F(F(p))=F2(p) is also an equivalence relationship preserved by FFF.
In general Fn(p)F^n(p)Fn(p) should be an equivalence relationship preserved by FFF, for any positive integer nnn.

Let's see what F2(p)F^2(p)F2(p) is for a∼pb  ⟺  a≅ba \sim_p b \iff a \cong ba∼p​b⟺a≅b.
a∼F2(p)b  ⟺  F(a)∼F(p)F(b)  ⟺  F2(a)∼pF2(b)  ⟺  F2(a)≅F2(b)a \sim_{F^2 (p)} b \iff F(a) \sim_{F(p)} F(b) \iff F^2(a) \sim_p F^2(b) \iff F^2(a) \cong F^2(b)a∼F2(p)​b⟺F(a)∼F(p)​F(b)⟺F2(a)∼p​F2(b)⟺F2(a)≅F2(b).
It appears that "isomorphic after two applications of FFF" is another equivalence relationship on CCC preserved by FFF. More generally, "eventually isomorphic after enough applications of FFF" should be another equivalence relationship preserved by FFF.
