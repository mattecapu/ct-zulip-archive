Topic: Functors of Fibrant Double Categories via Proarrow Equipment

Joshua Meyers (Mar 27 2024 at 14:09):

A (weak) fibrant double category D\mathbb{D}D induces a structure similar to a proarrow equipment but slightly weaker consisting of the following data:

  • A bicategory D\mathcal{D}D which is the horizontal bicategory of D\mathbb{D}D
  • A category D0\mathbb{D}_0D0​ which is the vertical category of D\mathbb{D}D
  • An identity-on-objects pseudofunctor (−)‾:D0→D\overline{(-)}:\mathbb{D}_0\to\mathcal{D}(−)​:D0​→D sending each vertical arrow to its companion, such that every morphism in the image is a left adjoint in D\mathcal{D}D

(this is differs from a proarrow equipment in that D\mathbb{D}D is a 1-category)

In @Mike Shulman's paper "Framed Bicategories and Monoidal Fibrations", he cites Dominic Verity who defined morphisms of these equipments essentially by making them into double categories and then using double functors --- so it would be tautologous to get morphisms of fibrant double categories from morphisms of eqiupments. However, I am not so sure.

Conjecture:

Let D\mathbb{D}D and E\mathbb{E}E be fibrant double categories. Then a strong double functor aka strong framed functor F:D→EF:\mathbb{D}\to\mathbb{E}F:D→E is equivalent to the following data:

  • A pseudo functor F:D→EF:\mathcal{D}\to\mathcal{E}F:D→E
  • A functor F:D0→E0F:\mathbb{D}_0\to\mathbb{E}_0F:D0​→E0​ agreeing with the pseudo functor FFF on objects (this allows us to use the same name FFF without ambiguity)
  • A pseudo natural transformation (−)‾∘F≅F∘(−)‾:D0→E\overline{(-)}\circ F\cong F\circ\overline{(-)}:\mathbb{D}_0\to\mathcal{E}(−)​∘F≅F∘(−)​:D0​→E sending objects to identities.

Is there any known result like this?
