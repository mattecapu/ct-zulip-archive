Topic: morphisms of monads and Kleisli categories

Paolo Perrone (Jan 20 2022 at 15:43):

It is well known that there is a bijection between morphisms of monads and (backward) functors between the Eilenberg-Moore categories commuting with the forgetful functors.
Is a similar statement known for Kleisli categories, does anyone know?

Nathanael Arkor (Jan 20 2022 at 16:27):

Yes. Monads morphisms are in bijection with functors between Kleisli categories commuting with the inclusion functors.

Nathanael Arkor (Jan 20 2022 at 16:27):

It's formally dual to the statement about EM categories.

Nathanael Arkor (Jan 20 2022 at 16:29):

The earliest reference is most likely Maranda's On fundamental constructions and adjoint functors, though it's also implicit in Linton's work.

Oscar Cunningham (Jan 20 2022 at 16:46):

BY 'inclusion' functors here do you mean the free functors? I think that's what it would be if it was dual to the EM result.

Nathanael Arkor (Jan 20 2022 at 16:53):

The functors C→Kl(T)\mathbf C \to \mathbf{Kl}(T)C→Kl(T) into the Kleisli categories, yes.

Paolo Perrone (Jan 21 2022 at 09:03):

Thank you!
