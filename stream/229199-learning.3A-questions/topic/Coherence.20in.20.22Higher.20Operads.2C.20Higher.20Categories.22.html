I have no idea what's going on here, but if P is the pullback of TM and E', to get a morphism from P to M you need morphisms from TM and E' to M (which must obey a property). Paolo Capriotti (Aug 26 2020 at 14:44):

Since TTT is Cartesian, there is a pullback square similar to the one on the left side of your diagram, but with PPP replaced by MMM. You then get the unitor from the universal property of the pullback.
