Topic: dinatural transformations

Zanzi (Apr 27 2023 at 21:42):

I'm looking for a setting where I could work with both natural and dinatural transformations. Since dinatural transformations don't compose by themselves, but compose with natural transformations, is there a structure that combines the two in a sensible way?

Emily (Apr 28 2023 at 17:30):

@fosco and I had been working on this around 2020–2021, although we had to stop our work temporarily due to me facing some health issues (these are on-going, although I hope I can get back to being able to work again with Fosco and finish those papers soon).

As it turns out, dinatural transformations are an instance of what we call WWW-weighted natural transformations, which work in the same way as natural transformations α ⁣:F⇒G\alpha\colon F\Rightarrow Gα:F⇒G between functors F,G ⁣:C→DF,G\colon\mathcal{C}\to\mathcal{D}F,G:C→D, but are weighted with respect to a profunctor W ⁣:Cop×C→SetsW\colon\mathcal{C}^\mathsf{op}\times\mathcal{C}\to\mathsf{Sets}W:Cop×C→Sets. These form a set NatW(F,G)\mathrm{Nat}^W(F,G)NatW(F,G), and we have suitable horizontal and vertical composition laws for them, with e.g. vertical composition taking the form of a map

∘ ⁣:NatW′(G,H)×NatW(F,G)→NatW×W′(F,H).\circ\colon\mathrm{Nat}^{W'}(G,H)\times\mathrm{Nat}^{W}(F,G)\to\mathrm{Nat}^{W\times W'}(F,H).∘:NatW′(G,H)×NatW(F,G)→NatW×W′(F,H).

This behaves like the multiplication in a graded ring, where ordinary natural transformations are "degree 0" and WWW-weighted ones are "degree 1", and so you can precompose or postcompose weighted natural transformations with ordinary ones, but when you compose two weighted natural transformations (weighted by WWW and W′W'W′, say) you get something in "degree 2", a (W×W′)(W\times W')(W×W′)-weighted natural transformations.

Now, dinatural transformations turn out to be exactly the WWW-weighted natural transformations with W=HomCW=\mathrm{Hom}_\mathcal{C}W=HomC​, and thus we end up with composition laws for these as well as a special case! In this case, dinatural transformations pre- or postcompose with natural transformations, but you can also compose dinaturals with dinaturals, obtaining a "(HomC×HomC)(\mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}\times\mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}})(HomC​×HomC​)-weighted natural transformation", and so on. You can then put together natural transformations, dinaturals, (HomC×HomC)(\mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}\times\mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}})(HomC​×HomC​)-weighted natural transformations, (HomC×HomC×HomC)(\mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}\times\mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}\times\mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}})(HomC​×HomC​×HomC​)-weighted natural transformations, and so on into an N\mathbb{N}N-graded ring :)
