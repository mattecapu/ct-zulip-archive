Peva Blanchard (Apr 05 2026 at 21:29):

David Egolf (he/him) said:

So, if I understand correctly, "left adjoints preserve colimits" is a nice special case of "left adjoints preserve left Kan extensions".

Actually, I think that, in some situations, the two statements are equivalent. If I remember correctly, a left Kan extension can be defined pointwise as a colimit, provided CCC is, e.g., cocomplete.

left kan extension as pointwise colimit

Consider functors F:A→BF : A \to BF:A→B and X:A→CX : A \to CX:A→C.
We want to define a left Kan extension L=LanF:B→CL = Lan_F : B \to CL=LanF​:B→C of XXX along FFF.

Fix an object bbb in BBB. First, consider the category F↓bF \downarrow bF↓b whose objects are arrows Fa→bF a \to bFa→b, indexed by objects aaa of AAA, and morphisms are the corresponding commuting triangles, indexed by the arrows of AAA. There is a functor F↓b→AF \downarrow b \to AF↓b→A that simply gives the underlying AAA-objects and AAA-morphisms. Composing with X:A→CX : A \to CX:A→C, we obtain a diagram in CCC

Jb:F↓b→A→XCJ_b : F \downarrow b \to A \xrightarrow{X} CJb​:F↓b→AX​C

We define,

Lb=colim JbL b = \text{colim}~J_bLb=colim Jb​

A bit diagram chasing and the universal property of colimits show that LLL is indeed a left Kan extension of XXX along FFF.

John Baez (Apr 07 2026 at 23:23):

Just two tiny points:

  1. When we do colimits in enriched categories, it's almost essential to use "weighted" colimits.

  2. Colimits resemble integrals, and weighted colimits resemble them even more. When we start out learning integrals we write ∫f(x)dx\int f(x) dx∫f(x)dx but focus our attention on the f(x)f(x)f(x): the dxd xdx feels like an annoying thing the teacher makes us write down. Later we learn measure theory and write ∫f(x)dμ(x)\int f(x) d\mu(x)∫f(x)dμ(x), and we learn that the function fff and measure dμd\mudμ are equally essential: integration is a way of pairing a function and a measure and getting a number. It turns out we had been blind to the measure's importance because the real line comes with a standard choice of measure. Similarly, when we do weighted colimits we learn that the "weight" is just as important as the diagram we are taking the colimit of. In the Set-enriched case we were blinded to its importance because there was a standard choice.
