Topic: Categorification of eigenvalues and eigenvectors

fosco (Apr 20 2025 at 15:30):

I want to submit to you the following shenanigan which I find very funny to play with.

Let p:Yo×Y→Setp : Y^o\times Y\to Setp:Yo×Y→Set be an endoprofunctor on a category YYY. Let Λ\LambdaΛ a set, and denote still as Λ\LambdaΛ the costant functor at Λ\LambdaΛ.

  • Λ\LambdaΛ is an eigenvalue of ppp if the category (p/Λ)(p/\Lambda)(p/Λ) of natural transformations p⇒Λp \Rightarrow \Lambdap⇒Λ is nonempty and... something else I'd like to figure out so that what follows makes sense (and avoids vacuous truths of sort).
  • A functor F:Yop→SetF : Y^{op}\to SetF:Yop→Set is an eigenvector wrt Λ\LambdaΛ if, when Λ\LambdaΛ is an eigenvalue, for every p⇒Λp\Rightarrow\Lambdap⇒Λ in (p/Λ)(p/\Lambda)(p/Λ), there is an initial cowedge p(x,y)×Fy→Λ×Fxp(x,y)\times Fy \to \Lambda\times Fxp(x,y)×Fy→Λ×Fx (so that the "matrix product" (p⊗F)x:=∫yp(x,y)×Fy(p\otimes F)x:= \int^y p(x,y)\times Fy(p⊗F)x:=∫yp(x,y)×Fy between the matrix ppp and the vector FFF is canonically isomorphic to the product Λ×F\Lambda\times FΛ×F of the "scalar" Λ\LambdaΛ and of FFF, evaluated at xxx, as prescribed by the theory of eigenvalues/eigenvectors).

fosco (Apr 20 2025 at 15:33):

I have a hunch that connectedness, or some sort of contractibility, of (p/Λ)(p/\Lambda)(p/Λ) could be required; I also suspect the second property is too strong, but note that (for example) with this definition the eigenspace of Λ\LambdaΛ for ppp (the subcategory spanned by all eigenvectors wrt Λ\LambdaΛ) is closed under colimits, so it can't be too trivial a subcategory of the small-cocomplete category [Yop,Set][Y^{op},Set][Yop,Set]. Note also that the only eigenvector of hom is the terminal object, with eigenspace the whole category, as god intended.

fosco (Apr 30 2025 at 12:00):

Well, if you squint your eyes a profunctor is a matrix.. and the coend computation yields almost instantaneosuly that if a profunctor results as p=L×Rp=L\times Rp=L×R for a copresheaf LLL and a presheaf RRR, then ppp has eigenvalue the functor tensor product L⊗R=∫xLx×RxL\otimes R=\int^x Lx\times RxL⊗R=∫xLx×Rx, with associated eigenvector LLL.

Compare with the analogue fact of life, that if a matrix AAA results as the tensor product v⊗wv\otimes wv⊗w, then AAA has the dot product v⋅wv\cdot wv⋅w as eigenvalue, and eigenspace ⟨v⟩\langle v\rangle⟨v⟩ (the other eigenvalue is 0, with eigenspace an orthogonal complement of v... that's more difficult to categorify).
