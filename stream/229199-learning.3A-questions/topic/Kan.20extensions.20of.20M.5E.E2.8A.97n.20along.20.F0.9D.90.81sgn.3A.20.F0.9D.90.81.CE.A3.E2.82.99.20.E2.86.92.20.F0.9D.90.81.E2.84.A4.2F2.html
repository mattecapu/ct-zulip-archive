The nth symmetric power Symn(M)\mathrm{Sym}^n(M)Symn(M) of a module MMM is the colimit of the functor BΣn→ModR\mathbf{B}\Sigma_n\to\mathsf{Mod}_RBΣn​→ModR​ picking M⊗nM^{\otimes n}M⊗n and acting on it via permutations. This is the same thing as the left Kan extension of BΣn→ModR\mathbf{B}\Sigma_n\to\mathsf{Mod}_RBΣn​→ModR​ along the terminal functor BΣn→B∗\mathbf{B}\Sigma_n\to\mathbf{B}*BΣn​→B∗. So a natural question arises: what about picking monoids or groups other than ∗*∗?
