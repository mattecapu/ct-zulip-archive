Matteo Capucci (he/him) (Mar 26 2020 at 10:33):

Profunctor (horizontal) composition should be the usual composition of relations, one level up in the catification hierarchy.
That is, if you have a relation R⊆X×YR \subseteq X \times YR⊆X×Y and a relation S⊆Y×ZS \subseteq Y \times ZS⊆Y×Z, you get a relation R;S⊆X×ZR;S \subseteq X \times ZR;S⊆X×Z by defining
xR;Sziff∃y∈Y(xRy∧ySz)x R;S z \quad \text{iff} \quad \exists y \in Y (x R y \land y S z)xR;Sziff∃y∈Y(xRy∧ySz)

Matteo Capucci (he/him) (Mar 26 2020 at 10:40):

Now ∃\exists∃ are akin to coends, ∧\land∧ is akin to the monoidal product of the enriching category (e.g. ×\times× in Set\bf{Set}Set) and obviously profunctors are akin to relations. Voilà: let F:C×Dop→SetF: \bf{C} \times \bf{D^{\rm{op}}} \to \bf{Set}F:C×Dop→Set and G:D×Eop→SetG: \bf{D} \times \bf{E}^{\rm{op}} \to \bf{Set}G:D×Eop→Set, then you get F;G:C×Eop→SetF;G : \bf{C} \times \bf{E}^{\rm{op}} \to \bf{Set}F;G:C×Eop→Set by defining
F;G(c,e)=∫d:DF(c,d)×G(d,e)F;G(c,e) = \int^{d:\bf{D}} F(c,d) \times G(d,e)F;G(c,e)=∫d:DF(c,d)×G(d,e)

Mike Stay (Mar 26 2020 at 15:59):

(Any insight appreciated.)

The elements of an adjacency matrix for a graph count the paths from one vertex to another. The elements of a profunctor are the sets of heteromorphisms from one object to another rather than just the number of them. To compose the adjacency matrices of two bipartite graphs, you multiply their adjacency matrices. To compose two profunctors, you do matrix multiplication, but where multiplication is all pairwise compositions and addition is union.

To define another structure of category on relations, you can modify the composition you have given to use a disjunction instead of a conjunction. This is an associative operation and it is unital with identities {(x,y)∣x≠y}\{(x,y) | x\neq y\}{(x,y)∣x​=y}). Max New (Mar 27 2020 at 15:42):

Beppe Metere (Mar 28 2020 at 14:51):

I'd like to add a complementary point of view.

A leit-motive in introducing profunctors is considering them as a sort of 222-dimensional version of the set-theoretical notion of relation. Actually, a relation from AAA to BBB can be identified with its characteristic function A×B→2A\times B\to 2A×B→2. The dimensional shift then suggests to define a notion where the sets AAA and BBB are replaced by categories, and the set 222 is replaced by the category Set\mathbf{Set}Set of sets. Hence, a profunctor from AAA to BBB is identified with a functor A∗×B→SetA^*\times B\to \mathbf{Set}A∗×B→Set, where A∗A^*A∗ is the opposite category of AAA, which is necessary in order to deal with arrows of categories. Given these definitions, profunctor composition is the 222-dimensional version of relation composition, which has been discussed above.

Now, there is (at least) another relevant approach to the notion of profunctor, which I prefer because it is more suitable for dealing with internal structures. Let me explain it, by starting again from the easier case of relations.

The relation A×B→2A\times B\to 2A×B→2 is in fact better known as a subset of the product A×BA\times BA×B, i.e. a subobject R→A×BR\to A\times BR→A×B. Composing with product projections, one gets a span A←R→BA \leftarrow R\rightarrow BA←R→B, where the two legs of the span are jointly monic. With this definition, the composition of RRR with the another relation, say B←S→CB \leftarrow S\rightarrow CB←S→C can be obtained by taking the composition of these two spans, and then shrinking the resulting big span. More precisely one takes the pullback RSRSRS of the cospan R→RS←SR\rightarrow RS\leftarrow SR→RS←S and compose projections with the other legs in order to get the new span A←R←RS→S→CA\leftarrow R\leftarrow RS\rightarrow S\rightarrow CA←R←RS→S→C. Unfortunately, this new span is not a relation, because its two legs ar not jointly monic, but this is not a big deal, since it suffices to take the image of the joint legs map RS→A×CRS\to A\times CRS→A×C, which is precisely the composition of relations R∘SR\circ SR∘S.

Relations in Set\mathbf{Set}Set can be considered as 'relative to a factorization system' namely, the image factorization. More precisely, given sets AAA and BBB, image factorization gives the local reflections Span(A,B)→Rel(A,B)\mathbf{Span}(A,B)\to\mathbf{Rel}(A,B)Span(A,B)→Rel(A,B).

It would be nice if something similar could be done for profunctors, and indeed it is so. The easier case is that of groupoids. Groupoids are categories where all arrows are iso, and a profunctor between groupoids is defined in the same way as for categories (but in the case of groupoids its definition can be simplified). In the category of groupoids there is a very nice factorization system that collapse three different factorization which are possible for functors between categories:

  1. final / discrete fibration
  2. initial / discrete opfibration
  3. full + essentially surjective / faithful

In fact it is possible to prove that profunctors between groupoids are precisely the relations relative to such factorization system. Therefore, one can calculate their composition by taking the pullback of the corresponding spans, and the taking the 'final' image of the resulting big span. All the details can be found in a recent paper of mine:

G. Metere, Distributors and the comprehensive factorization system for internal groupoids, Theory and Applications of Categories, Vol. 34 no. 1 (2019).

...where profunctors are called 'distributors', as they where named by Jean Benabou.

Concerning categories the situation is a bit more involved, since the three factorization systems above remain distinct. In fact it is still possible to compute the composition using a factorization system, but in this case one should interpret a profunctor from AAA to BBB as its corresponding 222-sided discrete fibration R→A×BR\to A\times BR→A×B fibred over AAA (or op-fibred over BBB), and then taking the initial image in Fib(B)\mathbf{Fib}(B)Fib(B) or the final image in opFib(A)\mathbf{opFib}(A)opFib(A).
