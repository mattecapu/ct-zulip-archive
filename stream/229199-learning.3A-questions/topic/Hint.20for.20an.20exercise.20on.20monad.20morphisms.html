Topic: Hint for an exercise on monad morphisms

Adrián Doña Mateo (Feb 20 2025 at 15:03):

Exercise 4.8.7 in Vol. 2 of Borceux's Handbook, asks the following:
Let TTT and SSS be monads on C\mathcal{C}C, and λ:S→T\lambda : S \to Tλ:S→T be a morphism of monads, which induces a functor Q:CT→CSQ : \mathcal{C}^T \to \mathcal{C}^SQ:CT→CS over C\mathcal{C}C. Show that QQQ is full and faithful iff λC\lambda_CλC​ is epic for all CCC in C\mathcal{C}C.

That λ\lambdaλ being objectwise epic implies that QQQ is full and faithful follows from a simple diagram chase. I am completely stumped by the other direction though. Does anyone have any hints or ideas?

I'll say briefly what I've tried. Since QQQ is always faithful, the hypothesis says that if (C,α)(C,\alpha)(C,α) and (D,β)(D,\beta)(D,β) are TTT-algebras, and f:C→Df : C \to Df:C→D is an SSS-algebra morphism Q(C,α)→Q(D,β)Q(C,\alpha) \to Q(D,\beta)Q(C,α)→Q(D,β), then fff is also an a TTT-algebra morphism (C,α)→(D,β)(C,\alpha) \to (D,\beta)(C,α)→(D,β). To apply this hypothesis, it seems like I need to get my hands on two TTT-algebras, and the only TTT-algebras that are guaranteed to exist are the free ones. But such an argument seems like it will only be able to say something about the components of λT\lambda TλT, and not those of λ\lambdaλ.

Nathanael Arkor (Feb 20 2025 at 18:43):

I believe the statement in Borceux is incorrect: being pointwise epic is sufficient, but not necessary. The question of the induced functor being fully faithful is the topic of Karazeris–Velebil's Dense morphisms of monads. See Remark 3.5 there in particular.
