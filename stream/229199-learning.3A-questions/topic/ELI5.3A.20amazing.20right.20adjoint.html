The exponential −yI{-}^{yI}−yI on C^=SetCop\hat C = \mathrm{Set}^{C^{\mathrm{op}}}C^=SetCop has left adjoint −×yI{-} \times yI−×yI (here yyy is the Yoneda embedding). Morgan Rogers (he/him) (Oct 13 2021 at 16:48):

It's a shame you ruled out presheaves, because they provide the easiest example in my opinion, thanks to Proposition 2.5 of the nLab page.

Let C\mathcal{C}C be a small category, and consider the category of functors E:=[C,Set]\mathcal{E}:= [\mathcal{C},\mathrm{Set}]E:=[C,Set]; this is the category of "copresheaves on C\mathcal{C}C", but the essential thing is that the objects of E\mathcal{E}E are functors and the morphisms are natural transformations. For any object FFF in this category, we can consider the representable functor HomE(F,−)\mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{E}}(F,-)HomE​(F,−) to sets, which always has a left adjoint given by sending any set AAA to an AAA-indexed coproduct of copies of FFF. On the other hand, this representable functor has a right adjoint if and only if it is a (retract of a) representable copresheaf the latter being a functor of the form HomC(C,−)\mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(C,-)HomC​(C,−) for some object CCC of C\mathcal{C}C.

Morgan Rogers (he/him) (Oct 13 2021 at 16:51):

The objects whose representable functors admit right adjoints are called "tiny objects", so the above says that we can identify the representable copresheaves on a category up to retracts as the subcategory of tiny objects.

Jens Hemelaer (Oct 15 2021 at 09:24):

Yes exactly... Here is the easiest example that I could come up with. Let E\mathcal{E}E be the category that has as objects the triples (f,A,B)(f,A,B)(f,A,B) where AAA and BBB are sets and f:A→Bf : A \to Bf:A→B is a function. We look at the object j:∅→{∗}j : \varnothing \to \{\ast\}j:∅→{∗} in E\mathcal{E}E. For this object, you can compute that the functor X↦XjX \mapsto X^jX↦Xj is given as follows: if f:A→Bf : A \to Bf:A→B is an object of E\mathcal{E}E, then fjf^jfj is the identity map B→BB \to BB→B. This exponential functor has a right adjoint (the amazing right adjoint), which sends an object g:C→Dg : C \to Dg:C→D in E\mathcal{E}E to the identity function C→CC \to CC→C.

Some theoretical background (using the terminology and results from the nlab page here): E\mathcal{E}E is the topos of presheaves on the category C\mathcal{C}C with two objects and precisely one non-identity morphism (from one object to the other). Because E=PSh(C)\mathcal{E}=\mathbf{PSh}(\mathcal{C})E=PSh(C) is a sheaf topos, the exponential (−)j(-)^j(−)j has a right adjoint if and only if it preserves colimits, so if and only if jjj is internally tiny. Further, because C\mathcal{C}C is a category admitting finite products, the internally tiny and externally tiny objects in E\mathcal{E}E agree (Proposition 2.4). Now from Example 2.2 it follows that jjj is tiny, because it is a representable presheaf. Conversely, you can use Theorem 2.14 here to show that jjj and the terminal object are the only tiny objects in E\mathcal{E}E.

Steve Awodey (Oct 17 2021 at 04:05):

Here's a very simple example, the "cartesian cubical sets" that @Reid Barton already mentioned. Let BBB be the category of finite , strictly bipointed sets and C=BopC = B^{op}C=Bop the cartesian cube category - which is the free finite product category on an interval 1⇉I1 \rightrightarrows I1⇉I. Write the objects in the form [n][n][n], so I=[1]I = [1]I=[1] and [n]×[m]=[n+m][n] \times [m] = [n+m][n]×[m]=[n+m]. Presheaves on CCC are called (Cartesian) cubical sets, written cSetcSetcSet. There is a functor S:C→CS : C \to CS:C→C taking [n][n][n] to [n+1][n+1][n+1] , which induces S∗:cSet→cSetS^* : cSet \to cSetS∗:cSet→cSet by restriction, and therefore has both left S!S_!S!​ and right S∗S_*S∗​ adjoints. One can easily calculate that

S!X=X×IS!X = X\times IS!X=X×I

so its adjoint must be

S∗X=XIS^*X = X^IS∗X=XI.

The further right adjoint S∗S_*S∗​ then also exists and is therefore an "amazing right adjoint" XI⊣S∗XX^I \dashv S_*XXI⊣S∗​X. We write it as XIX_IXI​ and call it the "IthI^{th}Ith root". In particular, III itself is therefore "tiny".
