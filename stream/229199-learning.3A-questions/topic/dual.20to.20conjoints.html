Topic: dual to conjoints

Nathanael Arkor (Jul 28 2022 at 17:03):

Given a double category with companions, a conjoint to a tight arrow f ⁣:A→Bf \colon A \to Bf:A→B is a loose arrow f∗ ⁣:B↛Af^* \colon B \not\to Af∗:B→A which is right adjoint to the companion f∗ ⁣:A↛Bf_* \colon A \not\to Bf∗​:A→B. Is there a standard notion dual to conjoints, which could be defined in terms of a left adjoint to companions? A motivating example of a (pseudo) double category admitting companions and co-conjoints would be that of small categories, functors, and coprofunctors (defined in terms of free completion rather than free cocompletion).

Nathanael Arkor (Jul 28 2022 at 17:21):

I think perhaps the concept I am looking for is a conjoint in the transpose of a double category, as least when the double category is strict. Does this have a name? (It could well be that I've forgotten to flip some arrows and this turns out to be the same as a conjoint or something similar.)

Mike Shulman (Jul 29 2022 at 03:47):

I don't think there's a name or a nice characterization of that. It's related to how left adjoints of restriction in a fibration have a nice characterization in terms of opcartesian arrows, but right adjoints don't.
