Topic: groupoid of objects equipped with automorphism

John Baez (Dec 12 2024 at 17:58):

Suppose you have a groupoid XXX. Then there's groupoid A(X)A(X)A(X) where

  • an object is an object x∈Xx \in Xx∈X together with an automorphism g:x→xg: x \to xg:x→x
  • a morphism from g:x→xg: x \to xg:x→x to h:y→yh: y \to yh:y→y is an isomorphism f:x→yf : x \to yf:x→y making the obvious square commute: fg=hff g = h ffg=hf

What are some good ways to think about this construction, or interesting facts about it?

Some initial thoughts:

  1. We can get a groupoid this way if XXX is any category, but it only depends on the underlying groupoid of that category, called its [[core groupoid]], so we might as well just talk about groupoids.
  2. A(X)A(X)A(X) is the subcategory of the [[arrow category]] of XXX where the objects are only endo-arrows and the morphisms are only pairs of arrows that are equal.
  3. When XXX is the one-object groupoid corresponding to a group GGG, A(X)A(X)A(X) is the groupoid whose objects are group elements and whose morphisms f:g→hf: g \to hf:g→h are group elements such that fgf−1=hf g f^{-1} = hfgf−1=h. It's the [[weak quotient]] of GGG by itself, where it acts on itself by conjugation, so it's often called G//GG//GG//G. Since GGG acts on itself as group automorphisms, G//GG//GG//G is actually a [[2-group]], called the [[inner automorphism 2-group]] of GGG.

Mike Shulman (Dec 12 2024 at 18:00):

A(X)A(X)A(X) is also the [[powering]] of XXX by the circle groupoid, just as the arrow category is the powering by the interval category.
