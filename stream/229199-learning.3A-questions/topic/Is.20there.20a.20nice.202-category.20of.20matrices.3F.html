Topic: Is there a nice 2-category of matrices?

Peva Blanchard (Jun 08 2024 at 10:32):

I'm learning about adjunctions in 2-categories other than the 2-category of (small) categories.

From what I understand, a 1-cell f:X→Yf : X \rightarrow Yf:X→Y is a left adjoint to a 1-cell g:Y→Xg: Y \rightarrow Xg:Y→X when

  • there exists a unit 2-cell η:idX→gf\eta: id_X \rightarrow gfη:idX​→gf
  • there exists a co-unit 2-cell ϵ:fg→idY\epsilon: fg \rightarrow id_Yϵ:fg→idY​
  • and they satisfy the triangle identities.

I've been trying to define a 2-category of matrices so that adjunction would correspond to transposition, in vain. Or more precisely, I can't find a right definition for the 2-cells.

  • the 0-cells are the natural numbers 0,1,2,…0, 1, 2, \dots0,1,2,…
  • a 1-cell M:n→mM: n \rightarrow mM:n→m is a (e.g., real-valued) matrices with mmm rows and nnn columns
  • and 2-cells are ???

If I think of a matrix as a profunctor enriched in V=(R,≤,+,0)\mathcal{V} = (\mathbb{R}, \le, +, 0)V=(R,≤,+,0), then 2-cells would correspond to V\mathcal{V}V natural transformations. Unfolding the definition, this means that there is a (unique) 2-cell A⇒BA \Rightarrow BA⇒B iff Aij≤BijA_{ij} \le B_{ij}Aij​≤Bij​ for all indices i,ji,ji,j.

But this definition doesn't seem to yield an adjunction between a matrix AAA and its transposition ATA^TAT.

Hence my question: is it possible to define a 2-category of matrices so that adjunction corresponds to transposition?

John Baez (Jun 08 2024 at 15:09):

A rather famous 2-category of with matrices as morphisms is the 2-category of [[Kapranov-Voevodsky 2-vector spaces]].

John Baez (Jun 08 2024 at 15:10):

The objects of this 2-category are categories of the form Vectn\mathsf{Vect}^nVectn, and the morphisms are m×nm \times nm×n matrices of vector spaces, and the 2-morphisms are m×nm \times nm×n matrices of linear maps.

John Baez (Jun 08 2024 at 15:12):

If we loosen up the definition to include are categories equivalent to Vectn\mathsf{Vect}^nVectn, as we should, then any category of representations of a finite group is a Kapranov-Voevodsky 2-vector space.

John Baez (Jun 08 2024 at 15:14):

We get the math of 2-vector spaces by taking the math of vector spaces replacing C\mathbb{C}C (or your favorite field) by Vect\mathbf{Vect}Vect, replacing +++ by ⊕\oplus⊕, and replacing ×\times× by ⊗\otimes⊗. So we are 'categorifying' linear algebra.

The 2-category of 2-vector spaces, usually called 2Vect\mathbf{2Vect}2Vect, has the property you hint at: every 1-morphism has both a left and right adjoint.
