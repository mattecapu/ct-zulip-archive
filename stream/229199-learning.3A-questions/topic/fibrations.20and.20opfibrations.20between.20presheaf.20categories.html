Topic: fibrations and opfibrations between presheaf categories

John Baez (Dec 27 2023 at 21:55):

Suppose I have a functor j:C→Dj: C \to Dj:C→D between small categories. This induces a functor between the presheaf categories

j∗:SetDop→SetCop j^\ast : \mathsf{Set}^{D^{\text{op}}} \to \mathsf{Set}^{C^{\text{op}}} j∗:SetDop→SetCop

called restriction along jjj: on objects it sends f:Cop→Setf : C^{\text{op}} \to \mathsf{Set}f:Cop→Set to f∘jf \circ jf∘j, and it does something similar on morphisms.

What I'd like is necessary and/or sufficient conditions for j∗j^\astj∗ to be a fibration or opfibration!

James Deikun (Dec 27 2023 at 23:52):

A fibration p:E→Bp : E \to Bp:E→B can be characterized in terms of the following square:

E→→p→B→cod↓↓codE→pB\begin{CD} \overrightarrow{E} @>{\overrightarrow{p}}>> \overrightarrow{B} \\ @V{cod}VV @VV{cod}V \\ E @>>{p}> B \end{CD}Ecod↓⏐​E​p​​p​​B↓⏐​codB​

If the comparison arrow that measures how far this square is from being a strict pullback has a right adjoint right inverse, ppp is a cloven fibration.

James Deikun (Dec 28 2023 at 00:12):

In the case you're interested in, this is determined by another square:

C→jDC×0↓↓D×0C×→→j×→D×→\begin{CD} C @>{j}>> D \\ @V{C \times 0}VV @VV{D \times 0}V \\ C \times \rightarrow @>>{j \times \rightarrow}> D \times \rightarrow \end{CD}CC×0↓⏐​C×→​j​j×→​​D↓⏐​D×0D×→​

If the comparison determining how far this is from a pushout square -- the [[pushout product]] of jjj and 000 -- induces, under the free cocompletion functor, an arrow with a right adjoint right inverse, then j∗j^*j∗ is a cloven fibration. One way for this to happen is if this arrow itself has (if I got the variances right) a right adjoint right inverse, but it can also happen because of limits existing in Set\mathsf{Set}Set, an example being the codomain fibration of Set\mathsf{Set}Set.

James Deikun (Dec 28 2023 at 01:16):

I think the extra condition in question is that the Kan extensions in question should be genuine extensions, i.e. the restriction of the extension of a presheaf should give back the original presheaf.

Then the formula for the Cartesian lift is you take your arrow in SetCop\mathsf{Set}^{C^{\mathrm{op}}}SetCop and your DDD-presheaf over the target and combine them into a presheaf in Set((C×→)⨿CD)op\mathsf{Set}^{((C \times \rightarrow) \amalg_C D)^{\mathrm{op}}}Set((C×→)⨿C​D)op and Kan extend it to a presheaf in Set(D×→)op\mathsf{Set}^{(D \times \rightarrow)^{\mathrm{op}}}Set(D×→)op which you interpret as the lifted arrow. The lift, assuming it truly is one, is Cartesian because of the universal property of Kan extensions.

James Deikun (Dec 28 2023 at 08:53):

In particular, since pointwise extensions into Set\mathsf{Set}Set always exist, being a fully faithful functor should be a sufficient condition in itself.

Mike Shulman (Dec 28 2023 at 09:06):

Another way to see that jjj being fully faithful is sufficient is to use the fact mentioned here (Theorem 2) that any pullback-preserving functor with a fully faithful right adjoint is a fibration, and dually. Since the Kan extension functors along a fully faithful functor are fully faithful, and restriction preserves all limits and colimits, the result follows.

Mike Shulman (Dec 28 2023 at 09:12):

Conversely, Theorem 1 at that link says that a fibration that preserves terminal objects must have a fully faithful right adjoint, and dually. Thus, if j∗j^*j∗ is a fibration, then Ranj\mathrm{Ran}_jRanj​ is fully faithful, and if j∗j^*j∗ is an opfibration, then Lanj\mathrm{Lan}_jLanj​ is fully faithful. I think Lanj\mathrm{Lan}_jLanj​ being fully faithful actually implies jjj is fully faithful, since Lanj\mathrm{Lan}_jLanj​ restricts to jjj on representables and the Yoneda embedding is fully faithful. Not sure about Ranj\mathrm{Ran}_jRanj​, but it seems like full-faithfulness of Ranj\mathrm{Ran}_jRanj​ shouldn't be much weaker than full-faithfulness of jjj.
