Topic: Universal property of the classifying topos

Leopold Schlicht (Mar 17 2022 at 17:54):

Let TTT be a classical first-order theory (in the sense of model theory: finitary and Boolean). Then the classifying topos ET\mathcal E_TET​ of TTT is the classifying topos of the Morleyization of TTT. Does that topos have a universal property that can be formulated without referring to the Morleyization?

I think for Boolean topoi E\mathcal EE we have Geom(E,ET)≃TMod(E)e\mathrm{Geom}(\mathcal E, \mathcal E_T)\simeq T\mathrm{Mod}(\mathcal E)_eGeom(E,ET​)≃TMod(E)e​ (the subscript eee indicates that the morphisms are elementary embeddings), is that correct?
