Topic: Minima and maxima in Archimedean ordered fields

Madeleine Birchfield (Feb 07 2024 at 15:10):

In constructive mathematics, does every Archimedean ordered (Heyting) field have binary meets (minima) and joins (maxima) for the poset relation defined by the negation of the strict order x<y≔¬(y<x)x \lt y \coloneqq \neg (y \lt x)x<y:=¬(y<x)? (Unlike some authors, for the purposes of this question we assume that Archimedean ordered fields do not have binary meets and joins)

Andrew Swan (Feb 07 2024 at 23:58):

In the topos of sheaves on R\mathbb{R}R you have an object of the sheaf of smooth functions R→R\mathbb{R} \to \mathbb{R}R→R. I think that would work as an ordered field where you don't have meets and joins. I'll think about it again in the morning and see if that still makes sense.

Madeleine Birchfield (Feb 08 2024 at 01:11):

The sheaf of smooth functions R→R\mathbb{R} \to \mathbb{R}R→R in the topos may be an ordered field without meets and joins, but is it Archimedean?

Andrew Swan (Feb 08 2024 at 09:59):

Yes, it is also Archimedean. It's a subsheaf of continuous functions R→R\mathbb{R} \to \mathbb{R}R→R, which is the Dedekind real numbers in the topos. Since it's a sub ordered field of Rd\mathbb{R}_dRd​ and that is Archimedean, it would be as well, I think, but you can also see it directly - you just have to show that for every point xxx of R\mathbb{R}R there is a number nnn and an open neighbourhood UUU such that f(x′)<nf(x') < nf(x′)<n for x′∈Ux' \in Ux′∈U.

Andrew Swan (Feb 08 2024 at 10:27):

Oh, I see you posted on mathoverflow as well - I'll put the same answer there for reference.

Madeleine Birchfield (Feb 08 2024 at 14:25):

Also, do the Cauchy real numbers form an ordered subfield of your example Archimedean ordered field in your topos?

Andrew Swan (Feb 08 2024 at 15:04):

Yes, the Cauchy reals are the constant functions R→R\mathbb{R} \to \mathbb{R}R→R, which are smooth.
