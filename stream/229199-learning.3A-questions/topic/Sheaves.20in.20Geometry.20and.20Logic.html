Paolo Capriotti (May 28 2020 at 10:19):

Soichiro Fujii said:

The poset of partial sections amounts to T⊆SubE(X)\mathcal{T}\subseteq \mathrm{Sub}_\mathcal{E}(X)T⊆SubE​(X) consisting of all s ⁣:M↣Xs\colon M\rightarrowtail Xs:M↣X such that ps ⁣:M→Ips\colon M\to Ips:M→I is a monomorphism. Now consider a chain (sj ⁣:Mj↣X)j∈J(s_j\colon M_j\rightarrowtail X)_{j\in J}(sj​:Mj​↣X)j∈J​ in T\mathcal{T}T. We can certainly take its union s ⁣:M↣Xs\colon M\rightarrowtail Xs:M↣X in SubE(X)\mathrm{Sub}_\mathcal{E}(X)SubE​(X). Then the question is: is it true that pspsps is mono, provided that each psjps_jpsj​ is?

Yes. The union of a chain is a filtered colimit, and since filtered colimits preserve monomorphisms, sss is also the colimit of sjs_jsj​ in E/X\mathcal{E}/XE/X. Now, composing with ppp preserves all colimits (it is left adjoint to pullback), hence pspsps is the colimit of psjp s_jpsj​ in E/I\mathcal{E}/IE/I, and so it is a monomorphism.

However, I find it slightly more intuitive to just take the union t:M→It: M \to It:M→I of the chain psjp s_jpsj​ directly. Then by the same argument this is a colimit in E/I\mathcal{E}/IE/I as well, which means you can lift it to a map s:M→Xs: M \to Xs:M→X such that ps=tps = tps=t.

Paolo Capriotti (May 29 2020 at 09:16):

Soichiro Fujii said:

However, if I understand correctly, your argument presupposes that E\mathcal{E}E has filtered colimits...? Of course the union of a chain in SubE(X)\mathrm{Sub}_\mathcal{E}(X)SubE​(X) is a filtered colimit in SubE(X)\mathrm{Sub}_\mathcal{E}(X)SubE​(X), and (as you pointed out) it is necessarily given by a filtered colimit in E\mathcal{E}E if E\mathcal{E}E has that filtered colimit, but does it still hold even without the a priori assumption that the filtered colimit exists in E\mathcal{E}E?

Good point, I did assume that E\mathcal EE had filtered colimits. The missing part is that filtered colimits in Sub(X)\mathrm{Sub}(X)Sub(X) are also colimits in E/X\mathcal{E}/XE/X. Here is a direct proof, although I suspect there are more elegant ways. Let MMM be the filtered colimit of MiM_iMi​ in Sub(X)\mathrm{Sub}(X)Sub(X). It is enough to prove that MMM is the colimit of MiM_iMi​ in E\mathcal EE. So let gi:Mi→Yg_i: M_i \to Ygi​:Mi​→Y be a cocone. Our goal is to find an extension M→YM \to YM→Y (uniqueness will follow easily by taking an equaliser). Let SiS_iSi​ be the "graph" of gig_igi​, i.e. the subobject of Mi×YM_i \times YMi​×Y corresponding to the morphism gi′:Mi→Y→PYg_i': M_i \to Y \to PYgi′​:Mi​→Y→PY through the power object adjunction. Since Mi×Y→X×YM_i \times Y \to X \times YMi​×Y→X×Y is mono, SiS_iSi​ can be regarded as a suboject of X×YX \times YX×Y. Take the union SSS of those subobjects. The key fact, which uses filteredness, is that the pullback of SSS to Mi×YM_i \times YMi​×Y is SiS_iSi​. Therefore, if g′:X→PYg': X \to PYg′:X→PY is the morphism corresponding to SSS, we get that g′g'g′ restricts to gi′g_i'gi′​ for all iii. Define g:X1→Yg: X_1 \to Yg:X1​→Y by pulling back g′g'g′ along Y→PYY \to PYY→PY. Now X1→XX_1 \to XX1​→X is a monomorphism, so we get a map M→X1M \to X_1M→X1​, and composing with ggg gives the sought extension.
