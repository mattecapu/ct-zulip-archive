Topic: Oppositization functor for Prof

Asad Saeeduddin (Jul 16 2020 at 09:42):

In Cat\mathbf{Cat}Cat, we have a mapping of 0-cells given by taking the opposite of categories, a mapping of 1-cells given by taking the opposite of functors (the action on objects and morphisms of the resulting functor is identical), and a mapping of 2-cells given by taking the opposite natural transformation (the families of morphisms selected by the resulting natural transformation are identical). Taken together, this gives us a 2-functor (−)op:Catco→Cat(-)^{\mathbf{op}} : \mathbf{Cat^{co}} \to \mathbf{Cat}(−)op:Catco→Cat, where Catco\mathbf{Cat^{co}}Catco is a 2-category similar to Cat\mathbf{Cat}Cat, except with a 2-cell η:F→G\eta : F \to Gη:F→G taken to mean a natural transformation from GGG to FFF.

Is it possible to extend this concept to 2-categories besides Cat\mathbf{Cat}Cat? E.g. there should be a similar pseudofunctor for the bicategory Prof\mathbf{Prof}Prof, no?

Nathanael Arkor (Jul 16 2020 at 10:36):

Yes, this is called a duality involution. Mike Shulman's Contravariance through enrichment could be a good place to start if you want to learn more.

Nathanael Arkor (Jul 20 2020 at 12:52):

Map(Prof) has a duality involution because Prof is a compact closed bicategory (see the second paragraph of page 5 of ibid.), which suggests Prof itself may not (at least as a bicategory).

Nathanael Arkor (Jul 20 2020 at 12:54):

But it seems plausible that Prof could have a duality involution as a pseudo-double category/proarrow equipment. I'm not sure if this has been worked out. Weber's Yoneda structures from 2-toposes has some suggestive formulations (e.g. see Definition 2.14).

Reid Barton (Jul 20 2020 at 13:09):

I think there's a different kind of duality (−)op:Profop→Prof(-)^{\mathbf{op}} : \mathbf{Prof}^\mathbf{op} \to \mathbf{Prof}(−)op:Profop→Prof. (If you represent a profunctor from CCC to DDD as a functor C×Dop→SetC \times D^\mathbf{op} \to \mathrm{Set}C×Dop→Set, then you can also regard it as a functor Dop×C→SetD^\mathbf{op} \times C \to \mathrm{Set}Dop×C→Set, that is, a profunctor from DopD^\mathbf{op}Dop to CopC^\mathbf{op}Cop.)

Reid Barton (Jul 20 2020 at 13:12):

Or a fancier perspective is that CCC and CopC^\mathbf{op}Cop are actually dual objects in Prof\mathbf{Prof}Prof, and this operation is the duality functor.

Asad Saeeduddin (Jul 20 2020 at 13:14):

Reid Barton (Jul 20 2020 at 13:15):

In general C×DopC \times D^\mathbf{op}C×Dop and Dop×CD^\mathbf{op} \times CDop×C are equivalent categories (of course), while C×DopC \times D^\mathbf{op}C×Dop and Cop×DC^\mathbf{op} \times DCop×D are not

Reid Barton (Jul 20 2020 at 13:17):

So you can have an identification Prof(C,D)=Prof(Dop,Cop)\mathbf{Prof}(C, D) = \mathbf{Prof}(D^\mathbf{op}, C^\mathbf{op})Prof(C,D)=Prof(Dop,Cop) but not an identification Prof(C,D)=Prof(Cop,Dop)\mathbf{Prof}(C, D) = \mathbf{Prof}(C^\mathbf{op}, D^\mathbf{op})Prof(C,D)=Prof(Cop,Dop)
