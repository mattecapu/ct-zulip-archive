Topic: Simplicial sets - where does this reasoning fail?

James Deikun (Jan 16 2024 at 18:43):

Okay, so here is a proof that the simplex category has no nontrivial Grothendieck topologies. Subtoposes of a presheaf topos correspond directly to Grothendieck topologies. So, the [[lattice of subtoposes]] of the topos of simplicial sets consists of bottom and a single atom.

Atoms of the lattice of subtoposes correspond to two-valued Boolean subtoposes. So the topos of simplicial sets is two-valued and Boolean, and in particular is Boolean. In a [[Boolean topos]], the subobject classifier in 1+11+11+1.

But the subobject classifier in simplicial sets is manifestly not isomorphic to 1+11+11+1. If it were, there would be exactly 2 sieves on each representable, while it is easy to exhibit 3 of them, for example, for the 1-simplex.
