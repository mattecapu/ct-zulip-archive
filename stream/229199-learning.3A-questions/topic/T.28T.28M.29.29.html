John Baez (Feb 25 2022 at 00:04):

Physicists think a lot about T(T(M)). A point in M can represent the position qqq of a particle. A point (q,v)∈(q,v) \in(q,v)∈ T(M) then represents its position and velocity. In the Lagrangian approach to dynamics, the way position and velocity change over time is described by a vector field on T(T(M)). So, for any point (q,v)(q,v)(q,v) in T(M) we get a point in T(T(M)) that describes the rate at which position and velocity change over time. Usually a consistency condition holds, which says that the rate at which position changes is equal to the velocity!

To think about these things mathematically, it's worth noting that like any bundle, there's a projection

πM:T(M)→M \pi_M : T(M) \to MπM​:T(M)→M

Taking the differential of πM\pi_MπM​, we get

dπM:T(T(M))→T(M)d \pi_M : T(T(M)) \to T(M)dπM​:T(T(M))→T(M).

(We could call this ddd here "TTT" to indicate that it's just the functor TTT applied to a morphism, but geometers don't do this.)

But also, because T(T(M))T(T(M))T(T(M)) is a bundle over T(M)T(M)T(M), we have a map

πT(M):T(T(M))→T(M) \pi_{T(M)} : T(T(M)) \to T(M)πT(M)​:T(T(M))→T(M)

So, you should think about comparing these two ways to get a guy in T(M)T(M)T(M) from a guy in T(T(M))T(T(M))T(T(M)). They're not equal!

Paolo Perrone (Feb 25 2022 at 12:21):

Every curve on MMM of coordinates {mi(t)}\{m_i(t)\}{mi​(t)} also gives a curve on TMTMTM by looking at the tangent vectors, that is, coordinates {mi(t),m˙j(t)}\{m_i(t), \dot{m}_j(t)\}{mi​(t),m˙j​(t)}. Iterating the procedure we get a curve on TTMTTMTTM but you see that, this way, the second and third components in TTMTTMTTM are going to be equal. (This is due to the fact that we started from MMM.)
Indeed in geometry (differential, symplectic, contact geometry,...) is interesting to see which vector fields on TTMTTMTTM come from motions on MMM and which do not -- and one can ask for similar questions on other bundles as well.
