Topic: biinterpretations and automorphisms

Oscar Cunningham (Sep 01 2021 at 08:19):

I'm trying to understand the effect that a biinterpretation between two first order theories has on the automorphisms of their models.

My expectation was that if models of each theory correspond to each other under the biinterpretation, then they have isomophic automorphism groups. This seems to agree with the nLab here saying that 'bi-interpretable theories have equivalent categories of models'.

However, consider TRT_RTR​ the theory of real closed fields, and TCT_CTC​ the corresponding theory for complex numbers, where importantly we include conjugation in the language of TCT_CTC​. I believe TCT_CTC​ can be stated as the theory of algebraically closed fields equipped with a self-inverse automorphism z↦zˉz\mapsto\bar{z}z↦zˉ such that zzˉ=−1z\bar{z}=-1zzˉ=−1 has no solutions. Then TRT_RTR​ is interpretable in TCT_CTC​ by sending a model of TCT_CTC​ to the fixed points of z↦zˉz\mapsto\bar{z}z↦zˉ, and TCT_CTC​ is interpretable in TRT_RTR​ by taking a model of TRT_RTR​ and equipping pairs (r0,r1)(r_0,r_1)(r0​,r1​) with operations to make them act like r0+r1ir_0 + r_1ir0​+r1​i. And it seems clear to me that this is a biinterpretation. Indeed Joel David Hamkins and Noah Schweber say so here.

But R\mathbb RR has only the trivial automorphism, whereas C\mathbb CC also has complex conjugation. What gives?

Jens Hemelaer (Sep 01 2021 at 08:56):

Oscar Cunningham (Sep 01 2021 at 09:01):

I think the relevant equation for an automorphism fff respecting conjugation isn't f(zˉ)=f(z)f(\bar{z}) = f(z)f(zˉ)=f(z) but rather f(zˉ)=f(z)‾f(\bar{z}) = \overline{f(z)}f(zˉ)=f(z)​. So this holds for conjugation itself since zˉ‾=zˉ‾\overline{\bar{z}} = \overline{\bar{z}}zˉ=zˉ.

Unfortunately, the word "biinterpretable" seems to be used by many people to mean that the two interpretations are inverse to each other. This constantly trips me up. So under that definition, your two theories are not "biinterpretable" even though they are "mutually interpretable".
