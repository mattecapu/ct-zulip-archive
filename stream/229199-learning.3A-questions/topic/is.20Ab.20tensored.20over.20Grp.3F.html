If you have a reflective subcategory of Ab with additive reflector, the reflector preserves tensors (and weighted colimits more generally). You can use that to figure out what the tensor is.

Reid Barton (Oct 19 2021 at 12:33):

What's going on is this. Let's write RRR for the ring Z/p\mathbb{Z}/pZ/p. The category of RRR-modules is actually a full subcategory of abelian groups, which I think is equivalent to RRR being a solid ring (m:R⊗R→Rm : R \otimes R \to Rm:R⊗R→R is an isomorphism). So the tensor product of a Z\mathbb{Z}Z-module and an RRR-module is an RRR-module. We can also calculate that tensor product A⊗TA \otimes TA⊗T by replacing AAA by R⊗AR \otimes AR⊗A = the free RRR-module on AAA first, if we like--in this case that's A/pAA/pAA/pA.

Mike Shulman (Oct 19 2021 at 16:01):

More generally, if CCC is any category and DDD is a reflective subcategory of CCC with a closed monoidal structure, then DDD has this sort of "tensors" over CCC. If LLL is the reflector and c∈Cc\in Cc∈C, d1,d2∈Dd_1,d_2\in Dd1​,d2​∈D, then D(Lc⊗d1,d2)≅D(Lc,[d1,d2])≅C(c,[d1,d2])D(Lc \otimes d_1, d_2) \cong D(Lc, [d_1,d_2]) \cong C(c,[d_1,d_2])D(Lc⊗d1​,d2​)≅D(Lc,[d1​,d2​])≅C(c,[d1​,d2​]).

However, the name "tensor" (or the more modern name "copower") is not really appropriate here, because DDD is not enriched over CCC, and indeed that question doesn't even make sense to ask because CCC may not be a monoidal category. In particular, in the original question, Gp\mathsf{Gp}Gp is not a monoidal category in any relevant way, and so Ab\mathsf{Ab}Ab can't be enriched over it.

However, if CCC is a closed monoidal category and DDD is a reflective subcategory closed under the tensor product and internal-hom, then DDD does become enriched over CCC and this gives its copowers. That's probably what's happening for ppp-torsion abelian groups.

Reid Barton (Oct 19 2021 at 20:11):

Mike Shulman said:

Gp\mathsf{Gp}Gp is not a monoidal category in any relevant way

Right, of course. But if we consider the graded category (= family of categories) (Cn)n≥0(C_n)_{n \ge 0}(Cn​)n≥0​ with CnC_nCn​ = nnn-fold group objects in Set, then the whole graded category has a "graded monoidal" structure involving functors ⊗n,m:Cn×Cm→Cn+m\otimes_{n,m} : C_n \times C_m \to C_{n+m}⊗n,m​:Cn​×Cm​→Cn+m​, of which the functor ⊙\odot⊙ under discussion is one component--actually many components, the functors ⊗1,m\otimes_{1,m}⊗1,m​ for any m≥2m \ge 2m≥2. So it does make sense to think of ⊙\odot⊙ as a kind of tensor product, insofar as we also consider the components of an action of a graded algebra on a graded module as a kind of multiplication.
