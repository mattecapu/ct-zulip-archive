Madeleine Birchfield (Jun 24 2024 at 20:21):

I think we might be able to prove that the higher inductive Cauchy reals constructed in section 11.3 of the HoTT book are a discrete type in real-cohesive HoTT like the usual Cauchy reals, assuming crisp LEM and C1. (I asked @Mike Shulman about this a few years ago but he didn't know how to prove it at the time.)

The idea is that the LPO for natural numbers holds for cohesive types in real-cohesive HoTT if crisp LEM and C1 holds, according to theorem 8.29 in Shulman's Brouwer's fixed point theorem in real-cohesive HoTT paper. Let us use RC\mathbb{R}_CRC​ for the Cauchy real numbers, RH\mathbb{R}_HRH​ for the higher inductive type reals defined in section 11.3 of the HoTT book, R2\mathbb{R}_2R2​ for the Dedekind real numbers constructed using decidable Dedekind cuts, which is allowed because LPO implies that the booleans are a sigma-frame, and RD\mathbb{R}_DRD​ for the usual Dedekind real numbers. We have inclusions of Archimedean ordered fields

RC⊆RH⊆R2⊆RD\mathbb{R}_C \subseteq \mathbb{R}_H \subseteq \mathbb{R}_2 \subseteq \mathbb{R}_DRC​⊆RH​⊆R2​⊆RD​

The LPO also implies that R2\mathbb{R}_2R2​ has decidable strict order, which is the analytic LPO for R2\mathbb{R}_2R2​, which in turns implies that R2\mathbb{R}_2R2​ is isomorphic to both RC\mathbb{R}_CRC​ and RH\mathbb{R}_HRH​ in the cohesive mode via the proof in corollary 11.4.3 in the HoTT book. Since C1 implies that RC\mathbb{R}_CRC​ is a discrete type by theorem 8.26, this implies that RH\mathbb{R}_HRH​ is a discrete type too.

This also implies that RC\mathbb{R}_CRC​ is Cauchy complete in the cohesive mode with only crisp excluded middle. One doesn't need the full crisp axiom of choice as used in theorem 8.27.

However, the other three notions of real numbers are still not equivalent to the usual Dedekind real numbers if R♭\mathbb{R} \flatR♭ holds.

Madeleine Birchfield (May 30 2025 at 20:06):

Madeleine Birchfield said:

Another question: is it consistent that every function on the initial σ\sigmaσ-frame is monotonic?

Yes. Let Σ\SigmaΣ denote the initial σ\sigmaσ-frame. (I'm assuming that we're defining Sierpinski space as the initial σ\sigmaσ-frame following Bidlingmaier et al in https://arxiv.org/abs/1912.07339.) According to section 10 of https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.13096, synthetic quasi-coherence for countably presented Σ\SigmaΣ-algebras holds in the sheaf topos on the category of countably presented σ\sigmaσ-frames with any admissible topology, which implies that every function on the initial σ\sigmaσ-frame is monotonic.
