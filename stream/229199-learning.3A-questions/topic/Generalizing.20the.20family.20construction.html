David Corfield (May 27 2025 at 08:48):

Speaking rather loosely, could one take the following to be a useful construction when looking to have spatially-indexed quantities?

When you have some category of quantities, CCC, Lawvere tell us that copresheaves [C,Set][C, Set][C,Set] are generalized quantities. More generally, take some topos-like DDD and form [C,D][C, D][C,D]. Then it's useful to have 'spatially'-indexed such generalized quantities, so form [C▹,D][C^{\triangleright}, D][C▹,D], which will generate quantities indexed over spatial objects of DDD, the images of the strict terminal object.
