Ralph Sarkis (Apr 23 2023 at 12:15):

This does not answer your question but it points towards an easier angle, can we find a way to see initial morphisms as initial objects of some category. Let me reproduce the definition of initial morphism because I don't think it is well-known and it will save other readers a trip to their copy of the book.

Definition: In a category A\mathbf{A}A concrete over X\mathbf{X}X with forgetful functor UUU, a morphism f∈HomA(A,B)f \in \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathbf{A}}(A,B)f∈HomA​(A,B) is an initial morphism provided that for every object C∈Ob(A)C \in \mathrm{Ob}(\mathbf{A})C∈Ob(A) and morphism g∈HomX(UC,UA)g \in \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathbf{X}}(UC,UA)g∈HomX​(UC,UA), if Uf∘gUf \circ gUf∘g is in the image of HomA(C,B)\mathrm{Hom}_{\mathbf{A}}(C,B)HomA​(C,B) under UUU then ggg is in the image of HomA(C,A)\mathrm{Hom}_{\mathbf{A}}(C,A)HomA​(C,A) under UUU.

Ralph Sarkis (Apr 23 2023 at 12:16):

To get more intuition, my favorite examples are in concrete categories over Set\mathbf{Set}Set:

  • In the category of posets, the initial morphisms are functions that are order-embeddings, i.e. a≤b⇔f(a)≤f(b)a \leq b \Leftrightarrow f(a) \leq f(b)a≤b⇔f(a)≤f(b) (which forces injectivity, but that is not the important part for initial morphisms).
  • In the category of metric spaces and nonexpansive maps, the initial morphisms are isometries (i.e. d(a,b)=d(f(a),f(b))d(a,b) = d(f(a),f(b))d(a,b)=d(f(a),f(b)) (same remark for injectivity).

Informally, an initial morphism (for concrete categories over Set\mathbf{Set}Set) is a function fff that preserves properties such that when you precompose it with any function ggg, the result preserves properties if and only if ggg preserves properties.

Ralph Sarkis (Apr 24 2023 at 08:52):

Here is an attempt at making it fit my intuition for genuine universal mapping property. I will do it for III being a singleton for simplicity.

Let (A,U)(\mathbf{A},U)(A,U) be concrete and f:A→Bf: A \to Bf:A→B a morphism in A\mathbf{A}A (also called a singleton source). We define the category of lifts of fff as follows.

  • Objects are pairs of morphisms g:UC→UAg: UC \to UAg:UC→UA and h:C→Bh: C \to Bh:C→B such that Uf∘g=UhUf \circ g = UhUf∘g=Uh. Since UUU is faithful, we can also say an object is a morphism g:UC→UAg: UC \to UAg:UC→UA such that there exists h:C→Bh: C \to Bh:C→B satisfying Uf∘g=hUf \circ g = hUf∘g=h.
  • Morphisms between g:UC→UAg:UC \to UAg:UC→UA and g′:UC′→UAg': UC' \to UAg′:UC′→UA are morphisms m:C→C′m: C \to C'm:C→C′ such that h′∘m=hh' \circ m = hh′∘m=h and g′∘Um=gg' \circ Um = gg′∘Um=g.

Now, for any singleton source f:A→Bf: A \to Bf:A→B, idUA:UA→UA\mathrm{id}_{UA} : UA \to UAidUA​:UA→UA is in the category of lifts because there exists h=fh = fh=f such that Uf∘idUA=UhUf \circ \mathrm{id}_{UA} = UhUf∘idUA​=Uh. A singleton source f:A→Bf: A \to Bf:A→B is UUU-initial if and only if idUA\mathrm{id}_{UA}idUA​ is final in the category of lifts. Let me unroll the two sides to make it clear why they are equivalent:

  • fff is UUU-initial means for every g:UC→UAg: UC \to UAg:UC→UA such that there is h:C→Bh: C \to Bh:C→B satisfying Uh=Uf∘gUh = Uf \circ gUh=Uf∘g, there exists a unique gˉ:C→A\bar{g}: C \to Agˉ​:C→A such that h=f∘gˉh = f \circ \bar{g}h=f∘gˉ​ and Ugˉ=gU\bar{g} = gUgˉ​=g (Definition 10.57 in Joy of Cats).
  • idUA\mathrm{id}_{UA}idUA​ is final in the category of lifts means that for every object in that category, i.e. g:UC→UAg: UC \to UAg:UC→UA such that there is h:C→Bh: C \to Bh:C→B satisfying Uh=Uf∘gUh = Uf \circ gUh=Uf∘g, there exists a unique morphism from ggg to idUA\mathrm{id}_{UA}idUA​, that is a unique morphism gˉ:C→A\bar{g}: C \to Agˉ​:C→A such that $$f \circ \bar{g} = h$ and idUA∘Ugˉ=g\mathrm{id}_{UA} \circ U\bar{g} = gidUA​∘Ugˉ​=g or equivalently Ugˉ=gU\bar{g} = gUgˉ​=g.
