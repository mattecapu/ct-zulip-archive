Topic: how to argue quotient maps form a natural transformation?

I think that the maps qA:F(A)→I(A)q_A : F(A) \to I(A)qA​:F(A)→I(A) are components of a natural transformation q:F⇒Iq: F \Rightarrow Iq:F⇒I. (I write natural transformations with a double arrow.) You can then right whisker this natural transformation with the functor Ω\OmegaΩ to get a natural transformation q∘Ω:F∘Ω⇒I∘Ωq \circ \Omega: F \circ \Omega \Rightarrow I\circ \Omegaq∘Ω:F∘Ω⇒I∘Ω.
