A filter, as used here, is a subset of a powerset which is closed under finite intersections and supersets; i.e. given a set SSS, a filter FFF satisfies the following conditions:

  1. The improper subset of SSS is in the filter FFF
  2. If subsets AAA and BBB of SSS are in FFF, then its intersection A∩BA \cap BA∩B is also in FFF
  3. Given subsets AAA and BBB of SSS, if AAA is in FFF and A⊆BA \subseteq BA⊆B, then BBB is also in FFF.

Then since filters of SSS are subsets of the powerset of SSS, they are elements of the double powerset of SSS, and one can define the set of all filters as a particular subset of the double powerset of SSS - whose elements are the ones satisfying the conditions above.
