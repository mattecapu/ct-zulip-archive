Topic: ✔ Set-toposes are cocomplete?

David Michael Roberts (May 31 2023 at 09:57):

Jens Hemelaer (May 31 2023 at 13:37):

I think Example B.3.1.8(b) in the Elephant gives a counterexample then: if GGG is a topological group, then there is a topos Unif(G)\mathbf{Unif}(G)Unif(G) of uniformly continuous GGG-sets. There is a geometric morphism Unif(G)→Sets\mathbf{Unif}(G) \to \mathbf{Sets}Unif(G)→Sets and this geometric morphism is bounded if and only if Unif(G)\mathbf{Unif}(G)Unif(G) has small coproducts, which is the case if and only if GGG has a smallest open subgroup.

David Michael Roberts (May 31 2023 at 23:44):

OK, so here's an example: take for GGG the infinite product (Z/2)N(\mathbb{Z}/2)^{\mathbb{N}}(Z/2)N with the normal filter of open subgroups given by the finite-index subgroups. A GGG-set has uniformly continuous action precisely when there is an upper bound on the size of its orbits (edit: this is wrong: it's necessary, but not sufficient. You also need the factors sufficiently far down the line, uniformly for all orbits, to act trivially). So one has lots of infinite coproducts, for example finite coproducts of infinite coproducts of the form ∐i∈IX\coprod_{i\in I} X∐i∈I​X, but not arbitrary coproducts. Since this is locally small the global sections functor from Unif(G)Unif(G)Unif(G) exists and is the direct image part of a geometric morphism to Set\mathbf{Set}Set. This topos has an nno, inheriting it from Set\mathbf{Set}Set, I believe.
