John Baez (Jun 09 2025 at 17:32):

Yes. Here's a nice example of that 3-cocycle business:

The category of (Z/2)3(\mathbb{Z}/2)^3(Z/2)3-graded vector spaces, with its usual tensor product - I could explain it if asked - has at least two different associators α\alphaα which together with the 'usual' λ\lambdaλ and ρ\rhoρ obey the pentagon and triangle identities. So we get two different monoidal categories with the same tensor product. In one of these - the less obvious one! - the octonions are a monoid object!

In short, while the octonions look nonassociative to our ordinary eyes, they are associative in the category of (Z/2)3(\mathbb{Z}/2)^3(Z/2)3-graded vector spaces with its usual tensor product but a funny associator.
