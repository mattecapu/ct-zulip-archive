Alexander Campbell (Dec 24 2020 at 00:52):

An interesting characterisation of the "co-fully-faithful" functors in Cat (see https://mathoverflow.net/a/354097/57405): a functor u ⁣:I→Ju \colon I \to Ju:I→J is co-fully-faithful if and only if it is "initial for ends", i.e. for any functor F ⁣:Jop×J→CF \colon J^{op} \times J \to CF:Jop×J→C, the canonical map ∫j∈JF(j,j)→∫i∈IF(u(i),u(i))\int_{j \in J} F(j,j) \to \int_{i \in I} F(u(i),u(i))∫j∈J​F(j,j)→∫i∈I​F(u(i),u(i)) is an isomorphism.

Alexander Campbell (Dec 24 2020 at 00:55):

In the paper (http://www.tac.mta.ca/tac/volumes/8/n20/8-20abs.html), these functors are called "lax epimorphisms", and the authors characterise them as the "absolutely dense" functors. They say that a functor u ⁣:I→Ju \colon I \to Ju:I→J is absolutely dense if each object j∈Jj \in Jj∈J is the colimit of the canonical diagram u/j→Ju/j \to Ju/j→J (i.e. uuu is dense) and moreover these colimits are absolute (i.e. preserved by all functors out of JJJ).
