Topic: conditions for points functor to have a left SMC adjoint?

Mike Stay (Feb 22 2021 at 18:17):

Given a symmetric monoidal closed category (V,⊗,I,⊸),(V, \otimes, I, \multimap),(V,⊗,I,⊸), under what conditions does the "points" forgetful functor V(I,−):V→SetV(I, -):V \to {\rm Set}V(I,−):V→Set induce a symmetric monoidal closed adjunction?

Martti Karvonen (Feb 22 2021 at 18:35):

If V has coproducts, then the functor has a left adjoint (This is e.g in Borceux Vol 2 prop 6.4.6). I don't know if this left adjoint is a SMC-functor without further conditions though.

Mike Shulman (Feb 22 2021 at 19:01):

The left adjoint takes copowers by the unit object, F(X)=X⋅IF(X) = X\cdot IF(X)=X⋅I. This is strong symmetric monoidal whenever VVV is closed (with coproducts), since then the tensor product preserves colimits (including copowers) in each variable.
