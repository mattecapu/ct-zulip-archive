Topic: Connected Category

Frank Tsai (Jun 09 2023 at 21:24):

Category theory in context: A category is connected if any pair of objects can be connected by a finite zig-zag of morphisms.
If I understand this correctly, for some objects xxx and yyy, we don't necessarily have a morphism x→yx \to yx→y because they are connected by a finite zig-zag of morphisms?

John Baez (Jun 09 2023 at 21:32):

Yes, if you have a category with just two objects xxx and yyy and one morphism f:y→xf: y \to xf:y→x together with the two identity morphisms, then xxx can be connected by a finite zig-zag of morphisms to yyy, even though there is no morphism from xxx to yyy.

John Baez (Jun 09 2023 at 21:34):

(In this example you can use a finite zig-zag that's very short, consisting of a single zag and no zig.)
