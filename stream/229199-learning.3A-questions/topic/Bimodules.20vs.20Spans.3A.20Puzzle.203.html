Anyway, Eric was completely correct in his first attempt to solve Puzzle 1.

Here's a more general way to think about this puzzle. In any monoidal category where the monoidal structure is given by coproduct, every object xxx becomes a monoid in a unique way.

The unit η:0→x\eta: 0 \to xη:0→x is unique because 000 is initial. The left and right unit laws force μ:x+x→x\mu: x + x \to xμ:x+x→x to be the codiagonal or 'fold' map ∇:x+x→x\nabla: x + x \to x∇:x+x→x.
