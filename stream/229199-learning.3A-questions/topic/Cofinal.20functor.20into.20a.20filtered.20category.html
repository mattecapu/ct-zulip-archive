Topic: Cofinal functor into a filtered category

fosco (Feb 05 2025 at 12:26):

A well-known fact about filtered categories is that if JJJ is filtered, one can always find a cofinal functor D:P→JD : P \to JD:P→J whose domain is a directed poset.

The proof that appears in "Locally Presentable and Accessible Categories" starts by observing that without loss of generality we can assume that "every finite subcategory of JJJ can be extended into a finite subcategory with a unique terminal object". I think that this wlog is implied by another (to my knowledge, equivalent) definition of filteredness, which is: JJJ is filtered if and only if every diagram F:K→JF : K \to JF:K→J with finite domain extends to F∗:K⊳→JF^* : K^\rhd \to JF∗:K⊳→J, where K⊳=K⋆1K^\rhd = K \star 1K⊳=K⋆1 is the "right cone" of KKK, the join of KKK with the point. If this is true, the proof goes essentially as in LPAC, just without the annoying wlog.

My question is: is this correct, am I missing something? I couldn't retrieve the same result in Makkai-Paré book (probably they use it somewhere, but due to the fact that the proof is simple if filteredness is taken in the extension-theoretic form, they omit the proof?)

Amar Hadzihasanovic (Feb 05 2025 at 12:48):

The indiscrete category on n>1n > 1n>1 objects is filtered but, as a finite subcategory of itself, it cannot be extended to a finite subcategory with a unique terminal object. I think the problem here is the requirement of strict uniqueness of terminal objects which in turn comes from the implied “skeletality” of the domain being a directed poset as opposed to a preorder.

Amar Hadzihasanovic (Feb 05 2025 at 12:55):

It seems likely to me that you can use the extension-theoretic def to get an alternative proof of the statement where a directed poset is replaced by a directed preorder, using something like the preorder reflection of a category whose objects are triples of a finite diagram FFF in JJJ, an object jjj of JJJ, and a cone under FFF with tip jjj...

daniel gratzer (Feb 05 2025 at 14:01):

Just to note the difference explicitly, since it wasn't obvious to me right away: it is true that for any filtered category CCC, one can extend F:K→CF : K \to CF:K→C to Fˉ:K⊳→C\bar{F} : K^\rhd \to CFˉ:K⊳→C but if FFF witnesses KKK as a subcategory than the same may not be true for K⊳K^\rhdK⊳ and Fˉ\bar{F}Fˉ. A similar move (strengthening the condition you gave above to one where the extension can be chosen to be monic when FFF was monic) is used by Lurie in Kerodon (https://kerodon.net/tag/02QB). In this case, the move is more obviously an encoding hack---the two categories are equivalent.
