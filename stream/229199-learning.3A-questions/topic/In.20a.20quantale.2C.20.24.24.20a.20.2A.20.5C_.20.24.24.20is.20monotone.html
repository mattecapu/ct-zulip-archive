Topic: In a quantale, $$ a * \_ $$ is monotone

fosco (Aug 19 2022 at 20:12):

To my shame, there's a seemingly simple fact about quantales that I don't see how to prove: let a,b,ca,b,ca,b,c be three elements of a quantale QQQ, and assume b≤cb \le cb≤c; then a∗b≤a∗ca * b \le a * ca∗b≤a∗c (and similarly multiplying with aaa on the other side).

This looks simple, right? So then why I can't see how to prove it, starting with the definition of quantale, that apparently does not require it (see for example the definition given in Kimmo Rosenthal's book: image.png ?

Mike Shulman (Aug 19 2022 at 20:15):

Note that b≤cb\le cb≤c if and only if c=b∨cc = b\vee cc=b∨c. Thus, if b≤cb\le cb≤c then by the axiom given, a∗c=a∗(b∨c)=(a∗b)∨(a∗c)a \ast c = a\ast (b\vee c) = (a\ast b) \vee (a \ast c)a∗c=a∗(b∨c)=(a∗b)∨(a∗c), which is equivalent to a∗b≤a∗ca \ast b \le a \ast ca∗b≤a∗c.
