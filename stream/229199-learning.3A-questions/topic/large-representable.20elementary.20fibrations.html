Topic: large-representable elementary fibrations

James Deikun (May 19 2025 at 16:51):

An elementary (i.e. faithful and conservative, i.e. fibered in posetal groupoids) fibration P:E→BP : \mathcal{E \to B}P:E→B is representable iff its total category has a terminal object. Then the fibration PPP is equivalent to the slice projection π:B/P(I)→B\pi : \mathcal B/P(I) \to \mathcal Bπ:B/P(I)→B. So far so good.

But there are some fibrations that seem to represent a large "representable" presheaf, for example the projection from the category of arrows (as objects) and pullback squares (as arrows) in B\mathcal BB, which represents the "slice over the true universe". Is there some known characterization of these elementary fibrations?
