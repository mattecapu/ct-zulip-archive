David Egolf (Jun 10 2024 at 15:57):

If the interchange law holds, and both operations are associative and unital, then is ⊗:X×X→X\otimes: X \times X \to X⊗:X×X→X necessarily a morphism of monoids? ⊗\otimes⊗ will preserve composition, because the interchange law holds. It remains to show it preserves the unit. Since by assumption the interchange law holds and both operations are unital, we know by the Eckmann-Hilton argument that the units of the two operations are the same. So, ⊗(1,1)=1⊗1=1\otimes(1, 1) = 1 \otimes 1 = 1⊗(1,1)=1⊗1=1 is indeed the unit of XXX with respect to the multiplication given by ∘\circ∘.

Similarly, if the interchange law holds, and both operations are associative and unital, then ∘:X×X→X\circ: X \times X \to X∘:X×X→X will be a monoid homomorphism (where X×XX \times XX×X and XXX are made into monoids using ⊗\otimes⊗).
