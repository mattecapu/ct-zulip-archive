Topic: Monad-like structure?

Mateo Torres-Ruiz (Jun 17 2025 at 08:31):

This might be a bit of a shot in the dark, but does the following ring a bell for anyone? I'm thinking of a monad-like structure with a functor T:C×C→CT:\mathcal{C}\times\mathcal{C}\to\mathcal{C}T:C×C→C, with unit X×Y→T(X,Y)X\times Y\to T(X,Y)X×Y→T(X,Y) and bind (X×Y→T(X′×Y′))→T(X×Y)→T(X′×Y′)(X\times Y\to T(X'\times Y'))\to T(X\times Y)\to T(X'\times Y')(X×Y→T(X′×Y′))→T(X×Y)→T(X′×Y′)

Nathanael Arkor (Jun 17 2025 at 08:34):

This looks exactly like a [[relative monad]] whose root is the product functor (×) ⁣:C×C→C(\times) \colon \mathcal C \times \mathcal C \to \mathcal C(×):C×C→C.

Morgan Rogers (he/him) (Jun 17 2025 at 08:35):

What is T(X×Y)T(X \times Y)T(X×Y)? Did you mean T(X,Y)T(X,Y)T(X,Y) etc in the bind formula?

Nathanael Arkor (Jun 17 2025 at 08:35):

(Which would be an interesting example, because most examples of relative monads have fully faithful roots.)

Kenji Maillard (Jun 17 2025 at 10:25):

We employed this kind of relative monads over a product to try to give a systematic account of (binary) relational program logics over monadic programs in our paper The next 700 relational program logics .
