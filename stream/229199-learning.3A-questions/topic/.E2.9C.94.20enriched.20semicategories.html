Ralph Sarkis (Oct 04 2021 at 14:18):

I think I am interested in semicategories enriched in ([0,∞],≥)([0,\infty],\geq)([0,∞],≥) which would be non-symmetric diffuse metrics (the term is coined here), i.e.: d:X×X→[0,∞]d:X\times X \rightarrow [0,\infty]d:X×X→[0,∞] satisfies only the triangular inequality. Hence, I was looking for references on enriched semicategories to see whether the category of [0,∞][0,\infty][0,∞]--semicategories and semifunctors (a category of diffuse metrics and non-expansive maps) has enough structure for my purposes (e.g.: products, coproducts, how monads work, etc.).

I saw some stuff here on regular semicategories and this follow-up paper but my parsing did not answer any of my questions.

Reid Barton (Oct 04 2021 at 14:40):

It looks like this category can be expressed as the category of models for a relational theory (based on the relations R(x,y,a):=(d(x,y)≤a)R(x, y, a) := (d(x, y) \le a)R(x,y,a):=(d(x,y)≤a)) or an essentially algebraic theory, so it is locally presentable.

Reid Barton (Oct 04 2021 at 14:51):

The main subtlety is that you need an axiom like "for any xxx, y∈Xy \in Xy∈X and a≥0a \ge 0a≥0, if R(x,y,b)R(x, y, b)R(x,y,b) for all b>ab > ab>a, then R(x,y,a)R(x, y, a)R(x,y,a)" which is not finitary.

James Deikun (Oct 04 2021 at 23:16):

You can also treat [0,∞][0,\infty][0,∞] as a concrete category (of upper Dedekind cuts) and that makes it more amenable to standard tools ...

James Deikun (Oct 04 2021 at 23:34):
