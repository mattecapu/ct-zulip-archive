Amar Hadzihasanovic (Jun 25 2021 at 09:06):

In your first picture, if the 2-cell is from α\alphaα to ψ′\psi'ψ′, then β1\beta_1β1​ actually goes the other way (from fff to f′f'f′) and in the equation you have to attach it to the left of ψ′\psi'ψ′.

Amar Hadzihasanovic (Jun 25 2021 at 09:07):

So it's like αβ2=β1ψ′\alpha \beta_2 = \beta_1 \psi'αβ2​=β1​ψ′.

Amar Hadzihasanovic (Jun 25 2021 at 09:08):

And if you use the same convention as the diagram above, fff is sent by DDD to a diagram rotated wrt the one you drew: the “horizontal” arrows are identities and the “vertical” ones are fff. And then you should be able to see that there is, in fact, a place to send a 2-cell α:f⇒f′\alpha: f \Rightarrow f'α:f⇒f′.
