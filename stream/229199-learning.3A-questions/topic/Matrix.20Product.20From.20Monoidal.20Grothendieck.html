Topic: Matrix Product From Monoidal Grothendieck

Jade Master (Oct 31 2020 at 18:23):

Does anyone recognize this matrix product? Let R be a ring equipped with a nice poset structure. You can construct a functor
F:Setop→MonCatF : \mathsf{Set}^{op} \to \mathsf{MonCat}F:Setop→MonCat
by:

  • sending a set XXX to the poset of matrices MatR(X)\mathsf{Mat_R}(X)MatR​(X) of matrices m:X×X→Rm : X \times X \to Rm:X×X→R,
  • and sending a function f:X→Yf: X \to Yf:X→Y to the monotone map
    MatR(Y)→MatR(X)\mathsf{Mat_R}(Y) \to \mathsf{Mat_R}(X)MatR​(Y)→MatR​(X) given by
    Y×Y→RY \times Y \to RY×Y→R going to X×X→f×fY×Y→RX \times X \xrightarrow{f \times f} Y \times Y \to RX×Xf×f​Y×Y→R.
    Moreover, each of these posets MatR(X)\mathsf{Mat_R}(X)MatR​(X) are monoidal under matrix product and FFF is monoidal with respect to cartesian product of sets. Therefore you can apply the fibrewise monoidal Grothendieck construction to get a monoidal product

⊕:∫F×∫F→∫F. \oplus : \int F \times \int F \to \int F.⊕:∫F×∫F→∫F.

I am wondering about what this monoidal product actually is. I think I have it worked out in terms of Kronecker product ⊗\otimes⊗. If (X,m:X×X→R)(X, m: X \times X \to R) (X,m:X×X→R) and (Y,n:Y×Y→R)(Y,n: Y \times Y \to R)(Y,n:Y×Y→R) are objects in ∫F\int F∫F, then
(X,m)⊕(Y,n)=(X×Y,(m⊗1(Y))∗(n⊗1(X)))(X,m) \oplus (Y,n) = (X \times Y, (m \otimes 1(Y) ) * (n \otimes 1(X)))(X,m)⊕(Y,n)=(X×Y,(m⊗1(Y))∗(n⊗1(X)))
where 1(X)1(X)1(X) and 1(Y)1(Y)1(Y) are the square matrices on XXX and YYY containing only 1's and ∗*∗ is the ordinary matrix product.

One thing to note is that m and n are on the same set then
(X,m:X×X→R)⊕(X,n,X×X→R)=(X×X,(m⊗1(X))∗(n⊗1(X)))=m∗n⊗1(X)∗1(X)(X,m: X \times X \to R) \oplus (X, n, X\times X \to R) = (X \times X, (m \otimes 1(X)) * (n \otimes 1(X))) = m*n \otimes 1(X) * 1(X)(X,m:X×X→R)⊕(X,n,X×X→R)=(X×X,(m⊗1(X))∗(n⊗1(X)))=m∗n⊗1(X)∗1(X)
because the Kronecker product satisfies the interchange law with respect to matrix multiplication. So in this sense it starts to look like more like generalizing ordinary matrix multiplication.
