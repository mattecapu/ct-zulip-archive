Topic: natural enough transformations of graph homomorphisms

Chad Nester (Mar 30 2021 at 10:12):

I'm currently working with the category RGraph\mathsf{RGraph}RGraph of reflexive graphs, and I'm wondering if the following phenomenon has a name.

It involves the well-known adjunction F:RGraph⊣Cat:UF : \mathsf{RGraph} \dashv \mathsf{Cat} : UF:RGraph⊣Cat:U, with F(G)F(G)F(G) being the category of paths in the graph GGG, and U(C)U(C)U(C) being the underlying graph of the category CCC.

If I have morphisms f,g:G→U(C)f,g : G \to U(C)f,g:G→U(C) in RGraph\mathsf{RGraph}RGraph, then I can define a "natural transformation" α:f→g\alpha : f \to gα:f→g to consist of a morphism αA:f(A)→g(A)\alpha_A : f(A) \to g(A)αA​:f(A)→g(A) in CCC for each vertex AAA of GGG, such that for any edge t:A→B t : A \to Bt:A→B of GGG we have αB∘f(t)=g(t)∘αA\alpha_B\circ f(t) = g(t) \circ \alpha_AαB​∘f(t)=g(t)∘αA​. Amar Hadzihasanovic (Mar 31 2021 at 00:36):

A relevant thing here seems to be this:

  • a natural transformation between functors FG→CFG \to CFG→C is the same as a functor I×FG→CI \times FG \to CI×FG→C where III is the walking arrow;
  • III is the free category on a graph, let's call it JJJ so I=FJI = FJI=FJ;
  • there is a monoidal product −□−-\square -−□− on graphs, the box product or (confusingly) the “Cartesian” product, which is taken by FFF to the “funny” tensor product −□−-\square -−□− of categories, so F(J□G)≃I□FGF(J \square G) \simeq I \square FGF(J□G)≃I□FG;
  • the cartesian product of two categories is canonically a quotient of their funny tensor product. (In fact FFF may be oplax monoidal from graphs + box product to categories + cartesian product)

So the natural transformation between two functors FG→CFG \to CFG→C can be defined as a functor F(J□G)→CF(J \square G) \to CF(J□G)→C, which is equivalently a morphism of graphs J□G→UCJ \square G \to UCJ□G→UC, satisfying some equations.

Mike Stay (Mar 31 2021 at 16:35):

Tom Hirschowitz said:

This looks nice. Could it be phrased by saying that RGph is enriched over graphs with the box product, and that the adjunction lifts to the enriched world?

There's a topos of (not-necessarily-reflexive) directed multigraphs, the topos of presheaves on the category with two objects and two parallel morphisms between them. The categorical product of two edges a→ba\to ba→b and c→dc \to dc→d is a "diagonal" (a,c)→(b,d)(a,c) \to (b, d)(a,c)→(b,d), and the exponential in the topos has nothing to do with graph homomorphisms. However, the category is also symmetric monoidal closed with respect to the box product, an unusual case where a category is symmetric monoidal closed in two different ways. The adjuntion involving the box product gives a notion of exponential graph whose vertices are homomorphisms and whose edges are graph transformations.

When we move to the topos of reflexive directed multigraphs, the categorical product of two edge graphs a→ba\to ba→b and c→dc \to dc→d (which look diagrammatically something like dumbbells because of the canonical self-edges on the vertices) is a box with a diagonal. Each side of the box comes from a use of a canonical self edge. The box makes the exponential graph be the graph of homomorphisms and graph transformations, and the extra diagonal happens to not screw anything up. So here the two symmetric monoidal closed structures coincide.
