Topic: When is the Alg(T) cocartesian?

Alexander Gietelink Oldenziel (Dec 16 2020 at 17:02):

GIven a monad TTT on a symmetric monoidal category CCC a sufficient (and necessary?) condition for the category of Alg(T) to have a symmetric monoidal product ⊗T\otimes_T⊗T​ such that the forgetful functor U:Alg(T)→CU: Alg(T) \to CU:Alg(T)→C preserves the tensor product is for the monad to be commutative/monoidal. See e.g. https://ncatlab.org/nlab/show/tensor+product+of+algebras+over+a+commutative+monad

What conditions do we require on the monad TTT such that said tensor product is actually a coproduct?

I am thinking of the case where C=k−VectC=k-VectC=k−Vect and TTT is the free algebra monad ('symmetric algebra'). In this case Alg(T)=k−AlgAlg(T)=k-AlgAlg(T)=k−Alg, the tensor is a coproduct (so that the category of affine schemes inherits a product structure!).

John Baez (Dec 16 2020 at 17:18):

Just to warn readers: you are using "free algebra" to mean "free commutative algebra" and k−Algk-Algk−Alg to mean "commutative algebras over the field kkk".

John Baez (Dec 16 2020 at 17:19):

Unless your algebras over a field are commutative it's not true that their tensor product is their coproduct.

John Baez (Dec 16 2020 at 17:21):

One reason the tensor product of commutative algebras over a field is their coproduct is this: the category of commutative monoid objects in any symmetric monoidal category is cocartesian.

John Baez (Dec 16 2020 at 17:21):

The only answers I can imagine to your question rely on this fact.

JS PL (he/him) (Dec 16 2020 at 22:08):

The answer you're looking for of turning the tensor product of the base category into a coproduct in the Eilenberg-Moore category is called a "comonoidal algebra modality", which is the dual of a "monoidal coalgebra modality" which is used in the categorical semantics of linear logic.

JS PL (he/him) (Dec 16 2020 at 22:09):

The dual of your result is found in these notes, where they explain how to show that tensor product in the base category turns into a product in the coEilenberg-Moore category

JS PL (he/him) (Dec 16 2020 at 22:11):

Essentially, let CCC be a symmetric monoidal category, with tensor ⊗\otimes⊗ and unit kkk

JS PL (he/him) (Dec 16 2020 at 22:16):

A comonoidal algebra modality is:

  1. A monad (T,μ,η)(T, \mu, \eta)(T,μ,η), where T:C→CT: C \to CT:C→C is the endofunctor, and μX:TT(X)→T(X)\mu_X: TT(X) \to T(X)μX​:TT(X)→T(X) and ηX:X→T(X)\eta_X: X \to T(X)ηX​:X→T(X) are the monad structure maps.
  2. Natural transformations ∇X:T(X)⊗T(X)→T(X)\nabla_X: T(X) \otimes T(X) \to T(X)∇X​:T(X)⊗T(X)→T(X) and uX:k→T(X)u_X: k \to T(X)uX​:k→T(X) such that (T(X),∇X,uX)(T(X), \nabla_X, u_X)(T(X),∇X​,uX​) is a commutative monoid and μX:(TT(X),∇T(X),uT(X))→(T(X),∇X,uX)\mu_X: (TT(X), \nabla_{T(X)}, u_{T(X)}) \to (T(X), \nabla_X, u_X)μX​:(TT(X),∇T(X)​,uT(X)​)→(T(X),∇X​,uX​) is a monoid morphism
  3. A natural transformation mX,Y:T(X⊗Y)→T(X)⊗T(Y)m_{X,Y}: T(X \otimes Y) \to T(X) \otimes T(Y)mX,Y​:T(X⊗Y)→T(X)⊗T(Y) and mk:T(k)→km_k: T(k) \to kmk​:T(k)→k such that (T,m,mk)(T, m, m_k)(T,m,mk​) is a symmetric comonoidal (lax) functor
  4. μ\muμ, η\etaη, ∇\nabla∇, and uuu are all monoidal transformations
  5. ∇\nabla∇ and uuu are also TTT-algebra maps

JS PL (he/him) (Dec 16 2020 at 22:18):

Then the Eilenberg-Moore category of a comonoidal algebra modality is coCartesian where ⊗\otimes⊗ from the base category becomes a coproduct in the Eilenberg-Moore category.

JS PL (he/him) (Dec 16 2020 at 22:23):

John Baez said:

The only answers I can imagine to your question rely on this fact.

That's correct! There is a forgetful functor from the Eilenberg-Moore category of an algebra modality (part 1 and 2 above) to the category of commutative monoids. Then parts 3,4,5 allow us to say that the tensor product is in fact a coproduct

I talk about algebra modalities and how to turn their algebras into commutative monoids in this paper:
https://arxiv.org/pdf/1803.02304.pdf
