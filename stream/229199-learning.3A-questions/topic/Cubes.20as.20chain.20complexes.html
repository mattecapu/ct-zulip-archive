Topic: Cubes as chain complexes

Amar Hadzihasanovic (Jan 17 2023 at 08:21):

Take your favourite [[category of cubes]].
Unless it's a really strange one, it should admit a functor to the category of augmented chain complexes:

  • You send the 1-cube to the complex with C1=ZC_1 = \mathbb{Z}C1​=Z, C0=Z⊕ZC_0 = \mathbb{Z} \oplus \mathbb{Z}C0​=Z⊕Z, where ∂0\partial_0∂0​ sends 111 to (1,−1)(1, -1)(1,−1), and the augmentation sends both generators of Z⊕Z\mathbb{Z} \oplus \mathbb{Z}Z⊕Z to 111.
  • You send the nnn-cube to the nnn-fold tensor product of this complex.
  • Unless it's a really strange category of cubes, there should be only one sensible way of extending to morphisms.

Furthermore, this functor should be faithful.
What I'd like to know is: Are there known characterisations of categories of cubes as subcategories of augmented chain complexes?

More specifically, I'd be interested in this:
The cubes-as-chain-complexes are free, and can be equipped with a “canonical” choice of generating basis.
I would like to characterise the category of cubes with connections as the full subcategory on those morphisms that send these basis elements either to basis elements or to 0.
