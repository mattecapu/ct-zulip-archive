The category LinRel(k)LinRel(k)LinRel(k) of linear relations is a handy category with lots of nice features and applications. In PROP form, it has as objects natural numbers (as always), morphisms m→nm \to nm→n are subspaces of the vector space R⊆km⊕knR \subseteq k^m \oplus k^nR⊆km⊕kn, and composition is done relation-style. That is, (u,w)∈(R;S)  ⟺  ∃v.(u,v)∈R∧(v,w)∈S(u,w) \in (R;S) \iff \exists v . (u,v) \in R \wedge (v,w) \in S(u,w)∈(R;S)⟺∃v.(u,v)∈R∧(v,w)∈S. One can then show (R;S)(R;S)(R;S) forms a subspace whenever RRR and SSS do, so it all fits very nicely together into a (symmetric monoidal) category.
