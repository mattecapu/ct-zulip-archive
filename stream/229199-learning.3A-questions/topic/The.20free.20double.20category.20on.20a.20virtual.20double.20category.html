Topic: The free double category on a virtual double category

Chad Nester (Dec 31 2025 at 11:40):

I'm looking at section 6.6 of Leinster's "Higher Operads, Higher Categories", and I'm trying to figure out how the adjunction between TTT-multicategories and TTT-structured categories works concretely in the case of the free category monad fc\mathbf{fc}fc. In particular, I'm confused about the action of the left adjoint FFF on objects.

Given a virtual double category X\mathbb{X}X (i.e., an fc\mathbf{fc}fc-mulicategory), I expect to obtain a strict double category F(X)F(\mathbb{X})F(X). It seems that cells of F(X)F(\mathbb{X})F(X) must be sequences (t1,…,tn)(t_1,\ldots,t_n)(t1​,…,tn​) of cells of X\mathbb{X}X in which the right boundary of tit_iti​ matches the left boundary of ti+1t_{i+1}ti+1​ for all iii. Here I have in mind the usual pasting-diagram-like way of picturing the cells of X\mathbb{X}X. These sequences are really paths in a graph with vertices the tight morphisms of X\mathbb{X}X and edges between fff and ggg given by cells with left boundary fff and right boundary ggg. In particular this means that the empty path from fff to fff gives a cell ()f()_f()f​ of F(X)F(\mathbb{X})F(X), which I imagine is the horizontal identity on fff.

What I do not understand is how to define vertical composition in F(X)F(\mathbb{X})F(X) in the presence of cells like ()f()_f()f​. In particular, if ttt is a cell of X\mathbb{X}X whose top boundary is the empty sequence, then should be able to vertically compose ()f()_f()f​ and (t)(t)(t) in F(X)F(\mathbb{X})F(X) (with ()f()_f()f​ "on top"). I don't see what sequence/path of cells of X\mathbb{X}X this could possibly be.

Where am I going wrong here?

James Deikun (Dec 31 2025 at 14:35):

The result is (t∘f)(t \circ f)(t∘f). In an fc\mathbf{fc}fc-multicategory there is a "whiskering" composition of triangles with vertical arrows, which is actually just the normal composition of cells, but the way it works out to be this is not obvious to everybody and descriptions of fc\mathbf{fc}fc-multicategories seldom mention it specifically.

Mike Shulman (Jan 02 2026 at 07:35):

It's "obvious" once you realize that "a list of horizontally-composable cells of length zero" means a single vertical arrow. Which is "obvious" from the fact that a list of composable arrows in a quiver means a single object. But it's one of those things that often only seems obvious in hindsight.
