Let's see, let f:A↪Bf:A\hookrightarrow Bf:A↪B be a full subcategory inclusion that is a Conduche functor, and B′B'B′ the full subcategory of BBB on the complement of AAA. Then Conduche-ness of fff means that no object of B′B'B′ can be both the codomain of an arrow whose domain is in AAA and the domain of an arrow whose codomain is in AAA. So we can partition the objects of B′B'B′ into B0B_0B0​, which have an arrow to AAA, B1B_1B1​ which have an arrow from AAA, and B2B_2B2​ which have neither.
