Any species F:B→BF: B \to BF:B→B defines an endofunctor F^:Set→Set\hat{F}: \mathsf{Set} \to \mathsf{Set}F^:Set→Set by

F^(X)=∑n=0∞F(n)×XnSn \widehat{F}(X) = \displaystyle{ \sum_{n= 0}^\infty \frac{F(n) \times X^n}{S_n} } F(X)=n=0∑∞​Sn​F(n)×Xn​

where I'm identifying nnn with some nnn-element set, and the symmetric group SnS_nSn​ acts on both F(n)F(n)F(n) and XnX^nXn in the obvious ways. This formula should remind you strongly of a Taylor series, and functors of the form F^\widehat{F}F are called analytic functors.

Composition of species corresponds to composition of analytic functors:

(F⊙G)^≅F^∘G^ \widehat{(F \odot G)} \cong \widehat{F} \circ \widehat{G} (F⊙G)​≅F∘G

Very smart!

A monoid object in the category of species with its composition tensor product is exactly an operad. This is one of the slickest ways to define operads.

So, unpacking this a bit: an operad is a species with a multiplication m:F⊙F⇒Fm: F \odot F \Rightarrow Fm:F⊙F⇒F and also the unit i:I→Fi: I \to Fi:I→F where III is the unit object for the composition tensor product, and mmm and iii need to obey the associative law and left/right unit laws.

It's very good to take this definition and unpack it even further to get a longer and very explicit definition of 'operad'.

John Baez (Jul 25 2024 at 13:45):

So we can say:

1) A monoid object in the monoidal category of endofunctors on Set with its composition tensor product is a monad.

2) A monoid object in the monoidal category of species with its composition tensor product is an operad.

It's not a coincidence that they rhyme: Peter May did that on purpose.

Even better, using the formula I wrote down, any operad FFF gives a monad F^\widehat{F}F! But we only get certain specially nice monads this way.

Todd Trimble (Jul 25 2024 at 20:20):

for the equivalence between species and analytic functors, Joyal's paper is the standard one, but I found a very well-written article of Adamek quite enlightening

Warning! This is not an equivalence between the category of species and the category of analytic functors, which I think I've seen asserted incorrectly in the literature. However, Joyal did show that it is an equivalence between the groupoid of species and the groupoid of analytic functors.

For example, take the species XXX which is terminal at a set of cardinality 111 and initial for all other finite cardinalities, and take the species X⊗2/S2X^{\otimes 2}/S_2X⊗2/S2​ which is terminal at a set of cardinality 222 and initial for all other finite cardinalities. There is no species map X→X⊗2/S2X \to X^{\otimes 2}/S_2X→X⊗2/S2​. However, there is a natural transformation between their analytic functors, where the analytic functor on XXX is the identity functor on Set\mathsf{Set}Set and the analytic functor on X⊗2/S2X^{\otimes 2}/S_2X⊗2/S2​ takes a set SSS to its symmetric square [the quotient of S×SS \times SS×S that identifies (x,y)(x, y)(x,y) with (y,x)(y, x)(y,x)]. This natural transformation is induced by the diagonal map S→S×SS \to S \times SS→S×S.
