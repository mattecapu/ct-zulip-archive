Topic: Mimicking categorical products in enriched categories

Peva Blanchard (Nov 20 2024 at 22:13):

I've continued to play with enriched categories, and I stumbled upon the following issue.

In usual category theory (Set\text{Set}Set-enriched), the categorical product a×ba \times ba×b can be defined by asking that the presheaf C(_,a)×C(_,b){\cal C}(\_,a) \times {\cal C}(\_, b)C(_,a)×C(_,b) be representable.

Let's try to do the same when C{\cal C}C is a V{\cal V}V-enriched category, with (V,⊗,I)({\cal V}, \otimes, I)(V,⊗,I) a closed monoidal category.
I.e., I'm defining a⊗ba \otimes ba⊗b as a representative of the enriched presheaf C(_,a)⊗C(_,b){\cal C}(\_, a) \otimes {\cal C}(\_, b)C(_,a)⊗C(_,b)

C(z,a⊗b)≅C(z,a)⊗C(z,b) {\cal C}(z, a \otimes b) \cong {\cal C}(z, a) \otimes {\cal C}(z,b)C(z,a⊗b)≅C(z,a)⊗C(z,b)

Of course, it may not exist.

I have two questions:

  1. How does this relate to weighted (co)limits? (I'm aware of the definition of weighted (co)limits)
  2. Is it possible to "freely ⊗\otimes⊗-complete" the category C{\cal C}C?

Mike Shulman (Nov 20 2024 at 22:20):

If V\mathcal{V}V isn't cartesian monoidal, then C(−,a)⊗C(−,b)\mathcal{C}(-,a) \otimes \mathcal{C}(-,b)C(−,a)⊗C(−,b) may not be an enriched presheaf: to define the functorial action, you need diagonals on hom-objects.

Actually, my original context was a bit more involved, and my question above tried to take a short-cut (in vain).
Fortunately, I think my original context doesn't suffer from the flaw above: I do get a functor it seems.
Here it is.

Category Cost\text{Cost}Cost

First, I use Lawvere-like category Cost=((−∞,∞],≥,+,0)\text{Cost} = ((-\infty,\infty], \ge, +, 0)Cost=((−∞,∞],≥,+,0).
Following John's blog post, I also define another tensor product

x∧y=−1βln⁡(e−βx+e−βy) x \wedge y = - \frac{1}{\beta}\ln \Big(e^{-\beta x} + e^{-\beta y}\Big)x∧y=−β1​ln(e−βx+e−βy)

where β>0\beta > 0β>0 is some constant.

It turns out +++ distributes over ∧\wedge∧

x+(y∧z)=(x+y)∧(x+z)x + (y \wedge z) = (x + y) \wedge (x + z)x+(y∧z)=(x+y)∧(x+z)

Category ESet\text{ESet}ESet of energetic sets

This is a slight variant (I think) of John's energetic sets from a previous thread.

  • objects are sets XXX with an energy function E:X→CostE : X \to \text{Cost}E:X→Cost

E(f)=sup⁡y (E(f(y))−E(y))E(f) = \sup_y ~ (E(f(y)) - E(y))E(f)=ysup​ (E(f(y))−E(y))

Tensor product ∧\wedge∧

I define a second symmetric tensor product

Almost distributive

Because both ⊗\otimes⊗ and ∧\wedge∧ have as underlying set the cartesian product of the respective sets, ⊗\otimes⊗ does not distribute over ∧\wedge∧.

But, thanks to the diagonal, we have a morphism

X⊗(Y∧Z)→(X⊗Y)∧(X⊗Z)X \otimes (Y \wedge Z) \to (X \otimes Y) \wedge (X \otimes Z)X⊗(Y∧Z)→(X⊗Y)∧(X⊗Z)

with energy equality E(x⊗(y∧z))=E((x⊗y)∧(x⊗z))E(x \otimes (y \wedge z)) = E((x \otimes y) \wedge (x \otimes z))E(x⊗(y∧z))=E((x⊗y)∧(x⊗z)).

Finally

I consider ESet\text{ESet}ESet with the monoidal structure (⊗,I)(\otimes, I)(⊗,I) as an enriching category.
Let C{\cal C}C be a ESet\text{ESet}ESet-enriched category.
Roughly speaking, C{\cal C}C is like a usual locally small category, but each arrow has "an energy cost", and the costs add up when composition.

Now, I can use the second tensor product to define

F=C(z,a)∧C(z,b) F = {\cal C}(z,a) \wedge {\cal C}(z,b)F=C(z,a)∧C(z,b)

Here is a shorter reformulation.

Let (V,⊗,I)({\cal V}, \otimes, I)(V,⊗,I) be a closed symmetric monoidal category, and C{\cal C}C a V{\cal V}V-enriched category.
Assume there is another symmetric monoidal structure (V,∧,∞)({\cal V}, \wedge, \infty)(V,∧,∞) on V{\cal V}V which has diagonals X→X∧XX \to X \wedge XX→X∧X, and such that we have "distributivity natural morphisms" (not iso)

X⊗(Y∧Z)→(X⊗Y)∧(X⊗Z)X \otimes (Y \wedge Z) \to (X \otimes Y) \wedge (X \otimes Z)X⊗(Y∧Z)→(X⊗Y)∧(X⊗Z)

F=C(_,a)∧C(_,b)F = {\cal C}(\_,a) \wedge {\cal C}(\_, b)F=C(_,a)∧C(_,b)
