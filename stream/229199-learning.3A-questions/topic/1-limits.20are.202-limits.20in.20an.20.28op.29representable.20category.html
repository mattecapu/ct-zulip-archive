Topic: 1-limits are 2-limits in an (op)representable category

Fernando Chu (May 26 2025 at 14:39):

The following is about strict 2-categories, functors, limits, etc. In a paper (p. 109), Street remarks

In a 2-category KKK which is both representable and oprepresentable, Φ\PhiΦ has a left 2-adjoint Ψ\PsiΨ and any limit which exists in K0K_0K0​ is automatically a 2-limit in KKK.

Here representable means that KKK has 2-pullbacks and all comma objects, and Φ\PhiΦ is defined on objects by Φ(A):=1A/1A\Phi(A):=1_A/1_AΦ(A):=1A​/1A​, the comma object. From what I can tell, the remark is meant to follow from:

  • The 2-adjunction Ψ⊣Φ\Psi \dashv \PhiΨ⊣Φ.
  • The isomorphism of categories K(S,Φ(A))≅Cat(2,K(S,A))K(S,\Phi(A)) \cong \textbf{Cat}(\mathbb{2}, K(S,A))K(S,Φ(A))≅Cat(2,K(S,A)), where 2\mathbb{2}2 is the walking arrow.
  • The 2-category of internal categories in a category with pullbacks is representable.

But I'm not sure what the argument is, could someone please elaborate on this? More generally, are there other nice conditions that imply that a 1-limit is also a 2-limit?

Mike Shulman (May 26 2025 at 15:01):

In general, for any enriching base VVV, if a VVV-category CCC has copowers (aka tensors) by objects composing some strong generating family for VVV, then any limit in C0C_0C0​ is also a conical limit in CCC, since then for each GGG in the generators

V0(G,C(X,lim⁡iYi))=C0(G⊙X,lim⁡iYi)=lim⁡iC0(G⊙X,Yi)=lim⁡iV0(G,C(X,Yi))=V0(Glim⁡iC(X,Yi))V_0(G,C(X,\lim_i Y_i)) = C_0(G\odot X, \lim_i Y_i) = \lim_i C_0(G\odot X, Y_i) = \lim_i V_0(G, C(X, Y_i)) = V_0(G \lim_i C(X, Y_i))V0​(G,C(X,ilim​Yi​))=C0​(G⊙X,ilim​Yi​)=ilim​C0​(G⊙X,Yi​)=ilim​V0​(G,C(X,Yi​))=V0​(Gilim​C(X,Yi​))

hence (after verifying that this isomorphism is the one induced by the canonical map) C(X,lim⁡iYi)=lim⁡iC(X,Yi)C(X, \lim_i Y_i) = \lim_i C(X,Y_i)C(X,limi​Yi​)=limi​C(X,Yi​). In Cat, 1 and 2\mathbf{2}2 form a strong generator, so copowers with those suffice.
