Chaitanya Leena Subramaniam (May 09 2025 at 23:30):

(I'm assuming you mean arbitrary wide pullbacks, not just binary/finite pullbacks --- in which case see Mike's comment)

Any accessible T:C→CT:\mathcal C\to\mathcal CT:C→C preserves wide pullbacks iff TTT admits strict generic factorisations.
(Weber, "Generic morphisms, parametric representations and weakly cartesian monads", Thm 6.6)

If T:C→CT:\mathcal C\to\mathcal CT:C→C admits strict generic factorisations, then TA:C/A→C/TAT_A : \mathcal C/A\to\mathcal C/TATA​:C/A→C/TA is a right adjoint for every AAA in C\mathcal CC (i.e. TTT is a local right adjoint).
(Weber, "Familial 2-functors and p.r.a.'s", Prop. 2.6)

So a wide pullback-preserving endofunctor TTT can fail to be p.r.a. in two ways:

  1. TTT is not accessible---I can't think of an example.
  2. TTT is accessible, and is a local right adjoint, but C\mathcal CC does not have a terminal object --- a nice example is the free-polynomial-monad monad on the category PolyEnd of polynomial endofunctors in Set (see J. Kock's "Polynomial functors and trees")

Mike Shulman (May 09 2025 at 23:38):

Inaccessible functors are generally rather tricky to get ahold of. Here is an example of an inaccessible functor between locally presentable categories that preserves all limits (hence a fortiori all wide pullbacks) but is not a right adjoint. It's not an endofunctor, but note that requiring the domain and codomain to be locally presentable also excludes examples like FinSet↪Set\rm FinSet \hookrightarrow SetFinSet↪Set.

Mike Shulman (May 09 2025 at 23:50):

An example of an inaccessible endofunctor (of Set, even!) that preserves finite limits (and also finite colimits!) is the ultrapower by the defining ultrafilter on a measurable cardinal (assuming such exists). In fact, Andreas Blass showed in "Exact functors and measurable cardinals" that the existence of a measurable cardinal is equivalent to the existence of a nonidentity endofunctor of Set that preserves finite limits and colimits.
