Topic: Sheafification, adjunction between bundles and presheaves

Peva Blanchard (May 17 2024 at 22:44):

Another side quest from @David Egolf 's thread on reading Baez topos series.

Let XXX be an arbitrary topological space.

First, we defined a functor Λ\LambdaΛ from the category Psh(X)\text{Psh}(X)Psh(X) of presheaves over XXX to the category Top/X\text{Top}/XTop/X of bundles over XXX. Λ\LambdaΛ maps a presheaf FFF over XXX to an étale space Λ(F)\Lambda(F)Λ(F) over XXX.

Second, we defined a functor Γ:Top/X→Psh(X)\Gamma : \text{Top}/X \rightarrow \text{Psh}(X)Γ:Top/X→Psh(X). Γ\GammaΓ maps a bundle E→XE \rightarrow XE→X to its sheaf of sections over XXX.

I was wondering if there were an adjunction Λ⊣Γ\Lambda \dashv \GammaΛ⊣Γ.

I managed to get a proof that, indeed, Λ\LambdaΛ is left adjoint to Γ\GammaΓ. But my proof is relatively long, and I am not sure I checked everything right. An external confirmation/refutation is welcome.

ps: According to John in the other thread, the composite Γ∘Λ\Gamma \circ \LambdaΓ∘Λ is called "sheafification", and appears as the left adjoint to the forgetful functor from sheaves to presheaves. This led me to wonder if sheafification were a monad.

John Baez (May 17 2024 at 23:16):

This led me to wonder if sheafification were a monad.

Since it's a left adjoint followed by a right adjoint, it's a monad. But even better, I believe it's an [[idempotent monad]]. That is, if you sheafify a presheaf that's already a sheaf, you don't get anything new, just something isomorphic to what you had.
