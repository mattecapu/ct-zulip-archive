Rémy Tuyéras (Aug 15 2024 at 20:22):

OK, no problem. Just in case you are curious about it, you can always go through these very mini exercises to quickly have a good intuition how fibrations are used:
1) Show that a surjection X→YX \to YX→Y is the same as a function that has the right lifting property wrt ∅→{∗}\emptyset \to \{\ast\}∅→{∗}
2) Show that an injection X→YX \to YX→Y is the same as a function that has the right lifting property wrt {∗,∗}→{∗}\{\ast,\ast\} \to \{\ast\}{∗,∗}→{∗}
3) Show that a full functor X→YX \to YX→Y is the same as a functor that has the right lifting property wrt {∙,∙}→{∙→∙}\{\bullet, \bullet\} \to \{\bullet \to \bullet\}{∙,∙}→{∙→∙}
4) Show that a faithful functor X→YX \to YX→Y is the same as a functor that has the right lifting property wrt {∙⇉∙}→{∙→∙}\{\bullet \rightrightarrows\bullet\} \to \{\bullet \to \bullet\}{∙⇉∙}→{∙→∙}

Exercise Show that if f:X→Yf:X\to Yf:X→Y is a morphism that has the right lifting property wrt to a class of arrows A\mathcal{A}A, then any pullback of fff (along any map) also has the right lifting property wrt to A\mathcal{A}A
