Topic: faces of convex sets

David Egolf (Feb 03 2022 at 17:34):

We talked about ordered monoids and quantales recently at #learning: questions > are measures functors?, in the context of an algebra of sets. I was reading about "faces" of convex sets (in "Basic Algebraic Topology" by Shastri) and was noticing some similarities.

A subset FFF of a convex set SSS is called a face of SSS if:

  • FFF is convex and
  • for any two distinct points y,zy,zy,z in SSS, if (y,z)∩F≠∅(y,z) \cap F \neq \empty(y,z)∩F=∅ then [y,z]⊆F[y,z] \subseteq F[y,z]⊆F.

Let F(S)\mathcal{F}(S)F(S) denote the set of all faces of SSS. We can form a monoid using F(S)\mathcal{F}(S)F(S) where the morphisms are the faces of SSS and composition corresponds to taking the intersection, so A∘B=A∩BA \circ B = A \cap BA∘B=A∩B for faces A,BA,BA,B. Since SSS is itself a face, we have an identity morphism.

We can order the element of F(S)\mathcal{F}(S)F(S) by inclusion. The resulting poset apparently forms a complete lattice, where the inf⁡\infinf of a collection of faces is their intersection, and the sup⁡\supsup of a collection of faces is the intersection of all the faces that contains the collection.

The monoid composition operation is compatible with the ordering, as A≤B  ⟹  A∘C≤B∘CA \leq B \implies A \circ C \leq B \circ CA≤B⟹A∘C≤B∘C corresponds to A⊆B  ⟹  A∩C⊆B∩CA \subseteq B \implies A \cap C \subseteq B \cap CA⊆B⟹A∩C⊆B∩C, for faces A,B,CA,B,CA,B,C. So, I think we have an ordered monoid of faces.

Does this form a quantale? We need the monoid operation to preserve sups (colimits) for this to be the case. That is, I think we need A∘sup⁡(C1,C2)=sup⁡(A∘C1,A∘C2)A \circ \sup(C_1, C_2) = \sup(A \circ C_1, A \circ C_2)A∘sup(C1​,C2​)=sup(A∘C1​,A∘C2​) to hold, for faces A,C1,C2A,C_1,C_2A,C1​,C2​, among other equations.
