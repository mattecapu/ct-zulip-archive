Mike Stay (Nov 26 2024 at 16:37):

The particular operation Matteo is looking at is (up to sign) also the one the Diffie–Hellman key exchange uses. In that setup, Alice and Bob pick a large prime ppp and a generator ggg and do all their computations modulo ppp. Each of them picks a secret exponent (say aaa and bbb, respectively). They compute A=gaA=g^aA=ga and B=gbB=g^bB=gb respectively and publish them. To communicate, Alice computes Ba=Blog⁡gAB^a = B^{\log_g A}Ba=Blogg​A and Bob computes Ab=Alog⁡gBA^b = A^{\log_g B}Ab=Alogg​B. Since computing the discrete log to the base ggg is hard, no one else can figure out their shared secret.

Mike Stay (Nov 26 2024 at 17:16):

We're exponentiating a bunch of times, adding, then taking the log a bunch of times. Whichever parameter starts larger gets much larger after exponentiating, at which point adding the two doesn't make much difference to the size of the larger, and then you take logs to bring them back down. So we have this hierarchy:
