Some definitions:
{}
Span(C,×)\mathsf{Span}(C,\times)Span(C,×) is a bicategory where

  • (C,×)(C,\times)(C,×) is a cartesian monoidal category with pullbacks (i.e. is finitely complete)
  • Objects are objects of (C,×).(C,\times).(C,×).
  • Morphisms are spans in (C,×).(C,\times).(C,×).
  • 2-morphisms are maps between spans.

{}
Bim(Cop,×)\mathsf{Bim}(C^\mathsf{op},\times)Bim(Cop,×) is a bicategory where

  • (C,×)(C,\times)(C,×) is a cartesian monoidal category with pullbacks (i.e. is finitely complete)
  • Objects are monoid objects of (Cop,×).(C^\mathsf{op},\times).(Cop,×).
  • Morphisms are bimodules in (Cop,×).(C^\mathsf{op},\times).(Cop,×).
  • 2-morphisms are bimodule morphisms.

Then,

Span(C,×)≅Bim(Cop,×).\mathsf{Span}(C,\times) \cong \mathsf{Bim}(C^\mathsf{op},\times).Span(C,×)≅Bim(Cop,×).

In other words:

  • Every object c∈(C,×)c\in (C,\times)c∈(C,×) is a monoid object in (Cop,×).(C^\mathsf{op},\times).(Cop,×).

Some corrections: "the tensor product of modules is a coproduct". It's true that (for example) the tensor product of commutative rings has, for its underlying additive abelian group, the tensor product of the additive groups of the given rings.
