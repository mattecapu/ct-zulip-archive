Todd Trimble (Jul 31 2023 at 17:46):

The coalgebraic formulation of [[well-founded relation]] is due to Paul Taylor; he discusses it in his book Practical Foundations of Mathematics. And yes, his notion of well-foundedness is quite far-reaching! It applies to coalgebras of any endofunctor T:C→CT: C \to CT:C→C, if CCC has pullbacks and TTT is taut (preserves inverse images of subobjects), and it provides a key for doing recursion theory in a nice categorical way.
