Ralph Sarkis (Nov 08 2022 at 18:31):

The notation SetCop\mathsf{Set}^{\mathsf{C}^{\mathrm{op}}}SetCop denotes the category of functors from Cop\mathsf{C}^{\mathrm{op}}Cop to Set\mathsf{Set}Set, also called the category of presheaves on C\mathsf{C}C. Its objects are functors F:Cop→SetF: \mathsf{C}^{\mathrm{op}} \to \mathsf{Set}F:Cop→Set and the morphisms are natural transformations between these functors (the composition is componentwise, i.e., (η∘ϕ)X=ηX∘ϕX(\eta \circ \phi)_X = \eta_X \circ \phi_X(η∘ϕ)X​=ηX​∘ϕX​, it is called the vertical composition of natural transformation). This is one way to understand natural transformations: they are morphisms of functors (where functors are morphisms of categories).

Ralph Sarkis (Nov 08 2022 at 19:29):

We say that two objects AAA and BBB are isomorphic in C\mathsf{C}C, denoted by A≅BA\cong BA≅B, if there is a morphism f:A→Bf: A \to Bf:A→B in C\mathsf{C}C with a two-sided inverse f−1:B→Af^{-1}:B \to Af−1:B→A (it satisfies f∘f−1=idBf \circ f^{-1} = \mathrm{id}_Bf∘f−1=idB​ and f−1∘f=idAf^{-1}\circ f = \mathrm{id}_Af−1∘f=idA​), we call such an fff an isomorphism. You can check that isomorphisms in the category of sets are precisely bijections.

In category theory, we do basically everything up to isomorphism, meaning if you are doing something with an object AAA, you can replace it with an isomorphic object BBB and it will not change the outcome.

The consequence of the Yoneda lemma we are discussing says that AAA and BBB are the same thing (up to isomorphism) if and only if their viewpoint on all other objects is the same (up to isomorphism).

Ralph Sarkis (Nov 08 2022 at 20:08):

The hom functors are valued in Set\mathsf{Set}Set because for every objects AAA and BBB, Hom(A,B)\mathrm{Hom}(A,B)Hom(A,B) is a set. There are so-called enriched versions of the Yoneda lemma that talk about categories where Hom(A,B)\mathrm{Hom}(A,B)Hom(A,B) is something else than a set, but I don't know any gentle introduction to enriched categories to recommend you. You will need to get really comfortable with basic category theory before moving on.
