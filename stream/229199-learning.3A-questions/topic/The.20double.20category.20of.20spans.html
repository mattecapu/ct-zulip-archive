Topic: The double category of spans

John Baez (Aug 27 2025 at 13:56):

Dawson, Pare and Pronk have the following nice characterization of the double category Csp(A)\mathbb{C}\mathbf{sp}(\mathsf{A})Csp(A) of cospans in a category A\mathsf{A}A with pushouts. Roughly it says that Csp(A)\mathbb{C}\mathbf{sp}(\mathsf{A})Csp(A) is the free fibrant double category on A\mathsf{A}A:

Theorem 3.15. Let A\mathsf{A}A be a category with pushouts and D\mathbb{D}D a fibrant double category. Then composing with the inclusion A→Csp(A)\mathsf{A} \to \mathbb{C}\mathbf{sp}(\mathsf{A})A→Csp(A) gives an equivalence between the category of normal lax double functors from Csp(A)\mathbb{C}\mathbf{sp}(\mathsf{A})Csp(A) to D\mathbb{D}D and the category of functors from A\mathsf{A}A to D0\mathbb{D}_0D0​ , the category of objects and tight morphisms in D\mathbb{D}D .

John Baez (Aug 27 2025 at 13:58):

However I'm wondering which functors from A\mathsf{A}A to D0\mathbb{D}_0D0​ correspond to ordinary (that is, pseudo) double functors from Csp(A)\mathbb{C}\mathbf{sp}(\mathsf{A})Csp(A) to D\mathbb{D}D, rather than merely normal lax ones.

James Deikun (Aug 27 2025 at 14:56):

It's pushout-preserving functors. You can see this by looking at the two obvious ways you can write the cospan in a pushout square as a composite of a conjoint and a companion (in different orders) in Csp(A)\mathbf{\mathbb Csp}(\mathsf A)Csp(A).

Kevin Carlson (Aug 27 2025 at 17:18):

Since Csp(A)\mathbb{C}\mathbf{sp}(\mathsf{A})Csp(A) satisfies Beck-Chevalley and double functors preserve companions and conjoints, if you have a pushout-preserving functor F:A→D0F:\mathsf{A}\to \mathbb{D}_0F:A→D0​, then for there to exist any extension to a pseudo-double functor Fˉ:Csp(A)→D,\bar F:\mathbb{C}\mathbf{sp}(\mathsf{A})\to \mathbb{D},Fˉ:Csp(A)→D, we can see FFF will have to send every pushout square in A\mathsf{A}A to a Beck-Chevalley square in D.\mathbb{D}.D. So that shows the Beck-Chevalley condition is essentially necessary, if you're going to start with a pushout-preserving functor on A.\mathsf{A}.A. For that matter, even if you don't start by assuming FFF preserves pushouts, it will still have to send pushout squares to some-kind-of-square-with-the-Beck-Chevalley-property!

Kevin Carlson (Aug 27 2025 at 17:20):

So perhaps there's a generalization of 3.24 where you decorate D0\mathbb{D}_0D0​ with the squares you know are Beck-Chevalley in D\mathbb{D}D, and then pseudo-functors Csp(A)→D\mathbb{C}\mathbf{sp}(\mathsf{A})\to \mathbb{D}Csp(A)→D correspond to arbitrary functors A→D0\mathsf{A}\to\mathbb{D}_0A→D0​ which send pushout squares to Beck-Chevalley squares. But that seems like so little gain over the theorem as stated, in particular by having to add extra structure to D0\mathbb{D}_0D0​, a structure which is hard to figure out if the Beck-Chevalley squares aren't just the pushouts, I think you can see why they didn't bother.

Nathanael Arkor (Aug 28 2025 at 07:33):

[DPS10] do not consider Span as a right adjoint; only as a left adjoint. Their universal property is therefore of a different nature to yours. The only place I am aware of in which Span is exhibited as a right adjoint (in the 2-categorical literature) is Vaugelade's 3-page note Un adjoint au foncteur Span. She establishes that:
Catpb(TσL(B),E)≅Bicatl(B,Span(E))\text{Cat}_{pb}(T\sigma L(\mathscr B), \mathcal E) \cong \text{Bicat}_l(\mathscr B, \mathbf{Span}(\mathcal E))Catpb​(TσL(B),E)≅Bicatl​(B,Span(E))
for some functor TσLT\sigma LTσL (here LLL is the lax morphism classifier for bicategories, and TσT\sigmaTσ is a construction arising from the theory of limit sketches).

Nathanael Arkor (Aug 28 2025 at 07:44):

So I would not be deterred from continuing your investigation (though it would be worth taking a look at Vaugelade's two papers to see whether there is anything to be learnt from her approach).
