Topic: ✔ Does evaluation of a functor at object define a functor?

Ignat Insarov (Apr 05 2024 at 05:20):

Say we have an object XXX in C\mathbb{C}C.

  • For any functor F:C→DF: \mathbb{C} → \mathbb{D}F:C→D, we can compute FXFXFX, thereby sending objects of DC\mathbb{D}^{\mathbb{C}}DC to objects of D\mathbb{D}D.
  • For any natural transformation τ:F→Gτ: F → Gτ:F→G, we can compute its component at XXX — τX:FX→GXτ_X: FX → GXτX​:FX→GX.
  • Since identity and composition of natural transformations is defined component-wise, we have υ=τ∘σ⇒υX=τX∘σXυ = τ ∘ σ ⇒ υ_X = τ_X ∘ σ_Xυ=τ∘σ⇒υX​=τX​∘σX​ and idXid_XidX​ is the identity of XXX.

So, for any object XXX in C\mathbb{C}C we can have a functor (⋅)X:DC→D(·)X: \mathbb{D}^{\mathbb{C}} → \mathbb{D}(⋅)X:DC→D defined as F↦FX,τ↦τXF ↦ FX, τ ↦ τ_XF↦FX,τ↦τX​.

Is this right?

Ralph Sarkis (Apr 05 2024 at 05:43):

That is correct, I have seen this functor called the evaluation functor at XXX.
The evaluation functor is what you get when you consider all XXX at once, it is of type ev:C×DC→D\mathsf{ev}: \mathbb{C} \times \mathbb{D}^{\mathbb{C}} \rightarrow \mathbb{D}ev:C×DC→D.
