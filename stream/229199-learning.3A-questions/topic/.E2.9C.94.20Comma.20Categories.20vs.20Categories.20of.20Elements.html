Topic: ✔ Comma Categories vs Categories of Elements

Ruby Khondaker (she/her) (Jul 30 2025 at 08:21):

To sum up what I think I learned regarding my original question:

  • It's convenient to think of the "category of elements" construction as fundamentally being one on profunctors rather than just presheaves. In this sense, it can be viewed as a generalisation of the arrow category construction.
  • Comma categories are then a particularly nice instance of these, arising as the category of elements of Hom(F−,G−)Hom(F-, G-)Hom(F−,G−) viewed as a profunctor from DDD to CCC, for F:C→EF : C \to EF:C→E and G:D→EG : D \to EG:D→E.
  • The category of elements of a profunctor gives a notion of graph of a profunctor. The other notions of graph come from the compact closed bicategory structure on Prof - explicitly, that profunctors C×D→EC \times D \to EC×D→E naturally correspond to profunctors C→Dop×EC \to D^\text{op} \times EC→Dop×E.

In praise of @Bryce Clarke's message which I found very enlightning a big month ago, let me share a small application of this viewpoint that I hope will illustrate its elegance.

I follow the notations of the quoted message. Let CCC be a monoidal category and ⊥\bot⊥ be an object of CCC. We consider the associated category of Chu spaces:

  • whose objects are triples (a,b,p)(a, b, p)(a,b,p) where aaa and bbb are two objects of CCC and p:a⊗b→⊥p : a \otimes b \to \botp:a⊗b→⊥ is a morphism of CCC,
  • whose morphisms (a,b,p)→(a′,b′,p′)(a, b, p) \to (a', b', p')(a,b,p)→(a′,b′,p′) are pairs of morphisms f:a→a′f : a \to a'f:a→a′ and g:b′→bg : b' \to bg:b′→b which verify the "adjunction-like" equation

p′∘(f⊗1b′)=p∘(1a⊗g)p' \circ (f \otimes 1_{b'}) \quad=\quad p \circ (1_{a} \otimes g)p′∘(f⊗1b′​)=p∘(1a​⊗g)

This category, of Chu spaces built from CCC and ⊥\bot⊥, is exactly the category of elements of an appropriate profunctor! namely P:C↛CopP : C \nrightarrow C^{\mathrm{op}}P:C↛Cop given by the formula
