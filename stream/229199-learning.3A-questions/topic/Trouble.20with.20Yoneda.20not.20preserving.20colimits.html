Topic: Trouble with Yoneda not preserving colimits

John Onstead (May 06 2025 at 11:24):

As I've been learning about presheaves and the co-Yoneda lemma, I've realized it's set up an interesting dichotomy between two ways of gluing objects in a category CCC. You can either take a colimit in CCC to get another object of CCC that acts like the objects in the diagram "glued together", or you could take the colimit in the presheaf category. This comes with the intuition of building up a space "locally modeled on" the objects of CCC that might not live in CCC itself. The reason this seems to be a dichotomy is because Yoneda embedding does not preserve colimits, so these two ways of gluing do not, on the surface, seem to correspond.

John Onstead (May 06 2025 at 11:25):

The reason I'm struggling with this is because there are situations where you have to choose between these options to do some sort of gluing you want, and it's not always obvious which choice to make. My question is: is there any general rule connecting these two ideas, and if so, what would be the conceptual underpinning (IE, in terms of what gluing is doing what). Maybe there's some formula to convert a colimit diagram in one setting into an "equivalent" one in the other setting?

James Deikun (May 06 2025 at 11:43):

To expand on this: subcanonical Grothendieck topologies on the category CCC present ways of gluing objects of CCC that interpolate between the two above methods, with the trivial Grothendieck topology representing the presheaf category and its colimits and the canonical Grothendieck topology representing, I believe, a completion of CCC that preserves any [[universal colimits]] it already has.

And for your question as to a formula to convert: you convert a colimit in C^\widehat CC to one in CCC by taking the [[weighted colimit]] of the identity functor in CCC weighted by the presheaf. This weighted colimit will exist precisely if taking the colimit in CCC in the first place would have.

Mike Shulman (May 06 2025 at 14:49):

More generally, for any collection Φ\PhiΦ of colimits that exist in CCC, you can consider the full subcategory of the presheaf category C^\widehat{C}C consisting of those presheaves that preserve the colimits in Φ\PhiΦ (that is, take them to limits in Set). This is (assuming CCC and Φ\PhiΦ are small) a reflective subcategory of C^\widehat{C}C, hence complete and cocomplete, and the Yoneda embedding factors through it by a functor that preserves Φ\PhiΦ-colimits. It has a universal property as the free cocompletion of CCC subject to preservation of Φ\PhiΦ-colimits.

Mike Shulman (May 07 2025 at 15:10):

Of course it depends on what "representations" you pick. For instance, there are in fact a few colimits that Yoneda does preserve, namely the [[absolute colimits]]. And if you choose a Φ\PhiΦ-colimit in CCC, then it would be preserved in C^Φ\widehat{C}_\PhiCΦ​. But if you choose an arbitrary colimit in CCC and an arbitrary one in C^Φ\widehat{C}_\PhiCΦ​ they might have totally different shapes even if they were both Φ\PhiΦ-colimits.

Mike Shulman (May 07 2025 at 21:28):

I don't know if this helps, but one analogy you can think about is the free group FGFGFG generated by the underlying set of a group GGG. There's an injection X→FXX\to FXX→FX for any set XXX, and so we can if we wish think of the generators inside FGFGFG as "being" the elements of GGG. But the multiplication in the group FGFGFG has nothing to do with the original multiplication in GGG; in particular the product of two generators is no longer a generator.
