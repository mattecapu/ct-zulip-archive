Kevin Carlson (Sep 12 2024 at 20:49):

Since the arrow category commutes with the pullback and the 2-functor of taking pullback preserves adjunctions, the left adjoint to the insertion-of-zero functor C×XD→(C×XD)→=C→×X→D→C\times_X D \to (C\times_X D)^\to= C^\to \times_{X^\to} D^\toC×X​D→(C×X​D)→=C→×X→​D→ will be given by the pullback of the analogous left adjoints on C,X,C,X,C,X, and D.D.D. In other words to take the cone on a morphism (f,g,H)(f,g,H)(f,g,H) you take the cones of f,gf,gf,g in C,D,C,D,C,D, and then take the cone on HHH as well, seen as a morphism in X→,X^\to,X→, using that the cone is a functor X→→X.X^\to \to X.X→→X.

Kevin Carlson (Sep 12 2024 at 20:51):

As far as formalities go, the 2-functor I'm referring to is happening in the homotopy 2-category of ∞\infty∞-categories, but I probably wouldn't worry too precisely about that until you've decided whether you feel good about the concrete answer at the end.
