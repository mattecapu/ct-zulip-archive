It seems to me it's enough to prove this fact for MMM free, since the naturality square for the quotient F→MF \to MF→M presenting MMM will then say αM=r−\alpha_M = r-αM​=r− for any other MMM.

That's an instance of the Yoneda lemma for one-object categories. And the Yoneda lemma is true for categories enriched over any monoidal category, hence also for one-object ones, i.e. monoid objects. At least if the monoidal category is closed and complete, you can state the result by saying that MMM is isomorphic to the object of left MMM-set maps from MMM to MMM, the latter defined as a subobject of the internal-hom cut out by an equalizer.
