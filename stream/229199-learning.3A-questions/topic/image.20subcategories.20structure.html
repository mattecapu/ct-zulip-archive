John Baez (Jan 16 2022 at 02:32):

If AAA is any subcategory of BBB, there is a functor F:A→BF: A \to BF:A→B that is the identity on objects of AAA and morphisms of AAA: this is usually called "the inclusion of AAA in BBB".

Note that A=ImFA = Im FA=ImF, i.e. AAA is the smallest subcategory of BBB that contains F(A)F(A)F(A) (=A= A=A).

So when you say

John Baez (Jan 16 2022 at 02:33):

Consider now the collection of all subcategories of BBB of the form ImFIm FImF for some functor F:A→BF: A \to BF:A→B.

this is equivalent to saying "consider now the collection of all subcategories of BBB". I think this is a simpler way to think about things.

John Baez (Jan 16 2022 at 02:34):

Does this collection have some natural kind of structure (perhaps ordered with respect to "informativeness")?

It is partially ordered by inclusion: that is, we can say A≤A′A \le A'A≤A′ if every object (resp. morphism) of AAA is an object (resp. morphism) of A′A'A′, and this relation ≤\le≤ is a partial order.

John Baez (Jan 16 2022 at 03:21):

Oh, wow! The great thing about this definition is that mathematicians will automatically find it familiar and interesting. In this situation we say "GGG factors through FFF". This concept makes sense and is widely studied, not just for categories and functors, but for objects and morphisms in any category.

Given objects AAA and BBB in any category, there's a preorder on the set of morphisms from AAA to BBB such that G≤FG \le FG≤F if GGG factors through FFF. You might enjoy checking that it's a preorder.

It's not a partial ordering because it's not true that

F≤G and G≤F  ⟹  F=GF \le G \text{ and } G \le F \implies F = GF≤G and G≤F⟹F=G

John Baez (Jan 16 2022 at 03:23):

Whenever we have any preorder, we can define F∼GF \sim GF∼G to mean F≤GF \le GF≤G and G≤FG \le FG≤F. Then ∼\sim∼ is an equivalence relation.

John Baez (Jan 16 2022 at 03:24):

In the particular case where the category is Cat, when G≤FG \le FG≤F you're saying "FFF is at least as informative as GGG", and when F∼GF \sim GF∼G you're saying "FFF and GGG are equally informative".

John Baez (Jan 16 2022 at 03:25):

You'll notice I'm telling you the various facts at a fairly high level of generality: "in any category" and "whenever we have any preorder". This is what category theorists like to do.

John Baez (Jan 16 2022 at 03:26):

That way we build up an arsenal of widely useful tools.
