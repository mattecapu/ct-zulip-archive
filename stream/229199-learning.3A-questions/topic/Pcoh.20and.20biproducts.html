Topic: Pcoh and biproducts

Alejandro Díaz-Caro (Mar 21 2024 at 21:24):

A quick question: Does the category of Probabilistic Coherent Spaces (Pcoh) have biproducts? I was checking Danos and Ehrhard's paper and I am not completelly sure. There is certainly a Cartesian product, noted as the additive conjunction &\&&, and it is said that ⊕iXi=(&iXi⊥)⊥\oplus_{i} X_{i} = (\&_{i} X_i^{\bot})^{\bot}⊕i​Xi​=(&i​Xi⊥​)⊥. But I am not sure if that implies that the coproduct is the same as the Cartesian product.

Morgan Rogers (he/him) (Mar 22 2024 at 09:29):

It seems like they're different: the ⊕\oplus⊕ notation should not be interpreted as a biproduct unless they explicitly say so.

Damiano Mazza (Mar 22 2024 at 09:52):

Yes, they are different. For example, if 1\mathbf 11 is the space ({∗},[0,1])(\{\ast\},[0,1])({∗},[0,1]) (the monoidal unit), then both 1&1\mathbf 1\mathrel{\&}\mathbf 11&1 and 1⊕11\oplus\mathbf 11⊕1 have web {0,1}\{0,1\}{0,1}, but P(1&1)=[0,1]×[0,1]\mathsf P(\mathbf 1\mathrel{\&}\mathbf 1)=[0,1]\times[0,1]P(1&1)=[0,1]×[0,1] (the unit square), whereas P(1⊕1)\mathsf P(\mathbf 1\oplus\mathbf 1)P(1⊕1) is the set of all pairs (α,β)∈[0,1]×[0,1](\alpha,\beta)\in[0,1]\times[0,1](α,β)∈[0,1]×[0,1] such that α+β≤1\alpha+\beta\leq 1α+β≤1 (the "unit triangle").

Damiano Mazza (Mar 22 2024 at 09:55):

Notice that the type 1⊕1\mathbf 1\oplus\mathbf 11⊕1 is the type of Booleans, and in fact the space 1⊕1\mathbf 1\oplus\mathbf 11⊕1 reflects this intuition in the probabilistic case: an element of P(1⊕1)\mathsf P(\mathbf 1\oplus\mathbf 1)P(1⊕1) is a sub-probability distribution on {0,1}\{0,1\}{0,1}, i.e., a probabilistic Boolean. On the other hand, since 1\mathbf 11 is the space of sub-probability distributions on the singleton (intuitively representing the probability that a program of unit type terminates), 1&1\mathbf 1\mathrel{\&}\mathbb 11&1 is just the space of pairs of such things.
