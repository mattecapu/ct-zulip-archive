Mike Shulman (Jun 28 2025 at 21:48):

IIRC uniform spaces can be defined as monads in a certain bicategory whose objects are sets and whose morphisms are filters of some kind. Metric spaces are monads in the bicategory of [0,∞][0,\infty][0,∞]-valued matrices, and the "underlying uniform space" functor of a metric space is induced by a functor between these bicategories, so you can think of it as a sort of "change of enrichment". Thus saying that two metric spaces are uniformly equivalent is analogous to saying, say, that two topologically enriched categories have equivalent underlying ordinary categories.
