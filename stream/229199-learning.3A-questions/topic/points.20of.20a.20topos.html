Topic: points of a topos

Leopold Schlicht (Aug 28 2021 at 18:19):

For which toposes is the category of its points small?

Mike Shulman (Aug 29 2021 at 01:19):

The category of points of a localic topos is always (essentially) small. Beyond that I don't know a general answer; e.g. I wouldn't expect there to be any clean criterion for which geometric theories have only a small number of isomorphism classes of models in Set.

Mike Shulman (Aug 29 2021 at 14:42):

Any topos has a "localic reflection", obtained from the hyperconnected-localic factorization system. Thus, the inclusion of locales in toposes has a left inverse which is in fact a left adjoint. Similarly, the inclusion of sober spaces in locales has a left inverse that is a right adjoint, which takes the space of points. So combined, the inclusion of sober spaces in toposes also has a left inverse, though it is no longer an adjoint on either side.

Jens Hemelaer (Sep 01 2021 at 08:54):

Hi @Leopold Schlicht, something close to
E↦(points of E, open subtoposes of E)\mathcal E\mapsto (\text{points of }\mathcal E\text{, open subtoposes of }\mathcal E)E↦(points of E, open subtoposes of E)
is described in this paper by Caramello, Section 2.1.

If you take a set of points XXX of the topos E\mathcal{E}E, then the topology on XXX given by the open subtoposes is called the subterminal topology. If XXX is a separating set of points, then Sh(X)\mathbf{Sh}(X)Sh(X) is the localic reflection of E\mathcal{E}E.

For a monoid MMM, points of PSh(M)\mathbf{PSh}(M)PSh(M) are the same thing as flat left MMM-sets. The topos PSh(M)\mathbf{PSh}(M)PSh(M) does not have any open subtoposes (other than the empty topos and the topos itself), so the subterminal topology will be the indiscrete topology. If you then turn this into a sober space, you get just one point.

Mike Shulman said:

I wouldn't expect there to be any clean criterion for which geometric theories have only a small number of isomorphism classes of models in Set.

At least for first-order theories TTT over a finite signature the following conditions are equivalent:

  1. TTT has a small number of models up to isomorphism.
  2. TTT has finitely many models up to isomorphism.
  3. TTT has no infinite model.

(3) implies (2) follows from the compactness theorem and (1) implies (3) follows from Löwenheim–Skolem.
