Topic: ✔ Free cocompletion are lax-idempotent 2-monads

Fernando Chu (Jun 04 2025 at 09:47):

The nlab states (paraphrasing)

The cocompletion under a class of certain diagrams gives a lax-idempotent 2-monad T:Cat→CatT:\text{Cat} \to \text{Cat}T:Cat→Cat, since then an algebra α:TC→C\alpha:TC \to Cα:TC→C is a category CCC with all such colimits, which implies TTT is lax-idempotent.

I am having trouble seeing the intermediate step, i.e. that algebras must have all such colimits. Am I missing something obvious? Can this be shown using only the universal property of the cocompletion, without reference to a particular presentation?

Writing η\etaη for the unit of TTT, if α⊣ηC\alpha \dashv \eta_Cα⊣ηC​ (as any algebra for a lax-idempotent 2-monads has), then for a diagram K:J→CK:J \to CK:J→C we have

C(α(Colim ηCK),X)≅TC(Colim ηCK,ηCX)≅[J,TC](ηCK,ΔηCX)≅∫jTC(ηCKj,ηCX)≅∫jC(Kj,X)≅[J,C](K,ΔX)C(\alpha(\text{Colim}\ \eta_C K), X) \cong TC(\text{Colim}\ \eta_C K, \eta_C X) \cong [J,TC](\eta_CK, \Delta_{\eta_CX}) \cong \int_j TC(\eta_CKj, \eta_CX) \cong \int_j C(Kj,X) \cong [J,C](K, \Delta_X)C(α(Colim ηC​K),X)≅TC(Colim ηC​K,ηC​X)≅[J,TC](ηC​K,ΔηC​X​)≅∫j​TC(ηC​Kj,ηC​X)≅∫j​C(Kj,X)≅[J,C](K,ΔX​)

So if we can show that TTT is indeed lax-idempotent, then the missing step holds; so I'd also be happy with such a proof. However, the nlab used the missing step to prove that TTT is lax-idempotent, so they probably had in mind some direct proof.

Nathanael Arkor (Jun 04 2025 at 10:02):

There are a few ways to prove this, but perhaps the most intuitive is to show that a category CCC admits Φ\PhiΦ-colimits (for some class of shapes Φ\PhiΦ) if and only if the inclusion C↪Φ(C)C \hookrightarrow \Phi(C)C↪Φ(C) into the free Φ\PhiΦ-cocompletion admits a left adjoint (which then tells you that the algebras for TTT, which are by definition the Φ\PhiΦ-cocomplete categories, are characterised by the adjointness condition that defines a lax-idempotent pseudomonad).

Nathanael Arkor (Jun 04 2025 at 10:03):

I think it's a good exercise to try to show this yourself, but if you get stuck, there's a proof in Proposition 3.17 of Notes on enriched categories with colimits of some class.

John Baez (Jun 04 2025 at 10:33):

Intiutively, α\alphaα preserving colimits of some sort is what says CCC has them.

It may help to think about a decategorified analogue like the monad TTT for commutative monoids, though here "having finite sums" is a structure, not a property, so this monad is not idempotent. In this case the map α:TS→S\alpha : TS \to Sα:TS→S preserves finite sums when SSS is a commutative monoid and thus has them.

Nathanael Arkor (Jun 04 2025 at 11:21):

Fernando Chu said:

I'm probably missing something extremely obvious. Why is this the case? An algebra for TTT should be a category CCC equipped with a (not necessarily Φ\PhiΦ-colimits-preserving) functor α:TC→C\alpha:TC\to Cα:TC→C such that some diagrams commute, right?

By definition, a "free Φ\PhiΦ-cocompletion" provides a left pseudoadjoint to the forgetful 2-functor from the 2-category of Φ\PhiΦ-cocomplete categories and Φ\PhiΦ-cocontinuous functors to the 2-category of categories.

Nathanael Arkor (Jun 04 2025 at 11:23):

This forgetful 2-functor is furthermore pseudomonadic. This is perhaps the point that's not obvious to you? It can be verified by checking a 2-dimensional version of the monadicity theorem, for instance.

Nathanael Arkor (Jun 04 2025 at 11:25):

However, if you'd like a really concrete proof that the algebras are precisely the Φ\PhiΦ-cocomplete categories (i.e. directly using the definition of (pseudo)algebra), without using any abstract machinery, @Philip Saville, Andrew Slattery and I give such a proof in §7 of Bicategories of algebras for relative pseudomonads.
