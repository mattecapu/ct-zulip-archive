Jean-Baptiste Vienney (Jul 07 2025 at 03:03):

Maybe, maybe, maybe you can find some interest in this presentation with regard to your question. I'm not sure but I'll post it in case! (To start with, I'm not even sure to understand what you want. What "a functor T:CJ→CT:\mathcal{C}^J \rightarrow \mathcal{C}T:CJ→C which is a monad in every j∈Jj \in Jj∈J is supposed to mean precisely?")

For every Set\mathbf{Set}Set-operad O\mathcal{O}O, a notion that is called a "O\mathcal{O}O-category" is defined. This a category with a bunch of functors Cn→C\mathcal{C}^n \rightarrow \mathcal{C}Cn→C for various integers nnn, multiplication operations and a unit operations. The various integers nnn depend on the choice of the operad O\mathcal{O}O

For some choice of O\mathcal{O}O it gives exactly a monad on C\mathcal{C}C. For some other choice of O\mathcal{O}O it gives exactly a structure of lax monoidal category on C\mathcal{C}C. And of course for other operads, it gives yet other categorical structures.

And then you can also define algebras over a O\mathcal{O}O-category and you recover algebras over a monad and monoid in a lax monoidal category for some choices of O\mathcal{O}O.
