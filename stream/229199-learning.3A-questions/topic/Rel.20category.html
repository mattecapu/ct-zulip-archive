Ralph Sarkis (Dec 24 2022 at 22:30):

I am not sure about Rel\mathbf{Rel}Rel as a simple category, but Rel\mathbf{Rel}Rel is also a thin 222-category or a poset-enriched category. No matter if these terms don't mean anything to you, the important part is that for two morphisms R,S:X→YR,S: X \rightarrow YR,S:X→Y, you can talk about inclusions of one in the other. We say that R⊆SR\subseteq SR⊆S if and only if R(x,y)  ⟹  S(x,y)R(x,y) \implies S(x,y)R(x,y)⟹S(x,y) for all (x,y)∈X×Y(x,y) \in X\times Y(x,y)∈X×Y. Now you can try to figure out a way to use this additional structure to talk about relfexivity, symmetry and transitivity.

Ralph Sarkis (Dec 28 2022 at 02:45):

Let us start with something else. Do you know about the opposite of a relation? If you have a relation R⊆X×YR\subseteq X\times YR⊆X×Y, the oppoiste of RRR is a relation Rop⊆Y×XR^{\mathrm{op}} \subseteq Y \times XRop⊆Y×X defined by (y,x)∈Rop⇔(x,y)∈R(y,x) \in R^{\mathrm{op}} \Leftrightarrow (x,y) \in R(y,x)∈Rop⇔(x,y)∈R.

In Rel\mathbf{Rel}Rel, RRR is a morphism X→YX \rightarrow YX→Y and Rop:Y→XR^{\mathrm{op}}: Y \rightarrow XRop:Y→X, so we have composites R;Rop:X→XR;R^{\mathrm{op}}: X \rightarrow XR;Rop:X→X and Rop;R:Y→YR^{\mathrm{op}}; R: Y \rightarrow YRop;R:Y→Y (I write composition of relations in diagrammatic order, i.e. R;Rop=Rop∘RR;R^{\mathrm{op}} = R^{\mathrm{op}} \circ RR;Rop=Rop∘R).

What does it mean for the first composite to be equal to idX\mathrm{id}_XidX​? What if the second is equal to idY\mathrm{id}_YidY​? What if both are true?

Evan Patterson (Feb 27 2023 at 03:53):

Peiyuan Zhu said:

Is there any work on algebra of Rel? Something like T:R1×R2×R3→RT:R_1\times R_2\times R_3\rightarrow RT:R1​×R2​×R3​→R that takes several relations and map to one relation

This sort of thing is well described by the cartesian bicategory of relations or, better yet, the cartesian double category of relations. In such a structure, you can take products of objects (sets) and products of relations, and then you can look at cells betweens relations, which include maps of the form you describe.
