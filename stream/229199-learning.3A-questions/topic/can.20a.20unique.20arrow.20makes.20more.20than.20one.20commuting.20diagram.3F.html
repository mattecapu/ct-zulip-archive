Ralph Sarkis (Apr 24 2023 at 15:26):

Your first commutative diagrams says that f1=π1∘h=f2f_1 = \pi_1 \circ h = f_2f1​=π1​∘h=f2​ and similarly for the ggg's. One way to say this is that the universal property of the product is equivalent to there being a bijection HomC(T,A×B)→HomC(T,A)×HomC(T,B)\mathrm{Hom}_{\mathbf{C}}(T,A\times B) \to \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathbf{C}}(T,A) \times \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathbf{C}}(T, B)HomC​(T,A×B)→HomC​(T,A)×HomC​(T,B) for every TTT (the second ×\times× is the Cartesian product of sets).
