Topic: Cohesion for (infinity,1)-categories of cubical objects

Madeleine Birchfield (Apr 09 2025 at 23:59):

Let E\mathcal{E}E be an (∞,1)(\infty,1)(∞,1)-topos. For which categories of cubes □\Box□ is the (∞,1)(\infty,1)(∞,1)-category E□op\mathcal{E}^{\Box^\mathrm{op}}E□op of cubical objects in E\mathcal{E}E cohesive over the base E\mathcal{E}E?

Adrian Clough (Apr 10 2025 at 06:48):

So for AAA any small (∞,1)(\infty,1)(∞,1)-category you get a triple adjunction between EAop\mathcal{E}^{A^{\mathrm{op}}}EAop and E\mathcal{E}E given by colim⊣const⊣lim\mathrm{colim} \dashv \mathrm{const} \dashv \mathrm{lim}colim⊣const⊣lim. If AAA admits a final object 111 then lim\mathrm{lim}lim is just given by evaluating at 111, thus commutes with colimits, and thus admits a right adjoint by AFT. So, all that you need to check is whether colim\mathrm{colim}colim commutes with finite products. This is equivalent to AAA being sifted. AFAIK all cube categories used in practice are test categories, and in this case being sifted is equivalent to being a strong test category. I don't know which cube categories are strong test categories, but now you can significantly narrow the scope of your search :smile:
