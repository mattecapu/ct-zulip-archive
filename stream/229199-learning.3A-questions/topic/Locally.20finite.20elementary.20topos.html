Topic: Locally finite elementary topos

Vincent Moreau (Oct 30 2024 at 10:27):

Hello everyone! I am looking for examples of essentially small elementary toposes which are locally finite, which by that I mean that every hom-set is a finite set.

If J\mathbf{J}J is a small category, then the functor category [J,FinSet][\mathbf{J}, \mathbf{FinSet}][J,FinSet] may or may not be an elementary topos, and may or may not be locally finite. I am interested in the case where both properties hold.

My question is the following: is every essentially small locally finite elementary topos of the form [J,FinSet][\mathbf{J}, \mathbf{FinSet}][J,FinSet] for some small J\mathbf{J}J?

Ryuya Hora (Oct 30 2024 at 11:12):

Vincent Moreau said:

My question is the following: is every essentially small locally finite elementary topos of the form [J,FinSet] for some small J?

For a topological group G, its continuous actions on finite sets form an essentially small locally finite elementary topos. But i don't know whether it is (accidentally?) of the form of [J, FinSet]. (For example, the topos of finite continuous actions of the profinite integers is equivalent to the topos [Z, FinSet].

What I've read in Sketches of an Elephant is Cor 2.2.22* which states that a topos EEE is of the form of [J,FinSet][\mathbf{J},\mathbf{FinSet}][J,FinSet] for a finite category J\mathbf{J}J, if and only if EEE is bounded over FinSet\mathbf{FinSet}FinSet.

If the essential smallness assumption (relative to a fixed Grothendieck universe U) is omitted, I think the topos of (U-)large group actions on FinSet provides a counter-example. For example, let EEE be a category whose objects are finite set XXX equipped with a (U-)large family of automorphisms {ρκ ⁣:X→X}κ: U-small cardinal\{\rho_{\kappa}\colon X\to X\}_{\kappa \text{: U-small cardinal}}{ρκ​:X→X}κ: U-small cardinal​. This category EEE is locally finite, boolean, elementary topos, but not essentially small.

Ryuya Hora (Nov 08 2024 at 01:33):

If we consider toposes of profinite topological group actions, we need to consider strongly non-complete one. (If G is strongly complete prof.top.grp., then the topos Cont(G)\mathrm{Cont}(G)Cont(G) is equivalent to [Disc(G),FinSet][\mathrm{Disc}(G), \mathbf{FinSet}][Disc(G),FinSet].)

So, what I've been thinking as a candidate is, the topos of continuous actions of (Z/2Z)N(\mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z})^{{\mathbb{N}} }(Z/2Z)N. (Although (Z/2Z)^ℕ has only countably many open subgroups, it has uncountably many index-2 subgroups.)

This topos can be written down as follows:

Its object is a set XXX equipped with a countable family of endofunctions {τi:X→X}i∈N\{\tau_i :X \to X\}_{i\in \mathbb{N}}{τi​:X→X}i∈N​ such that
・Each endofunction is an involution τi∘τi=idX\tau_i \circ \tau_i = \mathrm{id}_Xτi​∘τi​=idX​.
・The involutions are commutative τi∘τj=τj∘τi\tau_i \circ \tau_j =\tau_j \circ \tau_i τi​∘τj​=τj​∘τi​
・Only finitely many involutions are non-trivial ∃n∈N,∀i>n,τi=idX∃n\in \mathbb{N}, ∀i>n, \tau_i= \mathrm{id}_X∃n∈N,∀i>n,τi​=idX​
Morphisms are functions preserving all involutions.

This defines a boolean, connected, essentially small, and locally finite elementary topos.
