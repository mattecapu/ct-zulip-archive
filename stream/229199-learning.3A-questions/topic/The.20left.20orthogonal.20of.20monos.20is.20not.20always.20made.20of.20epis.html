Topic: The left orthogonal of monos is not always made of epis

fosco (Dec 30 2025 at 10:33):

The usual definition of the class of "strong epimorphisms" in a completely general category is Epis:=Epi∩⊥(Mono)Epi^s := Epi\cap {}^\perp(Mono)Epis:=Epi∩⊥(Mono): a "strong epi" in CCC is an epi p:E→Bp : E \to Bp:E→B which, in addition, is also left orthogonal to all monos.

The conditions under which asking ppp to be an epi is redundant are, however, extremely weak: it is enough for CCC to have equalizers, and in fact it seems to be enough that the diagonal B→B×BB\to B\times BB→B×B exists, and that ppp is orthogonal to it, to ensure ppp is an epimorphism.

How to build a counterexample, then? Can one find a (possibly finite) category CCC such that there exists a ppp, which is not an epimorphism, and yet p∈⊥(Mono)p\in {}^\perp(Mono)p∈⊥(Mono)?

Does this work? Take the free category on the graph [image], subject to the relations f∘p=g∘pf\circ p=g\circ pf∘p=g∘p, p∘ap=p∘bpp\circ a_p=p\circ b_pp∘ap​=p∘bp​, f∘af=f∘bff\circ a_f = f\circ b_ff∘af​=f∘bf​, g∘ag=g∘bgg\circ a_g=g\circ b_gg∘ag​=g∘bg​, chosen in order to make ppp not an epi, and p,f,gp,f,gp,f,g not monos; in particular, g∘af≠g∘bfg\circ a_f\ne g\circ b_fg∘af​=g∘bf​, f∘ag≠f∘bgf\circ a_g\neq f\circ b_gf∘ag​=f∘bg​, etc.: so, ppp is not an epi, and there are no nontrivial lifting problems to solve against the monos (for example, the arrows ax,bx:Zx→...a_x,b_x : Z_x \to ...ax​,bx​:Zx​→... are vacuously mono, but hom⁡(E,Zx)\hom(E,Z_x)hom(E,Zx​) is empty).
