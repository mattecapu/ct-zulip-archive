Topic: Countable reals

Madeleine Birchfield (Feb 03 2024 at 04:35):

One and a half years ago Andrej Bauer put out a video where he says that there is a topos in which the Dedekind real numbers are countable: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4CBFUojXoq4

The definition of countable used in the video is that there is a surjection N→R\mathbb{N} \to \mathbb{R}N→R. But without countable choice, which is used to prove that R\mathbb{R}R is uncountable, not every surjection out of the natural numbers has a section. Is it consistent that there is a split surjection N→R\mathbb{N} \to \mathbb{R}N→R in constructive mathematics? Without countable choice one has to distinguish between surjections and split surjections, and I suppose between split surjections which there merely exists a section and split surjections which have a choice of section.

Madeleine Birchfield (Feb 03 2024 at 06:30):

Also, is the locale of real numbers in that topos still uncountable? There are typically more opens than points in the locale of real numbers unless the locale of real numbers is spatial.

Andrew Swan (Feb 03 2024 at 08:44):

You can show there is no split surjection N→R\mathbb{N} \to \mathbb{R}N→R the same way you show there is no bijection. There cannot be both a surjection N→R\mathbb{N} \to \mathbb{R}N→R and an injection R→N\mathbb{R} \to \mathbb{N}R→N. The injection is necessarily discontinuous, which lets you derive WLPO, which allows you to use one of the classical logic proofs that there is no surjection.
