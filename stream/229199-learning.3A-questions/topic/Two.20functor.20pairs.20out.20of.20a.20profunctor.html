Your observation that they are composition (the second two) and right extensions/lifts (the right adjoints to compositions) when co/presheaves are profunctors to/from the point is correct. What other additional (deeper?) characterization would you like?

fosco (Mar 19 2024 at 08:51):

Simplifying a bit: in a bicategory K\cal KK, for a 1-cell f:X→Yf : X \to Yf:X→Y,

  • for every 0-cell AAA postcomposing with fff is a functor K(A,X)→K(A,Y){\cal K}(A,X)\to{\cal K}(A,Y)K(A,X)→K(A,Y); if this functor has a right adjoint, you call it the right lifting along fff
  • for every 0-cell AAA precomposing with fff is a functor K(Y,A)→K(X,A){\cal K}(Y,A)\to{\cal K}(X,A)K(Y,A)→K(X,A); if this functor has a right adjoint, you call it the right extension along fff.
