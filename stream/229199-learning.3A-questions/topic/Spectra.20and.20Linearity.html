John Baez (Mar 27 2025 at 23:59):

Keith wrote:

I mean, I doubt many people think of linear algebra as being about abelian structure, at least not it the immediate.

Most ordinary folks think about linear algebra as being about vector spaces, but many important features of linear algebra generalize to modules of an arbitrary commutative ring RRR, and when we take R=ZR = \mathbb{Z}R=Z these are none other than abelian groups. That's the simplest reason why "abelian" and "linear algebra" sound very similar to Urs (and me).

Next, the category of RRR-modules for any ring RRR is an [[abelian category]].

One way to see the proximity between the spectral side and linearity is through a construction known as the [[heart]], which picks out the "0-sector" of stable ∞\infty∞-categories. E.g., the heart of the ∞\infty∞-category of HKH \mathbb{K}HK module spectra is the category of K\mathbb{K}K-vector spaces. You can see these module spectra as a kind of categorified vector space.
