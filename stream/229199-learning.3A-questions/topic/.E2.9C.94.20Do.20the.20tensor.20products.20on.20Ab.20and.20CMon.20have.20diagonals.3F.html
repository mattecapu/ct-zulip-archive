Tobias Fritz (Jun 14 2025 at 08:20):

The only natural diagonal on Ab is to use the zero morphism A→A⊗AA \to A \otimes AA→A⊗A for every AAA.

In fact, already for A=ZA = \mathbb{Z}A=Z, the only morphism Z→Z⊗Z\mathbb{Z} \to \mathbb{Z} \otimes \mathbb{Z}Z→Z⊗Z that is natural with respect to endomorphisms of Z\mathbb{Z}Z is the zero morphism. That's because these endomorphisms are given by multiplication by a fixed integer nnn, and under Z⊗Z≅Z\mathbb{Z} \otimes \mathbb{Z} \cong \mathbb{Z}Z⊗Z≅Z, this corresponds to multiplication by n2n^2n2 on Z⊗Z\mathbb{Z} \otimes \mathbb{Z}Z⊗Z. But the only additive map f:Z→Zf : \mathbb{Z} \to \mathbb{Z}f:Z→Z that satisfies f(nx)=n2f(x)f(nx) = n^2 f(x)f(nx)=n2f(x) for all nnn is the zero one.
