fosco (Mar 31 2022 at 21:12):

A coend is a certain universal object attached to a functor of type Cop×C→DC^{op}\times C \to DCop×C→D; a way to produce such a functor is to take a covariant F:C→DF : C \to DF:C→D, a contravariant G:Cop→DG: C^{op}\to DG:Cop→D, a "product" ∗:D×D→D*:D\times D \to D∗:D×D→D, and consider the functor (c,c′)↦Gc∗Fc′(c,c')\mapsto Gc * Fc'(c,c′)↦Gc∗Fc′.

Dually, for an end, you might want to consider two functors F,G:C→DF,G : C\to DF,G:C→D of the same variance, and then a functor [−,−]:Dop×D→D[-,-] : D^{op}\times D \to D[−,−]:Dop×D→D, and then the functor (c,c′)↦[Fc,Gc′](c,c')\mapsto [Fc, Gc'](c,c′)↦[Fc,Gc′].

But this is not the only way to obtain such objects! There's plenty of functors that are not "decomposable" into F∗GF*GF∗G as above.

fosco (Mar 31 2022 at 21:14):

If the domain of F,GF,GF,G is small and their codomain is SetSetSet, the end of F∗GF*GF∗G always exist; it might be far from interesting, but you can compute it.

fosco (Apr 01 2022 at 19:42):

What you claim is not exact, but the tensor product of vector spaces (or R-modules, for that matter) can be characterised as a coend, yes. A left R-module is a functor R→AbR\to AbR→Ab, regarding the monoid (R,⋅)(R,\cdot)(R,⋅) as a category with a single object (in fact, an AbAbAb-enriched category with a single object!); dually, a right R-module is a functor Rop→AbR^{op}\to AbRop→Ab. The "functor tensor product" of the two is their tensor product as modules (or more precisely, its underlying abelian group)

fosco (Apr 01 2022 at 19:44):

Slightly more conceptually, you can define a bicategory ModModMod whose 0-cells are rings (all unital rings), and where the hom-category Mod(R,S)Mod(R,S)Mod(R,S) is the category RModS{}_R Mod_SR​ModS​ of left-RRR, right SSS-bimodules.

In this perspective, given modules M:R→SM : R \to SM:R→S and N:S→TN : S\to TN:S→T, their composition as 1-cells is the R,TR,TR,T-bimodule M⊗SNM\otimes_S NM⊗S​N.

fosco (Apr 01 2022 at 19:50):

In both cases the claim is easy to motivate because the coequaliser that defines the tensor product (modding out the abelian group M⊗NM\otimes NM⊗N by RRR-bilinearity) is precisely the coequaliser yielding the coend that defines the functor tensor product, identifying M,NM,NM,N as functors.

In the second picture, ModModMod "is" a bicategory of profunctors, and in fact can be identified with the subcategory of AbAbAb-enriched profunctors between "1-object AbAbAb-enriched categories", i.e. unital rings. Profunctor composition is defined by a coend, the same coend that defines the aforementioned (functor) tensor product.
