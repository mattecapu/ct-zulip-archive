John Baez (Jan 22 2025 at 08:02):

Just one comment for now - it's late here.

Then, very basically, what does it mean to be a "reflexive" graph? I might be overthinking it.

A reflexive graph has a set VVV of vertices, a set EEE of edges, two maps s:E→Vs: E \to Vs:E→V and t:E→Vt: E \to Vt:E→V, but also a map i:V→Ei: V \to Ei:V→E such that

s∘i=t∘i=1Vs \circ i = t \circ i = 1_Vs∘i=t∘i=1V​

In other words, it's a graph where every vertex is equipped with a distinguished edge from that vertex to itself.

By the way, I rarely understand what people mean when they "I may be overthinking it". Usually my undergraduate students said this when they were underthinking something. But here we have a math term, and if one doesn't know the definition one can't understand any sentence containing that term. It's not really a matter of thinking, it's a matter of finding out the definition. So I'm glad you asked!

David Egolf (Jan 26 2025 at 18:17):

On the topic of reflexive graphs, I think a reflexive graph as described above amounts to a presheaf/functor from (a category that describes how the edges and vertices relate) to (the category of sets). This should mean that the category of reflexive graphs is really nice - it should inherit limits and colimits from the category of sets, for example. In particular, this means you can take pullbacks in this category, which is important for setting up its category of spans.

John Baez (Jan 26 2025 at 19:18):

They are very different kinds of categories so you shouldn't expect the latter to directly inherit properties from the former. RGraph\mathsf{RGraph}RGraph is a category of presheaves, so it's a topos of a particularly simple and nice sort. Span(RGraph)\mathbf{Span}(\mathsf{RGraph})Span(RGraph) is a bicategory, and a compact closed symmetric monoidal bicategory. But it's a specially nice one, which Walters and company called a [[cartesian bicategory]].

When we water Span(RGraph)\mathbf{Span}(\mathsf{RGraph})Span(RGraph) down to a category Span(RGraph)\mathsf{Span}(\mathsf{RGraph})Span(RGraph) by working with isomorphism classes of spans, we get a category that's nothing like a topos. If I remember correctly, binary products in this category are the same as binary coproducts, which is what we expect from linear algebra:

A×B≅A+BA \times B \cong A + BA×B≅A+B

In a topos, products distribute over coproducts, which is what we expect from set theory:

A×(B+C)≅A×B+A×C A \times (B + C) \cong A \times B + A \times CA×(B+C)≅A×B+A×C

John Baez (Jan 26 2025 at 19:24):

Still, all the exceptionally good properties of RGraph\mathsf{RGraph}RGraph should transmute into different good properties of Span(RGraph)\mathbf{Span}(\mathsf{RGraph})Span(RGraph). This is already at work when we consider why Span(RGraph)\mathbf{Span}(\mathsf{RGraph})Span(RGraph) is a cartesian bicategory (a generalization of a 'category of relations'.)
