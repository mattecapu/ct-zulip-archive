John Baez (Jan 19 2021 at 05:59):

FinSet is "essentially small" : it's equivalent to a small category. So I would not worry about that case.

For the other examples, it's quite safe to assume your set, measure space, etc. has cardinality less than some inaccessible cardinal. If you do that - e.g., replace Set\mathsf{Set}Set by the category of sets Setκ\mathsf{Set}_\kappaSetκ​ whose cardinality is ≤κ\le \kappa≤κ for some inaccessible κ\kappaκ - you'll get a category that's essentially small. You can then replace Setκ\mathsf{Set}_{\kappa}Setκ​ by an equivalent small category X\mathsf{X}X and work with that. I bet everything will be fine and you'll never notice the difference.

Here's what I mean about the conditional independence thing. An element of S→M((B→M(T))×A)S\to M((B\to M(T))\times A)S→M((B→M(T))×A) is a thing that I give an SSS to, and it gives me an AAA together with something that I can give a BBB and it will give me a TTT. So the TTT can depend on the SSS and the BBB, but the AAA can only depend on the SSS. It's not quite the same thing as a morphism S×B→M(T×A)S\times B \to M(T\times A)S×B→M(T×A) with a conditional independence relation, but it seems like they ought to be isomorphic.
