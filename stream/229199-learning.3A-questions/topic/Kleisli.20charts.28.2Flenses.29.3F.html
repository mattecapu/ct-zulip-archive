Topic: Kleisli charts(/lenses)?

Harrison Grodin (Apr 28 2026 at 17:34):

A chart from (A−,A+)(A^-, A^+)(A−,A+) to (B−,B+)(B^-, B^+)(B−,B+) is typically represented as a pair of functions f:A+→B+f : A^+ \to B^+f:A+→B+ and f♭:A+×A−→B−f_\flat : A^+ \times A^- \to B^-f♭​:A+×A−→B−. Equivalently, using exponentials, it's a single function (f,f♭):A+→(B+×(A−⇒B−))(f, f_\flat) : A^+ \to (B^+ \times (A^- \Rightarrow B^-))(f,f♭​):A+→(B+×(A−⇒B−)).

I've seen @David Jaz Myers (and likely others) generalize charts such that the backwards map is "effectful" using some monad MMM, i.e. f♭:A+×A−→M(B−)f_\flat : A^+ \times A^- \to M(B^-)f♭​:A+×A−→M(B−). I've recently encountered a situation, though, that instead looks like a combined map A+→M(B+×(A−⇒M(B−)))A^+ \to M(B^+ \times (A^- \Rightarrow M(B^-)))A+→M(B+×(A−⇒M(B−))), where the "forward part" is also effectful, and the "reverse part" happens under that same monad. (This map can't literally be broken down using the universal property of the product, since MMM need not preserve products or anything.) For a concrete example, the MMM I have in mind is the finitely-supported distributions (ie, free convex set) monad.

Has anyone seen this structure before? It feels like a sort of "Kleisli chart"...

Jules Hedges (Apr 28 2026 at 21:30):

You can do this for any symmetric monoidal category: a morphism (A−,A+)→(B−,B+)(A^-, A+) \to (B^-, B^+)(A−,A+)→(B−,B+) is an element of the coend ∫MC(A+,M⊗B+)×C(M⊗A−,B−)\int^M \mathcal C (A^+, M \otimes B^+) \times \mathcal C (M \otimes A^-, B^-)∫MC(A+,M⊗B+)×C(M⊗A−,B−), aka an equivalence class of triples. (If ⊗\otimes⊗ is a cartesian product this is equivalent to the basic definition of charts). This is a standard way to generalise lenses (the reference for that is Riley, Categories of optics) and it can be usefully generalised more than that too (see Profunctor optics: A categorical update). I never saw anybody write down that it works for charts too, but it's foklore for sure

Bruno Gavranović (Apr 28 2026 at 23:27):

In addition to what was said already, perhaps its useful to point out another "extreme" direction you can take this to. Using lens terminology, you can distinguish between

  • the forward category C\mathcal{C}C together with an action of some monidal category M\mathcal{M}M on it
  • the backwards category D{D}D, and the action of some monoidal category M′\mathcal{M'}M′ on it
  • the "connecting tissue" of a lax monoidal functor M×M′→Set\mathcal{M} \times \mathcal{M'} \to \mathbf{Set}M×M′→Set that mediates the state between the forward and the backward pass.

Of course, all of this works for charts too, which is why I'm mentioning it. While not directly stated at the level of the charts, I unpacked all of this in my thesis (Section 4.3 Weighted Optics), and the trick of flipping the element of the coend that Jules mentioned should apply here too, without a problem.

I found it useful in providing a unifying story of both probability-shaped processes, and also gradient-based ones in a way where you can get a handle on the type of process taking part in each specific component of the lens/chart. It sounds like this might be what you're after here

Jules Hedges (Apr 29 2026 at 10:22):

If anyone wants a small puzzle: In the case that C\mathcal CC is cartesian, Lens(C)\mathbf{Lens} (\mathcal C)Lens(C) and Chart(C)\mathbf{Chart} (\mathcal C)Chart(C) are both fibred over C\mathcal CC and are fibrewise opposites. In the monoidal case neither of them are fibred over C\mathcal CC, there should be some 2-categorical sense in which they are probably fibrewise opposite over Copara(C)\mathbf{Copara} (\mathcal C)Copara(C), but this is much less obvious and afaik not written down anywhere

Jules Hedges (Apr 29 2026 at 10:24):

The main reason that's important is because you want them to be fibrewise opposites in order to form the monoidal version of Myers' double category of formal commutative squares where horizontal 1-cells are lenses and vertical 1-cells are charts
