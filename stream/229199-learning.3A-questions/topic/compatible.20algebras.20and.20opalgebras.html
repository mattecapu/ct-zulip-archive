Topic: compatible algebras and opalgebras

Nathanael Arkor (Nov 23 2021 at 14:31):

Let ttt be a monad on an object aaa in a 2-category. We can consider algebras and opalgebras for ttt (also called "left-modules" and "right-modules", see [[module over a monad]]). An algebra is a 1-cell x:b→ax : b \to ax:b→a and a 2-cell tx⇒xtx \Rightarrow xtx⇒x satisfying some conditions and an opalgebra is a 1-cell y:a→by : a \to by:a→b and a 2-cell yt⇒yyt \Rightarrow yyt⇒y satisfying some conditions. It seems natural then to consider a pair of an algebra and an opalgebra such that the two 2-cells ytx⇒yxytx \Rightarrow yxytx⇒yx are equal. Do these pairs have a name (alternatively: can anything interesting be said about them), or is there some observation that makes them not worth considering? (These are not the same as endo-bimodules, but they do appear when defining the composition of bimodules.)

Nathanael Arkor (Nov 23 2021 at 14:37):

An additional potential condition one could impose is that xy≅txy \cong txy≅t, which is satisfied for the Kleisli or EM object, or for (op)algebras induced by adjunctions generating ttt.
