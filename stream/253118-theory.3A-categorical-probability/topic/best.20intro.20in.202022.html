Paolo Perrone (Oct 05 2022 at 14:21):

Okay, here are a few things that could help you.

Written resources:

  • Chapter 1 of my PhD thesis has an intuitive overview, and has most of the material that you saw in my tutorial video;
  • Section 2 of this paper has a little overview of the idea behind Markov categories;
  • The bulk of the theory of Markov categories, with many details, can be found in this seminal paper by Tobias Fritz;
  • Two of the most recent results in the field are a categorical proof of de Finetti's theorem and a categorical characterization and generalization of Markov properties for graphical models.

Introductory videos, besides the videos of the Categorical Probability and Statistics Workshop (link for who didn't know it), could be the following:

  • An improvised talk of mine about Markov categories;
  • A (real) talk by Tobias Fritz on Markov categories and de Finetti's theorem;
  • This talk by Tobias Fritz about categorical probability and the law of large numbers (work in progress).

@Tobias Fritz, @Tomáš Gonda, @Dario Stein, and others may have more material.

There is also the computer-science side of things, but I feel I'm not the right person to explain that part.
