Consider the (easier) case where all the EbE_bEb​ are equal, so that the total space is just a product. (But we allow the measures debde_bdeb​ to vary, so that we still have a "dependent product" feeling.)
In this case we want debde_bdeb​ to be measurable in bbb in the sense that for every measurable A⊆EbA\subseteq E_bA⊆Eb​, the function b↦deb(A)b\mapsto de_b(A)b↦deb​(A) is measurable. This is precisely saying that we have a Markov kernel, (equivalently, a regular conditional distribution, or a disintegration).

Paolo Perrone (Jan 22 2025 at 14:35):

Well, the good news is that measurable spaces are very "coarse", and a lot of nice measurable spaces turn out to be isomorphic. (For example, R\mathbb{R}R, R2\mathbb{R}^2R2, S1S^1S1 and every separable Banach space of dimension >0 are isomorphic as measurable spaces!)
In particular, the only standard Borel spaces (which always admit disintegrations) are, up to iso, R\mathbb{R}R and its retracts (which are N\mathbb{N}N and the finite sets).
So, in very very many situations, it is not a real loss of generality to assume that the EbE_bEb​ are all the same (take them to be R\mathbb{R}R).
