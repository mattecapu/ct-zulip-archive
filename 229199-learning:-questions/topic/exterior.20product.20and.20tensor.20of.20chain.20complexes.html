Amar Hadzihasanovic (May 05 2021 at 12:21):

I would like to understand how these facts are connected.

  • The exterior product of differential forms takes a ppp-form ω\omegaω and a qqq-form η\etaη and returns a (p+q)(p+q)(p+q)-form ω∧η\omega \land \etaω∧η, such that ω∧η=(−1)pqη∧ω\omega \land \eta = (-1)^{pq} \eta \land \omegaω∧η=(−1)pqη∧ω.
  • The tensor product of chain complexes K⊗LK \otimes LK⊗L is generated by a (p+q)(p+q)(p+q)-chain x⊗yx \otimes yx⊗y for each ppp-chain xxx in KKK and qqq-chain yyy in LLL. The tensor product is symmetric with the symmetry defined by x⊗y↦(−1)pqy⊗xx \otimes y \mapsto (-1)^{pq} y \otimes xx⊗y↦(−1)pqy⊗x.
  • The generalised Stokes theorem establishes a kind of duality between differential ppp-forms on a manifold and singular ppp-chains in the manifold.

Altogether these make me think that the sign (−1)pq(-1)^{pq}(−1)pq in the exterior product is “the same” as the sign (−1)pq(-1)^{pq}(−1)pq in the symmetry on the tensor product of chain complexes. But I am failing to find a source that explains the connection. Can anyone help?

Simon Burton (May 05 2021 at 13:49):

Even before you get to chain complexes you can consider Z\mathbb{Z}Z-graded vector spaces with the same tensor (forget the boundary operation) and the same symmetry operation x⊗y↦(−1)pqy⊗xx \otimes y \mapsto (-1)^{pq} y \otimes xx⊗y↦(−1)pqy⊗x. Exterior product in grade zero is then the same thing as symmetric product in grade 1. So that's how I think of exterior product as a graded thing, it's the symmetrization of odd graded vector spaces...
