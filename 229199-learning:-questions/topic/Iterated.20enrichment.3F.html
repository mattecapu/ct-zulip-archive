Bruno Gavranovic (Mar 06 2021 at 12:52):

Can we do something like "iterated enrichment" where we consider chain of enrichments, and does that form something like a category?

In more detail:
Category being enriched in itself is like saying CCC is enriched in VVV and in some sense CCC is equivalent to VVV. Is there a way we do "iterated enrichment" i.e. where I could say CCC is enriched in VVV, and VVV is enriched in WWW and, say, CCC is equivalent to WWW?
It seems interesting to think about what place SetSetSet and CatCatCat have in it.

Fawzi Hreiki (Mar 06 2021 at 13:01):

Mike Shulman (Mar 06 2021 at 14:54):

If VVV is closed symmetric monoidal, then to give a closed symmetric monoidal VVV-enriched category CCC with copowers is equivalent to giving a closed symmetric monoidal ordinary category CCC and a symmetric monoidal adjunction V⇄CV \rightleftarrows CV⇄C. The right adjoint can then be applied homwise to make any CCC-enriched category into a VVV-enriched category. And of course, symmetric monoidal adjunctions can be composed.

Mike Shulman (Mar 06 2021 at 14:54):

Unfortunately this is kind of folklore; I'm not sure of a reference.

Mike Shulman (Mar 06 2021 at 14:55):

BTW, the word "equivalent" in your original question doesn't seem appropriate; I can't think of a nontrivial sense in which a VVV-enriched category is equivalent to VVV.

Mike Shulman (Mar 06 2021 at 15:00):

One place I'm familiar with where this sort of double enrichment arises naturally is in equivariant topology, see e.g. section 6.3 of http://www.math.uchicago.edu/~may/PAPERS/GMR.pdf.
