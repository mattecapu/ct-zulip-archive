Amar Hadzihasanovic (Jan 01 2021 at 12:03):

@Jonas Frey Why not call that “pointwise” or “objectwise” order-reflecting? It seems consistent with the use in e.g. “(co)limits of presheaves are computed pointwise/objectwise”.
You identify a morphism in CCC with a morphism of (enriched) presheaves on CCC via the Yoneda embedding; then “doing things pointwise” means “evaluating the presheaves at each object of CCC”. In this case you're saying that f:A→Bf: A \to Bf:A→B seen as the morphism of presheaves f∗:C(−,A)→C(−,B)f_*: C(-,A) \to C(-,B)f∗​:C(−,A)→C(−,B) is pointwise order-reflecting.
