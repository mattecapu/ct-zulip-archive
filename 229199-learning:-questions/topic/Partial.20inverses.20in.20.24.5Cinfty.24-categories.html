Reid Barton (May 11 2021 at 17:43):

John Baez (May 11 2021 at 18:30):

Remember, the simplicial set that looks like a 2-simplex contains degenerate simplexes of all higher dimensions. I think these provide all the higher data.

John Baez (May 11 2021 at 18:32):

(When I say "the simplicial set that looks like a 2-simplex", I really mean the "simplicial 2-simplex". There's a simplicial set called the simplicial n-simplex, which is really the representable of the object [n]∈Δ[n] \in \Delta[n]∈Δ. It contains a lot of higher-dimensional simplexes, but they're all degenerate.)
