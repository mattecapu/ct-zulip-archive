John Baez (Feb 22 2021 at 22:04):

Fermat's Last Theorem is now a corollary of a result called the modularity theorem; 90% of the work in proving FLT is proving the modularity theorem (or at least a big enough special case to get the job done), and the modularity theorem is vastly more interesting than FLT.

A good intro is Diamond and Shurman's book rather insolently titled A First Course in Modular Forms. (This link just gives you the table of contents and the introduction.)
