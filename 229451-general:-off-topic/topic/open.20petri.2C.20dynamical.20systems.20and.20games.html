Topic: open petri, dynamical systems and games

{% raw %}

Daniel Geisler (May 15 2020 at 02:12):

How do open petri nets, dynamical systems and game relate to each other in terms of generality?

Fabrizio Genovese (May 15 2020 at 08:07):

First of all, there are different flavors of open petri nets, which very unfortunately go by the same name

Fabrizio Genovese (May 15 2020 at 08:09):

So, if by "open petri nets" you mean the kind @John Baez does, which makes use of cospans (that can come in different flavors), then I guess you could compare this to some formalizations of dynamical systems that use cospans as well

Fabrizio Genovese (May 15 2020 at 08:10):

About game theory, if you look at open games, you'll see that they are monoidal categories. Since Petri nets present free monoidal categories, you may think about petri nets as "ways to present games", which may be similar to meta-games

Fabrizio Genovese (May 15 2020 at 08:12):

But, in general, the three things you have listed are still in the process of being studied/formalized categorically. At least in the case of open Petri nets, there are still many incompatible approaches that need to be unified. So i don't know if someone is giving some serious thinking about how to relate these things with each other. At least to me it feels like there's still too much to do in each field to seriously attempt to really draw similarities between them
