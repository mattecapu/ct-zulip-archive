David Tanzer (May 04 2020 at 03:41):

For those here who make presentations by hand drawing them, what specific techniques do you use? As a rough cut, I used my whiteboard and photographed with phone. I like the palpable expressivity and erasability of the whiteboard, and the way the different colored pens look. On the other hand, I ran into lighting issues using the camera. It tended to look dim, with a tan background (it was evening). But when I added extra lights, there was a brightness gradient if the bulb was off to the side. And if the bulb was too centered, there was a hotspot of brightness created by the reflectivity of the whiteboard. For a plan B, I may give up on the whiteboard and use plain white paper, with a bright overhead lamp, and some kind of apparatus for holding the iphone in place. Do people use schemes with drawing software / tablets which feel truly natural?
