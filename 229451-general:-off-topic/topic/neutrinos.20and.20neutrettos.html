John Baez (Feb 07 2021 at 21:38):

For Sustainability Week at ETH Zurich I'm going to give a talk modestly titled "Theoretical physics in the 21st century". I want to rapidly review the progress in 'fundamental physics' in the 20th century and how it slowed down after 1980 - according to a certain precise and widely-used definition of 'fundamental physics'. My point will be that now physicists should do other things.

As part of this review I got sucked into a little rabbit-hole trying to find out who first predicted the existence of various particles.

The muon neutrino is a specially tricky case. It turns out to have been predicted around 1942, but some people at the time called it the "neutretto". I decided to blog about it just so all the information is easy to find in one place:
