John Baez (Feb 06 2021 at 20:27):

Someone reminded me of this old advice of mine:

"To keep learning things, it's crucial to keep asking lots of
questions, which involves making a fool of oneself. I often
notice that the most famous mathematicians and physicists are
the ones who ask the most questions, including a fair number
of dumb questions, in seminars - while meanwhile the grad students,
completely lost, are busy trying to look like they know what's going
on. Of course, if they work it correctly, people with a reputation
for being smart can get into the position of being allowed to
ask lots of questions: everyone knows they're smart, they don't
need to prove it, so they can act dumb and get away with it."
-- John Baez, sci.physics 8 Dec 1997.
