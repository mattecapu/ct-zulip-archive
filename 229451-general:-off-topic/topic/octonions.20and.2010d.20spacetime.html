John Baez (Nov 11 2020 at 00:09):

I just wrote another article in my series:

  • Octonions and the Standard Model (part 8).

This starts giving a geometrical explanation of why we can describe the symmetry group E6\mathrm{E}_6E6​ of the exceptional Jordan algebra in terms of 10d Minkowski spacetime. It involves the octonionic projective plane OP2\mathbb{O}\mathrm{P}^2OP2, so as a warmup I look at the complex projective line CP1\mathbb{C}\mathrm{P}^1CP1, which is isomorphic to the 'heavenly sphere': the starry sky you'd see all around you if you were floating in space.

E6\mathrm{E}_6E6​ acts on OP2\mathbb{O}\mathrm{P}^2OP2 much as the Lorentz group acts on the heavenly sphere.
