Eric Forgy (Jan 28 2021 at 18:18):

For what it's worth, the Julia Zulip had a lengthy conversation - still ongoing actually - about archiving streams and, as a result, we are currently just archiving specific, well delineated, streams. For example, we have a #helpdesk, which is not archived, and a separate #helpdesk (published), which is archived publicly. Especially since this Zulip is not public. Some people might not appreciate having their comments available publicly. It is probably fine, but it might also be worth having a conversation before making all public streams archived / published.

Eric Forgy (Jan 28 2021 at 21:48):

Our original motivation for publishing stream archives was because we wanted certain streams, e.g. #helpdesk, to be indexed and searchable via Google. If there are no privacy concerns (most of us use our real names here) and although this Zulip is practically public, that is a little different than making everything here indexed and searchable by Google. Matteo Capucci (he/him) (Jan 28 2021 at 22:13):

I think Eric is raising an important issue. Indeed, even if the Zulip server is somewhat public, the feeling of intimacy is definitely here.
I personally feel the pros are more than the cons here, and that since this is, at then end of the day, a 'workspace', people should write and behave in publicly acceptable ways with or without a Googlable archive keeping them accountable to the rest of the world.
That said.
I can hide streams from the archive. So we could think about not archiving some of the less technical streams (#general, #general: meta, #general: off-topic), and even have some 'dark pit of oblivion' where we could move streams we don't want to archive.
Also let me remark that, while the internet remembers, the archive itself does not: if you edit/delete a message, that action is gonna be mirrored in the archive in the following 50 minutes.
