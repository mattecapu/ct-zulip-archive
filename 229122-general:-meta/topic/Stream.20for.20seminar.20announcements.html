Nathaniel Virgo (Apr 23 2020 at 08:36):

I'm finding that I sometimes miss really interesting talks just because I forget they're happening. This is partly due to my own inefficiency, but also because there are several different streams for seminar announcements, and they tend to get lost in the discussion threads.

I'm wondering if it might make sense to have a stream that's strictly for announcements only, where we can find the date, time and abstract of each talk, a reminder when it's about to start, the Zoom link, and the video once it's uploaded after the talk, but go elsewhere for discussion? That would also be a good way to have announcements and reminders for the various different seminars all in one place.

I realise one issue is that discussions will sometimes happen in the announcements threads anyway, and that might make this impractical until we can move topics between streams. But until then a possible solution is just to have a "discussion" topic within the announcements stream and always move discussions there.

I don't know how workable this is, but it seemed worth suggesting as a potentially useful way to organise things.
