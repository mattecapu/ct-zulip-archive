--- This code of conduct is strongly inspired by #haskell code of conduct on FreeNode. Any suggestion is appreciated ---
The ACT channel is a very friendly, welcoming place to hang out, teach and learn. The goal of ACT is to encourage learning and discussion of applied category theory and category theory in general. As part of this we welcome newbies, and encourage teaching.
This is not just a homework group, and technical, research-related questions are also encouraged. If a question results in a very long thread though, it is probably wiser to move discussion elsewhere for interested parties.

To maintain a friendly, open culture, the following is required:

  • Low to zero tolerance for ridiculing questions. Insulting users is unacceptable. New users should feel entirely comfortable asking questions.
  • Helpful answers should be encouraged. Acknowledgements in public may act as a reward for providing a good answer.
  • Avoid getting frustrated by negative comments and ambiguous questions. Approach them by asking for details (i.e. Socratic questioning), rather than challenging the competence of the writer (ad hominem). As the group grows, we see a diverse range of people with different backgrounds getting accustomed to ACT. Be patient and take satisfaction from spreading knowledge.

-- If you have any suggestion regarding the CoC just message any of the moderators in this group. -
