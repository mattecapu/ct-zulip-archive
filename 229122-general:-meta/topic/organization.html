T Murrills (Mar 27 2020 at 05:04):

one idea: it might be nice to have reading groups, possibly both for discussing papers and reading through books! There are a couple ways I can think of organizing them:

  • as topics in their subject-relevant stream, e.g. a topic in the stream #philosophy for Laws of Form.
  • as entire streams (i.e. a whole stream devoted to a given book or paper, e.g. a stream for Laws of Form), with different topics in the stream for different sections of the piece of media. This has the downside of proliferating streams, but the upside of the content's structure being more-or-less exposed, so that people can potentially read (and ask questions/discuss) asynchronously.
  • in a devoted Reading Groups stream, with a topic for each piece of media.

T Murrills (Mar 27 2020 at 05:11):

ah, i see there is already a reading group in #category theory ! in any case, I'd like to suggest the second option for ease of navigating reading groups, and also bring up the potential for providing a mechanism to encourage/organize the formation of reading groups. For instance, it might be useful to have a stream dedicated to reading group formation, where people could suggest materials, gauge interest, and coordinate the formation of a reading group.
