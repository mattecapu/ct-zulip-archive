Nathaniel Virgo (Apr 10 2020 at 13:11):

I had a question earlier about probability theory and logic. There didn't seem to be an appropriate stream - I don't know enough about #theory: topos theory to know if it should be there, it was too specific for #general, didn't seem quite right for #theory: category theory or #practice: applied ct . I posted in #learning: basic questions, but was rightly called out for that, since the question is not very beginner level. The resolution was that a new #theory: logic stream was created and I moved my post there, which is a fine place for it.

But I wonder whether this shows a need for some kind of catch-all stream, other than #general or #learning: basic questions . I don't know whether "advanced questions" is a good name or not, but the idea is just to have a place where we can post technical questions / discussions that don't belong in basic questions but also don't have an obvious 'theory' or 'practice' stream to go in.

I suggest this partly because I think there are some disadvantages to coarse-graining things too finely by topic - just as a personal preference I would prefer to have fewer streams, just because it would make navigating a bit easier. Having a good general-purpose stream for technical discussions would remove some of the need to create new streams for potentially niche topics. (I'm not saying that's the case for #theory: logic necessarily, but it does have quite a bit of overlap with #theory: topos theory and #theory: type theory, which may or may not mean it ends up being a bit low-traffic in practice.)

tl;dr I think a stream along the lines of "advanced questions" or "mathematics: other" might be a useful addition.
