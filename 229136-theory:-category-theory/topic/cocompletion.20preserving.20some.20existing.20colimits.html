Topic: cocompletion preserving some existing colimits

Reid Barton (Dec 04 2020 at 23:48):

Is there a standard reference for the following fact:
Let CCC be a small category and SSS a set of colimit cocones in CCC; then the category of presheaves on CCC which take the cocones of SSS to limit cones is the free cocompletion of CCC subject to the condition that the colimit cones of SSS are preserved.

Reid Barton (Dec 04 2020 at 23:48):

(Preferably a reference where understanding the statement of the theorem is easier than proving it yourself. I'm looking at you Basic Concepts of Enriched Category Theory...)

John Baez (Dec 05 2020 at 01:29):

Nathanael Arkor (Dec 05 2020 at 03:57):

The standard reference is Theorem 6.23 of Basic concepts, but a more approachable reference may be Velebil–Adámek's A remark on conservative cocompletions of categories, which at least sketches the proof.

Reid Barton (Dec 05 2020 at 13:24):

I think neither of these sources really contains the exact statement above, because they both construct the cocompletion by some kind of transfinite construction (e.g., closure under colimits), which is necessary if you want to adjoin only certain types of colimits. In this special case of a small category CCC and adjoining all colimits, there's this direct description of the cocompletion, and it takes more work to extract this description from the general construction (which is not hard, but then it is not a hard theorem to begin with).
