Stream: theory: category theory

Topic: Enriched laxness and tensor products

{% raw %}

Reuben Stern (they/them) (Jun 17 2020 at 12:29):

Let V\mathcal{V}V be a monoidal category, and C\mathcal{C}C and D\mathcal{D}D be V\mathcal{V}V-categories. Is there a good notion of a lax V\mathcal{V}V-functor F:C→DF: \mathcal{C} \to \mathcal{D}F:C→D? Thereafter, is there a good notion of the lax Gray tensor product for V\mathcal{V}V-categories, corepresenting pairs of lax V\mathcal{V}V-functors? Ultimately I'm looking toward the enriched (∞,1)(\infty, 1)(∞,1)-category world, where 2-categories are just categories enriched in categories, so this would recover the usual lax Gray tensor product.

Reid Barton (Jun 17 2020 at 14:17):

Seems to me you need some sort of extra structure on V\mathcal{V}V for this--the first one that comes to mind being a 2-category.

Reuben Stern (they/them) (Jun 17 2020 at 14:28):

If V\mathcal{V}V is presentably monoidal, then we can view V\mathcal{V}V as a V\mathcal{V}V-category, so maybe that's enough?

Reid Barton (Jun 17 2020 at 14:28):

Well, what do you expect to happen for V=Set\mathcal{V} = \mathrm{Set}V=Set, say?

Reid Barton (Jun 17 2020 at 14:29):

I think viewing V\mathcal{V}V as a V\mathcal{V}V-category only helps if you then have some other reason to be able to treat V\mathcal{V}V as basically Cat. For example, a directed interval object or something similar.

Reid Barton (Jun 17 2020 at 15:31):

Or from another point of view, how do you expect to get out lax functors as opposed to oplax functors?

Reuben Stern (they/them) (Jun 17 2020 at 15:34):

ah I see what you mean. I guess I have been thinking of V\mathcal{V}V as a 2-category secretly

John Baez (Jun 17 2020 at 19:36):

I don't think there should be a notion of lax V\mathcal{V}V-functor unless V\mathcal{V}V is a 2-category or more. After all, lax preservation of composition of 1-morphisms demands something like 2-morphisms.

John Baez (Jun 17 2020 at 19:39):

If V\mathcal{V}V has something like an interval object you could probably use that to define lax V\mathcal{V}V-functors. I say "something like" an interval object because there are various notions of interval object, and you'll need to choose one with the right properties to get the job done.
